AI Customer Data Deletion Request Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet when a customer asks to delete, correct, or stop using their information and your team has also used AI tools, chatbots, automations, vendors, or prompt history that may contain the same data.

AI-CUSTOMER-DATA-DELETION-READY

Use this when

  • A customer asks to delete, correct, or stop using their information.
  • Staff may have pasted customer data into AI prompts, summaries, chatbots, or support tools.
  • A vendor has separate retention, deletion, export, or support-ticket steps.
  • You need a safe owner-reviewed reply without inventing legal promises.

1. Copy/paste deletion request record

Customer request date/time: [DATE + TIME]
Customer/contact identifier: [NAME / ACCOUNT / EMAIL / PHONE]
Request type: [delete / correct / opt out / restrict use / stop contact / other]
Original channel: [email / form / phone / SMS / chat / in person]
Customer wording copied exactly: [PASTE]
Systems that may contain the data: [CRM / email / call notes / forms / files / AI tool / chatbot / vendor]
AI tools or vendors to check: [LIST]
Source proof for each system checked: [LINK / SCREENSHOT / TICKET ID]
Owner/reviewer: [NAME + ROLE]
Customer-safe response status: [received / in review / completed / cannot confirm yet / needs legal/privacy review]
Next action and due date: [ACTION + DATE]
Final proof location: [URL / INTERNAL FILE / TICKET ID]

2. AI/vendor cleanup checks

  • Prompt history, uploaded files, chat transcripts, and saved AI memories.
  • CRM notes or AI-generated summaries copied into records.
  • Helpdesk, chatbot, form, call-transcription, or meeting-note vendors.
  • Admin deletion/export tickets and expected support response times.
  • Whether deletion affects training data, retained logs, or backups.
  • Whether the business can verify completion or only request it from a vendor.

3. STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • Stop if the request includes legal, health, financial, HR, children’s, regulated, or safety-sensitive data.
  • Stop if no owner can verify where the data went.
  • Stop if a vendor promise, deletion right, legal deadline, or compliance status is unknown.
  • Stop if deleting a record could break accounting, safety, warranty, or dispute evidence.
  • Stop if customer-facing wording has not been reviewed by a responsible owner.

4. Customer-safe AI review prompt

You are helping a small business triage a customer data deletion/correction request. Use only the verified facts below. Do not invent legal rights, deletion completion, compliance status, vendor capabilities, backup behavior, customer consent, privacy-policy promises, or legal advice. Return: 1) missing evidence, 2) systems/vendors to check, 3) safe customer reply wording, 4) owner-review questions, 5) records that may require retention review, and 6) STOP AUTOMATION triggers.

Customer request: [PASTE EXACT WORDING]
Known systems/vendors: [LIST]
Current proof/ticket IDs: [PASTE]
Business policy/privacy text: [PASTE]
Data categories involved: [LIST]
Decision needed: [ACKNOWLEDGE / REQUEST MORE INFO / COMPLETE / ESCALATE]

5. Safe acknowledgement template

Thanks for letting us know. We received your request about [DATA/ACCOUNT]. We are reviewing the systems where this information may appear and will confirm the next step through [OFFICIAL CHANNEL]. We will not use AI to make legal or privacy decisions automatically; a responsible owner will review the request and vendor records before we send a final update.
