AI Search Environmental and Sustainability Claim Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before publishing, updating, or letting AI summarize product, packaging, service, disposal, or operations pages that mention recyclable, compostable, biodegradable, non-toxic, low-carbon, green, sustainable, eco-friendly, or other environmental claims.

Start with the free AI search visibility audit

Marker: AI-SEARCH-ENVIRONMENTAL-CLAIM-CHECKLIST-READY

Use this when: a retailer, maker, cleaning company, contractor, ecommerce brand, property team, consultant, or local service business publishes environmental wording that AI search tools might quote as a promise.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI invent environmental benefits, certifications, recyclability, biodegradability, compostability, carbon reduction, non-toxic status, safety, legal compliance, disposal instructions, or warranty/policy exceptions. A responsible human must verify every claim against an official source, certification, test, supplier document, legal-reviewed policy, or owner-approved proof first.

Environmental claim source card

FieldWhat to recordSafe example
Claim or wordingExact public phrase being reviewed, affected page, product/service, and audience.“Packaging is curbside recyclable in participating programs” on product FAQ
Official sourceSupplier spec, certificate, recycling program guidance, lab/test document, internal policy, or owner-approved proof.Packaging supplier spec dated 2026-05-22 plus city recycling guidance link
Limits and qualifiersGeography, facilities, material conditions, exclusions, customer action, expiration/review date.Applies to paper mailer only; remove labels; availability depends on local program.
Approved public wordingExact wording the business is willing to publish without overstating the claim.Our paper mailer may be recyclable where local facilities accept it. Check your local program before disposal.
Do-not-say listUnsupported shortcuts AI must not infer.Do not say zero-waste, carbon-neutral, universally recyclable, non-toxic, chemical-free, biodegradable, or certified unless the source proves it.
Reviewer / escalation pathWho approves missing or risky environmental claims before public use.Operations owner and qualified advisor review any certification, disposal, or compliance statement.

Customer-safe copy blocks

Short page note: Our approved environmental wording for [product/service/packaging] is: [approved wording]. Environmental handling can depend on local facilities, use conditions, and current supplier documents. If your use case involves safety, food contact, children/pets, regulated disposal, or compliance decisions, contact [owner/support path] before relying on a general answer.
Risky-answer handoff: I do not want to guess about [recyclability / compostability / carbon / certification / disposal / non-toxic status]. I am routing this to [review owner] and will answer only from the approved source card.
Page update checklist:
- Paste the latest environmental claim source card.
- Remove unsupported green, eco, non-toxic, recyclable, biodegradable, carbon, disposal, or certification claims.
- Add limits, qualifiers, geography, and review date.
- Mark any missing proof as NEEDS HUMAN REVIEW, not as a published answer.
