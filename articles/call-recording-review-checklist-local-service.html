Call Recording Review Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Use this worksheet before an owner, dispatcher, or manager turns a recorded call or transcript into CRM updates, coaching notes, or a customer follow-up. It keeps call-review decisions tied to the actual recording instead of memory, assumptions, or AI guesswork.

Use this when

  • A missed call, booked call, estimate request, complaint, or warranty callback was recorded and needs a clean CRM note.
  • A manager wants coaching feedback without shaming staff or inventing caller intent.
  • A transcript summary conflicts with the recording or lacks source evidence.

Important: this is an internal call-review worksheet, not legal, HR, or compliance advice. Follow recording-consent rules, company policy, and privacy obligations before storing, sharing, or summarizing recordings.

2) STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI invent caller intent, consent, service-area fit, call outcome, price, diagnosis, warranty coverage, refund status, appointment availability, emergency instructions, or team-member promises.
  • NEEDS OWNER REVIEW: calls involving safety hazards, active leaks, electrical risk, legal threats, refund/chargeback disputes, warranty coverage, discrimination/harassment concerns, or complaints about staff.
  • Do not paste private customer details or raw recordings into AI tools unless the business has approved that tool and data use.
  • Separate coaching notes from customer-facing CRM notes; do not put internal staff feedback into a reply template.

3) Safe follow-up snippets after a reviewed call

Need one missing detail:
Thanks for speaking with us. Before we confirm the next step, can you send the job address and a short description of what you need help with?

No promise until owner review:
Thanks — I am sending this to our team for review so we do not overpromise on price, timing, or scope. We will follow up with the next safe step.

Transcript conflict:
I want to make sure we recorded this correctly. Can you confirm whether the main issue is [issue] and whether [missing detail] is still accurate?

  • Which repeated coaching labels show a script, training, or staffing gap?
