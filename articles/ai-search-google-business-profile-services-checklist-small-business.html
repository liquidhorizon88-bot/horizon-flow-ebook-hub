AI Search Google Business Profile Services Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this free worksheet before updating a Google Business Profile so AI search tools, map results, and human buyers can understand what you actually offer, where, and how to book.

Best fit: local service businesses, clinics, consultants, property managers, agencies, franchise branches, and multi-location operators that rely on Google Business Profile, Maps, and local AI summaries for discovery.

Human-review rule: do not let AI invent categories, addresses, service areas, hours, emergency availability, staff names, licenses, prices, booking promises, reviews, photos, or guarantees. A business owner or location manager should verify every field before publishing profile updates.

10 checks before AI tools summarize your Google Business Profile

  1. Primary category fit. Confirm the primary category matches the main revenue-driving service, not a vague or aspirational label.
  2. Secondary categories. Add only real services you provide consistently; remove categories that create misleading leads.
  3. Service names. Use plain buyer language for each service and avoid stuffing cities, claims, or repeated keywords into service names.
  4. Service descriptions. Explain who each service is for, what is included, what is not included, and when a buyer should call to confirm fit.
  5. Products or packages. If you use product/package entries, list real packages with current inclusions, price ranges only when approved, and source-of-truth links.
  6. Service areas. Keep cities, neighborhoods, ZIP codes, and travel limits accurate. Do not imply coverage where you cannot reliably serve.
  7. Hours and response windows. Separate office hours, emergency availability, appointment windows, holiday exceptions, and after-hours lead response.
  8. Booking and contact path. Verify phone, appointment link, website link, quote form, message settings, and call tracking labels.
  9. Proof and photos. Match services to real photos, reviews, project examples, certifications, or FAQs without inventing proof.
  10. Stop-rule fields. Flag anything involving safety, regulated advice, prices, warranties, finance, medical/legal terms, emergency claims, or customer privacy for owner review.

Quick owner review before publishing

  • Can a buyer tell whether this business actually offers the service in their area?
  • Do services, categories, hours, photos, reviews, and website pages agree?
  • Are any claims about response time, guarantees, price, licensing, or emergencies unsupported?
  • Would a receptionist or dispatcher know how to route every lead generated by these profile fields?
  • Is there a source-of-truth page for each important service or package?
