Why this matters

Delivery questions often mix tracking scans, porch photos, signature names, pickup counters, carrier claims, refund policies, replacement exceptions, and fraud-sensitive customer history. If AI reads one tracking line without the supporting policy source, it can overpromise a replacement or accuse the customer without proof.

This worksheet gives owners and support teams a lightweight source-of-truth card before AI-visible answers mention delivery status, signatures, photos, refunds, replacements, or claims.

Delivery proof source card

FieldCopy/paste valueReview rule
Order / shipment ID[Order number, shipment ID, item/SKU, customer-visible order link]Match the order and item before commenting on delivery, replacement, refund, or claim status.
Carrier scan status[Delivered / attempted / held / returned / pickup ready / exception / unknown]Quote the carrier scan exactly; do not translate an exception into a final outcome.
Proof type[Signature, porch photo, GPS scan, pickup receipt, locker scan, driver note, none]Use only proof the business can actually access and share under policy.
Signature / pickup name[Name or blank; relationship if known; ID checked yes/no if recorded]Never invent household, employee, front-desk, or neighbor identity from a first name.
Photo / location note[Porch, mailbox, locker, front desk, side door, business address, unclear]Human review before saying the photo proves correct delivery or customer responsibility.
Customer-safe next step[Check address, contact carrier, open claim, wait window, replacement review, refund review]Owner/support review before promising refund, replacement, credit, denial, fraud finding, or claim reimbursement.
CRM/support label[delivered-not-received, signature-dispute, pickup-proof, claim-review, replacement-review]Keep labels factual; do not label a customer as fraudulent without policy-backed review.

Customer-safe wording starter

Thanks for flagging this. I’m checking the carrier scan, delivery proof, and our order policy before giving a final answer. The current source shows [scan/proof], and the next review step is [claim/replacement/refund/support review] by [owner/team/time].
I do not want to guess from one tracking line. If the proof is unclear, we will review [photo/signature/pickup/claim source] and confirm the approved next step through [official support channel].
