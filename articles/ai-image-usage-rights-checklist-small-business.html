AI Image Usage Rights Checklist for Small Businesses

Generated images, stock graphics, vendor photos, customer photos, screenshots, and before/after visuals can all help a small business look more credible. They can also create unsupported claims when AI captions, landing pages, or social posts imply permission, licensing, results, location, product fit, or customer proof that nobody verified.

AI-IMAGE-RIGHTS-READY

Use this before publishing images in

  • Service pages, product pages, landing pages, case studies, comparison pages, or AI-search source pages.
  • Google Business Profile posts, social updates, paid-ad drafts, email headers, or newsletter graphics.
  • Gumroad previews, lead magnets, carousel images, thumbnails, or marketplace gallery assets.
  • Customer proof blocks, before/after galleries, screenshots, staff photos, vendor logos, badges, or partner visuals.

STOP PUBLISHING

Do not publish the image or AI-written caption if the business cannot verify the source, license/permission, customer consent, sensitive content, approved claim wording, disclosure need, and owner review status.

AI caption and alt-text review prompt

You are helping a small business review an image before publishing it on a website, social post, marketplace page, or AI-search source page.
Use only the usage-rights card below. Do not invent license status, customer permission, product results, location, date, staff identity, warranty, rankings, before/after impact, endorsement, or ownership.

Return:
1. Missing rights or permission proof.
2. A safe public caption under 35 words.
3. Accessibility-focused alt text under 125 characters.
4. Claims this image is NOT allowed to support.
5. Required disclosure, if any.
6. STOP PUBLISHING if legal, privacy, customer-consent, platform-terms, or owner-review details are unclear.

Safer wording examples

  • Use: “AI-generated illustration of an office workflow dashboard; not a customer screenshot.”
  • Avoid: “This dashboard helped customers recover 40 percent more revenue.”
  • Use: “Licensed stock image used as a generic service-team header.”
  • Avoid: “Our certified technicians completed this exact job same-day.”
  • Use: “Customer-approved project photo; address and identifying details removed.”
  • Avoid: “Before and after proof of guaranteed results.”

Image-risk labels

  • safe_generic_illustration — concept art, no customer proof implied.
  • license_needs_source — asset may be usable but license proof is missing.
  • customer_permission_required — person, home, property, review, testimonial, or project proof involved.
  • disclosure_required — generated, staged, edited, stock, or mockup image could be mistaken for real proof.
  • owner_review_required — regulated, sensitive, before/after, legal, medical, safety, financial, or endorsement context.
  • do_not_publish — source, rights, privacy, or claim support cannot be verified.
