Use this worksheet before your website, chatbot, AI-search snippet, Google Business Profile Q&A, dispatcher script, estimate page, or booking form answers questions about where you serve, when travel fees apply, whether emergency service is available, and what happens when a customer is outside the normal territory.

Use this when

  • Customers ask whether you serve a city, ZIP, county, neighborhood, building type, or out-of-area location.
  • AI tools may mix old service pages, ads, review snippets, maps listings, and dispatcher notes into one unsupported coverage promise.
  • The team needs safe wording that routes travel-fee, emergency, scheduling, franchise/branch, and exception questions to a human reviewer.

Why service-area answers need a source card

Service-area promises affect revenue, dispatch time, reviews, ad waste, and customer trust. If AI search or a chatbot guesses coverage, it can promise service in a restricted territory, hide travel fees, invent emergency availability, route leads to the wrong branch, or create conflict when a customer calls expecting a price or appointment you never approved.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI publish service-area, travel-fee, trip-charge, emergency-availability, franchise/branch, out-of-area, appointment-window, or minimum-charge claims until the owner/dispatcher verifies the current territory source and approves exact public wording.

Internal note: Service-area / travel-fee review. Verify address, ZIP/city/county, branch/technician ownership, travel-fee rule, emergency status, minimum job requirement, existing customer status, and approved public wording before replying.

Review before publishing

  • Does the page match the current dispatcher map, CRM territory rules, Google Business Profile service areas, ads, booking software, franchise/branch rules, and technician coverage?
  • Does the wording avoid unsupported promises about travel fees, fee waivers, emergency ETA, same-day availability, warranty coverage, permits, or guaranteed arrival windows?
  • Are old city pages, landing pages, directory listings, and ad extensions updated or labeled as outdated if the territory changed?
  • Is there a clear human owner for borderline locations, out-of-area exceptions, safety/emergency issues, accessibility concerns, high-value jobs, and fee disputes?
