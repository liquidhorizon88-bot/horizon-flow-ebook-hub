AI Search Service Package Fit Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before publishing or refreshing a pricing, package, membership, retainer, plan, or service-tier page that buyers and AI answer engines may summarize.

Use this when

  • Customers ask which package is right for them.
  • Your site lists tiers but not enough fit/exclusion detail.
  • AI tools could recommend the wrong plan, membership, or service level.
  • You need plain-language package copy without invented prices or guarantees.

Why this matters

AI search can turn vague package pages into wrong recommendations.

Service package pages often say “best value,” “premium,” or “most popular” without explaining who each tier is actually for. Buyers then ask search engines and AI tools to compare plans. If the page lacks fit rules, exclusions, proof, and escalation notes, the summary may invent a recommendation, price, guarantee, or eligibility rule that your team never approved.

The goal is not more hype. The goal is a source-of-truth package page that helps humans choose and gives AI answer engines enough reviewed context to avoid unsupported claims.

Five checks before publishing a package page

  1. Name the customer situation, not just the tier. Example: “Best for one-time setup help” is clearer than “Pro.”
  2. Separate included from excluded. Call out add-ons, custom work, parts, taxes, third-party fees, emergency fees, and required approvals.
  3. Write the not-a-fit paragraph. Say when a buyer should request a custom quote, talk to a specialist, or choose a different option.
  4. Link to proof and policies. Package pages should connect to FAQs, guarantee/warranty pages, examples, testimonials, terms, refund/cancellation policies, and contact paths where appropriate.
  5. Add a review date. Prices, plan names, service areas, and response windows change. Add a visible or internal review cadence so stale package copy does not keep getting quoted.

Human-review stop rules

  • Do not invent price, discount, renewal, cancellation, refund, warranty, financing, response-time, or availability terms.
  • NEEDS OWNER REVIEW for package comparisons, legal/policy language, guarantees, eligibility, custom quotes, or claim language that could affect revenue.
  • STOP PUBLISHING when proof links, policy references, package names, or review dates are missing.
  • Keep AI in drafting mode. AI may organize the worksheet and suggest safer wording; a responsible human approves the final page.
