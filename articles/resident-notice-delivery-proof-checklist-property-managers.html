Resident Notice Delivery Proof Checklist for Property Managers

Use this checklist before a property manager, assistant, or AI tool summarizes, sends, or confirms a resident notice. It keeps official channel, delivery proof, resident-safe wording, and manager/legal review attached to each notice workflow.

Start with the free missed-lead checklist See Local Lead Rescue System

When to use it

Use this for resident notices that need proof of delivery or careful wording: entry notices, inspection notices, maintenance access windows, lease reminders, document requests, utility notices, policy reminders, payment-plan paperwork, application/move-in documents, and manager-approved follow-up after a resident says they did not receive a notice.

This is not legal advice and not a substitute for lease, local-law, fair-housing, or attorney review. It is a source-card and workflow checklist so AI does not invent delivery status, deadlines, rights, fees, or consequences.

AI review prompt

You are helping a property-management team review a resident notice workflow. Use only the source card and attached delivery records. Do not invent legal requirements, delivery status, resident consent, lease terms, fees, deadlines, access rights, or consequences.

Task:
1. Identify the exact notice type, official source, approved text/version, delivery channel, delivery proof, and response window.
2. Separate what is proven from what still needs manager/legal/fair-housing review.
3. Draft a short resident-safe acknowledgement that does not threaten, overpromise, or claim unverified delivery.
4. Add CRM labels and the next human owner/date.
5. Add a STOP AUTOMATION note for any legal, fee, access, eviction, accommodation, safety, or disputed-delivery issue.

Resident-safe snippets

  • Confirming receipt path: “We’re checking the notice record for [notice type] and will confirm the official delivery channel and timestamp before advising next steps.”
  • Missing proof: “I do not see complete delivery proof in the record yet. I’m routing this to [owner] for review before we rely on the notice status.”
  • Resident says they did not receive it: “Thanks for letting us know. We’ll review the official notice log and delivery method, then follow up through the approved channel.”
  • Internal task label: “Notice proof review needed — do not mark delivered until [source] and [delivery proof] are attached.”

STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • Stop before AI says a legal notice was served, delivered, valid, invalid, late, or enforceable.
  • Stop before changing fees, deadlines, access rights, lease status, application status, lockout handling, or eviction-related wording.
  • Stop if the delivery proof is missing, disputed, bounced, returned, incomplete, or only verbally described.
  • Stop if the notice involves accommodation, disability, protected-class, safety, domestic-violence, harassment, habitability, or fair-housing-sensitive facts.
  • Stop before sending notices through SMS or informal channels unless consent/policy/source records allow it.
