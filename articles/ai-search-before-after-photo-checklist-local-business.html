AI Search Before-and-After Photo Checklist for Local Businesses

Before-and-after photos can make a service page or portfolio page more trustworthy, but only when the business documents what is verified, what is private, and what must not be claimed. Use this worksheet before publishing project photos that AI tools or buyers might summarize.

Copy the checklist Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Project photos are strong but captions are vague.
  • A portfolio page has images without verified service details.
  • Before/after examples could imply claims you have not approved.
  • You want a reusable proof note before publishing or pitching the page.

Why this matters

Photos are proof only when the facts around them are controlled

Local-service sites often have useful project photos hidden in galleries, job-completion messages, Google Business Profile posts, or technician phones. Those photos can support service pages, portfolio pages, FAQ answers, and AI-search summaries, but captions and alt text must stay grounded in approved facts.

The goal is not keyword-stuffing. The goal is to help buyers and answer engines connect a real image to a verified project, service, location, permission status, and next step without inventing outcomes.

Copy/paste artifact

Before-and-after photo proof worksheet

Project/photo set: [job name or internal ID]
Page where photos will appear: [portfolio / service / location / case study URL]
Reviewer: [owner / manager / marketer]
Last reviewed: [date]

1. Photo inventory
Before photo file/URL: [file]
After photo file/URL: [file]
Optional in-progress photo: [file]
Are both photos from the same job? [yes/no/unknown]
If unknown, do not label as before-and-after.

2. Approved project facts
Service performed: [verified service]
Project type: [repair / install / cleanup / inspection / maintenance / other]
Service area or city: [only if approved]
Date or season: [only if approved]
Crew/member named publicly? [yes/no]
Customer or property details hidden? [yes/no]
Permission to use image publicly: [yes/no/not required]

3. What the photos actually prove
Visible change: [what can be seen]
Supported claim: [e.g., damaged panel replaced, overgrown area cleared]
Unsupported claims to avoid: [price, timeline, warranty, safety, health, code compliance, permanent result, customer satisfaction]

4. Caption draft
Before caption: [accurate visible fact]
After caption: [accurate visible fact]
Combined caption: [one sentence that does not exaggerate]

5. Alt text draft
Before alt text: [accurate image description]
After alt text: [accurate image description]
Do not hide sales copy or keyword lists in alt text.

6. Privacy and risk check
Faces, addresses, license plates, children, private rooms, documents, account numbers, medical/legal/financial details visible? [yes/no]
If yes, crop, blur, replace, or do not publish.

7. Human-review stop rules
Owner/manager must approve before publishing if the photos imply emergency response, safety outcome, warranty, guarantee, price, insurance coverage, licensing, before-and-after performance result, health/legal/financial outcome, or customer identity.

Implementation

Publish the first photo set safely

  1. Choose one project photo set that has clear permission and no sensitive details.
  2. Fill the worksheet before writing captions or asking AI for copy.
  3. Add captions near the photos, not just hidden alt text.
  4. Link the photo set to the relevant service page, portfolio page, FAQ, or booking/contact page.
  5. Set a review date so stale service, pricing, warranty, or availability assumptions do not live forever.

Human review rule: if the photo is evidence for a claim, the responsible owner or manager reviews the claim before it goes live.
