AI Search Warranty Claim Status Checklist for Small Businesses

Warranty pages, help-center answers, AI search snippets, and support macros can overpromise when coverage, proof, manufacturer review, repair status, replacement approval, and refunds live in different systems. Use this source card before publishing any customer-facing warranty-claim answer.

STOP PUBLISHING guardrail

WARRANTY-CLAIM-STATUS-CARD-READY: Do not publish, index, email, or let an AI assistant answer warranty-claim status unless a human can point to the exact policy, order, product, evidence, manufacturer/vendor status, repair/replacement decision, refund route, and approved customer wording. Mark missing facts as NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.

Warranty claim status source card

FieldSource to verifySafe wording note
Claim / case ID[Ticket, RMA, warranty claim, order, serial/lot, customer account]Match the customer and item before discussing coverage or status.
Policy version[Warranty page URL, PDF, purchase-date terms, SKU/model-specific exception]Do not quote a generic warranty if the product has model, date, region, or channel exceptions.
Eligibility evidence[Proof of purchase, registration, install/usage record, photos/video, diagnostic notes, inspection result]Say what evidence is received and what is still missing.
Review owner[Support lead, manufacturer, vendor, technician, manager, insurer, legal/compliance]Clarify whether the decision is internal, vendor-owned, or still pending.
Current status[Received / needs info / under review / approved / denied / repair pending / replacement pending / closed]Use only the current verified status. Never infer approval from intake.
Customer option[Repair, replacement, credit, refund, parts order, return inspection, escalation, no coverage]Do not promise a remedy without owner/manufacturer approval.
Timing and next update[Verified next check date, vendor ETA, inspection window, shipping/parts status]Use “next update by” rather than invented completion dates.
Money-sensitive language[Deductible, diagnostic fee, shipping cost, labor, restocking, refund, chargeback, credit]Route fee, refund, and chargeback language to owner/bookkeeper review.
Do-not-say list[Unsupported phrases AI must not repeat]Examples: “covered,” “free replacement,” “refund guaranteed,” “manufacturer approved,” “lifetime warranty.”

Customer-safe status snippets

Needs more evidence

“Thanks — we received your warranty request for [item/case]. Before we can confirm coverage, we still need [missing evidence]. Once that is received, [owner/team] will review it and update you by [date/time].”

Under manufacturer review

“Your claim is currently with [manufacturer/vendor] for review. We have [evidence received] on file. The next verified update is [next step/date]. We do not want to guess at the outcome before they respond.”

Approved next step

“Your warranty claim has been approved for [approved remedy]. The next step is [repair/replacement/return/shipping step]. Any fees, exclusions, or timing notes are listed here: [source/link].”

Denied or not covered

“After review, this request is not covered because [source-backed reason]. If you want us to review additional evidence, please send [specific evidence] and we will route it to [review owner].”
