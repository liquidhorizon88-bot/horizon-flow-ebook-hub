No-access visit text templates for local service businesses

A technician can lose an hour when the gate is locked, nobody answers, a key is missing, pets are loose, or the work area is unsafe. Use these templates to document the visit, ask for the next step, and reschedule without blaming the customer or inventing fees.

Use these when

  • The customer is not home or not responding during the arrival window
  • The gate, lockbox, key, code, parking, or access instructions are missing
  • The team needs to reschedule while documenting time, attempts, and next owner
  • A pet, unsafe area, or policy issue means the technician should not enter

Guardrail first

Do not invent fees, blame, arrival proof, access permission, or safety claims

These templates are for existing customers or active leads who already booked or requested service. Before sending, verify SMS consent, company no-show/access policies, technician safety rules, and whether fees must be approved by an owner or manager.

  1. State facts only: arrival window, contact attempt, gate/lock/key issue, and next needed action.
  2. Keep fees conditional: say “our office will review any trip-fee policy,” not “you owe a fee,” unless verified and approved.
  3. Protect safety: do not instruct technicians to enter unsafe areas, handle loose pets, or bypass locks.
  4. Log the handoff: dispatcher, technician, office manager, customer, property manager, or owner.

Copy/paste no-access visit text templates

1) No answer during arrival window

Hi {first_name}, this is {business_name}. Our technician is at/near {service_address} for {service}. We have not been able to reach you for access. Please reply ACCESS if you are available now, or RESCHEDULE if we should have the office find another time.

2) Locked gate or missing code

Hi {first_name}, we are ready for {service}, but the gate/entry code for {access_point} is not working or not on file. Please reply with the approved access instructions, or reply CALL if the office should contact you.

3) Key, lockbox, or onsite contact missing

Hi {first_name}, the team cannot locate the approved key/lockbox/contact for {service_address}. We do not want to guess or enter without permission. Please reply with the approved access step or RESCHEDULE.

4) Pets or unsafe access

Hi {first_name}, for safety, we cannot start {service} while {pet_or_access_issue} is unresolved. Please secure the area or tell us the best reschedule time. The office can help if you have questions.

5) Leaving after documented access attempts

Hi {first_name}, we tried to access {service_address} at {time_1} and {time_2}, but {access_issue} was still unresolved. The office will review the next available time and any policy notes before rescheduling.

6) Friendly reschedule handoff

Hi {first_name}, no problem — we can reschedule once access is ready. Reply with two preferred windows and any gate/key/contact instructions, and the office will confirm what is available.

7) CRM note for dispatch/office

CRM note: {technician} attempted {service} at {service_address}. No-access reason: {reason}. Contact attempts: {attempts}. Do not mark complete. Next owner: {office_or_customer}. Fee/policy review needed: {yes_no}. Next check: {date}.

Human review stop rules

  • Do not claim the customer refused access unless the evidence is documented.
  • Do not mention trip charges, lockout fees, deposits, or cancellation penalties unless policy and approval are verified.
  • Do not tell a technician to enter a locked area, bypass a gate, handle animals, or proceed where safety is uncertain.
  • Do not include private gate codes, lockbox codes, alarm codes, or sensitive access details in a public note or insecure message.
  • Have a dispatcher, owner, manager, or property manager review unusual access, fee, safety, or tenant/landlord situations.
