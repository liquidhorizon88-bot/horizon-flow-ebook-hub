3. Safe customer wording starters

Short notice

We are updating the price for [offer] on [effective date]. Your current record shows [verified impact]. Please use [official page/contact] for the current details; we do not want to guess about discounts, grandfathering, refunds, or renewal terms.

When facts are missing

Thanks for asking. I do not want to give you an unsupported answer about the price change. I am checking the approved policy/source record and will confirm the exact effective date, affected plan, and next step through [official channel].

5. Public-page QA before publishing

  • Does the public wording match the current price source, not a sales rep memory or old PDF?
  • Are effective dates, timezones, and affected customer segments explicit?
  • Are grandfathering, coupons, credits, refunds, and cancellation paths either verified or omitted?
  • Are old FAQ, pricing, checkout, help-center, PDF, and chatbot sources updated or linked to the new source?
  • Is there an owner-approved rollback note if the change is delayed?
