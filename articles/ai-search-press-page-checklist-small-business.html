AI Search Press Page Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet when your site has press mentions, podcast appearances, awards, local features, association badges, or media logos that AI tools might summarize. The goal is simple: show real proof, date it, link it, and keep unsupported claims out.

Who should use this

This is for owners, consultants, agencies, local businesses, creators, and service firms that have a press, media, awards, or “as seen in” section. It is especially useful when old mentions, logo bars, or vague award claims could confuse buyers or answer engines.

Human-review rule: do not invent press mentions, awards, dates, rankings, customer results, endorsements, or affiliations. If a proof item cannot be verified by a source URL, screenshot, contract, permission note, or internal record, leave it out or label it as internal.

Copy/paste press-page worksheet

  1. Business identity: exact public business name, short description, service area, category, and preferred founder/spokesperson name.
  2. Press mention: publication, podcast, newsletter, event, association, award program, or partner page name.
  3. Source URL: live link to the mention, archive link, or internal proof location if the public link is gone.
  4. Date and status: published date, award year, active/inactive status, and when the proof was last checked.
  5. What it proves: one sentence about what the mention actually supports: expertise, local presence, certification, interview, community involvement, or award.
  6. What it does not prove: limits that should not be implied, such as “not a guarantee,” “not a customer result,” “not an endorsement,” or “not current pricing.”
  7. Permission: confirm logo, quote, screenshot, and trademark usage rules before displaying the item.
  8. Buyer question answered: trust, experience, local credibility, qualifications, process, safety, or service fit.
  9. Internal links: link to the relevant service, about, case study, staff bio, contact, booking, or FAQ page.
  10. Review owner: assign a person to re-check press and award facts at least quarterly.

AI drafting prompt for a proof-safe press page

You are helping rewrite a small-business press/media page for clarity and AI-search visibility.
Use only the facts provided below. Do not invent press mentions, awards, quotes, dates, rankings, endorsements, customer results, or publication names.
For each proof item, write:
- Source/mention name
- Date or year
- What this proof supports
- What it does NOT prove
- Suggested internal link
- One buyer-friendly sentence
Flag any item that needs permission, a source URL, or human review before publishing.
Facts:
[PASTE VERIFIED PRESS/AWARD/MENTION FACTS HERE]
Business:
[BUSINESS NAME / SERVICE AREA / AUDIENCE]
Tone:
[PLAIN, CREDIBLE, NOT HYPE]

Press-page stop rules

  • Do not imply endorsement unless the source explicitly says it is an endorsement and usage is permitted.
  • Do not use logo bars blindly. Check each logo's usage policy and whether the relationship is current.
  • Do not quote rankings without context. Include date, category, region, and source.
  • Do not turn appearances into outcomes. A podcast interview does not prove customer results.
  • Do not bury stale proof. Old mentions can be useful, but label year/status honestly.

Simple page structure

  1. One-sentence credibility summary with no hype.
  2. Verified press/award cards with date, source, and context.
  3. Short “use of logos and mentions” note if logos are displayed.
  4. Links to related About, Staff Bio, Portfolio, Reviews, and Contact pages.
  5. Quarterly review date and owner in the internal CMS or checklist.
