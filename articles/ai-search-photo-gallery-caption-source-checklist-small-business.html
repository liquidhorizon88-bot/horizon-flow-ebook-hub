AI Search Photo Gallery Caption Source Checklist for Small Businesses

Photo galleries and portfolio pages are powerful proof, but AI tools can accidentally turn a simple image into invented claims about the job, location, customer, materials, timeline, warranty, or result. Use this worksheet before publishing captions, alt text, image schema, social snippets, or AI-search answer blocks.

AI-SEARCH-PHOTO-CAPTION-READY

When to use this checklist

  • A contractor, clinic, agency, consultant, property manager, or local business is adding project photos, before/after galleries, portfolio pages, or proof blocks.
  • AI is drafting captions, alt text, schema, case-study snippets, or social posts from images.
  • Images could imply customer permission, outcome, location, material, service type, date, compliance, warranty, or guarantee details.
  • You want AI-search pages to be more quotable without inventing visual proof.

STOP PUBLISHING

Do not publish AI-written captions if there is no approved source for the project date, location, customer permission, work performed, materials used, outcome, warranty, price, or before/after claim.

Safer caption examples

  • Use: “Completed cabinet refinishing project in a residential kitchen; final color approved by the homeowner.”
  • Avoid: “Our team transformed this kitchen in one day and increased home value.”
  • Use: “Technician documenting replaced part before system test; customer details removed.”
  • Avoid: “Emergency repair fixed the issue permanently with full warranty coverage.”

Mistakes to avoid

  • Letting AI identify brands, materials, damage causes, dates, or locations from pixels without source proof.
  • Publishing customer faces, addresses, license plates, or private property details without permission.
  • Turning a photo into an unsupported case study or guarantee claim.
  • Keyword-stuffing alt text instead of describing the image accessibly.
  • Forgetting to update captions when services, warranties, or before/after claims change.
