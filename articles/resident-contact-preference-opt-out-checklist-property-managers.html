Resident Contact Preference and Opt-Out Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before a leasing assistant, maintenance dispatcher, resident-services team, or AI workflow calls, texts, emails, or routes a resident update. It helps confirm which channel is allowed, whether the resident opted out, and when a manager must review the message before anything is sent.

RESIDENT-CONTACT-PREFERENCE-OPT-OUT-READY

When to use this

Use this source card when a resident changes a phone number or email address, asks not to be texted, prefers portal-only updates, wants a caregiver copied, reports a wrong-number text, asks for maintenance updates by a different channel, or when an AI-assisted workflow is about to choose the contact method automatically.

Human-review rule: contact preference is not blanket consent. A property manager should verify the source record, opt-in/opt-out status, lease/policy language, emergency exceptions, privacy limits, and message type before using a channel or involving a third party.

Resident-safe reply snippets

  • Preference update received: “Thanks — we received your contact-preference update. Our team will verify the record before using this channel for resident or maintenance updates.”
  • Text opt-out acknowledged: “We have noted your SMS opt-out request. Future non-emergency updates should use [approved channel] once the record is verified.”
  • Need verified channel: “To protect your account, please submit this contact change through [approved channel] or contact the office directly so we can verify the update before changing the record.”
  • Third-party boundary: “We can note the preferred contact path, but lease, payment, account, complaint, or private maintenance details still require resident or manager-approved authorization.”

STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION if a resident has opted out of SMS/calls or the consent status is unknown.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if AI is about to choose a contact channel without reading the latest CRM consent/preference label.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if the request could expose rent, lease, legal, medical, complaint, account, safety, or maintenance details to an unapproved third party.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if the message mentions harassment, domestic conflict, accessibility/accommodation, law enforcement, welfare check, emergency access, or legal notice.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if a vendor, owner, roommate, emergency contact, or caregiver asks to use a resident's phone/email for convenience without verified permission scope.
