AI Search Source Freshness Monitor for Small Businesses

AI answer engines can repeat old service details, stale prices, expired policies, or outdated proof if your website does not show what changed and when it was reviewed. Use this lightweight monitor before refreshing AI-search pages, FAQs, policy pages, service pages, and proof sections.

Start with the free sample library

Use this when

  • A page cites pricing, service areas, hours, policies, guarantees, staff, awards, or customer proof.
  • Search Console, AI answers, or customer questions suggest old information is still being found.
  • You need a human-reviewed source-of-truth check before publishing AI-assisted updates.
  • You want a monitor that flags stale source URLs without inventing new claims.

Goal: keep AI-search source pages fresh without letting an AI tool invent dates, policy changes, proof, availability, pricing, or customer outcomes.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not publish an AI-updated page when a source URL is missing, private, contradicted by another page, older than the review window, or not owner-approved.

AI audit prompt

You are auditing a small-business web page for AI-search source freshness.

Inputs:
- Page URL or draft text: [PASTE]
- Source URLs and review dates: [PASTE]
- Known changed facts: [PASTE]
- Owner-approved wording: [PASTE]

Task:
1. List every claim that depends on a source URL, policy, offer sheet, review, service-area rule, price, schedule, credential, or customer proof.
2. Mark each claim as VERIFIED, STALE, MISSING SOURCE, CONTRADICTED, or NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.
3. Suggest safer wording only for VERIFIED facts.
4. Do not invent source dates, policy changes, prices, service areas, customer proof, credentials, or availability.
5. Output a table with: claim, source, review date, risk, recommended action, and affected page section.

Source freshness stop rules

  • Do not invent review dates. If a source was not checked by a human, mark it NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.
  • Do not invent old-to-new changes. If there is no change log, say what must be verified.
  • Do not overstate availability. Hours, service areas, emergency response, appointment windows, guarantees, and pricing need an approved source.
  • Do not publish from one source when another page contradicts it. Resolve conflicts first, then update all affected pages.
  • Do not treat AI summaries as sources. AI can help find risks, but the source of truth must be a human-approved business record or public page.

Weekly review workflow

  1. Pick five pages or claims that buyers and AI tools are likely to quote: service pages, policies, FAQs, pricing/offer pages, proof pages, and location/service-area pages.
  2. Paste each claim into the worksheet with its source URL and last reviewed date.
  3. Use the audit prompt to identify missing or stale sources.
  4. Update the source first, then update the page, schema, internal links, and related profiles.
  5. Add the next review date so the page does not drift again.
