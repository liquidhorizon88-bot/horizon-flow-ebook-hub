Technician Handoff Checklist for Local Service Businesses

A lead is not truly handled until the field technician, dispatcher, or owner has the right facts. Use this checklist when a lead moves from inbox or phone call into a route, quote, diagnostic visit, or follow-up task.

Use the free AI Lead Response Quickstart See Local Lead Rescue System

Use this before sending a technician or estimator

  • Best fit: local-service teams that route calls, texts, web forms, marketplace leads, and repeat-customer messages to a technician or estimator.
  • Goal: reduce callbacks, wrong-address visits, missing photos, quote confusion, and unsafe customer promises.
  • Human review rule: a dispatcher, owner, or manager verifies schedule, access, safety, pricing, diagnosis, warranty, payment, and emergency status before customer-facing promises are sent.

Copy/paste technician handoff card

Lead/customer: [name, phone/email, preferred contact method]

Source: [missed call / web form / inbound text / repeat customer / referral / marketplace]

Job type: [requested service, issue summary, property type]

Address/access: [address verified? access notes? gate/pet/tenant/lockbox?]

Photos/files: [attached / requested / missing / not needed]

Customer expectation: [callback / estimate / diagnostic visit / emergency review / reschedule / closeout]

Known constraints: [budget, timing, warranty, HOA/permit, owner approval, safety risk]

Current promise made: [none / callback window / arrival window / quote review / owner will verify]

Technician next action: [call first / review photos / confirm access / inspect / quote / escalate]

STOP AUTOMATION: Do not invent customer history, consent, arrival time, price, diagnosis, warranty coverage, safety priority, payment status, permit/HOA approval, parts availability, or job completion.

Three customer-safe handoff updates

1) Dispatcher is routing to a technician

Thanks, [Name]. I’m routing this to the right person now. Before we confirm schedule, price, diagnosis, warranty, or next steps, we’ll review the current details and make sure the technician has the correct address/access notes.

2) Technician needs one missing detail

Hi [Name] — the technician can review this faster with one detail: [photo/access note/model number/best callback window]. Once we have that, we’ll confirm the safest next step instead of guessing.

3) Owner review is needed before a promise

Thanks for the details. This needs owner/manager review before we promise [price/timing/warranty/emergency priority/payment/refund/completion]. We’ll check the current record and follow up with the verified next step.

AI prompt for a dispatcher-to-technician summary

You are summarizing a local-service lead for technician handoff. Use only the provided notes. Return: customer/source, requested job, verified facts, missing details, access/safety constraints, current promise made, technician next action, customer-safe update, CRM label, and owner-review risks. If a fact is missing, write NEEDS OWNER REVIEW. Do not invent customer history, consent, address, arrival time, price, diagnosis, warranty, payment status, parts availability, emergency priority, permit/HOA approval, or job completion.
