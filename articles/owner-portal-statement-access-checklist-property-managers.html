Owner Portal Statement Access Checklist for Property Managers

Use this checklist before a property manager, VA, or AI assistant answers an owner asking for portal access, statement copies, distribution details, reserve balances, invoice links, or login help.

See the Local Lead Rescue System

STOP AUTOMATION first

STOP AUTOMATION if the message asks for payout timing, trust-account balances, legal/tax/accounting advice, confidential tenant details, ownership changes, banking changes, third-party access, or anything inconsistent with the management agreement. Route to the property manager/bookkeeper before sending.

Owner-safe acknowledgement snippets

  • Portal login help: "Thanks — I’m checking the owner record and approved contact on file before sending or resetting portal access. I’ll route this through the official portal path rather than sharing credentials in this thread."
  • Statement request: "Thanks — I’m checking the statement period and accounting source before replying so we do not send a partial or stale owner statement."
  • Reserve or distribution question: "I’m routing this for manager/bookkeeper review before quoting reserve, invoice, or distribution details."
  • Third-party access: "Before adding or sharing access with another person, we need to verify the authorization path and permission scope on the owner record."

AI review prompt

Review this owner portal or owner statement request.
Return:
1. request type,
2. verified source fields still missing,
3. whether manager/bookkeeper review is required,
4. a safe acknowledgement that does not invent balances, distributions, invoices, taxes, legal advice, tenant-private facts, portal permissions, or credential details,
5. CRM label and next action.
Use NEEDS MANAGER REVIEW if any accounting, access-permission, banking, ownership, reserve, distribution, legal, tax, or tenant-private detail is involved.

How this connects to Local Lead Rescue

Owner portal and statement-access questions often look like simple admin work, but a rushed reply can expose private details or invent payout/accounting promises.
