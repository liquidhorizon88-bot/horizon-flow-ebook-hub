Use this when

  • A customer asks whether a benefit, discount, community, class, appointment slot, download, or support lane is included with membership.
  • AI search may combine old launch pages, pricing tables, social posts, help articles, and checkout text into one overconfident membership answer.
  • The team needs safe wording that does not invent access, eligibility, free upgrades, cancellation exceptions, or lifetime benefits.

Why membership pages need a source card

Membership offers can change quietly: grandfathered tiers, founding-member bonuses, paused benefits, private-community access, booking priority, support levels, renewal windows, and exclusions may live in separate tools. AI systems can turn an expired sales page or old support macro into a public promise the business cannot honor.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI publish membership, member-benefit, eligibility, access, cancellation, grandfathered-tier, or renewal claims until an owner verifies the current source of truth.

Customer-safe reply snippets

Thanks for checking. The current approved membership benefits are listed here: [canonical membership page]. If your question involves a legacy plan, paused account, billing issue, or benefit exception, we need to verify your account record before promising access or adjustments.
I do not want to guess about member eligibility or included perks. Please send [account/email/order detail], and our team will compare it against the current membership terms before confirming benefits, exclusions, renewal timing, or cancellation options.
Internal note: Membership-benefits question. Verify canonical terms, checkout settings, CRM/account status, legacy-tier notes, billing history, and owner-approved exception rules before publishing or sending a final answer.

Review before publishing

  • Does the public page match checkout settings, member platform settings, CRM/account status, and current support policy?
  • Does the answer avoid promising benefits, free access, grandfathered perks, refunds, or exceptions that require manual review?
  • Are old sales pages, launch emails, social captions, PDFs, and help macros redirected or labeled as outdated?
  • Is there a clear human owner for legacy tiers, paused accounts, billing disputes, and eligibility edge cases?
