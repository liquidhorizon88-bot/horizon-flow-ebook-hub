AI Search Portfolio Page Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this free worksheet before AI tools, Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, or buyers summarize your project examples, before/after photos, case galleries, or portfolio pages.

Best fit: contractors, remodelers, designers, consultants, agencies, photographers, landscapers, property managers, clinics, coaches, local service companies, and any small business that uses project examples or visual proof to win trust.

Human-review rule: do not let AI invent project results, customer names, budgets, timelines, locations, certifications, permits, warranties, ROI, safety outcomes, diagnoses, or before/after claims. Use only approved project facts and permission-cleared images.

12 checks before AI tools summarize your portfolio page

  1. Name the project type clearly. Use buyer language such as kitchen remodel, roof repair, bookkeeping cleanup, landing-page redesign, tenant turnover, or CRM setup.
  2. Separate facts from interpretation. Facts are dates, services, materials, deliverables, locations, and approved constraints. Interpretation is why it mattered, what changed, or what the customer valued.
  3. Record permission status. Note whether the customer, tenant, property owner, or client approved public use of names, photos, logos, locations, quotes, and results.
  4. Protect private details. Remove addresses, license plates, faces, security systems, tenant names, financial records, medical details, internal system screenshots, and private customer data.
  5. Describe images for humans and AI. Add alt text and captions that state what is visible without exaggerating quality, safety, compliance, or outcomes.
  6. Use before/after carefully. Clarify the scope and conditions rather than implying guaranteed results for every customer.
  7. Connect each example to a service page. Link the portfolio item to the relevant service, FAQ, pricing, booking, or contact page so AI systems can understand the offer context.
  8. Add evidence where appropriate. Use approved testimonials, review snippets, project specs, public permits, inspection notes, or process photos only when permission and context are clear.
  9. Answer buyer questions. Include project timeline, preparation needed, common constraints, what the customer provided, what the business handled, and when someone should request a quote.
  10. Avoid unsupported superlatives. Do not say best, guaranteed, award-winning, certified, safest, fastest, or highest ROI unless the proof is public and current.
  11. Show limitations. Mention when photos are examples, when results vary, when weather/scope/materials affect timing, and when a human quote is required.
  12. Set stop rules. Owner review is required before publishing regulated claims, safety statements, legal/medical/financial claims, customer results, warranty language, or any client-identifying detail.

Quick portfolio-page QA before publishing

  • Can a buyer understand the project type, service fit, and next step without guessing?
  • Are photos, names, locations, quotes, and results permission-cleared?
  • Does the page avoid private data and unsupported before/after claims?
  • Do captions, alt text, and internal links give AI systems enough context?
  • Would an AI answer system summarize the page accurately without overstating results?
