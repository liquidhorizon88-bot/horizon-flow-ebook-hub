AI Search Free Trial and Demo Policy Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before AI assistants, website chatbots, sales reps, support macros, or answer engines summarize free-trial, demo, sample, freemium, cancellation, or introductory-offer promises that could be stale, incomplete, or invented.

Use this when

  • Your website, marketplace listing, onboarding email, pricing page, support macro, or ad mentions a free trial, demo, sample, freemium tier, consultation, proof-of-concept, or introductory offer.
  • Trial length, eligibility, auto-renewal, cancellation steps, conversion price, included features, or support access differs by plan, region, product, campaign, or customer segment.
  • AI search, chatbots, or sales enablement tools may quote old trial offers after a promotion ended.
  • You need a source-of-truth card before publishing new trial/demo wording.

FREE-TRIAL-DEMO-POLICY-CHECKLIST-READY. STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI invent free-trial length, demo availability, cancellation steps, credit-card requirements, included features, support levels, conversion pricing, refunds, extension eligibility, sample deliverables, data-retention terms, or automatic-renewal promises. Route uncertain wording to the owner, sales lead, support lead, or legal/policy reviewer before public pages change.

AI audit prompt

Review these free-trial/demo policy source cards and the draft public page below.

Goal: find unsupported, stale, or ambiguous trial/demo claims before publication.

Check for invented or unclear:
- trial length, start trigger, extension rules, and eligibility
- credit-card requirements, auto-renewal, conversion price, taxes, fees, and cancellation deadline
- included/excluded features, support level, seats, usage limits, setup help, and data export/deletion
- demo/sample customization, recording, prep requirements, and promised follow-up deliverables
- contradictions across pricing pages, checkout, FAQs, support macros, ads, onboarding emails, and terms

Return:
1. Claims safe to publish exactly as written.
2. Claims requiring owner/support/legal review.
3. Claims that should be removed until source proof exists.
4. A corrected public wording block that does not invent trial or demo promises.

STOP PUBLISHING if any key trial, demo, cancellation, payment, refund, or eligibility detail lacks source proof.

Customer-safe wording snippets

  • Trial availability: “Current trial availability and eligibility are shown on the checkout or pricing page. If a detail differs from an older ad or article, use the checkout/pricing page as the current source.”
  • Demo scope: “The demo shows the workflow and answers setup questions; it does not guarantee custom implementation, pricing, or results unless those items are listed in the written offer.”
  • Cancellation: “Cancellation and billing changes should be handled through the account or support path listed in the current policy.”
  • Uncertain support reply: “I do not want to guess on trial eligibility or billing. I’m routing this to the owner/support lead to confirm the current policy.”

Review checklist before publishing

  • Does the page match the checkout, pricing, terms, onboarding email, support macro, and CRM/source record?
  • Are old campaign pages redirected, noindexed, updated, or clearly expired?
  • Does the wording avoid invented free access, extensions, custom demos, refunds, support levels, or cancellation grace periods?
  • Is there an owner and next review date for trial/demo language?
  • Could a chatbot or AI answer engine quote this page without creating a billing or support promise the business cannot honor?
