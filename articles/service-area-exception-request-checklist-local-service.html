Service Area Exception Request Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Out-of-area leads are not always an automatic no. Use this checklist to decide when a job deserves an exception review, when to send a referral-safe decline, and what proof a dispatcher or owner needs before promising travel, emergency response, fees, or availability.

Copy the exception checklist Get Local Lead Rescue

Use this when

  • A lead is just outside the normal service radius.
  • A high-value job may justify a travel exception.
  • A property manager or referral partner asks for help in a borderline location.
  • Staff need safe wording before promising fees, arrival windows, or coverage.

Why this matters

Exception decisions need a paper trail

Local-service teams lose revenue when every borderline lead is rejected too quickly. They also create trust problems when staff casually promise a trip outside the normal area without owner approval, travel fees, licensing checks, vendor capacity, or realistic timing.

This resource gives dispatchers, front desks, and owner-operators a small source-of-truth card for deciding whether to accept, decline, refer, or hold a service-area exception request.

Customer-safe scripts

Copy/paste replies

Exception review reply:
Thanks for reaching out. Your location is outside our usual service area, but I can ask a manager to review whether we can help. Before we promise a visit, we need to confirm crew availability, travel time, and any minimum/travel fee. What is the service address and the best callback number?

Referral-safe decline:
Thanks for checking with us. That address is outside our current service area, so I do not want to promise a response we cannot support. If you have a preferred provider nearby, I would start there. If our coverage changes, we can revisit future requests.

More-info hold:
We may be able to review this as an exception. Please send the service address, job type, urgency, and any timing constraints. A manager will confirm whether we can take it before any appointment or fee is promised.

Guardrails before any message goes out

  • Do not promise availability, arrival windows, pricing, travel fees, emergency response, permits, insurance, licensing, or exceptions without manager approval.
  • Do not invent referral partners or recommend a specific provider unless that relationship is approved and current.
  • Do not accept work that may cross safety, legal, licensing, warranty, insurance, or jurisdiction boundaries without human review.
  • Do not close a high-value or partner-referred lead as lost until the exception decision and reason are recorded.
