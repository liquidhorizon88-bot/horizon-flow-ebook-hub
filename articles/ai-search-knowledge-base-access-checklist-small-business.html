AI Search Knowledge Base Access Checklist for Small Businesses

Before a small business lets AI draft FAQ answers, website updates, chatbot replies, support snippets, or AI-search content, it needs to know which internal documents the AI is allowed to trust. Use this worksheet to separate approved sources from stale notes, private records, draft policies, and unsupported claims.

AI-SEARCH-KNOWLEDGE-BASE-ACCESS-READY

When to use this checklist

  • A website chatbot, AI assistant, agency, or staff member is drafting customer-facing answers from internal docs.
  • FAQs, service pages, policy pages, and support scripts disagree with old SOPs or notes.
  • AI search tools may quote answers that depend on pricing, guarantees, eligibility, response times, service areas, or policies.
  • You need an owner-approved source map before updating a page for Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, or classic SEO.

STOP PUBLISHING

Do not publish, send, or automate an AI-generated answer if the source document is private, stale, draft-only, customer-specific, legally sensitive, or missing an owner-reviewed approval note.

AI audit prompt

You are helping a small business audit which knowledge-base documents can be used for AI search, website FAQs, chatbot answers, and support replies. Use only the source list provided. Do not invent document dates, owners, permissions, policies, prices, guarantees, response times, customer outcomes, or legal conclusions. Return: (1) approved sources, (2) blocked/private/draft sources, (3) conflicting sources to resolve, (4) risky claims needing owner review, (5) safe customer-facing wording options, and (6) a stop-rule checklist for staff.

Small-business workflow

  1. List the source documents AI may see before connecting any tool.
  2. Remove or mask private customer notes, employee records, contracts, payment details, and draft legal/policy language.
  3. Assign one owner for price, policy, warranty, service-area, and guarantee claims.
  4. Test the AI with five realistic buyer/support questions and check every answer against the approved source card.
  5. Publish only after a human verifies the final wording, links, schema/FAQ markup, and support script.

Mistakes to avoid

  • Letting AI pull from every document in Drive, Slack, Notion, or the CRM without source boundaries.
  • Using old SOPs or PDFs as if they were current policy.
  • Allowing AI to answer pricing, guarantee, warranty, emergency, or refund questions from memory.
  • Publishing FAQ/schema answers that staff scripts cannot support.
  • Forgetting to re-review source access after a tool, vendor, or staff-role change.
