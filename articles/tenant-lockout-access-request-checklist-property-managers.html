Tenant Lockout and Access Request Checklist for Property Managers

Lockouts, lost fobs, gate-code issues, key pickup requests, and after-hours access messages can quickly become safety, fee, lease, vendor, or liability questions. Use this worksheet before a staff member or AI assistant replies so the response stays source-checked, resident-safe, and routed to the right owner.

Use the worksheet Get Local Lead Rescue

When to use this

Use this when a resident reports being locked out, missing a key/fob, needing a gate or door code, requesting emergency entry help, asking someone else to pick up access credentials, or disputing an access/lockout fee. The goal is not to guess the answer; it is to confirm policy, identity, safety, ownership, and after-hours routing before sending a promise.

Resident-safe reply snippets

1. Acknowledge and route without promising entry

Hi [Name], thanks for contacting us. We logged this as [ticket/thread]. Because this involves access to the property or unit, we need to verify the approved process before promising entry, codes, keys, fobs, fees, or timing. We are routing it to [manager/team] and will update you by [time/date].

2. Identity/source details needed

Hi [Name], to route this safely, please use [approved identity/process channel] and include [approved details]. We cannot send codes, keys, fobs, or lockout instructions from an incomplete message.

3. After-hours or safety-sensitive request

Hi [Name], this request has been logged. If this is an active emergency or safety issue, use [approved emergency channel] immediately. For non-emergency lockout/access help, we are checking the approved after-hours process before confirming options, fees, or timing.

4. Fee, refund, or policy dispute

Hi [Name], I can confirm we received the access/lockout question. A manager must review the lease, access policy, and account/source record before anyone promises a fee waiver, refund, deposit change, access exception, or third-party pickup approval.

Human review rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION: do not send AI-written lockout/access replies when identity, emergency status, entry rights, fees/refunds, legal/security issues, third-party pickup, or after-hours vendor approval is unclear.
  • Do not invent: gate codes, fob status, key availability, vendor ETA, manager approval, locksmith coverage, fee waivers, refund eligibility, lease rights, access exceptions, or legal/security instructions.
  • Keep the official channel: document the request, verified identity path, source policy, assigned owner, next-check due time, and final resident update in the PMS/CRM.
