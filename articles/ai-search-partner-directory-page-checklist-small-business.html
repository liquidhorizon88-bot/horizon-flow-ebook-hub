AI Search Partner Directory Page Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this checklist before publishing a partner directory, vendor list, referral page, association page, certification page, resource roundup, or “recommended providers” page that AI search engines may treat as proof. The goal is to make every relationship claim sourced, current, permission-safe, and human-reviewed.

Why partner directories need source-of-truth guardrails

Partner and resource pages often contain relationship claims that become outdated quickly: preferred vendors, certifications, sponsorships, associations, referral partners, resellers, integrations, nonprofit partners, local chambers, or “trusted” providers. If those claims are stale or unsupported, AI search tools can repeat them as fact and create customer, legal, or reputation risk.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not publish or let AI rewrite a partner, referral, endorsement, certification, or association claim unless a human owner verifies the relationship type, permission to list, proof URL or source document, expiration/review date, disclosure language, and approved wording. Use NEEDS OWNER REVIEW for paid sponsorships, affiliate/referral compensation, regulated industries, certifications, nonprofit/government names, customer logos, testimonials, or any claim that implies endorsement.

Copy/paste page blocks

Disclosure: [none/affiliate/sponsor/referral]. Owner: [person]. Next review: [date]. Do not invent endorsement, exclusivity, certification, reseller status, or compensation terms.

AI audit prompt for partner pages

Act as a cautious AI-search source-of-truth reviewer. Use only the verified facts below. Do not invent endorsements, partner status, certification, reseller rights, integration support, affiliate terms, availability, pricing, exclusivity, geographic coverage, customer outcomes, or permission to use logos. Return: 1) unsupported relationship claims, 2) disclosure or permission gaps, 3) stale-source risks, 4) safer public wording, and 5) STOP PUBLISHING if owner/legal/partner review is needed.

Fast QA before publishing

  • Replace vague “trusted partner” wording with exact relationship types and proof links.
  • Add disclosure when the page includes sponsorship, affiliate, referral, resale, or paid placement relationships.
  • Do not imply certification, endorsement, exclusivity, support coverage, or reseller rights from a logo alone.
  • Give each partner/resource claim an owner, last-reviewed date, next review date, and removal trigger.
  • Ask AI to flag unsupported relationship claims before the page is indexed or syndicated.
