Move-in date change request checklist for property managers

Use this worksheet before an AI tool, leasing assistant, resident-services coordinator, or answering service drafts a reply to a tenant or applicant who wants to move in earlier, later, or on a different access date. It keeps the reply tied to verified lease, rent-ready, key/access, inspection, vendor, payment, and policy facts.

Use the free missed-lead checklist

When to use it

  • A resident, approved applicant, leasing agent, or property manager asks to change the move-in date, key pickup window, elevator reservation, or access handoff.
  • The unit may be waiting on cleaning, inspection, maintenance, paint, utilities, certificate, payment, insurance, lease signature, or owner approval.
  • The team needs a safe acknowledgement without promising rent-ready status, fee waivers, prorated rent, concessions, access rights, inspection results, or vendor completion.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI or a front-desk script promise a move-in date, key access, rent-ready status, fee waiver, concession, lease change, prorated rent, utility readiness, vendor completion, or legal/policy exception unless the approved source record explicitly confirms it.

Resident-safe reply snippets

We received your request to change the move-in/access date to [requested date/window]. We are checking the lease, payment, access, and unit-readiness records before confirming anything. We will update you through [official channel] once the approved next step is available.
Thanks for the update. We do not want to promise key pickup, elevator use, or unit readiness until the manager confirms the required records. We are attaching your request to the move-in file now.
At this point, [verified item] is complete and [open item] still needs review. The safest next step is [approved process step]. We will avoid changing rent, fees, dates, or access until the property manager confirms them.

Safe AI handoff prompt

Paste only verified facts into the prompt. Do not include private screening or payment details that are not needed for the move-in scheduling decision.

You are helping a property-management team summarize a move-in date or access change request for internal review. Use only the verified facts below.

Return:
1. Request summary and requested date/access window.
2. Source records already verified.
3. Missing records or dependencies.
4. Resident-safe acknowledgement draft.
5. Promises that must be blocked until manager approval.
6. Recommended CRM label, owner, and next check time.

Do not invent lease status, payment status, unit readiness, inspection results, key access, elevator reservations, utility readiness, fees, concessions, prorated rent, legal/policy exceptions, vendor completion, or move-in dates.

Verified notes:
[PASTE NOTES]

Human-review rule: a property manager, leasing manager, or approved operations lead reviews the summary before any resident-facing message confirms dates, access, rent, fees, concessions, policy exceptions, or unit readiness.
