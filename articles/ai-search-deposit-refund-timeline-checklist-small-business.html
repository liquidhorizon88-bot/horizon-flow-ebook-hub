Why deposit-refund timelines need source control

Refund timing is one of the easiest places for AI answers to overpromise. A support bot or answer engine can turn “usually 5–7 business days” into a guarantee, miss processor delays, ignore documentation requirements, promise exceptions, or confuse deposits, store credits, chargebacks, credits, and refunds. A source card gives the team one approved place to check before publishing or sending a final answer.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI publish deposit-refund, credit, refund-window, chargeback, booking-fee, store-credit, exception, processor-delay, cancellation, or return-timeline claims until the owner/bookkeeper/support lead verifies the current source record and approves exact public wording.

Copy/paste deposit-refund timeline source card

Deposit/refund policy name: [approved public/internal name]
Source owner: [owner, bookkeeper, support lead, operations manager]
Canonical policy URL or internal source record: [link]
Last verified date: [date]
Deposit type: [booking deposit / security deposit / retainer / partial payment / store credit / other]
Refund eligibility rule: [when refundable, non-refundable, partial, credit-only, or review-required]
Required customer documentation: [receipt, invoice, order/job number, cancellation notice, inspection, return proof]
Refund method: [original payment method / credit / check / manual review / not yet approved]
Timeline wording approved for customers: [e.g., after approval, processor timing, business days]
Processor/platform delay caveat: [Stripe/PayPal/bank/card issuer/marketplace timing if known]
Exception owner: [who can approve waiver, partial refund, expedited review, credit, or denial]
Do-not-promise list: [guaranteed date, legal outcome, chargeback result, fee waiver, deposit release, credit amount, cancellation approval]
Manual review triggers: [high amount, disputed charge, legal/lease issue, chargeback, policy exception, angry customer, fraud risk, missing source]
Internal routing label: [deposit-refund-review / support-policy-review / owner-approval]

Customer-safe reply snippets

Thanks for checking. I do not want to guess about refund timing or eligibility. I am verifying the current deposit/refund record and will confirm the approved next step before we promise a date, credit, waiver, or exception.
Your request may need owner or billing review before we can confirm refund eligibility, amount, method, or timing. Please send the order/job number, payment date, cancellation/return details, and any supporting receipt so we can check the current source record.
Internal note: Deposit/refund timeline review. Verify policy source, payment record, eligibility, documentation, processor delay, exception owner, and approved public wording before publishing or sending a final answer.

Review before publishing

  • Does the answer match the current policy page, checkout language, invoice/receipt wording, payment-processor settings, support macros, return/cancellation page, and internal exception rules?
  • Does the wording avoid guaranteed refund dates, chargeback outcomes, legal conclusions, waived fees, automatic approvals, or processor promises?
  • Are old FAQs, landing pages, product pages, marketplace listings, and saved support macros updated or labeled as outdated if refund rules changed?
  • Is there a named human owner for high-dollar refunds, disputed payments, missing documentation, chargebacks, legal-sensitive cases, and policy exceptions?
