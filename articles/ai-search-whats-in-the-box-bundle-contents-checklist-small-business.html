AI Search What's-in-the-Box and Bundle Contents Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this copy/paste worksheet before AI search tools, chatbots, marketplace summaries, or support macros answer “what comes with it?” for a product, kit, package, digital download, or service bundle.

Why this matters

Bundle-content mistakes create preventable support tickets: customers expect an accessory that is not included, assume a digital bonus is part of a cheaper plan, or ask AI tools for setup instructions based on the wrong kit version. This page gives small-business owners a source card that keeps public pages, FAQs, support replies, and AI-generated answers anchored to verified package contents.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI claim that a charger, cable, mounting kit, template, bonus file, warranty card, setup service, training call, subscription month, quantity, or compatible accessory is included unless the current product owner has verified the source and approved the exact wording.

Customer-safe answer template

Thanks for checking. Based on our current product page, {product_name} includes {verified_included_items}. It does not include {not_included_items} unless those are listed in your selected bundle or order confirmation.

Before ordering, please review {canonical_product_url} for the current package contents. If you need {accessory_or_service}, reply here and we can confirm the right bundle or add-on before you buy.

AI audit prompt

You are reviewing AI-generated product or offer copy for a small business. Compare the draft answer against the verified source card only. Do not add new claims. Flag any invented included item, quantity, accessory, digital file, service, subscription, setup support, warranty promise, compatibility statement, bonus, bundle deadline, or return-policy implication.

Return:
1. Safe to publish? yes/no
2. Unsupported claims to remove
3. Questions for owner review
4. A corrected customer-safe answer using only approved source-card wording

Five high-risk bundle-content checks

  1. Accessory assumptions: cables, chargers, mounts, batteries, adapters, licenses, or templates are often assumed but not always included.
  2. Plan and tier drift: a bonus in the premium bundle may not belong in the entry-level plan.
  3. Version and region drift: product kits can change by model year, supplier, country, marketplace, or fulfillment channel.
  4. Digital file confusion: buyers need to know whether they receive a PDF, ZIP, spreadsheet, prompt library, video, editable document, or access link.
  5. Setup promise creep: “includes installation,” “done for you,” “training included,” or “support included” should never be inferred from vague marketing copy.
