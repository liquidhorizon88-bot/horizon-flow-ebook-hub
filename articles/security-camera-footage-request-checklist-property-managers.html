Security Camera Footage Request Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before AI, a resident-services coordinator, leasing assistant, after-hours helper, or virtual assistant answers a request for camera footage, door/gate/package-room logs, incident video, police/insurance documentation, or resident access evidence. It keeps privacy-sensitive requests tied to approved policy, authorized reviewers, and source records.

When to use this checklist

Use it when a resident, owner, vendor, insurer, police officer, attorney, delivery carrier, or internal team member asks whether footage exists, asks for a copy, wants a time window checked, or needs a resident-safe status update after a building incident.

  • Separate simple intake from privacy, subpoena, law-enforcement, insurance, liability, discipline, lease-enforcement, and access-control decisions.
  • Capture the exact requested time window, location, incident description, requester identity/authority, and source log before anyone drafts a promise.
  • Do not promise footage exists, will be retained, will be released, proves fault, shows a person, or confirms a claim until an authorized reviewer verifies it.
  • Create a safe acknowledgement with next owner, review channel, due time, and STOP AUTOMATION rules.

STOP AUTOMATION if the request involves police/law enforcement, subpoenas, attorneys, insurance claims, alleged crime, injury, minors, domestic/conflict situations, privacy-sensitive footage, lease enforcement, discrimination/fair-housing concerns, employee/vendor discipline, or any release/retention promise.

Resident-safe acknowledgement templates

1. Intake received, review pending

Hi [Name], thanks for sending the details for [location/time]. We have logged the request and will route it to the approved reviewer before confirming whether any footage or access-log information can be checked or shared. We do not want to guess about availability, retention, release rules, or incident conclusions.

2. Need a narrower time window

Hi [Name], to route this correctly, could you send the approximate date, time window, location, and a short description of what you are asking us to review? Once we have that, we can send it through the approved property-management review path.

3. Sensitive request routed to manager

Hi [Name], this request may involve privacy-sensitive records or an official review process, so we have escalated it to [manager/team]. We will follow up by [time/date] with the verified next step through the approved channel.

Human-review stop rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION before confirming footage exists, describing what footage shows, releasing screenshots/video, promising retention, or drawing conclusions about fault, identity, criminal activity, insurance coverage, lease enforcement, or liability.
  • NEEDS MANAGER/LEGAL REVIEW for law-enforcement, subpoena, attorney, insurer, injury, alleged-crime, harassment, discrimination, minors, domestic/conflict, or privacy-sensitive requests.
  • Do not paste video files, screenshots, access logs, resident names, claim records, police details, or private incident notes into public AI tools.
  • Keep footage requests, retention decisions, and release approvals inside approved property-management, security, or legal workflows.
