AI Meeting Bot Consent and Summary Review Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this checklist before an AI note taker, meeting bot, call assistant, or transcript summarizer joins sales calls, client reviews, vendor calls, team meetings, interviews, or support escalations.

Why this matters

AI meeting assistants can save time, but they also create new risks: attendees may not know they are being recorded, transcripts may expose customer or employee information, action items can be assigned from misunderstood conversation, and summaries can turn tentative discussion into a false promise.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI invite a bot, start recording, distribute a summary, update a CRM, create tasks, promise next steps, or send customer-facing notes without a named owner confirming consent, source notes, attendee context, privacy boundaries, and follow-up wording.

Safe notice and follow-up snippets

Pre-call notice: We use [tool] to help create internal notes and action items. Please let us know before the meeting starts if you do not want the AI note taker included. Sensitive details can be handled outside the bot-recorded portion.
Internal review note: AI summary is a draft only. Confirm speaker, timestamp, decision owner, deadline, and source quote before creating tasks, sending a customer recap, or updating CRM fields.
Customer recap guardrail: Send only confirmed decisions, next steps, and dates. Do not include private internal notes, pricing assumptions, unresolved options, attendee sentiment, legal/HR details, or AI-generated interpretations.

AI review prompt

Act as a cautious small-business meeting-summary reviewer. Use only the verified notes below. Do not invent consent, recording permission, attendee intent, decisions, deadlines, prices, contract terms, approvals, customer promises, legal conclusions, or action-item owners. Return: 1) missing consent/transcript fields, 2) sensitive topics to redact or hold, 3) action items with proof quotes, 4) unsafe summary claims to remove, 5) customer-safe recap draft, and 6) STOP AUTOMATION items.

Fast QA before sending the summary

  • Confirm attendees knew the bot was present and that sensitive portions were handled according to policy.
  • Check the transcript for speaker confusion before assigning owners or deadlines.
  • Split internal notes from customer-facing summaries.
  • Remove private customer data, credentials, payment details, legal/HR speculation, and unapproved pricing or schedule promises.
