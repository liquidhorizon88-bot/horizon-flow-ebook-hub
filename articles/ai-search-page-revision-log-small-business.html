AI Search Page Revision Log for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before rewriting service pages, FAQs, product pages, or location pages for AI search so every change has a source, owner, approval note, and rollback path.

Start with the free sample library

Who this is for

This checklist is for small-business owners, marketers, and agencies improving pages for Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, and classic SEO. It is especially useful after a content refresh, service-area edit, FAQ rewrite, policy update, schema change, or conversion-copy test.

Copy/paste page revision log

  1. Page and section: URL, page type, section edited, previous headline or FAQ, and date changed.
  2. Reason for change: verified customer question, support ticket, Search Console query, sales objection, policy update, product change, or owner request.
  3. Source of truth: CRM note, call transcript, policy doc, price sheet, service-area list, proof page, analytics export, or owner-approved brief.
  4. Before state: what the page said before the edit, including screenshots or archived text when possible.
  5. After state: exact copy, FAQ, schema field, internal link, CTA, or proof item added or removed.
  6. Approval owner: who reviewed the change for facts, promises, pricing, eligibility, compliance, privacy, and brand voice.
  7. Risk check: could the edit imply a guarantee, service area, price, emergency promise, credential, testimonial, or result that is not supported?
  8. Measurement plan: baseline date, source-of-truth tool, UTM if used, query/page metric, lead-quality label, and review date.
  9. Rollback trigger: unsupported claim found, wrong-fit leads increase, conversion drops, AI tools summarize a bad promise, or owner rejects the wording.
  10. Next review: 14-day crawl/index check, 45-day lead-quality check, 90-day positioning check, or sooner if a customer reports confusion.

Human-review stop rules

  • Do not invent approval history, screenshots, analytics, traffic lift, ranking movement, or AI-search mentions.
  • Do not let AI rewrite guarantees, refunds, emergency promises, service areas, credentials, or pricing without owner/legal/compliance review where relevant.
  • Do not paste private customer details, call recordings, or confidential CRM notes into AI tools; summarize or anonymize first.
  • Do not change schema markup if the visible page does not support the same fact.
  • Do not treat one inquiry or one AI answer as proof that the page should be rewritten; log it, watch for a pattern, then update.
