AI Search Battery Charging Safety Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before a product page, FAQ, support macro, chatbot, or answer engine summarizes charging, battery, adapter, voltage, storage, disposal, travel, or warranty guidance for a product that uses replaceable, rechargeable, lithium, button-cell, or external battery power.

Marker: AI-SEARCH-BATTERY-CHARGING-SAFETY-CHECKLIST-READY

Use this when: a retailer, ecommerce brand, repair shop, productized service, or local business publishes battery or charging instructions that could be quoted by AI search, support automation, customer-service macros, or marketplace Q&A.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI invent battery chemistry, charger compatibility, voltage, wattage, temperature range, storage, disposal, shipping, airline/travel, child-safety, fire-safety, warranty, recall, repair, or emergency instructions. Route any electrical, lithium, overheating, swelling, leaking, smoke, fire, injury, regulated-product, or manufacturer-specific wording to the owner and a qualified reviewer before publishing.

Battery and charging source card

Do-not-say listClaims AI must not infer from similar products.Do not say universal charger, safe overnight, airline-approved, waterproof, child-safe, repairable, recyclable, warranty-safe, or no fire risk unless the source proves it.

Customer-safe copy blocks

Short page note: Use only the charging, storage, and battery-care instructions from [official source]. If your charger, cable, outlet, adapter, temperature, moisture, battery condition, or product version differs from the approved source, stop and contact [support/reviewer] before charging or using the product.
Risky-answer handoff: I do not want to guess about battery or charging safety. I am routing this to [review owner] and will answer only from the approved battery/charging source card.
STOP PUBLISHING checklist:
- Missing official manual/spec/safety source.
- AI added charger, voltage, wattage, storage, disposal, repair, travel, or warranty advice not present in the source.
- Customer reports heat, swelling, smoke, leaking, odor, injury, fire, water exposure, recall, or damaged battery.
- Page uses generic advice copied from a similar product instead of the exact model/source.
