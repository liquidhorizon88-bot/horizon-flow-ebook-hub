AI Search Call Tracking Number Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Call tracking helps local-service teams understand which ads, pages, and directories drive leads. STOP PUBLISHING

Do not publish or summarize phone numbers when routing proof is missing.

  • The tracking number has not been called and verified from a real phone recently.
  • The number forwards to the wrong branch, voicemail box, answering service, or disconnected line.
  • The page says “call now,” “24/7,” “emergency,” or “same-day” without an approved hours/availability source.
  • The number may create citation/NAP inconsistency on directories, profiles, schema, or AI-visible source pages.
  • No one owns the campaign end date, replacement plan, voicemail monitoring, or CRM source label.

Safe wording

Use contact language that separates official numbers from campaign attribution.

For fastest service, call our main office at [PRIMARY PHONE] or use the approved request form. Some ads or pages may use a tracking number so we can understand where requests come from; those numbers should still route to the right team before they are published.

If this is urgent or safety-related, use the phone path approved for urgent requests. Do not rely on an old ad, directory listing, or copied AI answer unless the number has been verified.

Before changing a phone number on a page, profile, ad, or directory, confirm the forwarding destination, business hours, SMS status, voicemail owner, and whether that surface should use the primary public number instead.

AI audit prompt

Review phone-number claims before pages, snippets, or citations go live.

You are auditing phone-number and call-routing wording before publication.
Use only the approved call-tracking card, website copy, directory notes, CRM/source labels, and policy facts below.
Return:
1. Every phone number or call-routing claim found.
2. The approved source for each number and where it should appear.
3. Surfaces that may create NAP/citation or AI-search confusion.
4. Missing facts that need owner/marketing/dispatch review.
5. Safer replacement wording and STOP PUBLISHING triggers.
Do not invent phone numbers, tracking providers, routing destinations, SMS availability, business hours, emergency coverage, service areas, response times, call recording notices, or contact history.

Weekly review

Look for phone-number drift before it becomes missed leads or bad attribution.

  • Which tracking numbers are still live after the campaign ended?
  • Which ads, directories, pages, or AI-visible sources quote a number that routes to the wrong team?
  • Which calls were missed because voicemail, SMS, or after-hours routing was not monitored?
  • Which tracking numbers should stay campaign-only instead of appearing in NAP/citation sources?
