AI Search Price Match and Competitor Offer Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before your website, FAQ, chatbot, Google Business Profile answer, helpdesk macro, marketplace page, or AI-search snippet answers questions about price matching, competitor offers, coupons, sale guarantees, advertised-price exclusions, or refund/credit exceptions.

Use this when

  • A customer asks whether you match a competitor's price, coupon, sale, marketplace listing, quote, or membership rate.
  • AI search may combine old promotion pages, screenshot offers, coupon posts, checkout notes, and support macros into one overconfident promise.
  • The team needs safe wording that does not invent price guarantees, eligibility, stackable discounts, retroactive refunds, or competitor-policy exceptions.

Why price-match claims need a source card

Price-match policies sound simple, but they usually depend on eligible competitors, identical SKUs or service scopes, proof requirements, time windows, coupon stacking, clearance exclusions, membership status, geographic limits, marketplace sellers, taxes/fees, and refund-credit rules. AI tools can turn a stale FAQ or promo snippet into a promise that changes margin or creates a customer-service dispute.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI publish price-match, competitor-offer, coupon, refund-credit, lowest-price, guarantee, or eligibility claims until an owner verifies the current source of truth and approves the exact public wording.

Copy/paste price-match source card

Policy name: [approved public name]
Source owner: [person/team responsible]
Canonical policy URL or internal record: [link]
Last verified date: [date]
Eligible products/services: [SKU, service tier, location, channel]
Eligible competitors/offers: [approved competitor types, marketplace rules, quote rules]
Proof required from customer: [URL, screenshot, written quote, date, exact match]
Time window: [before purchase, after purchase, expiration]
Coupon/discount stacking rules: [allowed / not allowed / owner review]
Exclusions: [clearance, used/refurbished, membership, third-party, marketplace, discontinued, service scope]
Tax, shipping, fees, installation, or add-on handling: [approved wording]
Refund, credit, or adjustment rules: [what can/cannot be promised]
Manual review triggers: [large adjustment, legal/regulatory, abuse/fraud, price error, custom quote]
Customer-safe summary: [approved 2-3 sentence answer]
Do-not-say list: [guaranteed lowest price, always match, retroactive refund, stackable, old promo]
AI/search audit owner: [who checks generated answers]

Customer-safe reply snippets

Thanks for asking. Price-match eligibility depends on the exact product/service, competitor offer, date, and policy source. Please send the competitor link or quote, and we will compare it against the current policy before confirming any adjustment.
I do not want to guess about discounts or credits. If the offer involves a coupon, marketplace seller, clearance item, custom quote, membership rate, refund, or past order, it needs owner/team review before we confirm.
Internal note: Price-match/competitor-offer question. Verify canonical policy, product/service match, competitor proof, coupon rules, tax/fee handling, refund/credit rules, and owner-approved exceptions before publishing or sending a final answer.

AI audit prompt

You are reviewing a small-business price-match or competitor-offer page for AI-search accuracy. Use only the approved source card below. Identify any draft claims about matching prices, honoring competitor coupons, lowest-price guarantees, refunds, credits, sale windows, marketplace sellers, eligible products/services, or exclusions that are unsupported or ambiguous. Return: (1) safe public answer, (2) risky claims to remove, (3) source gaps to verify, and (4) STOP PUBLISHING triggers.

Review before publishing

  • Does the public page match current ecommerce/POS rules, support macros, checkout behavior, quote rules, and owner-approved policy wording?
  • Does the answer avoid promising guaranteed lowest price, automatic matching, coupon stacking, refund credits, or competitor equivalence without evidence?
  • Are old sale emails, coupon posts, social screenshots, PDFs, and help articles redirected or labeled as outdated?
  • Is there a clear owner for disputed competitor proof, price errors, high-value adjustments, tax/fee questions, and abuse/fraud review?
