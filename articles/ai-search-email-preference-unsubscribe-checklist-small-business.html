EMAIL-PREFERENCE-UNSUBSCRIBE-CARD-READY — Use this worksheet before a website FAQ, support macro, chatbot, AI answer engine snippet, or marketing assistant explains email preferences, unsubscribe links, consent status, re-subscribe paths, transactional messages, or suppression lists.

Use when

  • A customer asks how to unsubscribe, change newsletter preferences, or stop promotional emails.
  • A support reply needs to separate marketing opt-outs from transactional receipts, account, safety, or legal notices.
  • An AI search answer or chatbot may summarize email-consent, preference-center, or re-subscribe rules from stale pages.
  • The business needs proof before saying someone is subscribed, unsubscribed, suppressed, or eligible to rejoin a list.

Stop rule before AI answers

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI claim a person is subscribed, unsubscribed, opted in, opted out, re-subscribed, permanently suppressed, or exempt from marketing rules unless the ESP, CRM, consent log, preference-center copy, and approved policy source support it. Route privacy/legal, compliance, account-security, and sensitive-data questions to a qualified owner before publishing or sending.

1) Unsubscribe request received

Hi {first_name}, thanks for letting us know. We are checking the email-preference record before confirming status. If you used the unsubscribe link, the preference center should show the latest marketing-email setting.

2) Preference-center path

You can update marketing email categories through {preference_center_link}. If the link is expired or does not match your email address, reply through the approved support channel so we can verify the record without guessing.

3) Transactional-message boundary

Marketing preferences are separate from necessary account, receipt, security, legal, or service messages. We are checking the approved policy source before saying which message types apply to this account.

4) Re-subscribe or accidental opt-out

We can review the current preference record and the approved re-subscribe path. We will not add an address back to marketing messages unless the source record shows the required consent step is complete.

AI audit prompt

You are reviewing a website, FAQ, support macro, chatbot answer, or AI-search snippet about email preferences, unsubscribe, re-subscribe, consent, suppression lists, or transactional messages. Use only the verified source card below. Do not invent consent status, subscription status, legal requirements, suppression-list behavior, re-subscribe eligibility, timing, or message categories. Return: 1) missing source facts, 2) risky claims in the draft, 3) customer-safe wording, 4) which items require owner/privacy/legal review.
