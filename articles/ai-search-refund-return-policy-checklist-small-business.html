Why this matters

Refund and return rules often live in several places: product pages, invoices, receipts, terms pages, booking widgets, PDFs, FAQs, marketplace listings, email macros, and old sales pages. AI answer engines can stitch those sources together and accidentally present stale or unsupported promises as current policy.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI write or summarize refund, return, credit, cancellation, deposit, warranty, or exception promises until a named owner verifies the current source, effective date, exceptions, and customer-safe wording.

Safe public wording starter

Refund/return summary: Our current policy is summarized at [canonical URL]. Eligibility depends on [approved condition]. If your situation involves [exception trigger], please contact [owner/team] before assuming a refund, return, credit, cancellation, or replacement is available.
Stale-source correction: This page replaced older refund/return language on [date]. If you find a different version in a PDF, marketplace listing, receipt, or email, use [canonical URL] as the current source and ask [owner] to review the conflict.

Fast QA before AI or search engines quote it

  • Search the site, PDFs, marketplace listings, receipts, email templates, and helpdesk macros for old policy wording.
  • Confirm the canonical page links from pricing, checkout, FAQ, terms, product, and support pages.
  • Make exception language clear enough for customers but strict enough that AI does not approve edge cases.
  • Set a review date whenever pricing, delivery, digital-download files, service areas, warranty terms, or payment processors change.
