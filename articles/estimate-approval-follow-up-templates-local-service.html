Estimate Approval Follow-Up Templates for Local Service Businesses

A quote that never gets a clean follow-up can quietly turn into lost revenue. Use these templates to remind prospects, answer the next-step question, and close the loop without inventing discounts, timelines, warranties, or pressure.

Best fit: HVAC, plumbing, roofing, cleaning, landscaping, pest control, painting, handyman, remodelers, and other local service teams that send estimates but do not always have a consistent approval follow-up.

Before sending any estimate follow-up

  • Confirm the exact estimate number, quoted scope, expiration date, deposit terms, booking link, and responsible staff member.
  • Do not invent new pricing, discounts, availability, warranty terms, or financing promises.
  • Use the same channel the customer used unless consent or company policy requires a different channel.
  • Stop follow-up when the customer declines, asks not to be contacted, or needs a manager review.

1. Same-day estimate received check

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Company]. I wanted to make sure you received the estimate for [service/project]. If anything looks unclear, reply here and I can point you to the right section or have [owner/estimator] review it.

2. Two-day next-step reminder

Hi [First Name], just checking in on the estimate for [project]. If you would like to move forward, the next step is [approve online / choose a start window / schedule a call]. If you are still comparing options, no problem — is there one question we can answer?

3. Scope recap without pressure

Quick recap of the estimate: [confirmed scope]. It does not include [known exclusions, if any]. Before you decide, please review the scope and let us know if anything is missing so we do not assume the wrong work.

4. Deposit or scheduling handoff

Thanks, [First Name]. To reserve the project, the next step is [deposit / approval / scheduling call] using [approved link/process]. Once that is complete, [team member] will confirm the schedule. Please review the estimate terms before approving.

5. Stale estimate closeout

Hi [First Name], we have not heard back about estimate #[estimate number], so we are going to close this follow-up for now. If you want to revisit it later, reply here and we can check whether pricing, availability, and scope are still current.

Simple CRM labels

  • estimate-sent-needs-confirmation — quote was sent; receipt not confirmed.
  • estimate-follow-up-day-2 — polite next-step reminder due.
  • scope-question-open — customer needs clarification before approval.
  • approval-ready — customer asked for next step, deposit, or schedule.
  • stale-estimate-closed — closeout sent; no more nudges without a new signal.

Estimate number: [estimate number]
Confirmed scope: [only confirmed scope]
Known next step: [approve / deposit / schedule call / answer question / close out]
Channel: [SMS / email]
Known constraints: [expiration date, deposit rule, service area, office hours]

Draft one SMS under 450 characters and one email under 130 words.
Rules:
- Do not invent discounts, prices, warranties, diagnoses, availability, or financing terms.
- Do not pressure the customer or imply scarcity unless it is a confirmed company policy.
- Ask one clear next-step question.
