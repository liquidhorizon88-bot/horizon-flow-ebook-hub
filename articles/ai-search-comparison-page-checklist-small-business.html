AI Search Comparison Page Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before publishing a comparison page, alternative page, package comparison, vendor-selection guide, or “us vs. other options” page that AI answer engines may quote.

Copy the worksheet Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Prospects compare your service with DIY, marketplaces, agencies, or competitors.
  • Your site has alternatives, packages, or “best for” pages.
  • AI tools could summarize your offer against another option.
  • You need comparison copy without unsupported claims.

Why this matters

AI answers reward clear comparisons — and amplify sloppy ones

People often search by comparison: “best option for,” “service vs. DIY,” “agency vs. freelancer,” “package A vs. package B,” or “alternatives to.” AI search tools can summarize those pages into short recommendations. If your page is vague, self-congratulatory, or unsupported, the summary can become inaccurate fast.

A useful comparison page does not need to attack competitors. It should explain who each option is for, what facts are known, what is unknown, what proof supports your claims, and where a human should review the final wording.

3. Fair comparison table
Columns to use:
- Best fit
- Not a fit when
- Main benefit
- Main limitation
- Proof/source

4. Claims that need support
[ ] “best” / “top” / “leading”
[ ] “faster” / “cheaper” / “higher ROI”
[ ] “guaranteed” / “risk free” / “no hassle”
[ ] legal, financial, medical, safety, insurance, tax, or regulated claims
[ ] competitor-specific details
For each checked item, add a source or remove the claim.

5. FAQ answers to add
- When should someone choose our option?
- When should someone choose a different option?
- What information do we need before recommending a package?
- What proof supports the recommendation?
- What should buyers verify before deciding?

6. AI-search stop rules
Do not publish or let AI summarize until a responsible human verifies:
[ ] no invented competitor facts
[ ] no unsupported rankings or superiority claims
[ ] no fake customer results or testimonials
[ ] no hidden price/terms/warranty promises
[ ] no misleading service-area or availability claims
[ ] every comparison has a source, proof item, or “not confirmed” note

Implementation

Publish one comparison page safely

  1. Pick one comparison buyers already ask about.
  2. List only verified source facts before drafting copy.
  3. State who each option is best for instead of declaring one universal winner.
  4. Add proof, examples, FAQs, and limitations.
  5. Remove or source every “best,” “faster,” “cheaper,” or “guaranteed” claim.
  6. Schedule a factual review so stale competitor or pricing statements do not stay live.

Human review rule: a person responsible for sales, operations, and public claims should review comparison pages before publication. AI can organize facts and flag gaps, but it should not invent advantages or decide what is legally safe to say.
