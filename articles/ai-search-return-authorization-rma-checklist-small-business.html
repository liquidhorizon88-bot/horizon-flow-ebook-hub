AI Search Return Authorization and RMA Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this source card before an AI assistant, FAQ page, chatbot, support macro, or AI-search answer tells a customer whether a return is authorized, which RMA number applies, who pays for a label, when a refund starts, or whether an exception is allowed. The goal is to answer quickly without inventing approval, refund, carrier, label, or inspection details.

When to use this

  • A product, subscription, replacement part, digital deliverable, or shipped order needs a return authorization before support replies publicly or privately.
  • Customers ask AI search, a chatbot, a help center, or a support rep whether an item is eligible to return, exchange, inspect, repair, replace, or refund.
  • Your website has separate rules for return windows, labels, restocking fees, warranty claims, exchanges, damage claims, or final-sale items.
  • You need a customer-safe source card before publishing RMA instructions, return-label promises, inspection timelines, refund timing, or exception wording.

STOP PUBLISHING guardrails

  • STOP PUBLISHING before AI says a return is approved, an RMA exists, a label is prepaid, or a refund has started unless the source card proves it.
  • STOP PUBLISHING if final-sale, opened-item, hazardous, custom, subscription, warranty, digital-access, or regulated-product rules might apply.
  • STOP PUBLISHING before promising refund timing, carrier pickup, replacement shipment, fee waiver, store credit, or exception approval.
  • STOP PUBLISHING if the answer relies on a generic policy page but the order, SKU, condition, delivery date, or support-ticket source is missing.
  • STOP PUBLISHING before blaming a carrier, customer, vendor, or warehouse, or before admitting liability for damage, delay, loss, or missing accessories.

Customer-safe snippets

Return request received

“Thanks — we received your return request. A person needs to verify eligibility, authorization details, and any label or inspection steps before we confirm next steps.”

RMA number not yet verified

“We do not want to give you the wrong return authorization number. We’re checking the order record and return source before sending return instructions.”

Label or fee review needed

“We’re reviewing whether a prepaid label, customer-paid label, or other return-shipping rule applies to this order before we confirm label details.”

Inspection or condition hold

“The return may require condition or accessory verification. We’ll confirm what evidence is needed before promising a refund, exchange, or replacement.”

Why this matters for AI search

Return authorization pages are easy for AI systems to over-summarize because policy pages, order records, carrier labels, and inspection rules often live in different places. This checklist helps a small team keep the approved source separate from guesses so AI Overviews, chatbots, and support macros do not publish customer-facing return promises that the business cannot honor.
