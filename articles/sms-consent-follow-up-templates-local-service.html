SMS Consent Follow-Up Templates for Local Service Leads

Texting can recover missed leads quickly, but only when the business has a clear consent trail, an approved sender number, and a stop rule. Use these templates to ask for permission, move a prospect to an approved channel, and keep AI-assisted replies inside safe boundaries.

When to use SMS consent follow-up templates

Use this page when a lead left a phone number in a form, voicemail, chat, Google Business Profile message, or referral note and your team wants to text without guessing at consent or next steps.

  • Best fit: plumbers, HVAC companies, roofers, landscapers, cleaners, med spas, consultants, and property-service teams that receive phone-number leads.
  • Human review rule: confirm the source of the number, consent language, approved texting platform, opt-out wording, emergency routing, service area, and any state/industry rules before sending.
  • Stop rule: send only the consent request or one approved service follow-up plus one final close-the-loop note; stop immediately on STOP/opt-out, wrong number, complaint, legal/privacy concern, or no response after the final check-in.

Template 1: permission-first text

Use when: the lead provided a phone number, but the form did not clearly say they agreed to SMS follow-up.

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Business]. We received your request about [service]. Is it okay if we text you here about this request? Reply YES and we can send the next step, or call us at [approved phone] if you prefer. Reply STOP to opt out.

Template 2: consent confirmed, ask one qualifier

Use when: the lead has opted in or the original source clearly allows SMS about this specific request.

Thanks, [First Name]. To route this correctly, please reply with [one qualifier: ZIP/address, preferred callback window, photo if safe, or short issue summary]. We will review before confirming price, timing, or diagnosis.

Template 3: wrong number or unclear identity

Use when: the number may be wrong, shared, or imported from an old CRM record.

Hi, this is [Business]. We may have the wrong number for a recent [service] request. If this was not you, sorry about that — reply STOP and we will not text again. If it was you, reply with the best callback window.

Template 4: one final SMS follow-up

Hi [First Name], checking once more on your [service] request. If you still want help, reply with [one easy next step]. If not, no action needed and we will close the loop. Reply STOP to opt out.

CRM labels for SMS follow-up

  • Consent status: SMS consent unknown, requested, confirmed, opted out, wrong number, phone-only preferred.
  • Lead source: missed call, web form, live chat, Google Business Profile, DM, referral, old CRM record.
  • Risk flags: emergency/safety, warranty/refund, payment dispute, medical/legal/privacy, minor/customer identity unclear.
  • Next owner: dispatcher, office manager, estimator, owner review, emergency phone route, do-not-contact closeout.

AI summary prompt for SMS consent follow-up

Copy/paste prompt:

You are helping summarize a local service lead before SMS follow-up. Use only the facts provided. Do not invent consent, price, availability, diagnosis, warranty coverage, legal advice, or urgency. Return: lead source, whether SMS consent is clear/unclear/opted out, requested service, location clues, one safest next question, approved reply draft, CRM labels, opt-out wording needed, and human reviewer. If consent is unclear, the first draft must ask permission before discussing service details. If the lead includes STOP, wrong number, complaint, legal/privacy, payment dispute, medical, safety, or emergency issues, say STOP: human manager review required.
