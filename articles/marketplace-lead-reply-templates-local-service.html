Why this matters

Marketplace leads need fast replies plus source discipline

Unlike a website form, a marketplace lead may show only a short job description, a rough location, and a platform-specific message thread. A rushed reply can accidentally promise a price, a time slot, a diagnosis, or a discount that the business has not verified. A better first response acknowledges the source, asks for the next useful detail, and moves the lead toward a confirmed call or appointment.

  1. Name the source: tag the lead as Angi, Thumbtack, Yelp, Local Services Ads, Facebook, referral, or other before follow-up.
  2. Ask for the missing detail: photo, address/service area, urgency, access, decision maker, budget range, or timing.
  3. Avoid unsupported promises: no invented diagnosis, exact price, same-day availability, warranty coverage, financing approval, or licensing claim.
  4. Set a human-review rule: owner/dispatcher review is required before any high-cost, safety, legal, refund, fee, or emergency wording goes out.
