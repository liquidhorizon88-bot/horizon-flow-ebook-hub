Why recall and safety wording needs a source card

A small business might have a manufacturer notice, email from a supplier, internal spreadsheet, order history, warranty page, replacement form, and customer-service macro that do not say the same thing. AI tools can compress that into dangerous certainty: “all units recalled,” “free replacement for everyone,” “safe to keep using,” or “refund guaranteed.” A source card gives the owner one approved reference before AI-assisted content or answers discuss safety, eligibility, or next steps.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI invent affected products, serial-number ranges, severity, safety instructions, regulatory status, legal conclusions, warranty coverage, refund eligibility, replacement timelines, repair instructions, disposal advice, or customer-specific promises. Route safety, legal, compliance, product-liability, medical, child-safety, food, electrical, battery, vehicle, and regulated-product wording to the owner and qualified reviewer.

Notice scopeProduct name, SKU, lot/serial range, model, vendor/manufacturer, date issued, affected channel, and official source URL or document.Do not apply one notice to all products, variants, locations, or customers unless the official source says so.
Risk statementApproved plain-language risk, known incident source, customer action needed, and where the risk statement came from.Never downplay, exaggerate, diagnose, or reinterpret a safety risk from an AI draft.
Customer actionStop-use rule, contact path, inspection step, replacement form, repair path, return/refund path, disposal instruction, and escalation owner.Owner/legal/compliance review is required before telling customers to keep using, stop using, modify, return, dispose, or repair a product.
Eligibility and proofReceipt/order proof, purchase date, serial/lot/photo proof, location, warranty status, customer-safe verification language, and exception process.Do not promise eligibility, refunds, replacements, credits, warranty coverage, or timelines without the active source.
Public wording boundaryApproved short answer, forbidden claims, internal-only details, customer support script, and update cadence.Keep public wording narrow: what is affected, where to verify, what customers should do, and who reviews exceptions.

Safe public wording templates

Recall/safety notice answer: Please verify whether your [product/model/SKU/lot/serial range] is included in the current notice at [official notice/support path]. Do not rely on AI summaries for eligibility, safety, warranty, refund, or replacement decisions.
Customer action note: If your item may be affected, stop and follow the current instructions at [official path] or contact [support path]. Our team will review [order/serial/photo/proof] before confirming next steps.
Internal AI note: STOP PUBLISHING. Do not invent affected products, risk level, safety instructions, legal/regulatory status, refund eligibility, replacement timelines, warranty coverage, or repair/disposal steps. Use only the approved source card.

Weekly source-maintenance questions

  • Do product pages, marketplace listings, PDFs, support macros, recall pages, email templates, and warranty pages point to the same official notice?
  • Are affected SKUs, serial ranges, lots, dates, and customer actions clearly linked to a dated source?
  • Can customers verify their item through an official path without relying on an AI summary?
  • Which safety, legal, compliance, warranty, refund, replacement, or disposal claims require owner or qualified reviewer sign-off?
