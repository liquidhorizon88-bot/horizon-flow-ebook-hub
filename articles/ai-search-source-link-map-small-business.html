AI Search Source Link Map for Small Businesses

AI search tools are more likely to summarize the wrong thing when your website has helpful claims but weak source paths. Use this copy/paste map to connect service pages, FAQ answers, policies, testimonials, pricing notes, and proof URLs before you ask AI to draft or audit anything.

When this is useful

  • Your website has service, FAQ, policy, review, and case-study pages, but they do not link to each other clearly.
  • AI tools quote old claims because the current source-of-truth page is not obvious.
  • Staff or contractors are using AI to refresh content and need a simple source list before drafting.
  • You want a lightweight worksheet before creating schema markup, FAQ pages, comparison pages, or AI-search answer blocks.

Starter map example

  • Emergency service wording: source-of-truth = emergency service page; proof = hours/dispatch policy; link from homepage, contact page, and FAQ; STOP if availability is not verified.
  • Guarantee or warranty wording: source-of-truth = guarantee/refund policy; proof = terms page; link from pricing, service page, quote page, and FAQ; STOP if legal or manager review is missing.
  • Review or testimonial proof: source-of-truth = testimonial page; proof = permission log or public review URL; link from service pages and case studies; STOP if customer identity or result claims are unclear.
  • Pricing or estimate expectations: source-of-truth = pricing/quote page; proof = current owner-approved pricing note; link from booking, contact, FAQ, and quote resources; STOP if price varies by scope.

AI audit prompt

You are auditing a small-business website for AI-search source clarity. Use only the URLs and notes I provide. Do not invent business claims, proof, pricing, availability, guarantees, reviews, locations, credentials, or performance results.

Website topic: {business_or_service_topic}
Source link map rows:
{paste_rows}

Return:
1. Topics with no clear source-of-truth URL
2. Claims that need owner/legal/policy review before publishing
3. Pages that should add a buyer-helpful internal link
4. Pages with outdated or conflicting source links
5. A short revised source-link map row for each fix
6. STOP PUBLISHING warnings for any unverifiable claim

STOP PUBLISHING checks

  • Do not publish AI-written answer blocks when the source URL is missing, private, stale, or contradicted elsewhere.
  • Do not make health, legal, financial, insurance, warranty, emergency, or guarantee claims without owner/professional review.
  • Do not imply a customer result, certification, service area, or response time unless a public approved proof source supports it.
  • Do not create fake internal links just for SEO. Every link should help a buyer verify or act on the answer.
