AI Customer Disclosure Checklist for Small Businesses

AI can help draft faster replies, summaries, and follow-up notes. The risky part is not the tool; it is sending AI-assisted customer messages without deciding what should be disclosed, what facts need proof, and what must stay human-reviewed.

Use this when

  • Your team uses AI to draft customer emails, SMS replies, review responses, FAQs, or support notes.
  • You need a simple rule for when to mention AI assistance.
  • Staff are unsure whether a reply is a draft, recommendation, promise, or decision.
  • You want customer trust without over-disclosing harmless internal drafting help.

1. Decide what kind of AI help was used

AI useDisclosure needReview rule
Grammar, tone, or formatting of a human-written replyUsually low; no special disclosure unless policy requires it.Human reads final text before sending.
Drafted reply from customer notes, call transcript, ticket, or CRM recordMedium; consider a plain AI-assisted drafting note for sensitive topics.Verify names, dates, amounts, policies, commitments, and next steps.
Summarized a customer issue or recommended a next actionMedium/high; state that a team member reviewed the summary before action.Owner or manager approves the decision, not the model.
Pricing, refund, warranty, safety, legal, financing, account access, hiring, or emergency guidanceHigh; do not send as an automated AI decision.STOP AUTOMATION and route to qualified human review.

2. Copy/paste AI-use disclosure decision card

Customer message/task: [paste short description]
AI was used for: [grammar / first draft / summary / recommendation / other]
Customer-visible impact: [none / wording only / facts summarized / next step suggested / decision affected]
Sensitive category present: [pricing / refund / warranty / safety / legal / account access / private data / emergency / none]
Source facts checked: [CRM note / invoice / policy / transcript / owner approval / public page / none]
Disclosure choice: [no special note / plain AI-assisted drafting note / human-reviewed summary note / do not send]
Responsible reviewer: [name/role]
Approved final wording: [yes/no]
Next review date: [date]

3. Customer-facing wording options

Plain drafting note

We used an internal drafting tool to help organize this reply, and a team member reviewed it before sending.

Summary reviewed by a human

We used your message and our internal notes to prepare this summary. A team member reviewed the details before deciding the next step.

Not an automated decision

This is not an automated decision. We reviewed the relevant records and will have [owner/manager/team role] confirm [price/refund/warranty/schedule/account step] before we act.

Hold for review

Thanks for the details. This needs a human review before we give an answer. We will check [source/policy/record] and follow up by [date/time].

4. STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION if the message changes price, scope, refund, warranty, contract, legal position, safety instruction, or account access.
  • Do not invent customer consent, approval history, policy exceptions, timelines, discounts, guarantees, or prior staff decisions.
  • Do not paste private customer data into AI tools unless the tool and data-use policy are approved.
  • Do not imply “AI decided” anything. Assign a human owner for the final decision.
  • If customers ask whether AI was used, answer plainly and route policy questions to the owner/manager.

5. Owner-review prompt

You are helping a small business review an AI-assisted customer message. Use only the source facts below. Do not invent customer history, consent, approvals, pricing, warranty coverage, legal advice, safety status, refunds, timelines, or policy exceptions.

Source facts:
[paste approved notes]

Draft message:
[paste draft]

Check:
1. What facts need verification before sending?
2. Does the message require an AI-use disclosure note?
3. Does it create any unsupported promise or decision?
4. Which sentence should be held for owner/manager review?
5. Rewrite the message in a clear, human, non-automated tone.
