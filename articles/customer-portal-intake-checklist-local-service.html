Customer Portal Intake Checklist for Local Service Businesses

When leads arrive through a customer portal, property-management portal, warranty portal, marketplace inbox, or shared intake form, the biggest risk is not the software. It is missing the next owner, evidence, consent, deadline, or safe first reply. Use this worksheet before an AI assistant summarizes or drafts a response.

2. First reply templates

Portal received, needs review

Thanks — we received your request through the portal. We are reviewing the details and will follow up with the next step. If there is an urgent safety issue, please call the main office line instead of relying only on the portal message.

Missing details

Thanks for sending this over. Before we can route it correctly, could you add the best callback number, the service address, and any photos that show the issue? Once we have those details, we can assign the right next step.

Owner handoff

I am assigning this portal request to [OWNER] for review by [DUE TIME]. The current notes show [SUMMARY]. Please check the portal record before sending any price, schedule, diagnosis, warranty, or availability details.

3. AI summary prompt for internal use

Summarize this customer portal request for an internal dispatcher.
Use only the portal notes provided below.
Return: customer need, urgency, missing details, suggested CRM label, owner, due time, and a safe first-reply draft.
Do not invent consent, contact history, diagnosis, pricing, warranty coverage, availability, payment status, approval status, or portal deadlines.
If a detail is missing, write MISSING.
Portal notes:
[PASTE NOTES]

4. STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION if the portal mentions safety, emergency, medical risk, gas/electrical/water shutoff, legal notice, eviction, insurance, warranty denial, refund, payment dispute, or cancellation fee.
  • Do not promise arrival windows, diagnosis, price, refund, warranty coverage, or parts availability unless a human verified the source record.
  • Do not move the conversation off the official portal if the customer, property manager, warranty company, or marketplace requires portal-based communication.
  • Keep the original portal link or ID in the CRM note so the team can audit what was actually submitted.
