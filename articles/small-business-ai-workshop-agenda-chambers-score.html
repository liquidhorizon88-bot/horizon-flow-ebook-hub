Small Business AI Workshop Agenda for Chambers and SCORE Mentors

Use this as a no-cost, no-hype workshop outline for a chamber lunch-and-learn, SCORE mentoring session, SBA partner class, or local business meetup. It teaches owners how to pick one useful AI workflow, draft safely, and keep human review in the loop.

Who this helps: chamber directors, SCORE mentors, SBA resource partners, local consultants, and small-business owners who want a practical AI session without promising that AI can replace judgment, legal advice, customer service ownership, or finance decisions.

Disclosure: Horizon Flow publishes practical small-business AI workflow guides. This page is useful on its own and links to optional free samples and paid playbooks with UTM tags for attribution.

45-minute agenda

  1. 0–5 minutes: Set the safety frame. AI is a drafting and summarizing assistant, not an owner, lawyer, accountant, HR manager, dispatcher, or policy maker.
  2. 5–12 minutes: Choose one workflow. Pick one repetitive task that already has source facts: quote follow-up, review response drafts, meeting-note summaries, FAQ drafts, intake triage, or weekly business review notes.
  3. 12–22 minutes: Build the prompt card. Define input facts, allowed data, forbidden claims, tone, reviewer, and stop rules.
  4. 22–32 minutes: Live drafting exercise. Turn a messy example into a draft, then mark every sentence that needs a fact check or owner approval.
  5. 32–40 minutes: Pilot plan. Decide what success means for the next 30 days: minutes saved, fewer missed replies, less editing, better routing, or clearer follow-up.
  6. 40–45 minutes: Q&A and next step. Each attendee leaves with one workflow, one prompt card, one reviewer, and one metric.

Safe starter prompt for the exercise

You are helping draft a small-business workflow output. Use only the facts I provide. Do not invent prices, policies, guarantees, availability, legal advice, financial advice, medical advice, dispatch promises, or customer claims.

Business context: [business type]
Task: [reply to a lead / summarize notes / draft FAQ / create checklist]
Facts to use: [paste approved facts]
Tone: clear, friendly, concise
Output format: [email / SMS / bullet summary / checklist]
Human-review rule: include a short "Needs owner check" list at the end.

Facilitator talking points

  • Start boring. A weekly summary, FAQ draft, quote follow-up, or meeting-note cleanup usually beats a risky chatbot launch.
  • Use approved facts. AI should not make up prices, policies, service areas, testimonials, staff credentials, or turnaround promises.
  • Protect private data. Remove customer names, addresses, payment details, employee records, and sensitive case details unless the business has approved tools and policies.
  • Keep a reviewer. Every customer-facing draft needs an owner or trained team member before sending.
  • Measure the pilot. Continue only when the workflow saves time, improves clarity, or reduces missed follow-up without increasing risk.
