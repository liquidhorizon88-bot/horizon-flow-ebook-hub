AI Search Review Proof Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before adding reviews, testimonials, case snippets, or “proof” sections to pages you want Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, and human buyers to understand accurately.

Copy the worksheet Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Review proof is scattered across Google, email, screenshots, or invoices.
  • A service page says “trusted” but shows little evidence.
  • AI tools are drafting pages or summaries from customer comments.
  • You need consent, privacy, and risky-claim stop rules before publishing.

Why this matters

AI search rewards clarity, but bad proof creates risk

Customer proof is useful because it shows what buyers experienced in plain language. But proof can become risky when a business edits reviews too aggressively, removes context, exposes private details, or lets AI turn one happy customer into a broad claim.

The safer goal is to build a review-proof inventory: what customers actually said, what the business is allowed to quote, which service or location the proof supports, and what claims still need a human owner to approve.

[anything the review does not actually prove]

3. Page placement
Where this proof belongs:
[ ] homepage
[ ] About page
[ ] service page
[ ] service-area page
[ ] FAQ answer
[ ] case study
[ ] proposal/quote follow-up

4. AI-search clarity checks
[ ] The proof is near the service or answer it supports.
[ ] The page explains who the customer is, what service happened, and where/audience fit.
[ ] The quote has not been rewritten to change meaning.
[ ] The page avoids ranking guarantees, revenue guarantees, medical/legal/financial claims, or fake statistics.
[ ] The same business facts match Google Business Profile, directory listings, and service pages.

5. Human review stop rules
Do not publish until a responsible human verifies permission, privacy, service accuracy, outcome claims, review-platform rules, endorsement rules, and any legal/compliance requirements.

Implementation

Start with one money page and three proof items

  1. Choose the service, location, FAQ, or About page closest to revenue.
  2. Collect three proof items that are real, recent, and permission-safe.
  3. Map each proof item to a specific claim on the page.
  4. Remove private details and avoid changing the customer's meaning.
  5. Add a short proof section plus one FAQ that answers a buyer's concern.
  6. Run the human review stop rules before publishing.

Human review rule: customer proof is not a place to improvise. A responsible owner, manager, attorney, compliance reviewer, or subject-matter expert should approve permission, privacy, endorsement, refund/warranty, safety, legal, medical, financial, and outcome-related claims before publication.
