AI Search Return Inspection Hold Checklist for Small Businesses

A returned item arrives and the refund is not ready yet because the team still needs condition proof, damage review, missing-parts checks, restocking-fee approval, or exception routing. This worksheet keeps AI answers, support macros, chatbots, and AI-search snippets from turning an inspection hold into an invented refund promise.

Use this when

  • Refund timing depends on warehouse, technician, support, or owner inspection.
  • Returned products can be damaged, incomplete, used, mismatched, or outside the approved condition rules.
  • AI-generated support answers could overpromise refunds, credits, exchanges, restocking-fee waivers, or return-label outcomes.

Why this matters

RETURN-INSPECTION-HOLD-SOURCE-CONTROL-READY

Return-inspection holds are one of the easiest places for answer engines and AI support tools to mislead customers. A public FAQ or chatbot might say “refunds are processed when we receive your return” even when the real policy requires inspection, serial-number match, photos, missing-accessory review, or manager approval.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI, support macros, chatbots, FAQ snippets, or AI-search pages publish refund approval, inspection timing, condition outcome, damage responsibility, missing-part charges, restocking fees, return-label promises, or exception language until the return record, inspection evidence, policy source, and responsible owner are verified.

Return-inspection hold source card

FieldWhat to captureSafe wording rule
Return and order contextOrder ID, RMA/return ID, received date, item/SKU, serial number if relevant, support ticket, and current owner.Never imply a returned item is approved only because it arrived.
Inspection stageNot received, received awaiting inspection, inspecting, missing information, condition issue found, approved, partially approved, denied, escalated, or exception review.Use the exact stage from the source record; never translate “received” into “refund approved.”
Evidence and condition proofPhotos, checklist, warehouse notes, technician review, package condition, accessory completeness, serial/model match, and customer-submitted proof.Do not blame the customer, carrier, warehouse, or supplier unless the approved record explicitly says so.
Policy and fee pathReturn condition policy, restocking-fee rules, missing-part fee path, exchange option, manager exception owner, and legal/accounting review if needed.Fee, denial, or exception wording should say “review” until an authorized owner approves the exact outcome.
Customer-safe next updateWhat is being checked, who owns the inspection, next update date/time, and what evidence the customer can provide if needed.Give one verified next step; avoid invented deadlines, guaranteed approvals, or pressure language.

Copy/paste customer-safe snippets

1) Return received, inspection pending

Thanks for checking on return {return_id}. We have the return record marked as received, and it is currently waiting for inspection against the item and policy record. I do not want to guess on the outcome, so we will confirm the next update by {next_update_time}.

2) More evidence needed

The team needs one more review step before we can give a final refund status: {evidence_needed}. I am routing the return record, inspection notes, and policy source to {owner} so the next response is accurate.

3) Exception review needed

This return requires manager review because {reason}. Until that review is complete, we should describe the status as “under inspection/review,” not approved or denied.

4) Internal source note

Return inspection hold: order={order_id}; return/RMA={return_id}; stage={received/inspection/missing-info/exception-review/approved/denied}; evidence={photos/warehouse-note/policy-link}; owner={person}; customer-safe next update={date/time}. STOP PUBLISHING until owner confirms final wording.
