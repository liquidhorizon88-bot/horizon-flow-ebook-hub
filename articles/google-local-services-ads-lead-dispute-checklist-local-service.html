Google Local Services Ads Lead Dispute Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Use this worksheet before a dispatcher, owner, or marketing manager disputes a paid lead, closes it as bad fit, or sends a customer-facing reply. It helps keep Google Local Services Ads and other marketplace-lead decisions tied to evidence instead of guesswork.

Use this when

  • A paid lead looks spammy, out of area, duplicate, wrong service, or unreachable.
  • A team member wants to dispute, credit-request, or close a lead without complete notes.
  • You need a consistent CRM reason code before comparing marketplace lead quality.

Important: this is an internal review worksheet, not legal, advertising-platform, or reimbursement advice. Always follow the platform's current policy and keep screenshots/call records only where your business is allowed to store them.

2) STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI invent call outcomes, customer intent, platform policy, refund/credit eligibility, service area fit, pricing, appointment availability, warranty status, or prior contact history.
  • NEEDS OWNER REVIEW: any lead-dispute decision involving billing, platform credits, legal/compliance wording, reviews, threats, chargebacks, or account health.
  • Keep internal dispute notes separate from customer-facing replies; do not blame the platform or customer in a reply template.
  • Use only verified CRM/call-center/source evidence; if evidence is missing, mark it as missing instead of filling the gap.

3) Safe customer reply snippets

Need one detail:
Thanks for reaching out — we can help check fit. What city is the job in, and what service are you looking for?

Out of service area:
Thanks for contacting us. Based on the location you shared, this appears to be outside our current service area. If that changes, we will update you before scheduling anything.

Wrong service:
Thanks for the details. We do not want to overpromise — this request may be outside the services we currently handle. I am marking it for review before anyone gives a quote or booking window.

5) Weekly lead-quality review questions

  • Which paid sources created the most valid booked leads, not just the most raw inquiries?
  • Which reason codes repeat: out-of-area, wrong service, duplicate, unreachable, price-only, or incomplete intake?
  • Which intake fields would prevent the same dispute next week?
  • Which customer replies need to be faster, clearer, or safer?
