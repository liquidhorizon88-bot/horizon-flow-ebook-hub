Utility Shutoff and Service Interruption Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before AI, an after-hours assistant, resident-services coordinator, maintenance dispatcher, or virtual assistant drafts a response about water shutoffs, power outages, gas interruptions, internet/cable issues, elevator/service outages, planned utility work, or restoration updates. It keeps resident messages tied to verified utility/vendor/property records and routes safety-sensitive or policy-sensitive answers to a human.

When to use this checklist

Use it when a resident reports a utility interruption, asks whether a shutoff is planned, needs restoration timing, wants reimbursement or rent credit, mentions a safety issue, or needs access instructions while a vendor or utility provider investigates.

  • Separate verified utility/provider updates from guesses about cause, scope, restoration time, responsibility, fees, credits, or lease impact.
  • Capture source records before AI turns an outage rumor, neighbor report, vendor text, or utility notice into a resident-facing promise.
  • Route safety-sensitive issues such as gas smell, electrical hazards, no heat/cooling in severe weather, medical-device risk, flooding, or elevator entrapment to human/emergency review.
  • Create a neutral resident update with next owner, next update time, approved channel, and STOP AUTOMATION guardrails.

STOP AUTOMATION if the message involves gas smell, active leak/flooding, electrical hazard, medical-device dependency, elevator entrapment, no heat/cooling in severe weather, reimbursement/rent credit, legal/lease consequences, access rights, liability, or unverified restoration timing.

Resident-safe reply templates

1. Report received, source check pending

Hi [Name], thanks for reporting the [utility/service] issue at [property/unit]. We are checking the approved source records and routing it to [team/vendor/provider] so we do not guess about the cause, affected area, or restoration timing. We will share the next verified update by [time/date] or sooner if the provider confirms more detail.

2. Planned shutoff notice

Hi [Name], the current verified notice says [utility/service] work is planned for [date/window] affecting [area]. Please use [approved preparation step] if needed. We will update residents through [approved channel] if the schedule changes. If you have a safety or access concern, reply here so we can route it for manager review.

3. Safety-sensitive escalation

Hi [Name], because your message mentions [safety trigger], we are routing this for urgent human review instead of sending an automated answer. If there is immediate danger, please contact emergency services or the utility emergency line as appropriate, then notify the property team through [approved channel].

AI review prompt

Do not invent outage cause, restoration time, utility responsibility, lease consequences, reimbursement, rent credit, access rights, safety advice, legal advice, or provider promises. If a required fact is missing or the issue is safety-sensitive, output NEEDS MANAGER REVIEW.

Create: (1) internal triage summary, (2) missing facts, (3) escalation bucket, (4) resident-safe draft under 120 words, and (5) CRM/PMS note.

Human-review stop rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION before promising restoration timing, reimbursement, rent credit, fee waiver, lease consequences, access rights, liability, or safety outcomes.
  • NEEDS MANAGER / EMERGENCY REVIEW if the message mentions gas, fire, electrical hazard, flooding, elevator entrapment, medical equipment, vulnerable residents, extreme heat/cold, or repeated unresolved outage reports.
  • Do not paste private resident medical, account, utility-billing, or access details into public AI tools.
  • Keep resident updates tied to verified property, vendor, utility-provider, or city records and include the next update owner/time when available.
