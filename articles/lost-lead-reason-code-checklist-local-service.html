Lost Lead Reason Code Checklist for Local Service Businesses

If every closed lead is just marked “lost,” the business cannot learn whether the real issue was slow response, price fit, service area, missing photos, no answer, duplicate records, scheduling, or no clear next step. Use this worksheet to tag lost leads honestly, review patterns weekly, and decide which follow-up scripts or pages need improvement.

When to use this checklist

Use this when a local-service lead is closed, paused, or reactivation-ready and the team needs a consistent reason code instead of a vague note. It works for missed calls, booking forms, quote requests, marketplace leads, property-manager requests, and stale CRM opportunities.

  • Best fit: HVAC, plumbing, roofing, landscaping, cleaning, pest control, appliance repair, restoration, property-maintenance, and other appointment-driven local-service teams.
  • Human review rule: only tag a reason that is supported by the actual call, message, CRM note, estimate, or booking status. Do not infer budget, competitor choice, urgency, or customer intent without evidence.
  • Stop rule: stop automation on opt-out, wrong number, disputed identity, abusive refusal, legal/privacy issue, emergency/safety concern, or unclear consent. Route those records to owner/manager review instead of reactivation.

Copy/paste lost-lead reason codes

  • no_answer_after_cadence: approved touches completed; no response.
  • slow_first_response: first human/approved reply missed the target window.
  • outside_service_area: address or property is outside the verified service area.
  • not_service_fit: request is not a service the business offers.
  • price_or_budget_not_fit: customer stated budget/price mismatch or declined the quote.
  • schedule_not_fit: requested date, time, or urgency could not be met.
  • missing_detail_or_photo: the team could not quote/schedule because needed details were not provided.
  • duplicate_or_existing_customer: record duplicates another contact, job, tenant, or property record.
  • wrong_number_or_identity_unclear: recipient disputes the request or identity is unclear. Stop automation.
  • do_not_contact_or_opt_out: customer requested no more contact. Stop automation.
  • won_elsewhere_confirmed: customer confirmed they hired someone else.
  • reactivation_candidate: safe to revisit later because no opt-out, wrong-number, legal/privacy, or safety stop rule is present.

CRM fields to add

  • Lost reason code: one primary code from the approved list.
  • Reason evidence: call note, SMS/email line, form field, quote status, or staff note that supports the code.
  • Response timing: first-response time bucket: under 5 minutes, 5–15, 15–60, same day, next day, over 24 hours, unknown.
  • Next safe action: none, manager review, add to nurture, reactivation date, send missing-detail request, update website/service page, update intake form.
  • Page or process fix: service-area page, pricing/financing page, booking form, photo request, quote follow-up, response SLA, CRM cleanup, staff training.

Weekly lost-lead review questions

  1. Which three reason codes appeared most often this week?
  2. How many lost leads had a first response after the target window?
  3. Which lost leads were caused by missing details, photos, schedule mismatch, or service-area confusion?
  4. Which website pages, forms, or scripts should be updated before buying more traffic?
  5. Which records are safe reactivation candidates, and which must stay closed?

AI summary prompt for owner review

Copy/paste prompt:

You are helping a local service business review lost leads. Use only the CRM notes, call summaries, messages, estimates, and timestamps provided. Do not invent a reason a lead was lost, customer budget, competitor choice, consent, diagnosis, price, warranty status, emergency status, or intent. For each record, return: supported lost reason code, evidence quote or note, response timing bucket, next safe action, page/process fix if any, and whether automation must stop. If there is opt-out, wrong number, disputed identity, legal/privacy complaint, abusive refusal, or unclear consent, say STOP AUTOMATION and route to owner review.
