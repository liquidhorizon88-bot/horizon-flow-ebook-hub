Unauthorized Entry and Lock Change Incident Checklist for Property Managers

A source-card worksheet for property managers, leasing teams, resident-services staff, security teams, and after-hours coordinators handling suspected unauthorized entry, lock tampering, key/fob misuse, lock-change requests, police reports, guest disputes, domestic-conflict concerns, privacy-sensitive incidents, or access-code resets without letting AI or staff invent liability, legal advice, culprit identity, camera findings, fee waivers, or emergency/security promises.

UNAUTHORIZED-ENTRY-LOCK-CHANGE-CARD-READY

Use this before any AI-assisted entry or lock-change reply

STOP AUTOMATION: do not send access instructions, identify a responsible person, promise a lock change, confirm camera footage, discuss police/legal conclusions, waive fees, blame residents, approve guest access, or summarize a security incident until a manager has verified the lease/occupancy record, authorized-contact list, lock/key/fob logs, access-control history, incident report, resident communication channel, property policy, police/risk/legal routing, and emergency context.

  • Escalate active danger, domestic violence, stalking/harassment, child/elderly risk, threats, break-in evidence, law-enforcement involvement, or damaged exterior locks to immediate human review.
  • Do not disclose private access logs, camera footage, codes, lockbox details, police-report details, or another resident's information in an automated reply.
  • Do not promise lock-change timing, locksmith cost coverage, reimbursement, entry permissions, legal outcomes, or security-camera findings without verified source records and manager approval.

Copy/paste unauthorized entry source card

  1. Reporter and unit: resident name, unit/building, channel, callback number on file, relationship to leaseholder, authorized-contact status, and any missing identity facts.
  2. Incident type: suspected unauthorized entry, lock tampering, missing key/fob, access-code concern, guest/roommate dispute, maintenance-entry question, police report, break-in, harassment/stalking concern, or requested lock change.
  3. Evidence/source fields: date/time noticed, entry point, photos, damaged lock, camera/access-log request, key/fob serial if available, prior work orders, vendor/maintenance activity, police report number if provided, and exact resident wording.
  4. Policy and routing sources: lease/house rules, key/fob/access policy, lock-change policy, fee schedule, security-camera/privacy policy, police/risk/legal routing, vendor/locksmith procedure, and official communication channel.
  5. Stop-rule notes: immediate safety risk, suspected crime, domestic conflict, harassment, unauthorized occupant/guest dispute, request for private footage/logs, request to accuse another person, fee/reimbursement demand, or pressure to bypass identity/security checks.
  6. Safe owner and CRM label: property manager, security/concierge, on-call maintenance, locksmith/vendor, risk/legal, law enforcement, or resident-services lead; labels: unauthorized_entry_report, lock_change_review, access_log_privacy_review, security_incident_human_review, or stop_automation_access_sensitive.

Resident-safe snippets

Initial acknowledgement

We received your report about possible unauthorized entry or a lock/access concern for [unit/building]. For resident safety and privacy, we are routing this for human review before confirming access records, lock-change options, fees, camera/log details, or next steps.

Access-log or camera request

We cannot share private access logs, camera footage, or another person's information through this channel. The property team will review the approved privacy/security process and let you know what can be confirmed or escalated.

Safety concern or suspected crime

If there is immediate danger, evidence of a break-in, threats, or a safety risk, please use emergency services or the property's emergency process now. We are escalating the report and will avoid guessing about responsibility, footage, lock-change timing, fees, or legal outcomes until source records are checked.
