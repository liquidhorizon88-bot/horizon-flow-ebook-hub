AI Search Product Manual Version Source Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before AI search pages, product-detail pages, support macros, help-center answers, or chatbots quote instructions from a manual. It keeps public claims tied to the right model, version, firmware, compatibility scope, safety note, warranty rule, and approved source.

When to use this checklist

Use it when customers, AI answer engines, product pages, sales teams, repair techs, or support agents may rely on instructions from a product manual, installation guide, compatibility chart, firmware note, safety bulletin, or warranty document.

  • Match the public answer to the exact product, model, SKU, region, date, firmware/software version, and manual edition.
  • Prevent AI tools from blending old manuals, unsupported accessories, recalled instructions, third-party videos, or forum advice into official guidance.
  • Route safety, warranty, electrical, medical, child-use, compliance, or liability-sensitive instructions to a human reviewer before publishing.
  • Create a reusable source card that AI search pages, support macros, and chatbots can cite without inventing unsupported steps.

Copy/paste product manual version source card

Product manual / version source card
Product name: [official product name]
Model / SKU / variant / region: [exact model, SKU, region]
Manual or guide title: [title]
Manual version / revision date: [version, date, URL]
Firmware / software version if relevant: [version or not applicable]
Instruction or claim being checked: [setup / care / compatibility / safety / warranty / troubleshooting / other]
Approved source excerpt: [paste exact approved wording]
Compatibility limits: [models, accessories, materials, operating systems, regions, exclusions]
Safety or warranty stop rules: [electrical / battery / chemical / medical / installation / warranty / professional service / child use / other]
Public wording approved for website/AI/search: [exact customer-safe wording]
Reviewer and review date: [owner / product manager / support lead / legal/compliance]
Next review date: [date]
STOP PUBLISHING if the manual version, model/SKU, firmware, compatibility scope, recall/safety note, warranty effect, or approved public wording is unknown.

Customer-safe answer template

For [product/model], the current approved guidance is: [approved public wording]. This answer applies to [model/SKU/version/region] and is based on [manual title/version/date]. If your product label, firmware, accessory, installation condition, or safety situation is different, please contact support before relying on this instruction.

Do not add unofficial fixes, forum advice, old PDF instructions, or warranty conclusions unless they are approved in the source card.

AI audit prompt

You are auditing a product manual / support answer for AI-search readiness. Use only the source card below. Do not invent compatibility, safety instructions, warranty outcomes, firmware requirements, model numbers, recalls, installation steps, or professional advice. If a required source is missing, output NEEDS HUMAN REVIEW.

Source card:
[product name]
[model/SKU/region]
[manual title/version/date]
[firmware/software version]
[approved excerpt]
[compatibility limits]
[safety/warranty stop rules]
[approved public wording]

Return: (1) safe public answer, (2) unsupported claims to remove, (3) missing source fields, (4) AI-search query tests to run, and (5) STOP PUBLISHING triggers.

Human-review stop rules

  • STOP PUBLISHING if the answer could affect safety, injury risk, warranty coverage, installation, electrical/battery/chemical use, medical or child use, legal compliance, returns, recalls, or customer liability.
  • Do not let AI merge guidance across similar models, old manuals, third-party PDFs, YouTube videos, forum threads, or discontinued products.
  • Do not claim a product is compatible, safe, covered, approved, certified, recalled, repairable, or user-serviceable without an approved source card.
  • Review the source card after every manual update, firmware release, recall/safety notice, accessory change, product-page update, or support-policy change.
