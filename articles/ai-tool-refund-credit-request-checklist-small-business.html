AI Tool Refund and Credit Request Checklist for Small Businesses

AI subscriptions, meeting bots, image generators, transcription tools, and workflow platforms sometimes create duplicate seats, overages, wrong-plan renewals, or surprise usage charges. Use this worksheet before asking a vendor for a refund, credit, usage adjustment, or billing explanation so the request is factual, polite, and owner-reviewed.

Why this exists

A weak billing message says “we were charged too much, please refund us.” A stronger request shows the invoice, plan, usage report, seat list, contract terms, expected amount, missing proof, and requested outcome. That makes vendor support easier and prevents AI from inventing policy, tax, contract, or refund promises.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI dispute a charge, promise a refund, threaten a vendor, reverse a payment, cancel a subscription, alter billing settings, change tax/accounting treatment, or approve a credit memo. A named owner/bookkeeper must verify source proof, vendor terms, and business impact first.

Vendor-safe request snippets

Billing explanation request: Hi [Vendor Support], we are reconciling invoice [ID/date] for [workspace]. Could you provide the usage and seat details behind [charge/overage], including [specific line item], so our owner/bookkeeper can review it accurately?
Credit request: Hi [Vendor Support], our records show [source-backed issue: duplicate seat / unused trial conversion / unexpected overage / wrong plan renewal]. Could you review whether a refund, account credit, or corrected invoice is available under your policy? Source proof is attached: [invoice/export/screenshots].
Internal hold note: Hold approval for [invoice/tool] until [missing proof] is verified. Do not dispute, refund, cancel, downgrade, or change payment settings from an AI draft alone.

Resolution tracking questions

  • Was the charge legitimate usage, duplicate billing, stale seats, wrong plan, accidental automation, or unclear vendor calculation?
  • Did the vendor provide a case ID, corrected invoice, account credit, refund, usage report, or policy explanation?
  • Should the business run a seat audit, renewal review, cancellation/access shutoff, or spend-control review next?
  • Which workflow owner must confirm the tool is still worth the cost after review time and risk are counted?
