Unauthorized Pet and Damage Response Checklist for Property Managers

Use this free worksheet before replying to an unauthorized pet report, pet-damage question, animal-noise complaint, lease-pet-policy issue, or move-out pet-cleaning dispute.

Copy the checklist Get Local Lead Rescue

Use this when

  • A resident, owner, neighbor, vendor, or inspector reports an animal or pet-related property issue.
  • Your team needs to acknowledge the concern without promising fines, lease action, denials, deposits, cleaning charges, or eviction steps.
  • Photos, timestamps, lease/pet-addendum references, assistance-animal sensitivity checks, or manager review are missing.
  • You want AI to summarize a record, not decide enforcement or legal/policy outcomes.

Why this matters

Pet issues can combine customer-service, evidence, privacy, disability-accommodation, and deposit risk.

Property managers often need to respond quickly when someone reports an animal, damaged flooring, yard cleanup, odor, noise, pet fees, unauthorized occupancy, or move-out cleaning costs. A rushed or AI-written response can accidentally cite the wrong lease term, disclose private details, mishandle assistance-animal language, or promise a charge before evidence is reviewed.

This checklist keeps the first response neutral: collect source facts, separate evidence from assumptions, route sensitive items to a manager, and stop automation before making unsupported promises.

Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

Thanks for the update. We have received the message about [issue type] and are routing it for review. Please send any relevant photos, dates, or messages through [approved channel] so the record stays complete.

We cannot confirm charges, lease action, or outcomes in this first message, but we are logging the concern and checking it against the approved property record.

If this involves an accommodation or sensitive resident matter, we will route it through the manager's approved review process before responding further.

Do not say yet

  • “The pet is unauthorized” before checking the approved resident/pet/accommodation record.
  • “You owe a fee / deposit deduction / cleaning charge” before source review and manager approval.
  • Medical, disability, accommodation, assistance-animal, or private resident details in casual messages.
  • Eviction, lease violation, entry, legal, or fair-housing conclusions without an approved reviewer.

Manager-review stop rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION before mentioning unauthorized status, fees, deposits, cleaning charges, lease violations, notices, eviction, entry, assistance-animal status, disability accommodation, fair-housing, or legal deadlines.
  • NEEDS MANAGER REVIEW when evidence is incomplete, the source is disputed, resident privacy is involved, or accommodation sensitivity is possible.
  • Use approved systems. Store evidence, photos, invoices, and sensitive notes inside the property-management record, not scattered text threads.
  • AI is draft-only. AI may summarize intake fields and draft neutral acknowledgements; a human reviewer decides next steps.
