Use when

  • A public page, AI answer, chatbot, social caption, ad draft, or gallery description mentions permission to use customer media.
  • Marketing wants to reuse event photos, class recordings, before/after images, reviews, screenshots, or client story snippets.
  • Consent lives in separate systems: intake forms, event waivers, contract terms, email approvals, CRM notes, or platform permissions.
  • There may be minors, private addresses, license plates, health/legal/financial details, sensitive locations, employees, or third-party logos in the media.

Stop rule before AI publishes or replies

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI, a chatbot, an image captioner, a landing-page rewrite, or a support macro claim that a photo/video/testimonial can be used, licensed, reposted, edited, sold, tagged, recorded, shared, or archived unless the exact source record and owner-approved usage boundary support that claim. Route minors, medical/legal/financial details, employee images, private property, event recordings, accessibility/accommodation details, copyright questions, paid ads, and revocation requests to a human owner or legal/privacy reviewer.

1) Permission not verified

We need to verify the media-release source before using this photo, video, testimonial, or recording publicly. Until the approval scope is confirmed, do not publish, caption, tag, advertise, or reuse it.

2) Limited-scope consent

The consent record appears limited to {approved_channel_or_use}. Do not expand it to paid ads, testimonials, AI training, public galleries, or unrelated campaigns unless the owner approves a new source record.

3) Privacy-sensitive media

This asset may show private, identifying, regulated, or sensitive details. Route it for review and remove/blur sensitive items before any public use.

4) Revocation/update request

We received a media-use update or removal request. Please pause reuse of this asset while the source record, published pages, captions, and galleries are reviewed.

AI audit prompt

You are reviewing a photo/video caption, event gallery, before/after page, testimonial page, case study, social post, ad draft, or AI-search answer. Use only the media consent source card below. Do not invent consent, usage rights, release scope, edit rights, identity clearance, copyright ownership, legal/privacy compliance, minor authorization, revocation status, or permission for paid advertising. Return: 1) missing facts, 2) risky claims to remove, 3) safe public wording, 4) pages/captions to update, and 5) STOP PUBLISHING items.

Weekly source-control questions

  • Do the website gallery, event page, social captions, ad drafts, testimonials, and CRM notes all match the same consent scope?
  • Which AI-generated snippets imply permission, ownership, or reuse rights that are not in the source card?
  • Which older media assets should be blurred, removed, noindexed, archived, or re-approved before reuse?
