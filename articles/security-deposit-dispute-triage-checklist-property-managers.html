Security Deposit Dispute Triage Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before AI or a leasing assistant drafts a reply about a deposit deduction, refund timing, cleaning charge, damage claim, or move-out dispute. It keeps the answer tied to verified records and escalates lease/legal/accounting questions to a human.

When to use this checklist

Use it when a tenant asks why a deposit was withheld, disputes a cleaning or damage charge, asks for a refund date, claims move-out photos were wrong, or requests an exception after a move-out inspection. It is written for property managers, leasing coordinators, virtual assistants, and AI-assisted inbox workflows.

  • Sorting deposit questions into accounting, evidence, manager review, or legal/escalation buckets.
  • Checking whether the reply can be answered from approved move-out records or needs a manager.
  • Creating a tenant-safe response that does not invent deadlines, waivers, lease terms, or deduction outcomes.
  • Documenting the dispute in a CRM/work-order note before a follow-up is sent.

STOP AUTOMATION if legal deadline, habitability, fair-housing, accommodation, debt collection, attorney threat, chargeback, or disputed damage evidence appears.

Tenant-safe reply templates

1. Evidence review needed

Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out. I am going to review the deposit statement, move-out notes, and any photo or invoice records tied to [unit/address] before giving you a specific answer. We will follow up by [time/date] with the verified information or the next review step.

2. Accounting/manager review needed

Hi [Name], your question about [deposit item] needs manager/accounting review before we respond. I do not want to guess on the amount, timing, or decision. We have logged the request and will update you by [time/date] or ask for any missing information.

3. Missing information request

Hi [Name], to route this correctly, could you send [photo / invoice question / forwarding address / specific line item] related to the deposit question? Once we have that, [team member] can compare it with the approved move-out record and follow up.

Do not invent lease terms, legal deadlines, refund timing, deductions, fee waivers, damage conclusions, accounting status, fair-housing answers, or collection language. Create: (1) a short internal triage summary, (2) missing facts, (3) escalation bucket, (4) a tenant-safe draft under 120 words, and (5) the next CRM note.

Human-review stop rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION before mentioning legal deadlines, deposit-return law, debt collection, attorney contact, fair-housing/accommodation issues, eviction history, chargebacks, or any deduction decision not already approved.
  • NEEDS OWNER REVIEW if the tenant disputes evidence, asks for a waiver, alleges damage was pre-existing, raises habitability/safety, or challenges accounting accuracy.
  • Do not promise refund timing, charge removal, payment amount, legal compliance, inspection outcome, or tenant liability unless the approved process explicitly says so.
  • Keep tenant records, photos, invoices, and payment details inside the approved property-management system; do not paste private evidence into public or shared channels.
