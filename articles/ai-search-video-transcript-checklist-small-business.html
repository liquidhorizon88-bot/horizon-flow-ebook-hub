AI Search Video Transcript Checklist for Small Business Websites

Website videos often contain the best explanations of a service, process, proof, or owner story — but AI answer engines cannot reliably use that value if the page has no transcript, summary, or verified context. Use this checklist to make videos readable, quotable, and safe.

Copy the checklist Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Service videos have no transcript or captions.
  • Testimonials or project videos need proof-safe summaries.
  • AI tools misunderstand what a video claims.
  • You want video content to strengthen service, FAQ, and booking pages.

Why this matters

Videos need text evidence around them

A short video can explain a process better than a paragraph, but it still needs crawlable text: a transcript, a short summary, key questions answered, and links to the relevant page. That helps human buyers who skim, accessibility users who need captions, and AI answer systems that need clear source text.

The goal is not to stuff a page with keywords. The goal is to document what the video actually says, what claims are supported, and where a human reviewer has confirmed sensitive details.

Copy/paste artifact

Video transcript and AI-search proof worksheet

Video reviewed: [title / URL / file]
Page where video appears: [URL]
Reviewer: [owner / manager / marketer]
Last reviewed: [date]

1. Video purpose
What buyer question does this video answer?
[service explanation / proof / FAQ / process / team intro / testimonial / booking help]

2. Transcript status
Transcript exists? [yes/no]
Captions exist? [yes/no]
Transcript source: [manual / platform captions / AI draft]
Accuracy spot-check complete? [yes/no]

3. Verified facts from the video
Services mentioned: [only what is actually stated]
Locations mentioned: [only verified locations]
Pricing or guarantee claims: [quote exactly or mark none]
Credentials, safety, legal, health, finance, or warranty claims: [quote exactly or mark none]

4. Page summary draft
Write a 2–3 sentence visible summary using only the verified transcript.
Summary: [plain-language summary]
Do not invent results, service areas, pricing, credentials, or customer outcomes.

5. FAQ opportunities
Buyer question answered in video: [question]
Short answer from transcript: [answer]
Needs owner/legal/safety review? [yes/no]

6. Links and placement
Best page for this video: [service / FAQ / About / portfolio / booking / support]
Related internal links to add: [service page, proof page, booking page, FAQ]

7. Permission and privacy
Customer or employee permission confirmed? [yes/no/not needed]
Private addresses, faces, license plates, account info, job details, or sensitive records visible? [yes/no]
If yes, edit, blur, replace, or remove before publishing.

8. Human-review stop rules
Owner or responsible manager must review before publishing if the transcript or summary mentions price, guarantees, medical/legal/financial advice, safety outcomes, certifications, warranties, emergency service, customer identity, before-and-after results, or location coverage.

AI drafting prompt

Use AI only after the transcript is approved

You are helping a small business turn an approved video transcript into website copy for buyers and AI search.
Use only the transcript and notes below. Do not invent services, locations, pricing, credentials, guarantees, customer results, safety claims, or availability.
Return:
1. a 2-sentence summary,
2. 3 buyer FAQs answered by the video,
3. suggested internal links,
4. claims that need owner review,
5. privacy or permission risks.
Approved transcript and notes:
[paste transcript, page URL, reviewer notes, and permission status]

Implementation

Fix the first three videos that affect buying decisions

  1. Start with a homepage explainer, top service page video, and proof/testimonial/project video.
  2. Add or correct captions, then publish a visible transcript or summary below the video.
  3. Pull 2–3 real buyer questions from the transcript and answer them on the page.
  4. Add one internal link to the relevant service, proof, booking, or FAQ page.
  5. Mark sensitive claims for owner review before publishing or letting AI summarize the page.

Human-review rule: Do not let AI turn a rough transcript into public claims unless a human has verified the facts, permissions, and stop-rule items.
