AI Training Data Opt-Out Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before a small business connects customer notes, emails, call recordings, reviews, files, or internal SOPs to an AI tool. It keeps training-data settings, opt-out evidence, approved data classes, and owner review visible.

Use this when

  • A team is trialing a new AI tool or model.
  • Vendor settings mention training, retention, sharing, or human review.
  • Customer, staff, vendor, or confidential business data may be pasted into prompts.
  • You need a safer decision record before rollout.

1. Copy/paste opt-out decision card

AI tool / vendor: [NAME]
Business owner/reviewer: [NAME + ROLE]
Use case: [WHAT THE TOOL WILL DO]
Data categories involved: [customer notes / emails / call recordings / reviews / photos / SOPs / pricing / vendor files]
Training setting found: [opt-out available / training disabled / enterprise no-train promise / unknown]
Source proof link or screenshot location: [URL OR INTERNAL FILE]
Approved data: [WHAT MAY BE USED]
Blocked data: [WHAT MUST NOT BE USED]
Retention/delete setting: [SETTING + REVIEW DATE]
Human review rule: [WHO APPROVES OUTPUT BEFORE CUSTOMER/STAFF USE]
Decision: [approve pilot / approve with limits / pause / reject]
Next review date: [DATE]

2. Settings to verify

  • Training or model-improvement toggle.
  • Data retention and deletion controls.
  • Admin visibility into prompts, files, and conversations.
  • Whether vendor humans can review submitted content.
  • Whether uploaded files, integrations, and API calls follow the same rules.
  • Enterprise, team, and free-tier differences.

3. STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • Stop if training/retention settings are unknown.
  • Stop if customer consent, regulated data, legal, health, financial, HR, or children’s data is involved.
  • Stop if staff would paste passwords, payment details, private customer history, or trade secrets.
  • Stop if the vendor terms conflict with the company privacy promise.
  • Stop if no owner can verify screenshots/source links.

4. Customer-safe AI review prompt

You are helping a small business review whether an AI tool should be allowed to use business or customer data. Use only the verified facts below. Do not invent vendor promises, opt-out status, legal compliance, customer consent, privacy-policy terms, retention settings, deletion rights, or security certifications. Return: 1) missing evidence, 2) data categories that should be blocked, 3) settings to verify, 4) safe pilot limits, 5) owner-review questions, and 6) STOP AUTOMATION triggers.

Tool/vendor: [NAME]
Use case: [USE CASE]
Data categories: [LIST]
Vendor source links/screenshots: [PASTE]
Current settings: [PASTE]
Existing customer/privacy promises: [PASTE]
Decision needed: [PILOT / PAUSE / APPROVE WITH LIMITS]

5. Plain-language staff note

Before using [AI TOOL], do not paste customer records, call transcripts, payment details, private staff notes, vendor contracts, or confidential files unless [OWNER] has approved the data category. If the tool asks to improve its models or train on our data, stop and send the setting screenshot to [OWNER].
