Local service quote follow-up

Quote Expiry Reminder Text Templates for Local Service Businesses

A quote deadline should help a customer make a clear decision — not pressure them with fake scarcity. Use these reminders to keep open estimates moving while protecting scope, price, availability, and customer trust.

Best fit: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, cleaning, remodeling, pest control, handyman, and property-maintenance teams that send estimates with real validity windows, material-price limits, scheduling windows, or seasonal availability constraints.

Before sending any expiry reminder

  • Verify the quote amount, scope, expiration date, included/excluded items, deposit rules, and scheduling availability in the CRM or proposal system.
  • Do not invent a deadline, discount, warranty, price increase, crew availability, or material shortage.
  • If the quote depends on inspection findings, permit rules, financing, insurance, safety, or legal language, route the message to an owner/manager before sending.
  • Honor SMS/email consent and stop rules. If the customer opted out, do not send marketing-style follow-up by that channel.

1. Three-day friendly quote reminder

Hi [First Name], quick reminder that your quote for [service/project] is still open through [expiry date]. If you have questions about the scope, timing, or next step, reply here and I can help get the right person to review it with you.

2. One-day deadline reminder without pressure

Hi [First Name], your current quote for [service/project] is set to expire tomorrow, [date]. No pressure — I just wanted to make sure you had the deadline before we close it out or refresh pricing/scheduling. Want us to keep it active for review?

3. Scope-check reminder when the customer went quiet

Hi [First Name], I wanted to check whether the quote still matches what you need. If the scope changed, we can update the details before you decide. If not, the current quote remains valid until [expiry date].

4. Expired quote re-open request

Hi [First Name], the quote for [service/project] has now expired, so I do not want to assume the same price or schedule is still available. If you are still interested, reply here and we can review the scope and refresh the quote.

5. Reschedule or revisit later

No problem if now is not the right time. Would you like us to close this quote, or should we check back around [month/date] to see whether the project is still on your list?

CRM labels to add

  • quote-open-expiring — valid quote is inside the final reminder window.
  • quote-scope-check-needed — customer may need updated scope before deciding.
  • quote-expired-refresh-needed — old quote should not be reused without review.
  • quote-close-no-pressure — customer declined or went inactive; stop routine reminders.
  • future-check-in-requested — customer asked to revisit at a later date.

Approved quote amount: [amount]
Confirmed scope: [scope]
Quote expiry date: [date]
Current status: [3 days left / 1 day left / expired / scope changed / future check-in]
Approved next steps: [answer questions / owner call / refresh quote / close quote / schedule]
Channel: [SMS/email]

Draft one message in a calm, helpful tone.
Rules:
- Do not invent deadlines, discounts, warranties, financing, material shortages, price increases, or availability.
- Do not pressure the customer or imply false urgency.
- If the quote is expired, say it needs review before reuse.
- Include a human-review checklist before sending.

Human-review checklist

  • The quote deadline is real and copied from the proposal/CRM.
  • The scope, amount, and availability language match approved records.
  • No false scarcity, unapproved discount, or invented price-change warning is included.
  • The channel is allowed under the customer’s communication consent/opt-out status.
  • The message gives the customer a clear, respectful way to ask questions, refresh the quote, close it, or revisit later.
