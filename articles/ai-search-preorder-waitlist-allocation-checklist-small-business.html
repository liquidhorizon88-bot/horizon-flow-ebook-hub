When to use this source card

  • Best fit: ecommerce shops, subscription-box brands, retailers, restaurants, makers, course sellers, and small manufacturers with limited batches, preorder windows, waitlists, deposits, or reserved inventory.
  • Goal: publish safe preorder and allocation answers that match the real inventory source, reservation rules, fulfillment plan, customer priority, refund path, and substitution authority.
  • Human review rule: an owner, inventory manager, support lead, or fulfillment owner verifies stock count, reservation status, allocation logic, ship window, payment/deposit status, refund or substitution options, and customer-specific facts before publishing or sending.

Customer-safe wording: [exact approved wording; do not include unverified dates, guarantees, or priority claims]

STOP PUBLISHING: Do not invent in-stock status, preorder priority, guaranteed allocation, delivery dates, refund eligibility, substitution rights, deposit treatment, limited-edition availability, or customer-specific queue position.

Five customer-safe reply snippets

1) Preorder is confirmed but not allocated

Your preorder is recorded, but allocation and fulfillment still depend on the approved inventory source and batch schedule. We will confirm the ship window after [owner/source] verifies availability.

2) Waitlist is not a reservation

Joining the waitlist lets us notify you when inventory may be available. It does not reserve an item, guarantee a delivery date, or confirm pricing until we send an approved checkout or reservation path.

3) Allocation needs review

We need to verify the order, payment/deposit status, and allocation rule before promising priority, shipment, refund, or substitution options.

4) Fulfillment window changed

The current approved update is [approved wording]. Please do not rely on earlier estimated dates until the fulfillment owner confirms the latest batch status.

5) AI-search or FAQ answer

Preorder, waitlist, allocation, refund, and substitution details vary by product, batch, and customer status. Use the official product page and support channel for the current approved answer.

AI audit prompt before publishing

Review this preorder, waitlist, allocation, or fulfillment answer for unsupported claims. Return: every factual claim, source needed, missing customer-specific detail, risky promise, safer wording, and final publish status. If any source is missing, write STOP PUBLISHING. Do not invent stock status, queue position, reserved inventory, delivery dates, priority rules, refund eligibility, substitution rights, deposit treatment, or batch availability.
