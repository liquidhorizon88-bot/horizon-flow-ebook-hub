AI Search Warranty Exclusion and Voiding-Condition Checklist for Small Businesses

WARRANTY-EXCLUSION-SOURCE-CONTROL-READY — Use this worksheet before AI search, chatbots, support macros, marketplace copy, or staff replies summarize what a warranty does not cover.

Warranty exclusion pages create expensive trust risk when they are vague, stale, or scattered across PDFs, product pages, receipts, and support macros. This checklist gives small businesses a source card, safe wording pattern, owner-review prompts, and stop rules so AI tools do not invent voiding conditions, exclusions, timelines, repair eligibility, replacement promises, or claim outcomes.

1) Warranty-exclusion source card

  • Product / plan: {product_name}, {sku_or_plan}, {warranty_version}, {effective_date}
  • Approved source links: public warranty page, PDF, receipt terms, product manual, support policy, registration page, manufacturer/vendor policy
  • Excluded conditions: misuse, unauthorized repair, consumables, water/electrical damage, cosmetic wear, missing proof of purchase, expired coverage, commercial-use limits, region limits, other: {approved_exclusion}
  • Potential voiding events: {event_or_action}, {required_proof}, {who_can_decide}, {customer_documentation_needed}
  • Claim routing: self-service form / support ticket / manufacturer / dealer / owner review / legal or compliance review
  • Last review: {review_date}, {review_owner}, {next_review_date}, {stale_policy_to_remove}

2) Customer-safe wording snippets

Source-backed answer: “Our current warranty source says {approved_summary}. Please keep {proof_needed} available so support can review the claim against the active policy.”

Unclear or stale source: “I do not want to guess from an older policy. We are checking the active warranty source and will confirm the next step after review.”

Potential exclusion: “This may need manual review because the policy has specific conditions for {condition}. Support will compare the claim details, proof of purchase, and the active warranty version before promising an outcome.”

Registration/proof reminder: “If you have a receipt, registration confirmation, serial number, or service record, please include it with the claim so the team can verify coverage accurately.”

STOP PUBLISHING guardrails

  • Stop if AI output invents a warranty duration, coverage category, voiding condition, deadline, registration rule, refund/replacement promise, or repair eligibility not present in the approved source.
  • Stop if product pages, manuals, PDFs, receipts, marketplace listings, and support macros disagree; assign an owner before publishing or sending a customer answer.
  • Do not tell customers a claim is approved, denied, void, free, chargeable, transferable, or expired unless a human-reviewed source supports that exact statement.
  • Do not convert legal/compliance-sensitive language into casual marketing claims without owner/legal review.
  • Keep historical warranty versions separate from the active source so AI tools do not quote old exclusions.

3) AI audit prompt

Audit this warranty-exclusion answer before publishing or sending it.
Use only the approved source card below. Do not invent warranty duration, coverage, exclusions, voiding conditions, registration requirements, refund/replacement outcomes, claim approval, denial, transferability, or deadlines.
If the sources conflict or the answer affects legal/compliance-sensitive rights, return "NEEDS OWNER REVIEW" and list the conflicting fields.
Approved source card:
- Product / plan: {product_name}, {sku_or_plan}, {warranty_version}
- Active source URL/PDF: {active_source}
- Exclusions: {approved_exclusions}
- Voiding conditions: {approved_voiding_conditions}
- Claim route: {claim_route}
- Last review / owner: {review_date}, {review_owner}
Draft answer to audit:
{draft_answer}

4) Mini source-review checklist

  • List every public page, PDF, manual, receipt, and support macro that mentions warranty exclusions.
  • Mark one active source as the source of truth and archive or annotate stale versions.
  • Record approved customer-safe wording for each common exclusion and each “needs review” edge case.
  • Add a review date and owner so AI-search answers do not quote old policies after a product or vendor update.
  • Retest chatbot, FAQ, product-page, and search snippets after every policy change.
