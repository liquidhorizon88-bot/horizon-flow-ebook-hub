Use this when

  • A customer, creator, agency partner, influencer, vendor, or referral source asks how a referral or affiliate program works.
  • AI search may combine old launch emails, coupon pages, partner badges, social posts, help articles, and checkout text into one overconfident answer.
  • The team needs safe wording that does not invent commissions, payout dates, affiliate approval, referral credits, coupon stacking, or relationship status.

Why affiliate/referral claims need a source card

Affiliate and referral programs can change across platforms: partner tiers, approval rules, coupon permissions, commission rates, cookie windows, payout thresholds, tax forms, refund clawbacks, brand-use rules, excluded products, and regional limitations may live in separate tools. AI systems can turn an old promo page or creator post into a promise the business cannot honor.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI publish affiliate, referral, partner, coupon, commission, payout, eligibility, approval, or tracking claims until an owner verifies the current source of truth.

Customer-safe reply snippets

Thanks for asking. The current approved affiliate/referral program details are here: [canonical program page]. If your question involves eligibility, payout timing, a refund, coupon stacking, tax paperwork, or a program exception, we need to verify the account/program record before confirming.
I do not want to guess about referral rewards or affiliate terms. Please send [partner email/code/order detail], and our team will compare it against the current program terms before confirming commissions, credits, payout status, or exclusions.
Internal note: Affiliate/referral-program question. Verify canonical terms, affiliate platform settings, coupon rules, order/refund status, partner approval, attribution window, and owner-approved exception rules before publishing or sending a final answer.

Review before publishing

  • Does the public page match affiliate-platform settings, coupon rules, checkout behavior, payout policy, and current partner terms?
  • Does the answer avoid promising guaranteed approval, income, coupon stacking, payout dates, or partner status without account-level verification?
  • Are old launch emails, partner decks, social captions, PDFs, and help macros redirected or labeled as outdated?
  • Is there a clear human owner for tax/payout paperwork, fraud checks, refund clawbacks, brand-use exceptions, and disputed attribution?
