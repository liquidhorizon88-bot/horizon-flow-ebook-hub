Why this matters

AI summaries need source-backed support promises

Customers ask direct questions: “Do they offer refunds?”, “How fast do they respond?”, “Is there a warranty?”, “Who handles urgent problems?” If the website buries or overstates the answer, AI search tools may quote a confusing policy. A support policy page should make real boundaries visible without inventing guarantees.

  1. Name the policy owner: who approves support, refund, warranty, cancellation, and escalation language?
  2. List the exact promise: response window, eligibility, exclusions, fees, dates, documents, or review steps.
  3. Show the source: contract, invoice terms, service agreement, help-center article, or owner-approved FAQ.
  4. Add a stop rule: require human review before AI drafts or publishes exceptions, legal language, pricing, refunds, or safety claims.
