AI Search Setup and Onboarding Fee Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before an AI draft, chatbot, support macro, pricing page, sales page, or answer engine summarizes setup fees, onboarding fees, implementation charges, activation costs, migration fees, waiver rules, or refund exceptions.

AI-SEARCH-SETUP-ONBOARDING-FEE-CHECKLIST-READY

Best fit: SaaS, local service, ecommerce, consulting, agencies, courses, software, repair, subscription, and professional-service businesses that show setup/onboarding/activation fees online or in support replies.

Use with: SEO After AI when public pages, PDFs, checkout copy, quote templates, help-center articles, and AI answer tools need source-controlled fee language.

1. Copy/paste setup-fee source card

Offer or product: [name]
Fee name shown publicly: [setup fee / onboarding fee / activation fee / implementation fee / migration fee / installation fee]
Exact amount or range: [source]
When it applies: [new customer / plan change / custom install / account migration / rush setup / hardware install]
When it does NOT apply: [waiver, promo, existing customer, self-service, plan-specific exception]
Refund/credit rule: [non-refundable / credited after purchase / refundable if not started / manager review required]
Timeline attached to fee: [onboarding call / account setup / installation / migration / provisioning]
Approved source: [pricing page / checkout / contract / quote template / policy / owner approval]
Review date and owner: [date / person]
Unknowns to verify before publishing: [taxes, discounts, region, plan, cancellation, refund, timeline]

2. STOP PUBLISHING before these claims

  • Do not invent fee amounts. AI should not guess setup, onboarding, activation, migration, installation, deposit, or implementation charges from old pages, sales notes, or competitor examples.
  • Do not promise waivers or credits. Verify the promo, plan, manager approval, contract terms, and checkout behavior before saying a fee can be waived, credited, refunded, or discounted.
  • Do not attach unsupported timelines. Onboarding duration, installation windows, migration completion, account activation, or launch dates need source proof.
  • Do not blend one-time and recurring charges. Keep setup fees, subscription fees, deposits, service-call charges, taxes, add-ons, and usage fees clearly separated.
  • Do not publish regulated or contract-sensitive exceptions without owner review. Use NEEDS OWNER REVIEW for financing, refunds, chargebacks, contract terms, taxes, legal/compliance, custom quotes, or enterprise terms.

3. Customer-safe wording snippets

Fee applies, details verified

For [offer], the verified one-time [fee name] is [amount]. It covers [verified setup/onboarding item]. Recurring charges, taxes, optional add-ons, and custom work are listed separately in [source].

Fee may vary or needs review

The setup/onboarding fee can vary based on [verified factor]. I do not want to quote the wrong amount, so we should confirm the current pricing source or quote before publishing or replying.

Waiver or refund not verified

I cannot promise a waiver, credit, or refund from the information available. Please route this to [owner/manager/billing team] with the order, plan, quote, and policy source for review.

4. AI audit prompt

Review this page, help article, chatbot answer, or sales reply for setup/onboarding fee accuracy.
Return: (1) every fee claim, (2) source needed, (3) whether the fee is one-time or recurring, (4) waiver/refund claims, (5) timeline claims, (6) plan/region/customer exceptions, (7) customer-safe wording, and (8) STOP PUBLISHING risks.
Rules: do not invent fee amounts, onboarding deliverables, implementation timelines, waiver eligibility, refund rights, tax treatment, financing terms, contract exceptions, or checkout behavior. If a source is missing, mark NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.
