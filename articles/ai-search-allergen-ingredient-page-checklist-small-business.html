AI Search Allergen and Ingredient Page Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before a website, chatbot, menu page, PDF, Google Business Profile answer, or AI-search result summarizes ingredient lists, allergen warnings, substitutions, dietary fit, product materials, or preparation notes. The goal is not to make medical claims; it is to keep source facts traceable and stop AI from inventing unsafe promises.

Why this deserves a source-of-truth card

AI tools can turn a vague menu note, old PDF, staff answer, or supplier description into a confident public statement. That is risky when customers rely on ingredient, allergen, material, or dietary information. Keep the page practical: approved facts, source dates, who reviewed them, and what AI must not say.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI claim that a product, dish, service, or material is allergen-free, gluten-free, vegan, safe for a medical condition, non-toxic, child-safe, pet-safe, certified, organic, FDA-approved, hypoallergenic, nut-free, dairy-free, or suitable for a specific dietary need unless the owner has a verified source and approved wording. When in doubt, route to a human reviewer and recommend direct staff confirmation.

Safe wording patterns

We list ingredients and allergen notes from our current source records when available, but recipes, suppliers, preparation methods, and substitutions can change. Please contact our team directly before ordering if you have an allergy, dietary restriction, or safety-sensitive question.
Internal note for AI/content tools: use only the approved source card. Do not infer allergens, dietary suitability, certifications, medical safety, or ingredient absence from similar products or old pages.
Customer-safe fallback: I do not want to guess on this. Let me verify the current ingredient/source record with a manager before giving you a specific answer.

AI audit prompt

You are reviewing a public page for ingredient, allergen, material, dietary, or suitability claims. Compare the draft below against the verified source card only. Do not add new claims. Flag any invented allergen-free, gluten-free, vegan, organic, hypoallergenic, medical, safety, certification, child-safe, pet-safe, or FDA/regulatory wording.

Return: 1) exact unsupported claims, 2) source fields needed, 3) safer wording, 4) reviewer questions, and 5) STOP PUBLISHING triggers.

Fast QA before publication

  • Does every ingredient, allergen, dietary, certification, or material claim point to a dated source?
  • Does the draft avoid implying medical safety, guaranteed absence, or suitability for a condition?
  • Does it include a human contact path for allergy-sensitive or safety-sensitive questions?
  • Does it handle recipe, supplier, substitution, seasonal, or preparation variability without overclaiming?
  • Does the AI audit compare against current facts rather than old PDFs, web snippets, or similar products?
