AI Chatbot Handoff Failure Log for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet when a website chatbot, AI inbox helper, or automated support draft misses context, gives a risky answer, or fails to hand a customer to a person. It gives small teams a simple record, owner, fix path, and customer-safe response before the mistake repeats.

When to use this log

  • A chatbot answers with the wrong price, availability, policy, refund, service detail, or escalation path.
  • A customer asks for a human but the conversation remains stuck in automation.
  • An AI draft looks plausible but lacks the source note, CRM context, or owner approval needed before replying.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let the bot send follow-up promises about refunds, legal/policy decisions, health/safety issues, payments, warranties, access, scheduling, or service availability until a human owner reviews the source record.

Customer-safe handoff note

Hi [Name] — thanks for flagging this. I am moving this to a person so we can check the right source before replying with a firm answer.

What we are checking: [policy/order/job/source]
Human owner: [name/team]
Expected update window: [safe window]

We do not want the automated reply to overpromise. We will confirm the accurate answer after review.

Weekly fix review

  • Group failures by root cause: stale source, missing escalation rule, unclear customer-status field, or risky prompt wording.
  • Update the public/internal source page before changing the prompt; otherwise the chatbot may keep guessing.
  • Add a handoff rule for sensitive topics: billing, refunds, legal/policy, medical/safety, warranty, identity, tenant/access, pricing, and schedule guarantees.
  • Re-test the exact customer scenario and keep screenshots or transcripts as proof.

AI review prompt

You are reviewing an AI chatbot handoff failure for a small business. Compare the transcript, source record, proposed customer update, and escalation rule. Flag unsupported promises, missing source proof, sensitive topics needing human review, and unclear owner/due-date fields. Return: (1) safe response draft, (2) hold-for-owner reasons, (3) source/prompt/integration fix, and (4) re-test scenario.
