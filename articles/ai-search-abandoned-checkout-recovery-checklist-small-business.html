AI Search Abandoned Checkout Recovery Checklist for Small Businesses

Abandoned-cart emails, AI search snippets, support macros, and chatbots can accidentally promise a discount, reserved inventory, free shipping, payment retry, or privacy status that is not true. Use this source card before publishing an AI-assisted checkout recovery answer or sending a follow-up.

STOP PUBLISHING guardrail

ABANDONED-CHECKOUT-RECOVERY-CARD-READY: Do not publish, email, text, index, or let an AI assistant answer abandoned-checkout recovery questions unless a human can point to the exact cart state, customer consent, product availability, discount/coupon rules, shipping/tax rules, payment retry path, privacy rule, and approved follow-up wording. Mark missing facts as NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.

Abandoned checkout recovery source card

FieldSource to verifySafe wording note
Cart / checkout ID[Cart record, ecommerce platform, CRM, checkout session]Match the follow-up to the exact session; do not guess what was in the cart.
Customer contact permission[Opt-in, checkout terms, SMS/email consent, suppression list]Stop if the channel is not allowed or the customer opted out.
Product availability[Inventory, SKU, variant, backorder, digital-product access]Do not imply an item is reserved or in stock unless the system says so.
Discount / coupon eligibility[Promotion rules, expiry, minimum order, customer segment]Never invent a coupon, extension, stackable discount, or price guarantee.
Shipping, tax, delivery, fulfillment[Checkout calculator, policy page, carrier/service rules]Use “estimated at checkout” unless the final amount is source-backed.
Payment retry path[Checkout URL, payment provider, failed-payment status]Do not ask for card details by email or text. Point only to the official secure checkout.
Privacy / sensitive products[Customer privacy policy, support rules, product category]Avoid exposing product names or personal details in subject lines or texts when sensitive.
Do-not-say list[Unsupported phrases AI must not repeat]Examples: “reserved for you,” “discount guaranteed,” “free shipping applies,” “payment approved,” “we saved your card.”

Customer-safe recovery snippets

Helpful reminder

“It looks like checkout may not have been completed. If you still want [item/category], you can return through the secure checkout link here: [official link]. Availability, shipping, tax, and discounts are confirmed at checkout.”

Discount only when verified

“If your cart qualifies for [promotion name], the checkout will apply it automatically or show the code field. If it does not appear, reply here and we can review eligibility before promising a discount.”

Stock-sensitive

“We cannot reserve inventory from an incomplete checkout. Please check the product page or checkout for the latest availability before placing the order.”

Privacy-safe SMS

“Thanks for visiting [business]. If you need help completing checkout, use the official link: [link]. Reply STOP if you do not want these reminders.”
