Q&A bank

10 useful answers

What is the safest first AI workflow for a small business?

Start with a repeatable draft that a human already reviews: FAQ answers, estimate follow-ups, SOP drafts, review responses, or weekly summaries. Avoid legal, hiring, medical, financial, and final pricing decisions until you have a mature review process.

How do I stop AI from inventing details?

Tell it to use only the facts you provide and to create a “Needs human confirmation” list for missing prices, dates, policies, or availability. Make that list part of the output every time.

What should a small business measure when testing AI?

Measure one simple outcome: minutes saved, faster reply time, fewer missed steps, cleaner SOPs, or fewer drafts needing major edits. If you cannot measure it weekly, the workflow is probably too vague.

How fast should local businesses respond to leads?

Same day is the minimum; faster is better for high-intent calls and forms. But speed without clarity still loses customers. Ask one useful question and give one clear next step.

What is a non-spammy follow-up rhythm?

Try three touches: immediate reply, next-business-day check-in, and one final close-the-loop message. Stop if the customer opts out or the lead is no longer relevant.

Should AI send customer replies automatically?

Not at first. Let AI draft and organize. A person should approve messages that include prices, timing, availability, warranties, legal/compliance language, or sensitive customer situations.

Why do paid leads still go cold?

Usually because there is no owner, no response-time rule, or no tracking after the first touch. Before buying more ads, audit missed calls, web forms, DMs, old estimates, and referral follow-ups.

What should go into a lead tracker?

Source, date received, customer name, service type, location, urgency, owner, next action, last touch, status, and outcome. Keep it simple enough that the team will actually update it.

What makes an AI prompt reusable?

Reusable prompts have variables, examples, output format, tone rules, banned claims, and a human review checklist. A one-off prompt becomes a system when someone else can run it correctly.
