First-Reply Quality Checklist for Local Service Leads

Fast replies help only if the message is useful. Use this checklist before saving a missed-call, web-form, DM, or marketplace response as a reusable template. The goal is a first reply that confirms the request, asks for the missing detail, offers a clear next step, and avoids invented availability, pricing, diagnosis, or promises.

When to use this checklist

  • Before turning one good reply into a reusable SMS, email, phone, DM, or marketplace lead template.
  • When a lead source is generating replies but not bookings, estimates, or qualified conversations.
  • Before asking AI to rewrite or personalize lead responses from CRM notes.
  • Human-review rule: route messages involving emergencies, safety, legal issues, tenant/owner disputes, warranty/refund promises, financing, or pricing exceptions to a manager before sending.

Copy/paste first-reply QA worksheet

Lead source: phone / web form / text / Google Business Profile / Facebook / Yelp / Angi / Thumbtack / referral / other

Customer need in their words: [paste only the non-private request summary]

Reply goal: book appointment / qualify job / request photos / confirm service area / route urgent issue / explain next step / close out politely

Speed check: response sent within [target window] or escalated to [owner] if target was missed.

Specificity check: mention the request type, location/service area only if verified, and one concrete next step.

Missing detail to ask: address or ZIP / photos / model or issue / access window / decision maker / budget range / timeline / safety concern / other.

Do not invent: availability, diagnosis, price, discounts, warranty, financing terms, referral promises, emergency coverage, crew ETA, or prior customer history.

CRM label: new lead / needs info / urgent review / quote requested / booked / out of area / not a fit / no response / STOP AUTOMATION

Owner review needed? yes/no — reviewer: [name]

Five quality checks before sending

  1. It is fast enough: the lead receives a reply while the request is still fresh, or the delay is acknowledged without excuses.
  2. It is specific enough: the first sentence proves a human or reviewed workflow understood the request.
  3. It asks for one next detail: do not bury the lead under five questions; ask for the single piece of information needed to move forward.
  4. It protects trust: no made-up time slots, prices, guarantees, diagnosis, or “we can definitely” promises.
  5. It creates a trackable next step: the CRM/status label tells the team who owns the follow-up and when to check again.

Reusable first-reply template

Hi [First Name], thanks for reaching out about [service/request]. We can help review the next step. To route this correctly, can you send [one missing detail]? Once we have that, [owner/team] can confirm [appointment/estimate/next-step] options. If this involves safety, active damage, or an urgent issue, please call [verified phone] so we can triage it faster.
