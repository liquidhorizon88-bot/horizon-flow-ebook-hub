AI Tool Incident Response Checklist for Small Businesses

When an AI-assisted workflow sends the wrong draft, summarizes the wrong facts, exposes sensitive input, or creates a customer-trust problem, the safest next step is not panic or denial. Use this checklist to pause, document, correct, and decide whether the workflow can restart.

Use this when

  • An AI draft used wrong facts, tone, pricing, policy, or customer details.
  • A prompt included data that should not have been shared with the tool.
  • A team member skipped the human-review step.
  • You need a simple owner-reviewed correction workflow before restarting.

Why this matters

AI mistakes become expensive when no one owns the correction

Small businesses can use AI safely for drafting, sorting, summarizing, and templating, but only if weak outputs are caught and reviewed. A lightweight incident process turns mistakes into better prompts, clearer data rules, and stronger approval gates.

This worksheet is educational. It is not legal, privacy, security, HR, accounting, or compliance advice. Escalate incidents involving sensitive data, regulated information, safety, money, employment, contracts, or legal claims to the right professional before responding.

Copy/paste artifact

AI incident triage checklist

  1. Pause the workflow: stop further sends, uploads, automations, or reuse of the prompt/output until reviewed.
  2. Save the evidence: original input, AI output, tool name, date/time, user, source document, destination, and who saw it.
  3. Classify the issue: wrong facts, wrong customer, private data, tone/brand issue, policy/pricing claim, unsafe advice, hallucinated source, or skipped review.
  4. Name the owner: assign one person to decide correction wording, customer/internal update, and restart rules.
  5. Correct the record: update CRM, document, website, email draft, ticket, or customer note with the verified correction.
  6. Notify only when useful and truthful: do not overstate certainty, blame, severity, or legal conclusions.
  7. Fix the workflow: improve the prompt, remove risky data, add missing source fields, require approval, or retire the workflow.
  8. Restart only after review: document the new rule and test one safe example before team-wide use.

Human-review rule: a human owner must verify impact, customer wording, privacy, security, safety, money, contracts, HR, policy, legal, and compliance details before any correction is sent. Do not let AI invent severity, blame, exposure, legal obligations, customer promises, source facts, or remediation claims.

Customer/internal update templates

Short correction messages

Internal team note

Quick correction: the AI-assisted [draft/summary/template] for [workflow/customer/project] included [verified issue]. Please do not use that output. The corrected source of truth is [verified fact/source]. [Owner] is reviewing the workflow before it is used again.

Customer-safe correction

Hi [Name] — I want to correct an earlier note about [topic]. The accurate information is [verified correction]. Sorry for the confusion. We are reviewing the workflow that produced the draft so this does not repeat.

Restart note

Workflow restart decision: [workflow] may resume only when [source field], [reviewer], and [approval step] are present. Do not use it for [restricted topic/data]. Next audit date: [date].
