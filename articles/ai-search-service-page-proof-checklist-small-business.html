AI Search Service Page Proof Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before rewriting a service page for Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, or classic SEO. The goal is simple: make the page specific enough to quote and cautious enough to trust.

Copy the worksheet Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • A service page is vague about who it helps.
  • AI summaries miss key service, location, or proof details.
  • The page has claims that need owner/legal review.
  • You need a safer brief before hiring a writer or using AI.

Why this matters

Service pages need evidence, not hype

AI answer engines and search snippets often compress a service page into a few claims. If the page is generic, unsupported, or inconsistent with public profiles, the summary can become vague or wrong. A proof checklist gives owners and marketers a simple way to separate facts that are safe to publish from claims that need review.

This is not a shortcut for ranking. It is a quality-control layer for service pages that should help both human buyers and AI systems understand the offer accurately.

Service-page proof worksheet

Last proof review: [date]
Primary buyer: [homeowner / property manager / founder / marketer / other]
Service area or audience: [locations, industries, or remote fit]

1. Plain-language service promise
Write one sentence that names the customer, service, outcome, and location/audience.
Supported sentence:
[We help ...]
Needs review before publishing:
[anything involving prices, guarantees, timelines, licenses, results, safety, legal, medical, or financial claims]

2. Approved service facts
List what is definitely true and current:
- Services included:
- Services not included:
- Required customer inputs:
- Typical process steps:
- Service-area limits:

3. Proof inventory
Check only proof that is approved for public use:
[ ] reviews/testimonials with permission
[ ] certifications/licenses/memberships
[ ] years in business or team experience
[ ] before/after examples with consent
[ ] process screenshots or photos
[ ] policy or warranty language that matches the real policy
[ ] case examples with sensitive details removed

4. Buyer questions the page should answer
- What problem does this service solve?
- Who is and is not a fit?
- What happens after someone contacts us?
- What does the customer need to prepare?
- What factors affect price, timing, or availability?
- What proof shows the business can do this?

5. AI-search clarity checks
[ ] Page title names the service and audience/location.
[ ] H1 says the real service, not a slogan.
[ ] First paragraph explains who, what, where, and next step.
[ ] FAQ answers are specific but not overpromising.
[ ] Internal links point to related services, proof, contact, and FAQ pages.
[ ] Organization/contact details match public profiles.

6. Human review stop rules
Do not publish until a responsible human verifies prices, guarantees, licenses, safety claims, legal/compliance language, medical/financial advice, customer proof, and service-area promises.

Use only the service-page copy and approved business notes pasted below.
Do not invent prices, outcomes, service areas, guarantees, certifications, customer stories, statistics, legal claims, safety claims, or timelines.

1. the clearest supported service promise,

Implementation

Improve one high-intent service page first

  1. Pick the service page closest to revenue, not the easiest page.
  2. Fill in the worksheet with facts the business can verify.
  3. Rewrite the first screen so the customer, service, location/audience, proof, and next step are obvious.
  4. Add a short FAQ with only approved answers.
  5. Link to one proof page, one related service, one free resource, and one contact/booking step.
  6. Run the stop rules before publishing.

Human review rule: a responsible business owner, manager, attorney, compliance reviewer, or subject-matter expert should approve any claim that could affect money, safety, law, health, warranty, availability, or customer expectations.
