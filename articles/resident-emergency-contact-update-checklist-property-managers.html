Resident Emergency Contact Update Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before AI, an after-hours coordinator, leasing assistant, maintenance dispatcher, or resident-services team relies on emergency-contact details. It helps separate routine contact updates from true safety escalation and keeps private resident information out of unsupported automated replies.

RESIDENT-EMERGENCY-CONTACT-CARD-READY

When to use this

Use this source card when a resident changes an emergency contact, asks who is on file, wants a relative or caregiver added, reports a disconnected phone, asks staff to contact someone else about maintenance access, or when a vendor/manager needs to know whether a third party can receive updates.

Human-review rule: emergency contacts are not automatic permission to discuss rent, lease, legal, medical, payment, complaint, or maintenance details. A property manager should verify policy, lease language, resident consent, privacy rules, and urgency before using or sharing contact information.

Resident-safe confirmation snippets

  • Routine update received: “Thanks — we received your emergency-contact update request for [contact name]. Our team will verify the record before using it for any resident or maintenance communication.”
  • Need source proof: “To keep your account private, please submit this update through [approved channel] or contact the office directly so we can verify the change before editing the record.”
  • Limited-scope contact: “We can note [contact name] for [specific purpose], but lease/account details still need to be handled through the resident or an approved manager-reviewed authorization.”
  • Emergency/safety boundary: “If this is an immediate safety, medical, fire, police, or utility emergency, please contact the appropriate emergency service first, then update the property team through the official channel.”

STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION if the requester is not verified as the resident, authorized agent, or approved staff member.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if the message asks staff to reveal another person's phone, email, account status, payment, complaint, lease, medical, or legal details.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if a contact update appears tied to domestic conflict, harassment, lockout, elder/medical issue, law enforcement, restraining order, accessibility/accommodation, or emergency welfare concern.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if AI is about to call, text, email, or disclose details to a third party without a verified permission scope.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if a vendor, owner, or resident asks to use emergency-contact details for convenience instead of an approved property-management process.
