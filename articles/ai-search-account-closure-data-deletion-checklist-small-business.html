AI Search Account Closure and Data Deletion Request Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this source card before a chatbot, AI-search result, privacy FAQ, cancellation page, support macro, or staff draft tells a customer what will happen when they close an account, request data deletion, export records, cancel service, or ask whether billing and retained records disappear.

Why this matters

Account-closure and data-deletion answers are easy for AI tools to overstate. A stale help page or support macro can accidentally promise that all records disappear, that billing stops immediately, that invoices are erased, that backups are purged, or that a request is legally complete before a human privacy/account owner has reviewed the facts.

This worksheet helps small SaaS, ecommerce, agencies, membership businesses, and service teams map the approved sources before AI-visible content mentions deletion scope, export timing, retention exceptions, cancellation, refunds, identity checks, or account-access changes. It is operational guidance, not legal advice.

FieldCopy/paste valueReview rule
Request type[Close account / delete personal data / export data / cancel subscription / remove user / revoke access / other]Separate account access, billing, privacy records, and marketing preference requests.
Identity and authority proof[Verified email/domain, account owner, admin role, customer ID, ticket ID, signed request, delegated user]Do not confirm or change account data until identity and authority are verified through the approved channel.
System/source inventory[App account, CRM, billing processor, email list, support desk, analytics, backups, file storage, third-party tools]Do not say “all data” if downstream processors, backups, invoices, legal/accounting records, or logs have exceptions.
Export or access path[Portal export, manual CSV/PDF, support ticket, not available, owner review needed]State only the approved export path and expected status; avoid inventing formats or dates.
Retention / exception source[Policy URL, contract, accounting rule, fraud/security log, legal hold, backup window, compliance owner]Use cautious language and route legal/privacy interpretations to the owner or privacy reviewer.
Billing and subscription impact[Cancellation date, renewal status, invoice balance, refund/credit rule, payment processor source]Do not promise refunds, credit, charge reversal, or immediate billing deletion without approved billing proof.
Completion proof and customer-safe note[Ticket ID, reviewer, timestamp, systems updated, remaining exceptions, follow-up channel]Keep internal notes private; send only the approved customer-safe summary.

Customer-safe wording starters

Thanks for the request. I’m verifying the account owner, affected systems, billing status, and any retention exceptions before promising what can be closed, exported, or deleted. The next safe step is [identity check / owner review / export path / privacy ticket].
I do not want to overstate this. Some records such as invoices, security logs, backups, or legally required business records may follow a separate retention rule. We will use the approved policy/source and confirm the request through [official channel].
