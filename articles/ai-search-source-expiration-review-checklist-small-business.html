AI Search Source Expiration Review Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this before updating service pages, FAQs, schema, AI-chatbot answers, or answer-engine optimization notes that depend on dated sources. It helps a small business avoid letting AI tools repeat expired offers, old policies, stale hours, dead proof links, or unsupported service claims.

2. What to update before AI tools quote the old answer

  • Expired offers: remove or mark expired coupons, seasonal pricing, financing notes, and limited-time language before AI summaries reuse them.
  • Stale policies: check cancellation, refund, warranty, privacy, accessibility, and service-limit wording against the source owner.
  • Old proof links: repair 404s, moved case studies, changed partner pages, or outdated testimonials before using them as citation anchors.
  • Conflicting pages: choose one canonical source before updating schema, internal links, and answer-engine prompts.

3. AI audit prompt

You are auditing a small-business website for AI-search source expiration risk.
Use only the source-expiration review card below. Do not invent current prices, policies, availability, guarantees, certifications, partnerships, legal/compliance claims, hours, or service limits.
Review card: [paste fields]
Return:
1) claims that are safe to keep;
2) claims that need owner review;
3) claims that should be removed, updated, redirected, or marked expired;
4) affected surfaces to update: page copy, FAQ, schema, GBP, chatbot, directory, sitemap/internal links;
5) a customer-safe replacement sentence only when the source is current.
If the source is missing or expired, write NEEDS OWNER REVIEW instead of a final answer.

STOP PUBLISHING

  • Do not let AI publish or rewrite dated claims without a current source owner and review date.
  • Do not invent discounts, hours, eligibility, guarantees, coverage, certification status, partner status, legal/compliance language, customer proof, or availability.
  • Stop if two public pages conflict and no owner has chosen the canonical source.
  • Use NEEDS OWNER REVIEW for source gaps, expired offers, dead proof links, or policy-sensitive promises.

4. Weekly review questions

  • Which AI-search-visible pages contain dates, deadlines, policy promises, or proof links with no owner?
  • Which pages did AI tools or chatbot drafts quote after the source expired?
