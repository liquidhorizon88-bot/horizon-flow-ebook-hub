Permit and HOA approval text templates for local service businesses

Some estimates stall because the customer needs a permit, HOA approval, landlord sign-off, utility locate, access document, or internal owner approval before scheduling. Use these templates to keep the lead warm without pretending approval is finished or giving legal/permit advice by text.

Use these when

  • A project cannot be scheduled until a permit, HOA, landlord, or owner approval is complete
  • A customer needs a safe reminder of missing documents or next steps
  • The office needs a CRM note that separates approval status from scheduling readiness
  • A rep needs to avoid inventing dates, code requirements, fees, or approval guarantees

Guardrail first

Do not invent permit, HOA, landlord, utility, or legal approval status

These templates are for existing leads or customers who already asked about a job, estimate, or schedule. They are not cold-text scripts. Before sending, verify your business has permission to text the customer and that the approval step belongs in your normal process.

  1. Name the blocker plainly: permit, HOA, landlord, utility locate, access form, owner approval, insurance document, or internal review.
  2. Do not give legal or code advice: point to the customer, office, municipality, association, landlord, or approved specialist.
  3. Keep scheduling conditional: use “after approval is confirmed,” not “approved” or “guaranteed.”
  4. Log the next owner: customer, office manager, dispatcher, estimator, property manager, or owner.

Copy/paste approval follow-up text templates

1) Missing approval reminder

Hi {first_name}, quick note on the {service} estimate: before we can schedule {next_step}, we still need {approval_or_document} confirmed. Once you have it, reply READY and we can review the next available window.

2) HOA or landlord approval handoff

Hi {first_name}, for this {project_type}, {business_name} needs the HOA/landlord approval status confirmed before scheduling. We cannot confirm approval by text, but if you send the approved document or contact instructions, we will update the job file.

3) Permit/process status check

Hi {first_name}, checking in on the {permit_or_process} step for {service}. We do not want to guess at requirements or timing. Has the approval been confirmed, or should we keep the estimate open while you check?

4) Utility locate or access document reminder

Hi {first_name}, before {crew_or_estimator} can proceed with {service}, we need {locate_access_or_document} confirmed. Reply DONE when it is ready, or QUESTIONS if you want the office to review what is still missing.

5) Scheduling-hold without fake urgency

Hi {first_name}, we can hold the next scheduling step until {approval_or_document} is confirmed. No pressure — reply READY when it is complete, and we will check the current schedule then.

6) CRM note for approval-blocked estimates

CRM note: {customer_name} / {estimate_id} is blocked by {approval_or_document}. Do not mark approved or scheduled until {evidence_or_owner} confirms. Next check: {date} by {team_member}.

Human review stop rules

  • Do not say a permit, HOA, landlord, utility locate, inspection, or owner approval is complete unless the approved evidence is in the job file.
  • Do not invent fees, deadlines, code requirements, legal interpretations, association rules, or municipality timelines.
  • Do not pressure the customer with fake scarcity. Keep the scheduling step conditional and truthful.
  • Do not collect sensitive documents over insecure channels if your business has a secure portal or approved upload process.
  • Have an owner, manager, property manager, or permit-trained team member review anything beyond the approved script.
