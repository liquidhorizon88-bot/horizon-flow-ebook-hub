Daily Lead Queue Triage Board for Local Service Businesses

A fast first reply only matters if the team can see every open lead in one place. Use this copy/paste board to sort missed calls, web forms, texts, Google Business Profile messages, DMs, quotes, and stale leads by urgency, owner, due time, consent, and stop rule before another lead disappears.

When to use this board

  • Your calls, forms, texts, DMs, marketplace leads, referrals, and voicemails are handled by different people or tools.
  • Leads get answered once, then disappear when the customer does not reply immediately.
  • Dispatch, estimating, office staff, and owners argue over who should follow up next.
  • You need a daily 10-minute review that shows which leads are hot, blocked, stale, unsafe for automation, or ready to close out.

Daily triage workflow

  1. Pull every source. Check missed calls, voicemail, web forms, inbox, text platform, Google messages, DMs, marketplace apps, and quote pipeline before the daily review.
  2. Add one row per open lead. Do not bury leads inside notes or chat threads. If a lead still needs action, it gets a board row.
  3. Assign priority. Emergency and safety items move to a live human immediately. Same-day revenue opportunities get a due time within the workday. Nurture and closeout rows get clear next review dates.
  4. Assign one owner. If two people are responsible, nobody is responsible. Put one name in the owner column and use notes for collaborators.
  5. Pick the next action. Choose the smallest useful move: call, text, ask for photo, confirm access, request decision-maker, schedule, send quote, or close out.
  6. Update CRM labels. Suggested labels: new-unassigned, same-day-callback, needs-photo, quote-review, waiting-customer, manager-review, do-not-contact, closed-lost, booked.
  7. Review old rows. Anything older than its due time must be reassigned, escalated, or closed with a final helpful note.

STOP AUTOMATION rules

Stop automation and route to a human owner before any AI-drafted reply is sent when the row includes:

  • Emergency, safety issue, active leak, electrical hazard, lockout, or health risk.
  • Tenant/landlord, property-access, lease, insurance, permit, HOA, legal, refund, warranty, financing, or collections issue.
  • Pricing exception, scope dispute, angry customer, bad review risk, promised callback already missed, or manager approval needed.
  • Opt-out, wrong number, no SMS consent, platform rules that prohibit off-platform contact, or unknown contact permission.

AI can help summarize the row and draft options, but it should not invent availability, price, diagnosis, approval, warranty coverage, or urgency.

Board-ready first-touch templates

Same-day callback needed

Hi {first_name}, this is {business_name}. We saw your {source} about {verified_need}. {owner_name} is reviewing it now and can follow up {callback_window}. If this is urgent or unsafe, please call {phone} so a person can help right away.

Missing detail before quote

Hi {first_name}, thanks for reaching out to {business_name}. To route this to the right person, could you send {missing_detail}? Once we have that, {owner_name} will review the next step by {due_time}.

Closeout after no answer

Hi {first_name}, we did not want to keep bothering you. We are closing this request for now, but you can reply here or call {phone} if you still want help with {verified_need}. We will reopen it for review.

10-minute daily review agenda

  1. Open the board and sort by overdue due time, then emergency/same-day priority.
  2. Clear new-unassigned rows first.
  3. Confirm every active row has owner, due time, next action, and allowed channel.
  4. Move human-review rows to the right manager or owner before any automated follow-up is drafted.
  5. Close out stale rows only after the final helpful touch and CRM note are complete.
