Commercial clients, property managers, HOAs, and larger jobs often need a certificate of insurance before scheduling. Use these copy/paste messages to request the exact certificate-holder details, keep the lead moving, and avoid inventing coverage, limits, approvals, or legal promises.

Why this matters

COI paperwork can stall qualified jobs

A certificate of insurance request is a buying signal, but a sloppy reply can lose momentum. The goal is not to debate coverage in a text thread. The goal is to collect exact certificate instructions, route them to the right internal owner or insurance agent, and set a realistic follow-up without promising approval before the document is issued and accepted.

  1. Collect exact wording: certificate holder name, mailing address, email recipient, job address, additional-insured requirements, waiver/subrogation wording, and deadline.
  2. Separate request from approval: confirm the request was received, not that coverage is approved or sufficient.
  3. Assign an owner: office/admin/owner/insurance agent should own the COI task and next check time.
  4. Use stop rules: human review is required before coverage limits, legal compliance, indemnity, additional insured, or warranty language is sent.
