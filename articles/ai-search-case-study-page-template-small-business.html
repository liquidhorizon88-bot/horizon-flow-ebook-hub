AI Search Case Study Page Template for Small Businesses

AI search tools are more likely to quote pages that explain who was helped, what changed, what evidence supports the claim, and what limits apply. Use this worksheet to publish case studies that are useful without inventing customer results.

Start with the review-proof checklist

Best fit

Consultants, agencies, local-service companies, SaaS teams, and small businesses that have real customer wins but need a cleaner case-study page for Google, AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Gemini, and Copilot.

A case study page should not be a vague victory lap. It should be a source page: clear facts, customer permission, before/after context, proof boundaries, and answerable questions. If an AI tool summarizes the page, it should know exactly what is supported and what is not.

Before publishing, confirm the customer has approved any name, logo, quote, screenshot, metric, or story detail. If you do not have permission, anonymize the example and label it honestly.

Copy/paste page structure

Case study page template

1. Page title

[Customer type] improved [specific outcome] with [service/product/process]

Review: Use a supported outcome, not a broad claim like “doubled revenue” unless you can prove it.

2. One-sentence summary

[Customer/business type] needed [problem]. We helped by [action]. The result was [supported result or observable improvement] over [time period].

Review: Include dates or a time window when available.

3. Customer context

Industry: [industry]. Size: [team/location/customer count if approved]. Starting situation: [what was happening before]. Constraints: [budget, staffing, season, system limits].

Review: Remove private details, addresses, account IDs, and anything the customer has not approved.

4. Problem section

The main challenge was [problem]. It caused [operational effect]. The team needed [decision criteria or goal].

Review: Do not exaggerate pain points or make the customer look careless.

5. Work performed

We implemented [step 1], [step 2], and [step 3]. Tools/processes used: [approved tools]. Human review stayed in place for [quality/safety/approval step].

Review: Separate your work from the customer’s internal work.

6. Evidence and result

Supported result: [metric/quote/observable change]. Evidence source: [CRM/export/email/customer quote/screenshot/manual count]. Date range: [range].

Review: Mark estimates as estimates. Do not invent screenshots, testimonials, revenue, rankings, or customer quotes.

7. Limitations

This result may not apply to [different customer type/market/channel]. The outcome depended on [inputs, offer, traffic, staffing, seasonality].

Review: Limitations make the page more trustworthy and reduce risky claims.

8. Next-step CTA

If your business has a similar [problem], start by auditing [free checklist/resource]. Then decide whether [service/product] is the next step.

Review: Prefer a helpful free-resource CTA before a paid offer.

AI drafting prompt

Use AI to organize approved proof, not invent a win

You are helping a small business draft a proof-safe case study page for AI search visibility.
Facts supplied by the business: [paste customer-approved facts, source notes, quote approvals, metric date ranges, screenshots/descriptions].
Return: (1) case study title options, (2) one-sentence summary, (3) problem/work/result/limitations sections, (4) FAQ questions AI search tools may answer, (5) claims that need proof or customer approval before publishing.
Rules: do not invent customer names, quotes, revenue, rankings, percentages, screenshots, dates, or results. If proof is missing, write "needs proof" and suggest a safer wording.
  • Human review: Confirm customer permission, metric source, quote wording, screenshots, claims, privacy, and legal/compliance concerns before publishing.
  • Stop rule: Escalate health, finance, legal, employment, safety, regulated-industry, or unusually large performance claims to the owner or qualified reviewer.
  • AI-search FAQ ideas: who was this for, what changed, how long it took, what evidence supports it, what limitations apply, and what the next step is.

Publication checklist

Before the case study goes live

  1. Customer permission is documented for names, logos, quotes, screenshots, metrics, and story details.
  2. Every number has a source, date range, and owner.
  3. Claims are specific enough to be useful but not broader than the evidence.
  4. The page links to relevant service/product pages and a free resource for readers who are not ready to buy.
  5. FAQ answers are written for humans first and can be summarized accurately by AI tools.
  6. Review, privacy, legal, and compliance stop rules are visible in the internal checklist.
