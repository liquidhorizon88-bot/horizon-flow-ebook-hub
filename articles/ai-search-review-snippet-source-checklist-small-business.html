AI Search Review Snippet Source Checklist for Small Businesses

AI search tools and website snippets can flatten one customer quote into an unsupported promise. Use this worksheet before reusing reviews, testimonials, ratings, or case-study lines on pages that answer engines may summarize.

Use when

  • A page, FAQ, landing page, or AI-generated draft quotes customer reviews or testimonials.
  • A business wants AI search tools to understand proof without inventing guarantees, outcomes, credentials, or ratings.
  • A marketer needs a source-of-truth card before publishing review snippets across website, GBP, social, directories, or sales pages.

Safe snippet templates

1. Public review quote

Customer review highlight: “[exact public quote].” Source: [platform/source]. This quote describes one customer's experience and should not be rewritten as a guarantee, average result, ranking claim, or universal outcome.

2. Anonymous project proof

For a [industry/project type] client, the approved proof point is: [specific source-backed detail]. Keep the customer anonymous and do not add revenue, timing, compliance, or performance claims that are not in the source record.

3. Rating or count mention

Current review source: [platform]. Verified rating/count on [date]: [rating/count]. Recheck before publishing and avoid implying the number is permanent.

4. Case-study quote boundary

Approved case-study line: [quote]. Do not turn this into a guarantee, forecast, ranking, legal/compliance claim, or promise that a new buyer will get the same result.

AI audit prompt

You are auditing a small-business web page or AI-search answer draft that uses customer reviews, testimonials, ratings, or proof snippets.
Use only the verified source card below. Do not invent customer names, outcomes, star ratings, rankings, savings, revenue, certifications, compliance status, guarantees, permission history, or review counts.

4. If any required proof is missing, output: STOP PUBLISHING — NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.

STOP PUBLISHING guardrails

  • STOP PUBLISHING: the exact quote, review source, permission status, or last-verified date is missing.
  • NEEDS OWNER REVIEW: the snippet mentions savings, revenue, rankings, compliance, licenses, warranties, guarantees, legal claims, or medical/financial outcomes.
  • Do not turn one customer's quote into a universal result or guaranteed outcome.
  • Do not invent star ratings, review counts, customer identity, project details, response-time metrics, or before/after performance.
  • Use labels such as REVIEW-SOURCE-VERIFIED, QUOTE-PERMISSION-NEEDED, ANONYMIZE, OWNER-REVIEW, and PROOF-STALE.
