Customer-Supplied Parts Policy Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Use this checklist when a customer asks your team to install, repair, diagnose around, warranty, or quote work involving a part, fixture, material, appliance, equipment item, or component they bought themselves. The goal is to protect the customer relationship without inventing warranty, liability, compatibility, or code-compliance promises.

Why customer-supplied parts need a written handoff

Customer-provided parts can be reasonable in some jobs and risky in others. The problem is not the customer's request; it is answering too quickly without knowing whether the business allows outside parts, whether the part fits, who covers warranty, whether the quote changes, and what the technician should document before installing or declining the work.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not promise installation, compatibility, warranty coverage, code compliance, labor warranty, refund, discount, diagnosis, scheduling priority, or acceptance of customer-supplied parts from AI output alone. A human owner, service manager, licensed technician, estimator, or dispatcher must verify company policy, manufacturer requirements, safety/code constraints, payment terms, and customer consent. Use NEEDS OWNER REVIEW for warranty, liability, safety, permit, code, insurance, high-dollar, tenant/property-manager, or customer-dispute situations.

Copy/paste customer-safe replies

Hi [Name] — thanks for checking before we schedule. We can review customer-supplied parts on a case-by-case basis. Please send the part name/model, photos of the part and packaging, and where it was purchased. Our team will confirm whether it fits our policy before we promise installation or warranty terms.
Thanks for sending the details. I do not want to guess on compatibility or warranty coverage. The next step is [technician/service manager/estimator] review. We will confirm whether this is allowed, whether it changes labor/warranty terms, and what the safest scheduling option is.
Internal note: CUSTOMER-SUPPLIED-PARTS. Part/source: [details]. Policy status: [allowed / not allowed / manager review]. Warranty/liability wording approved by: [name]. Customer reply sent: [date/time]. Owner: [person]. Next step: [diagnostic / quote revision / decline / schedule]. Do not promise compatibility, labor warranty, or code compliance until reviewed.

Fast QA before responding

  • Separate “we can review it” from “we will install it.”
  • Do not promise warranty coverage unless approved policy explicitly allows it.
  • Ask for photos, model numbers, receipts, and packaging before the technician is on site.
  • Route safety, code, permit, insurance, gas/electrical/water, high-dollar, or dispute-prone cases to a human manager.
  • Add a CRM owner, policy status, and next step so the customer does not receive conflicting answers.
