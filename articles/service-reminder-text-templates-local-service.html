Service reminder text templates for local service businesses

Past customers often need seasonal maintenance, annual checkups, filter changes, inspections, or warranty callbacks — but most small teams only remember when the phone is quiet. Use these reminders to re-open the conversation respectfully without inventing urgency or pushing work the customer does not need.

Use these when

  • A recurring maintenance window is coming due
  • A seasonal service should be offered before peak demand
  • A warranty or callback check-in is appropriate
  • A quote/customer went quiet but opted-in follow-up is allowed

Reminder rhythm

Send helpful reminders with a clear opt-out path

  1. Segment by real history: only message customers with a known service, date, warranty, or opt-in relationship.
  2. Lead with the reason: explain why now matters without false scarcity or made-up safety claims.
  3. Give one next step: reply BOOK, send photos, choose a window, or ignore if not needed.
  4. Stop cleanly: respect STOP/unsubscribe, platform rules, and any customer preference stored in the CRM.

Do not use these scripts to cold-text strangers. They are for existing customers/leads where consent, relationship, and platform rules permit outreach.

Copy/paste service reminder text templates

1) Annual maintenance reminder

Hi {first_name}, this is {business_name}. We helped with {service} around {last_service_month}. If you want the annual maintenance/checkup window, reply BOOK and we will send open times.

2) Seasonal tune-up reminder

Hi {first_name}, quick seasonal reminder from {business_name}: {seasonal_service} is usually easiest to schedule before {busy_period}. Reply 1 for available windows or 2 if this is not needed.

3) Filter / supply / consumable check

Hi {first_name}, checking in from {business_name}. Based on your last {service}, it may be time to review {filter_or_supply_item}. Reply HELP if you want us to check options, or STOP to opt out.

4) Warranty or callback check-in

Hi {first_name}, this is {business_name} following up on {job_or_product} from {date}. Is everything still working as expected? Reply YES, or reply with what needs attention and we will review.

5) Quote reactivation with no pressure

Hi {first_name}, we still have your {service} quote from {quote_month}. If the project is back on your list, reply UPDATE and we can confirm scope/pricing. If not, no problem — reply CLOSE and we will mark it inactive.

6) Maintenance plan renewal

Hi {first_name}, your {plan_name} with {business_name} is due for renewal on {renewal_date}. Reply RENEW for details, QUESTIONS if you want a quick review, or STOP if you do not want plan reminders.

7) Review-before-send CRM note

CRM note: service reminder sent for {customer_name} / {service} / {last_service_date}. Confirmed consent source: {consent_source}. Next action owner: {team_member}. Stop if customer declines or opts out.

Human review stop rules

  • Do not text if the customer opted out, never opted in, or the platform/account rules do not allow it.
  • Do not invent maintenance requirements, warranty deadlines, safety risks, discounts, or scarcity.
  • Check the last service date, job type, location, and customer status before sending.
  • Use exact STOP/unsubscribe language when SMS rules, carriers, or software require it.
  • Do not send sensitive diagnosis, billing, or legal/policy claims without owner review.
