Local service lead routing

Lead routing checklist for local service businesses

A fast reply is only useful when the right person owns the next step. Use this worksheet to route missed calls, web forms, Google Business Profile messages, DMs, email inquiries, referrals, and urgent jobs before they get buried.

Use the free AI Lead Response Quickstart

Best fit

Home-service, repair, cleaning, landscaping, HVAC, plumbing, pest control, field-service, and property-maintenance teams that need a simple routing map before adding more automation.

Local-service leads get lost when every channel has a different owner, every urgent job sounds the same, and nobody knows when to stop an AI-assisted reply and call a human. This checklist gives the dispatcher, owner, or admin a repeatable routing pass.

Do not use automation to decide safety, eligibility, pricing, legal terms, warranties, or emergency instructions. AI can summarize facts and draft a handoff note; a human should approve the route, response, and escalation.

Copy/paste routing worksheet

12 lead routing fields to add before follow-up

1. Lead source

Call / voicemail / form / email / Google Business Profile / Facebook or Instagram DM / referral / marketplace / repeat customer.

Review: Keep the original source so attribution and reply expectations stay clear.

2. Response owner

Owner: [dispatcher / office admin / estimator / technician / manager]. Backup owner: [name]. Next check time: [time].

Review: A lead is not routed until one human owns the next action.

3. Urgency bucket

Emergency / same-day / this week / quote request / warranty or callback / general question / not a fit.

Review: Emergency and safety language must follow company policy, not AI guesses.

4. Job category

Service requested: [repair / install / maintenance / inspection / estimate / follow-up]. Unknowns: [list what is missing].

Review: Tag unknowns instead of inventing scope.

5. Location and service area

Customer area: [city/neighborhood]. Inside service area? [yes/no/needs review]. Travel or access note: [note].

Review: Do not promise service in locations the business has not approved.

6. Contact permission

Preferred channel: [call/text/email/DM]. Consent or opt-in note: [yes/no/unknown]. Best callback time: [time].

Review: Follow SMS/email consent and platform rules before sending repeated messages.

7. Quote readiness

Enough facts to quote? [yes/no]. Missing: photos / measurements / model number / access / timing / budget / decision-maker.

Review: Ask one or two high-value questions instead of a long interrogation.

8. Appointment or visit path

Next step: quick callback / booking link / estimator review / tech call / manager approval / no automation.

Review: Booking links and windows must match real availability.

9. Customer risk flags

Frustrated / repeated issue / safety concern / refund or fee question / warranty claim / legal or insurance wording / vulnerable customer.

Review: Escalate these before sending AI-drafted language.

10. First reply template

Template to use: missed call / web form / after-hours / quote follow-up / urgent triage / callback scheduling / custom manager reply.

Review: Match the template to the source and urgency bucket.

11. Internal handoff note

[Lead source] from [name] in [area]. Request: [facts]. Urgency: [bucket]. Missing: [unknowns]. Owner: [name]. Next action due: [time].

Review: This note should be factual enough that the next person can act without rereading the whole thread.

12. Follow-up timer

If no response: check in after [time], second touch after [time], final helpful close-loop after [time]. Stop if customer declines or asks not to be contacted.

Review: Do not keep nudging beyond the approved cadence.

AI prompt and stop rules

Use AI to summarize routing facts, not to choose risky outcomes

Paste the lead facts into this prompt after removing private details that are not needed for routing. The output should help a human decide, not replace the decision.

You are helping a local service business route an inbound lead.
Facts only: [paste call note, form fields, email/DM text, source, time received, known location, requested service, customer preference].
Return: (1) urgency bucket, (2) missing facts, (3) safest first reply draft under 500 characters, (4) internal handoff note, (5) stop-rule flags for manager review.
Rules: do not invent pricing, availability, service area, safety status, warranty coverage, refunds, legal/insurance advice, diagnosis, emergency instructions, or consent.
  • Human review: Confirm source, owner, urgency, service area, consent, and next action before any reply is sent.
  • Stop rule: Escalate safety, emergency, refund, warranty, insurance, legal, angry-customer, vulnerable-customer, and exact-price or exact-arrival requests.
  • Weekly metric: Track leads routed within 15 minutes, ownerless leads, first-response time by source, jobs booked, and leads lost because the next step was unclear.

Pair this with the missed web-form response templates, Google Business Profile message responses, and urgent service triage questions.
