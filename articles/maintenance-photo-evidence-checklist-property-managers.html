Maintenance Photo Evidence Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before asking residents, vendors, or onsite staff for repair photos — and before AI turns photos into owner updates, dispute notes, chargeback evidence, or resident replies. The goal is useful proof without inventing diagnosis, access, billing, warranty, or safety claims.

Copy the checklist See Local Lead Rescue System

Use this when

  • A resident reports damage or a repair status question and photos would clarify the next step.
  • A vendor sends completion photos that may affect invoices, owner approvals, chargebacks, or warranty notes.
  • Staff want AI to summarize images into a work-order, owner update, or resident reply.
  • The issue touches access, safety, habitability, fair-housing, deposit, fee, lease, or insurance sensitivity.

Why maintenance photos need evidence rules

Photos can speed up maintenance triage, but they can also create unsupported conclusions. A blurry image does not prove cause, completion, chargeability, tenant responsibility, vendor quality, warranty status, or safety. A property manager needs photo source, date, context, permissions, and a human review path before the photo becomes a decision.

This checklist keeps photo requests useful and resident-safe while giving AI assistants a strict evidence boundary.

Resident-safe photo request snippets

Thanks for the update. If it is safe and comfortable, please send: 1) one wide photo of the full area, 2) one close-up of the issue, and 3) any label/model photo if this is an appliance or fixture. Please do not include people, documents, keys, codes, or private personal items. A manager will review before confirming next steps.
We received the photo(s). We can use them to help route the request, but they do not by themselves confirm cause, responsibility, warranty, charges, entry approval, or completion. The maintenance team/manager will review and follow up with the next step.
Vendor completion-photo note: Attach the completion photos to work order [ID], confirm date/time, work performed, parts used, unresolved items, and whether a follow-up visit is needed. Do not mark final/chargeable until manager review if the resident disputes the work or photos are unclear.

STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP if photos involve people, minors, private documents, keys/codes, personal belongings, medical/safety details, or sensitive living areas.
  • STOP if the photo is being used to justify fees, deposits, chargebacks, lease action, insurance, warranty denial, or legal notice.
  • STOP if AI describes cause, blame, habitability, code compliance, safety, completion, or repair quality beyond what a manager/professional verified.
  • STOP if access/entry rights, fair-housing accommodation, emergency priority, tenant privacy, or vendor availability is uncertain.
