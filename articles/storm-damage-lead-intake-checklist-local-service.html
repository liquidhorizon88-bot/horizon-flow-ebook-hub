Storm Damage Lead Intake Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Use this worksheet when storms, floods, wind, freezes, power outages, fallen branches, roof leaks, or urgent property damage create a surge of calls and web forms. It helps the office gather facts quickly without letting AI invent emergency priority, safety advice, insurance coverage, diagnosis, or crew availability.

Why this matters

Storm events compress a week of lead volume into a few hours. If intake is sloppy, teams lose high-intent leads, overpromise emergency response, miss safety flags, or create insurance and warranty misunderstandings. This card gives dispatchers, owners, and AI-assisted intake a fact-first path.

Marker: STORM-DAMAGE-LEAD-INTAKE-READY. Use it only when customer contact details, location, damage type, safety status, access limits, approved response wording, and follow-up owner are documented.

First reply when help is possible

Hi {customer_name}, we received your storm-damage request about {damage_summary} at {property_address}. Please send photos if safe, and avoid entering any area with electrical, structural, gas, or flooding risk. We are reviewing availability now and will confirm real next options before promising a time or diagnosis.

Photo request without unsafe pressure

If it is safe to do so, please send 2–4 photos from a distance: the affected area, the widest view, any visible water/impact, and the best access point. If taking photos would put you near water, electricity, unstable materials, or traffic, skip the photos and tell us that instead.

Owner review note

Storm lead review needed: customer {customer}, property {address}, reported damage {damage}, safety flags {safety_flags}, photos {photo_status}, tenant/insurance contacts {contacts}, requested timing {request}, available crew options {options}. Please approve next reply and whether this is emergency, estimate, mitigation, referral, or no-fit.

CRM labels

  • STORM-DAMAGE-LEAD-INTAKE-READY — facts, safety screen, and follow-up owner are documented.
  • STORM-SAFETY-REVIEW — electrical, gas, flooding, structural, medical, or access concern requires human handling.
  • STORM-PHOTO-NEEDED — safe photos or clearer damage details are needed before triage.
  • STORM-OWNER-REVIEW — insurance, warranty, diagnosis, price, emergency priority, or crew capacity needs approval.

STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • Do not invent emergency status, crew availability, arrival windows, diagnosis, repair scope, temporary mitigation, price, warranty coverage, insurance coverage, or claim outcome.
  • Do not tell a customer to enter unsafe areas, touch electrical hazards, climb ladders, stand near traffic, inspect roofs, handle gas/water hazards, or delay emergency services.
  • Do not blame weather, utilities, vendors, tenants, owners, insurers, or prior contractors unless a verified source supports it.
  • Do not mark a storm lead as closed until a human confirms the follow-up owner, due time, and customer-safe next step.

AI review prompt for storm lead triage

You are helping a local-service office triage storm-damage leads. Use only the source notes below. Do not invent safety advice, emergency status, arrival windows, crew capacity, diagnosis, price, warranty, insurance coverage, claim outcome, or customer approval. Return: (1) lead summary, (2) missing facts, (3) safety flags requiring human review, (4) safe first reply, (5) CRM label, (6) owner-review questions, and (7) STOP AUTOMATION reason if any high-risk fact is unverified.
