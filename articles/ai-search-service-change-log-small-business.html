AI Search Service Change Log for Small Businesses

AI search tools can repeat stale service details long after a business changes an offer, service area, booking rule, price factor, or availability promise. Use this worksheet to log every change with a source URL, proof owner, review date, and stop rule before updating pages, FAQs, schema, ads, or AI-drafted summaries.

Use the source link map See SEO After AI

When to use the log

  • A service is added, paused, renamed, or limited by location, season, staffing, or licensing.
  • A price, fee, deposit, warranty, turnaround time, guarantee, or refund promise changes.
  • A website page, FAQ, Google Business Profile, directory listing, schema field, or sales script needs the same updated fact.
  • An AI tool is helping draft page copy, but a human needs to prevent stale or invented claims.

Update workflow

  1. Capture the change. Write the exact old wording, new wording, and source proof before editing any public page.
  2. Map affected surfaces. Check service pages, FAQs, local listings, schema, footer, sales scripts, lead forms, and free-resource links.
  3. Assign owner review. The person closest to operations verifies legal, pricing, service-area, safety, and availability constraints.
  4. Draft with source limits. AI may rewrite approved facts, but it must not add new claims, guarantees, timelines, locations, credentials, or outcomes.
  5. Publish and log. After publishing, paste the updated URL and review date into the log.
  6. Recheck after distribution. When an article, directory, social post, or resource roundup links to the page, verify the same source-backed claim is still present.

AI audit prompt

You are reviewing a small-business website update for AI-search accuracy. Use only the facts I provide. Do not invent service availability, pricing, guarantees, credentials, locations, review dates, licensing, refund rules, or outcomes.

Service change log row:

- Changed fact: {changed_fact}
- Approved wording: {approved_wording}
- Source URL/document: {source_url}
- Affected pages/listings: {affected_surfaces}
- Proof owner: {proof_owner}
- Review date: {review_date}

Return:
1. Missing source or owner-review issues
2. Surfaces that need the same update
3. Risky claims that should be removed or softened
4. Suggested source-backed rewrite
5. STOP PUBLISHING warnings

STOP PUBLISHING guardrails

  • STOP PUBLISHING if the new claim has no source URL, owner, or review date.
  • Do not invent service areas, emergency availability, price ranges, credentials, warranties, discounts, refunds, or guaranteed outcomes.
  • Do not copy internal-only notes into public copy or AI tools without removing private customer, employee, vendor, or contract data.
  • Do not let old pages, schema, FAQs, or directory listings contradict the new approved wording.
