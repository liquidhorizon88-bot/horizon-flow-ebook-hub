AI Search Appointment Cancellation Policy Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before publishing cancellation, reschedule, no-show, refund, deposit, late-arrival, or exception-policy wording that AI search tools, website chatbots, booking assistants, or support macros may summarize for buyers.

Copy the worksheet Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Your booking, appointment, checkout, FAQ, or policy page mentions cancellation windows.
  • Deposits, no-show fees, reschedule fees, refund exceptions, or late-arrival rules vary by service, location, customer type, or season.
  • AI tools might turn “call us to reschedule” into a guaranteed refund or waived-fee promise.
  • You need owner-approved wording before a chatbot, support macro, or answer engine quotes booking policy facts.

Why this matters

AI search can flatten policy nuance into a promise

Small businesses often keep cancellation policies in several places: scheduling pages, checkout notes, invoices, confirmation texts, FAQs, and staff macros. If those sources disagree, AI answer engines may surface the most generous version, omit conditions, or imply that every customer receives the same refund, fee waiver, or reschedule window.

This source card helps owners make booking-policy language quotable without inventing legal, refund, fee, or availability promises.

5. AI-search audit prompt
Paste this into your AI/search QA tool:
"Review this booking, checkout, FAQ, invoice, confirmation, SMS reminder, chatbot, and support-macro copy. List every appointment cancellation, reschedule, no-show, deposit, refund, fee, late-arrival, emergency, and exception-policy claim. Flag any claim that lacks a source URL, covered window, exception owner, customer/service scope, or approved public wording. Do not invent refunds, fee waivers, deposit terms, legal obligations, guaranteed appointment openings, emergency exceptions, or chargeback outcomes."

STOP PUBLISHING if:
- The policy is not backed by an owner-approved source.
- Booking pages, confirmations, reminders, invoices, and chat/support macros disagree.
- A fee, deposit, refund, or exception can be promised without human review.
- AI/chatbot/support replies could turn a reschedule request into a guaranteed refund or waived fee.
- The owner has not approved the exact public wording.

Customer-safe wording examples

  • General cancellation: “Cancellation and reschedule requests are reviewed under the policy shown on our booking page or agreement.”
  • Deposit caution: “Deposits, fees, credits, or refunds depend on the approved policy and may require manager review.”
  • AI-search guardrail: “Do not summarize a reschedule request as a guaranteed refund, waived no-show fee, or available appointment unless the approved source explicitly says so.”
