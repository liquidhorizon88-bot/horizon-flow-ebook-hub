AI Search UTM Taxonomy Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet to name campaign links consistently before you distribute AI-search resources, free checklists, Gist copies, Telegra.ph articles, or product-page CTAs.

Start with the free sample library

Why this matters

Small businesses often publish a helpful page, a free checklist, a social post, and a product link in the same week. If every link uses a different naming style, later analytics become guesswork. This checklist gives owners and consultants a simple source of truth for naming links without claiming that AI search generated a lead unless the source evidence actually says so.

Copy/paste UTM taxonomy worksheet

  1. Campaign family: choose one stable family such as seo_after_ai_launch, ebook_marketing, local_lead_rescue, or small_business_ai_profit_kit.
  2. Source: use the platform or surface: github_pages, gist, telegraph, gmail_editorial, resource_roundup, linkedin, reddit, or direct.
  3. Medium: use the channel type: free, ugc, editorial_pitch, community_answer, directory_submission, or owned_seo.
  4. Content label: describe the exact CTA or asset, for example conversion-tracking-hero, content-pruning-bottom, sample-library-link, or product-cta.
  5. Destination: record whether the link points to a free sample, owned article, product page, subscribe page, or public Gist resource.
  6. Owner: assign who can change the link naming convention. Do not let every new post invent a new format.
  7. Baseline date: write the date before distribution begins so later checks do not mix old and new traffic.
  8. Review date: schedule a 14-day and 45-day readout. Avoid changing UTMs before the first readout unless a link is broken.

Starter naming convention

utm_source=github_pages
utm_medium=free
utm_campaign=seo_after_ai_launch
utm_content=utm_taxonomy_[asset_or_cta]

Keep every value lowercase, hyphenated or underscored, and short enough that a human can recognize it later in analytics exports.

Human-review stop rules

  • Do not invent channel performance when the source is direct, unknown, or manually entered.
  • Do not rename live links mid-campaign unless the current link is broken or misleading.
  • Do not combine paid-product clicks, free-sample clicks, and subscribe clicks into one “conversion” without labeling them separately.
  • Do not claim ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews caused a sale unless the referral/source evidence supports it.
  • Do not expose private customer, call, form, CRM, or email data in a public UTM log.
