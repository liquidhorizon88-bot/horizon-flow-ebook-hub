Lead Response Promise Audit for Local Service Businesses

A response promise is any claim like “we call back in 5 minutes,” “same-day quotes,” “24/7 emergency service,” or “we text before arrival.” If that promise appears on a website, ad, booking form, chatbot, review reply, or AI-generated answer, the team needs proof it is true and a fallback when capacity changes. Use this worksheet before buyers or AI search tools repeat promises the business cannot keep.

When to use this audit

  • Before publishing: new service pages, landing pages, ad copy, marketplace profiles, booking forms, chat widgets, and quote request pages.
  • Before automation: AI-written replies, autoresponders, review responses, missed-call texts, and after-hours scripts.
  • Before more traffic: if leads complain about slow replies, missed callbacks, unclear arrival windows, or promises that changed with staffing/capacity.
  • Human review rule: only approve a promise when the owner or manager can point to operating hours, staffing, workflow ownership, and recent response data that supports it.

Promise types to check

  1. First reply: “within 5 minutes,” “same day,” “next business day,” “we answer 24/7.”
  2. Quote timing: “free quote today,” “estimate in 24 hours,” “instant quote,” “same-day inspection.”
  3. Arrival window: “on the way,” “running late,” “2-hour window,” “text before arrival.”
  4. Emergency wording: “24/7 emergency,” “same-day emergency,” “after-hours service,” “urgent dispatch.”
  5. Financing or payment: “financing available,” “deposit required,” “payment link,” “approval before scheduling.”
  6. Warranty/callback: “covered callback,” “free inspection,” “guaranteed fix,” “manager review.”
  7. Closeout and follow-up: “we send photos,” “completion note,” “maintenance reminder,” “review request.”

AI audit prompt

You are helping a local service business audit response promises before publishing copy or sending automated follow-up. Use only the website copy, form text, ads, CRM/SLA notes, operating hours, and response-time evidence provided. Do not invent staffing levels, emergency availability, quote timing, financing approval, warranty coverage, dispatch capacity, or customer consent. Return a table with: exact promise, page/channel, promise type, evidence provided, risk level, safer fallback wording, owner to confirm, and next review date. If a promise is unsupported or capacity-dependent, mark NEEDS OWNER REVIEW before publishing.

Safer replacement wording examples

  • Instead of “instant quote,” use “send photos/details and we will confirm whether we can quote remotely or need an inspection.”
  • Instead of “24/7 emergency service” when coverage varies, use “after-hours requests are reviewed as capacity allows; call emergency services for immediate safety risks.”
  • Instead of “same-day service guaranteed,” use “same-day openings may be available; we will confirm the earliest safe appointment window.”
  • Instead of “we always text before arrival,” use “when mobile numbers and consent are available, we send arrival updates through the approved channel.”
