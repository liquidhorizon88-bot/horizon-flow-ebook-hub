Why capacity promises need a source of truth

When phones, forms, and messages stack up during busy weeks, teams can accidentally promise the same opening to multiple customers, imply emergency priority, or let AI turn a rough note into a confirmed schedule. A capacity/waitlist card keeps the customer informed while protecting dispatch reality.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not promise arrival windows, same-day service, emergency priority, cancellation-list placement, discounts, or crew availability from AI output alone. A dispatcher/owner must verify live calendar capacity, service area, crew skill, part availability, travel time, safety priority, and customer consent before confirming.

Fast QA before replying

  • Check the live dispatch board before sending any time window or cancellation-list promise.
  • Mark safety or property-damage triggers for human triage instead of letting AI prioritize.
  • Give a specific next-update time when there is no confirmed opening.
  • Keep waitlist leads in the CRM with owner, due time, and status so they do not disappear during busy season.
