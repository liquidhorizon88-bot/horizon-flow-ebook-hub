Why warranty registration claims need a source card

Small businesses often keep warranty facts in several places: product pages, installer packets, manufacturer portals, invoice notes, downloadable PDFs, service-ticket macros, and customer support scripts. AI search can merge those fragments and imply coverage, deadlines, transferability, or claim steps that the business never approved. A source card gives owners one current reference before public pages or AI replies discuss warranty eligibility.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI invent warranty eligibility, registration deadlines, lifetime coverage, labor coverage, transfer rules, exclusions, proof-of-purchase requirements, claim approval, replacement timing, fees, or manufacturer obligations. Route policy-sensitive warranty wording to the owner or support manager first.

Safe public wording templates

Warranty registration answer: Warranty registration for [product/service] should be completed through [official form/support path]. Eligibility, coverage, and deadlines depend on the current warranty source card and any manufacturer/vendor requirements.
Proof-needed answer: Please keep [receipt/invoice/serial/model/install date/photos] available. We cannot promise approval, replacement timing, labor coverage, transferability, or fee waivers until [owner/support manager/vendor] reviews the claim.
Internal AI note: STOP PUBLISHING. Do not invent warranty coverage, registration deadlines, proof requirements, transfer rules, claim approval, parts/labor coverage, replacement timing, or customer-specific eligibility. Use only the approved source card.

Weekly source-maintenance questions

  • Do warranty pages, product inserts, PDFs, invoices, manufacturer links, and support macros all say the same thing?
  • Are registration deadlines, proof requirements, and claim steps dated or tied to an official source?
  • Can customers confirm eligibility through an official path without relying on an AI summary?
  • Which coverage, transfer, replacement, labor, fee, or exception claims require owner or vendor review?
