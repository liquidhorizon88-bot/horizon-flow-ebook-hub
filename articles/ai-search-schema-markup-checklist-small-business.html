AI Search Schema Markup Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this checklist before adding JSON-LD or schema plugins so AI tools and search engines read the same facts customers can verify on the page.

Copy the schema worksheet Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • A site has no structured-data plan.
  • A plugin generated vague or stale schema.
  • Service areas, hours, reviews, or offer facts changed.
  • You need do-not-invent rules before using AI to draft markup.

Why this matters

Schema should confirm visible facts, not create new claims

Schema markup helps search engines and AI answer engines understand business entities, services, pages, and proof. But structured data becomes risky when it contains hours, prices, ratings, guarantees, locations, or credentials that are not visible, current, or approved on the page.

This worksheet is a practical preflight for owners, marketers, and web teams. It does not promise rankings. It helps make website facts clearer, source-backed, and easier to audit.

Recommended schema types to consider:
- Organization or LocalBusiness: only if name, contact, location/service-area, and profile links are verified.
- Service: only for services actually described on the page.
- FAQPage: only for Q&A text visible to users.
- Article or BlogPosting: only for educational content pages.
- BreadcrumbList: only if page hierarchy is clear.
- Review/AggregateRating: only if platform policy, customer permission, and visible review proof support it.

Human review rule: verify every generated field against visible page content, business records, platform policies, and legal/compliance constraints before publishing.

Stop rules

Do not publish schema until these are true

  • The markup matches visible page content or a clearly linked source page.
  • Reviews, ratings, testimonials, and awards have permission and policy support.
  • Prices, service areas, hours, booking rules, refund policies, warranties, and guarantees are current.
  • No medical, legal, financial, safety, or regulated-service claim was invented by AI.
  • A human owner knows who will update the schema when facts change.
