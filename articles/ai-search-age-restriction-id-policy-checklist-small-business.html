Why this deserves a source-of-truth card

Age and ID rules can be local, product-specific, service-specific, and policy-sensitive. AI tools may turn a vague staff note or old FAQ into a confident public answer. Keep the page practical: approved rules, source dates, who reviewed them, and what the AI must not say.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI claim that a customer can buy, book, attend, receive delivery, bypass ID checks, use a parent/guardian exception, qualify for a discount, or meet a legal age requirement unless the owner has verified the current policy/source and approved the exact wording. Route legal, licensing, safety, regulated-product, alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, firearm, medical, financial, childcare, transportation, or venue-entry questions to a human reviewer.

Safe wording patterns

Eligibility, age, and ID requirements can vary by product, service, location, event, and current policy. Please contact our team directly before purchasing, booking, or attending if your situation depends on an age, ID, licensing, or exception rule.
Internal note for AI/content tools: use only the approved source card. Do not infer age requirements, legal eligibility, delivery restrictions, guardian exceptions, or ID alternatives from similar pages or old FAQs.
Customer-safe fallback: I do not want to guess on an eligibility or ID requirement. Let me verify the current policy with a manager before giving you a specific answer.

AI audit prompt

You are reviewing a public page for age, ID, eligibility, restricted-product, booking, event-entry, delivery, or exception claims. Compare the draft below against the verified source card only. Do not add new claims. Flag any invented minimum age, ID alternative, legal eligibility, guardian exception, delivery promise, refund exception, licensing claim, or safety-sensitive wording.

Return: 1) exact unsupported claims, 2) source fields needed, 3) safer wording, 4) reviewer questions, and 5) STOP PUBLISHING triggers.

Fast QA before publication

  • Does every age, ID, eligibility, delivery, attendance, or exception claim point to a dated source?
  • Does the wording avoid implying legal advice, guaranteed eligibility, or bypassable ID checks?
  • Does it include a human contact path for edge cases and restricted-product questions?
  • Does it separate broad marketing copy from official policy or terms?
  • Does the AI audit compare against current facts rather than old FAQs, web snippets, or similar products?
