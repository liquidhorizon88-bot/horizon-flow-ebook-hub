Insurance Adjuster Appointment Checklist for Local Service Businesses

A source-card worksheet for restoration, roofing, plumbing, HVAC, remediation, and other local-service teams coordinating insurance adjuster appointments without letting AI or staff invent claim status, coverage, liability, approval, repair timing, reimbursement, or scope outcomes.

INSURANCE-ADJUSTER-APPOINTMENT-CARD-READY

Use this before sending any AI-assisted customer update

STOP AUTOMATION: do not confirm adjuster timing, claim approval, coverage, reimbursement, supplement eligibility, repair authorization, liability, or job-start dates until an owner/manager has checked the claim record, insurer/adjuster communication, customer consent, scope notes, photos, estimate status, and approved customer communication path.

  • Do not invent whether insurance will pay, whether a repair is approved, or whether a customer will owe a deductible or balance.
  • Do not promise that the technician can speak for the carrier, public adjuster, customer, or mortgage company.
  • Do not send photos, documents, claim details, or access instructions until the customer-authorized channel and privacy/source record are confirmed.

Source card fields

  1. Customer / job / claim: customer name, service address, job number, claim number if provided, carrier/adjuster contact, appointment date/window, and customer-authorized communication channel.
  2. Scope boundary: what the local-service company is inspecting, what is still unknown, which estimate/supplement is draft vs submitted, and who may discuss pricing, coverage, or next steps.
  3. Evidence packet: photos, moisture readings, measurements, serial/model numbers, invoices, prior repair notes, storm/date source, customer-uploaded documents, and missing items.
  4. Access and safety: site access instructions, pets/tenants/gate codes, ladder/roof/confined-space restrictions, safety concerns, and whether the customer or property manager must be present.
  5. Customer-safe update owner: dispatcher, estimator, project manager, owner review, approved message template, and what cannot be promised before adjuster/insurer review.
  6. CRM label: insurance_adjuster_appointment, claim_docs_pending, scope_review_needed, customer_authorization_required, or owner_review_before_send.

Copy/paste customer-safe snippets

Appointment confirmation

We have your adjuster appointment noted for [date/window]. Before we confirm any repair timing, coverage, price, or approval status, our team will review the claim/source record, current estimate notes, photos, and the approved communication path.

Documents or photos still needed

To keep the claim file accurate, please send only the requested photos/documents through the approved channel. We will attach them to the job record for manager review before sharing any conclusions about scope, reimbursement, or next steps.

Customer asks if insurance will pay

We do not want to guess about coverage, deductible, approval, or payment outcomes. The project manager will review the adjuster/carrier record and our scope notes before giving you a source-backed update.

AI audit prompt

Review this draft customer update about an insurance adjuster appointment, claim photos, estimate, supplement, repair authorization, deductible, reimbursement, or job schedule. Flag invented claims about coverage, approval, liability, price, adjuster decisions, insurer timing, scope, safety, or document access. Rewrite it as a source-backed acknowledgement with owner/project-manager review, clear unknowns, customer-safe wording, and STOP AUTOMATION guardrails.
