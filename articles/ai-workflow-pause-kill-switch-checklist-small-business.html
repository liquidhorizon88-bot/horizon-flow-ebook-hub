AI Workflow Pause and Kill-Switch Checklist for Small Businesses

Most small-business AI projects fail quietly: a prompt starts drifting, a tool changes behavior, a teammate skips review, or a customer-facing draft goes out before anyone checks the source facts. Use this checklist to decide when an AI workflow should pause, get fixed, or be retired.

Use this when

An AI workflow is useful, but not safe enough to keep running unattended.

This is not a technical incident-response plan for enterprise teams. It is a practical owner checklist for small businesses using AI for replies, summaries, SOPs, sales notes, content drafts, lead follow-up, or internal decisions. The goal is to catch weak workflows before they become customer promises.

Good fit

  • AI drafts that touch customers, prices, appointments, refunds, policies, or lead follow-up.
  • Shared prompts where multiple team members can change variables or source notes.
  • Weekly reviews after a pilot, tool update, staff change, or quality complaint.

Do not use it to

  • Hide AI mistakes from customers or staff.
  • Restart a workflow when required evidence is still missing.
  • Invent performance results, approvals, policy exceptions, customer history, or source facts.

STOP AUTOMATION

Pause immediately when any trigger is present.

  • Private customer data was pasted into a tool that is not approved for that data.
  • The AI output makes up prices, availability, refunds, warranties, diagnosis, revenue, legal language, or customer history.
  • The workflow depends on an old policy, old offer, stale source page, or undocumented staff knowledge.
  • A customer-facing draft skipped human review or was sent from the wrong channel.
  • A tool/model changed behavior and the old prompt no longer produces a reliable draft.

Restart checklist

Do not restart until the owner can answer these questions.

Source control

What source document, CRM record, policy page, or owner-approved note proves the workflow output? If the source is missing, write NEEDS OWNER REVIEW instead of guessing.

Review control

Who must approve the output before customers see it? Name the reviewer and the exact fields they check: facts, tone, policy, price, timing, privacy, and channel consent.

Failure control

What happens if the workflow fails again? Define the safe fallback: manual reply, manager review, support ticket, callback task, or pause until the next weekly review.

AI prompt

Review a workflow from evidence only.

You are reviewing a small-business AI workflow for safety.
Use only the source notes, examples, and policy snippets below.
Return:
1. Whether the workflow should continue, pause, be fixed, or be retired.
2. The highest-risk output pattern.
3. The missing source evidence, if any.
4. The owner/reviewer who should approve restart.
5. A revised human-review rule.
