Appliance Outage Resident Update Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before an AI draft, leasing assistant, or resident inbox answers a tenant who asks about a broken refrigerator, stove, washer, dryer, dishwasher, HVAC unit, appliance replacement, temporary workaround, vendor access, or repair timeline.

APPLIANCE-OUTAGE-RESIDENT-UPDATE-CHECKLIST-READY

Best fit: property managers, maintenance coordinators, and resident-services teams that need to acknowledge appliance issues without inventing repair dates, replacement approvals, vendor capacity, warranty coverage, rent credits, habitability/legal conclusions, or emergency priority.

Use with: Local Lead Rescue System for source-record fields, CRM labels, owner/manager review, and safe AI-assisted response drafts.

1. Capture the outage before drafting

  • Resident name and unit: [name / unit]
  • Appliance or system: [refrigerator / stove / oven / dishwasher / washer / dryer / HVAC / water heater / other]
  • Problem reported: [not working / leaking / noisy / unsafe smell / no heat/cool / intermittent / damaged / replacement request]
  • When it started and resident impact: [date/time / food loss / laundry blocked / temperature issue / access constraint]
  • Source records to check: [maintenance ticket / lease terms / appliance inventory / warranty record / vendor note / prior work order / parts order / owner approval]
  • What is not verified yet: [diagnosis / ETA / parts status / replacement approval / credit/concession / emergency priority / vendor access]

2. STOP AUTOMATION before these claims

  • Do not promise repair or replacement timing. Verify maintenance/vendor schedules, parts status, owner approval, access window, and property policy before quoting dates.
  • Do not decide credits, rent offsets, food-loss reimbursement, or hotel/temporary accommodations. Route those through manager/legal/accounting review and approved policy language.
  • Do not diagnose safety, habitability, or code status from a short message. Escalate urgent heat, cooling, gas, electrical, leak, fire, carbon monoxide, or water-intrusion concerns to the approved emergency process.
  • Do not invent warranty or manufacturer coverage. Check the appliance inventory, purchase record, warranty, vendor note, and owner approval before explaining coverage or replacement responsibility.
  • Do not expose another resident, vendor, owner, or staff detail. Keep the update unit-specific and source-backed.

3. Resident-safe update snippets

Issue received, source check pending

Hi [Name] — thanks for reporting this. I’m checking the maintenance ticket, appliance record, and vendor/parts status before giving a repair or replacement timeline. I’ll follow up with the verified next step or route it for manager review if needed.

Use when: the issue is real but timing, diagnosis, responsibility, or approval is not verified yet.

Access or vendor scheduling needed

Hi [Name] — we need an approved access window before assigning the next maintenance/vendor step. Please confirm [access option] or share another approved time window. I’ll avoid guessing at completion timing until the source record is updated.

Use when: the repair depends on entry, vendor availability, parts, or owner approval.

Potential urgent/safety route

Hi [Name] — because this may involve [heat/cooling/gas/electrical/leak/safety], I’m routing it through the approved urgent review path rather than giving a quick answer here. Please use [emergency process/phone] if the situation creates immediate risk.

Use when: the report may be habitability-, safety-, leak-, heat/cooling-, gas-, electrical-, or emergency-sensitive.

4. CRM labels and review fields

  • appliance_outage_reported
  • maintenance_source_check_needed
  • vendor_or_parts_eta_pending
  • replacement_or_owner_approval_review
  • resident_access_window_needed
  • urgent_safety_route_review

Manager review prompt: “Check the maintenance ticket, lease/policy language, appliance inventory, vendor/parts status, owner approval, access window, safety/emergency routing, and approved resident wording before promising repair timing, replacement, credits, accommodations, warranty coverage, or completion status.”

5. AI drafting prompt

Draft a short resident-safe appliance outage update using only these verified facts:
- Resident issue and appliance: [paste]
- Unit/property: [paste]
- Source records checked: [paste]
- Maintenance/vendor/parts status: [paste]
- Access window: [paste]
- What is NOT verified yet: [paste]
- Required manager/legal/emergency review: [yes/no]
Rules: do not invent diagnosis, ETA, replacement approval, warranty coverage, rent credit, food-loss reimbursement, hotel/accommodation, emergency priority, vendor capacity, parts arrival, or legal/habitability conclusions. If the answer depends on manager, legal, emergency, owner, vendor, warranty, or parts review, say we are checking the source record and will follow up.
