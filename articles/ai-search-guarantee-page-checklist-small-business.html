AI Search Guarantee Page Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before publishing guarantees, warranties, satisfaction promises, refund rules, service windows, or replacement policies that AI search tools may summarize for buyers.

Copy the worksheet Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Your website promises a warranty, guarantee, refund, replacement, or service window.
  • AI tools might summarize policy pages into stronger promises than you intended.
  • Old sales pages mention outdated satisfaction guarantees or service commitments.
  • You need a human-reviewed source of truth for customer-facing promises.

Why this matters

Customer promises need source-of-truth wording

Guarantees and warranties are conversion assets, but they can create trust and compliance risk when the scope is unclear. A vague sentence like “100% satisfaction guaranteed” can be misread by buyers, sales staff, AI assistants, and answer engines unless the site explains exactly what is covered.

This checklist helps a small business publish policy facts in a way that humans can verify and AI search tools can quote without inventing refunds, timelines, or outcomes.

Implementation

Make one canonical page before spreading promises across the site

  1. Inventory guarantees, warranties, refund rules, service promises, and cancellation terms across the site.
  2. Choose one canonical policy page or trust-center section as the source of truth.
  3. Rewrite sales pages, FAQs, checkout copy, and support templates so they point back to the same approved wording.
  4. Add review dates and an owner so stale guarantees do not linger after policies change.
  5. Only add FAQ/schema or policy markup after a responsible human confirms the wording.

Human review rule: policy promises should be approved by the owner, operations lead, support lead, or legal/compliance reviewer. AI can find vague language and organize facts, but it should not create guarantees, refunds, warranty terms, or exceptions.
