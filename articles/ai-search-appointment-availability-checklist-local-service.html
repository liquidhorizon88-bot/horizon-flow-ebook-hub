STOP PUBLISHING

Do not publish or let AI summarize availability when proof is missing.

  • The calendar, branch capacity, emergency rule, or service-area constraint has not been reviewed this week.
  • The wording says “same day,” “24/7,” “next available,” “guaranteed,” “emergency,” or “book now” without an approved source.
  • The claim depends on weather, parts, crew skills, permits, tenant access, safety, warranty, or manager approval.
  • The page, chatbot, ad, or AI prompt could turn a tentative window into a promise.
  • The dispatcher or owner would not be comfortable honoring the wording for the next real lead.

Safe wording

Use flexible appointment language that points to a real check.

We can help with [service]. Appointment timing depends on service area, crew capacity, parts, and urgency. Call or send the form so our team can check the current schedule before promising a window.

For urgent issues, contact us directly so a person can review safety, location, and crew availability. Do not rely on this page as a guaranteed emergency dispatch promise.

If we are booked out, we can still record the request, confirm the right service category, and explain the next available option or waitlist path after checking the live schedule.

Weekly review

Look for availability drift before it becomes a lead-quality problem.

  • Which pages, ads, profiles, or chatbot answers still mention outdated appointment windows?
  • Which leads expected same-day or emergency service because the wording was too strong?
  • Which branches, service areas, or job types need separate availability wording?
  • Which source-of-truth owner should refresh the availability card each week?
