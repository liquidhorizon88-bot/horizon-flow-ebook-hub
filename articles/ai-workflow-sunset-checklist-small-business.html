Why sunset rules matter

AI workflows should earn the right to stay

Small businesses often add AI one task at a time: summarize calls, draft emails, rewrite service pages, sort inquiries, or turn messy notes into SOPs. The hidden risk is workflow creep. A prompt that was safe for internal brainstorming may later be used for customer replies, refunds, pricing, hiring, diagnosis, or policy promises. A sunset checklist gives the owner a simple review rhythm.

  1. Measure the saved time honestly: include setup, source gathering, review, edits, mistakes, and rework.
  2. Look at correction patterns: repeated fact, privacy, tone, policy, or pricing errors are workflow signals, not one-off annoyances.
  3. Check source freshness: AI should not fill gaps when the underlying SOP, price sheet, service page, or policy is out of date.
  4. Keep human authority clear: AI can draft and organize; a person approves anything customer-facing or operationally binding.

Prompt to review an AI workflow safely

You are helping a small business owner review one AI-assisted workflow. Use only the facts I provide. Do not invent metrics, customer results, policy, pricing, legal requirements, or compliance advice. Ask for missing source information instead of guessing. Return: 1) keep/fix/pause/retire recommendation, 2) evidence from the correction log, 3) source documents that need updates, 4) human-review risks, 5) suggested prompt changes, and 6) a one-paragraph owner decision note.
