Lease Break and Early Termination Request Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before an AI draft, leasing assistant, or resident-services inbox answers a resident who asks to break a lease, move out early, transfer, sublet, waive a fee, or change notice timing.

LEASE-BREAK-EARLY-TERMINATION-CHECKLIST-READY

Best fit: property managers, leasing coordinators, and resident-services teams who need a careful way to acknowledge early-termination requests without inventing legal advice, fees, approval rights, replacement-renter promises, or policy exceptions.

Use with: Local Lead Rescue System for source-record fields, CRM labels, owner review, and safe AI-assisted response drafts.

1. Capture the request before drafting

  • Resident/contact name: [name]
  • Property/unit: [property/unit]
  • Lease term and current end date: [start date / end date]
  • Requested move-out or termination date: [requested date]
  • Request type: [lease break / early termination / job relocation / hardship / military / transfer / sublet / roommate change / fee waiver]
  • Source record to check: [lease clause / resident ledger / local policy / state rule / manager notes / prior written approval]
  • Open money or maintenance issue tied to the request: [rent balance / deposit / repair / concession / notice]

2. STOP AUTOMATION before these claims

  • Do not give legal advice. Route lease interpretation, required notices, statutory exceptions, military orders, domestic-violence protections, habitability disputes, or eviction-sensitive details to manager/legal review.
  • Do not invent fees or waivers. Verify the lease clause, ledger, policy, and approval authority before mentioning early-termination fees, reletting charges, rent responsibility, deposit impact, credits, or waivers.
  • Do not promise release from the lease. Only a verified approval path can confirm whether the resident is released, conditionally approved, or still responsible.
  • Do not promise replacement-renter timing. Avoid saying a new applicant will be found by a date or that charges stop when a unit is marketed unless verified policy supports it.
  • Do not approve sublets, transfers, or roommate changes in the first reply. Use a source-based acknowledgement and request the required process instead.

3. Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

Early termination request received

Hi [Name] — thanks for letting us know. I’m going to review the lease record and the early-termination process for [property/unit] before giving an answer. I’ll follow up with the next verified step or route it to the manager if approval is required.

Use when: the resident asks to move out early, break the lease, or change the lease end date.

Fee or deposit impact question

Hi [Name] — I don’t want to guess on fees, balances, or deposit impact. I’m checking the lease clause and ledger first, then I’ll send the confirmed next step or have the manager/accounting team review if approval is needed.

Use when: the resident asks whether a fee can be waived, rent stops early, a deposit changes, or a balance will be forgiven.

Sublet, transfer, or replacement-renter request

Hi [Name] — requests involving a transfer, sublet, roommate change, or replacement applicant need to follow the approved process. I’m checking the policy and will send the correct next step rather than giving an unsupported approval here.

Use when: the resident suggests finding someone else, assigning the lease, transferring units, or changing occupants.

4. CRM labels and manager review fields

  • lease_break_request
  • early_termination_review
  • lease_clause_check_required
  • fee_or_deposit_impact_review
  • sublet_transfer_request
  • manager_or_legal_review_required

Manager review prompt: “Check the lease clause, notice date, ledger, policy, protected-category or statutory exception risk, and approval authority before approving the reply. Flag any fee, waiver, deposit, release, replacement-renter, sublet, transfer, or legal-sensitive wording for manager/legal/accounting review.”

5. AI drafting prompt

Draft a short resident-safe acknowledgement using only these verified facts:
- Resident request: [paste]
- Property/unit: [paste]
- Lease end date and requested move-out date: [paste]
- Relevant lease/policy excerpt: [paste]
- Ledger or deposit facts verified so far: [paste]
- What is NOT verified yet: [paste]
- Required manager/legal/accounting review: [yes/no]
Rules: do not invent legal advice, release approval, fee waiver, refund, deposit impact, replacement-renter timing, sublet approval, transfer approval, or notice rules. If the answer depends on lease/policy/manager review, say we are checking the record and will follow up.
