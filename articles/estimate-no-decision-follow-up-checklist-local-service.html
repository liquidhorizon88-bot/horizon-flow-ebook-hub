Estimate No-Decision Follow-Up Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Use this worksheet when a customer received an estimate, quote, or proposal but has not accepted, declined, or asked a clear next question. The goal is to restart the conversation without pressure, fake scarcity, or invented pricing promises.

Use this when: an estimate has been open for a few days, the customer has gone quiet after receiving options, a technician or estimator needs a clean handoff, or the team is unsure whether the opportunity is still active.

Human-review rule: before sending any follow-up, verify the exact estimate, scope, date sent, price, financing/payment terms, expiration language, warranty notes, access requirements, and the last customer message. AI can summarize; it cannot create a discount, deadline, financing approval, diagnosis, warranty promise, or customer intent.

1. Five-minute no-decision estimate worksheet

Customer / job: [name, address or CRM id]
Estimate sent on: [date/time]
Estimate amount / range: [exact approved amount]
Scope included: [exact approved scope]
Decision maker(s): [known / unknown / needs confirmation]
Last customer response: [exact wording or none]
Last team promise: [callback date, hold, discount, option, warranty, financing, none]
Open question: [price / timing / access / scope / financing / competing quote / unknown]
Risk flags: [expired quote, safety issue, permit/HOA, financing not approved, warranty, refund, legal/policy]
Recommended status: [needs-friendly-check-in / needs-scope-clarification / needs-owner-review / not-ready / closed-lost]
Owner/reviewer: [name]
Next safe message: [copy/paste script below]
Next check date: [date]

2. Safe decision-status labels

  • NEEDS-FRIENDLY-CHECK-IN: estimate is still valid and there is no known blocker.
  • NEEDS-SCOPE-CLARIFICATION: customer may need detail on what is included or excluded.
  • NEEDS-DECISION-MAKER: another owner, spouse, manager, landlord, HOA, or budget holder may need the estimate.
  • NEEDS-OWNER-REVIEW: pricing, discount, warranty, financing, refund, legal/policy, or safety wording must be checked by the owner or manager.
  • NOT-READY: customer is waiting on timing, budget, parts, permit/HOA, another quote, or internal approval.
  • CLOSED-LOST-WITH-REASON: only after a real customer response or owner-approved reason code.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI mark the estimate lost, invent a reason, offer a lower price, create urgency, promise a financing result, or change scope without human approval. NO-DECISION-FOLLOW-UP-READY means the record is ready for a reviewed follow-up, not automatic sending.

3. Copy/paste follow-up scripts

Friendly check-in

Hi [Name], just checking whether you had any questions about the estimate we sent for [project]. If you are still considering it, I can help clarify scope, timing, or next steps.

Review: confirm the estimate is still valid before sending.

Scope clarification

Hi [Name], I wanted to make sure the estimate was clear. The current scope includes [approved included items] and does not include [approved exclusions]. If you want, we can review whether anything needs to be adjusted before you decide.

Review: use only approved scope language from the estimate.

Decision-maker handoff

Hi [Name], if someone else needs to review the estimate, I can resend the summary or answer specific questions. We want to make the decision easy without duplicating messages.

Review: do not share private customer/job details with a new contact until the team confirms permission.

Final gentle closeout

Hi [Name], I do not want to keep bothering you. Should we keep this estimate open for a later date, adjust the scope, or close it out for now?

Review: only use a final closeout after the approved follow-up cadence and consent rules are met.

5. Weekly pipeline review questions

  • Which estimates are open with no next owner or next check date?
  • Which no-decision estimates are actually waiting on missing scope, financing, permit/HOA, access, or a decision maker?
  • Which follow-ups created replies without discounting or pressure?
  • Which estimates were marked lost without a real reason code?
  • Does the team need better scope templates, decision-maker handoffs, or final closeout scripts?
