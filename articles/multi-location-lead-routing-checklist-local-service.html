Multi-Location Lead Routing Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Multi-location service teams lose leads when a web form, call, marketplace message, or chat transcript lands between branches. Use this worksheet to assign the right location owner, confirm the service area, and send a safe first reply before anyone invents availability, pricing, diagnosis, or travel rules.

Use this when

A lead could belong to more than one branch, crew, territory, or service-area rule.

This is built for local-service teams with multiple locations, franchise territories, mobile crews, after-hours dispatch zones, or property-management portfolios. The goal is simple: route quickly without inventing what a branch can do.

Good fit

  • Shared inbox leads where the address or ZIP decides the owner.
  • Marketplace or referral leads that arrive without full location details.
  • Overflow calls where one branch may help another branch but only after approval.

Do not use it to

  • Promise service in a restricted territory without owner approval.
  • Quote travel fees, warranties, emergency availability, or appointment windows from memory.
  • Hide an out-of-area lead instead of sending a clear next step.

STOP AUTOMATION

Do not let AI or intake staff guess routing facts.

  • Service address, ZIP, building type, or property name is missing.
  • The lead asks for a branch, territory, emergency visit, warranty, discount, or arrival window that is not documented.
  • Two locations could own the lead and no single owner has accepted it.
  • The AI summary invents contact history, service-area coverage, price, diagnosis, permit/license status, or manager approval.
  • The customer has consented to one channel but the proposed reply moves to another channel without permission.

First replies

Use neutral routing language while you verify.

Thanks — I’m checking the right local team for your address so we do not send you to the wrong location. Could you confirm the service address or ZIP code and the best callback number?

Thanks for the details. I’m routing this to the right branch/crew now. If we need manager review for the service area or schedule, we’ll say that clearly before confirming a time.

I do not want to guess on coverage from here. I’m sending this to our local owner for review and will follow up with the safest next step.

AI prompt

Summarize routing evidence without inventing coverage.

You are helping route a local-service lead between possible locations.
Use only the lead notes and routing evidence below.
Return:
1. The likely branch/location owner, or NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.
2. Missing routing facts.
3. Any STOP AUTOMATION triggers.
4. A safe first reply that does not promise price, availability, diagnosis, warranty, travel fee, or service-area coverage unless the evidence states it.
