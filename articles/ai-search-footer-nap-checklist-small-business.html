AI Search Footer and NAP Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this free worksheet to make the small but important website facts — name, address, phone, hours, service area, policies, and proof links — easier for customers and AI search tools to understand.

Best fit: local service companies, consultants, clinics, agencies, studios, property managers, contractors, and any small business whose website footer, contact details, or service-area facts may be copied into search snippets or AI answers.

Human-review rule: do not let AI invent addresses, phone numbers, licensing details, emergency availability, legal disclaimers, service areas, review counts, fees, or guarantees. A business owner or manager should verify every footer/NAP fact against the source of truth before publishing.

9 footer and NAP checks before AI tools summarize your business

  1. Exact business name. Use the same public name in the footer, contact page, Google Business Profile, invoices, and main citations.
  2. Phone and contact routing. Show the primary phone number, text/call rules if relevant, and the best contact path for urgent versus non-urgent requests.
  3. Address or service-area clarity. State whether customers can visit, whether the address is mailing-only, or whether the business serves customers at their location.
  4. Hours and response windows. Separate office hours, service windows, emergency availability, holiday exceptions, and “we reply by” expectations.
  5. Service-area boundaries. List real cities, counties, neighborhoods, or radius limits without implying coverage you do not actually provide.
  6. License, insurance, or credential proof. Link only to current, verifiable proof and avoid claims that require legal, medical, financial, or trade-board review.
  7. Policy links. Link to privacy, refund, cancellation, booking, warranty, or accessibility notes when those affect buyer decisions.
  8. Internal proof links. Connect the footer/contact facts to service pages, staff bios, portfolio examples, reviews, FAQs, and contact/booking pages.
  9. Consistency across templates. Check that mobile footer, desktop footer, landing pages, blog pages, and checkout/booking pages all use current facts.

Return:

2. Facts that are missing or unclear.
3. Any contradiction between footer, contact page, service pages, or policy links.
4. A revised footer/contact snippet using only verified facts.
5. A final checklist of facts a human must verify before publishing.

Stop rules

  • Stop if AI adds a location, service area, emergency promise, legal/medical/financial credential, or warranty term that was not in the source facts.
  • Stop if the page mixes a public storefront address with a private home, mailing-only, or service-area-only address.
  • Stop if review counts, star ratings, licenses, awards, insurance, or “licensed and insured” claims cannot be verified.
  • Stop if local regulations, accessibility, privacy, SMS consent, refund, or cancellation policies need expert or owner review.

Why this matters for AI search

AI search systems often pull compact facts from repeatable page areas such as headers, footers, contact pages, and structured summaries. Clear, consistent footer/NAP data helps reduce wrong answers about where the business operates, how customers can reach it, and what promises are actually verified. This is not a ranking trick; it is basic fact hygiene for customers, crawlers, and answer engines.
