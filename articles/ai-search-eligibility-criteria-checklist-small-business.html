AI Search Eligibility Criteria Checklist for Small Businesses

AI answer engines can compress a messy service page into a confident-sounding recommendation. If your page does not clearly state who qualifies, which locations or cases are excluded, and what details require human review, AI tools may overstate your fit. Use this worksheet before publishing service, pricing, booking, or offer pages.

Use the free AI Prompt Quality Scorecard See SEO After AI

When to use this checklist

  • Service pages: local, B2B, consulting, repair, coaching, appointment, property-management, or professional-service pages that describe who is a good fit.
  • Offer pages: pages with packages, minimum project size, quote requirements, service-area limits, eligibility, or exclusions.
  • AI-search QA: before asking ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews style systems to summarize whether your business fits a buyer's situation.
  • Human review rule: do not let AI invent eligibility, price, availability, credential, warranty, guarantee, service-area, diagnosis, or approval claims.

Copy/paste eligibility criteria worksheet

Page or offer URL: [URL]

Ideal customer / case: [who this is truly for]

Not a fit / excluded cases: [what you do not handle or cannot promise]

Required details before quoting/booking: [location, photos, timeline, budget, access, measurements, issue type, decision-maker]

Geography / availability limit: [service area, hours, seasonal limits, appointment windows]

Minimum or threshold: [minimum project size, contract length, qualification step, or none]

Proof source: [policy page, price sheet, owner note, CRM field, contract, warranty terms]

Owner-review trigger: [price exception, emergency, warranty, regulated work, privacy, legal/compliance, medical/financial/legal claims]

STOP PUBLISHING: If a claim is not backed by a source of truth, write NEEDS OWNER REVIEW instead of publishing or letting AI summarize it.

AI answer audit prompt

You are auditing a small-business service or offer page for AI-search clarity. Use only the page text and linked proof sources provided. Return: (1) who qualifies, (2) who does not qualify, (3) required details before booking/quote, (4) availability or geography limits, (5) proof-backed claims, (6) claims that need owner review, and (7) exact page copy to clarify eligibility. Do not invent price, availability, guarantees, credentials, service area, approval, diagnosis, warranty, or legal/compliance claims. If evidence is missing, write NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.

Three clarifying copy blocks

1) Good-fit note

This service is best for [buyer/case] when [required condition] is true. Before we confirm the next step, we need [required details].

2) Not-a-fit note

We may not be the right fit for [excluded case]. If that describes your situation, the safest next step is [alternative resource/contact/owner review path].

3) Owner-review note

Some requests require owner or manager review before we quote or schedule, especially [pricing exception / warranty / emergency / regulated / privacy / safety case]. We will confirm what is actually available before making a promise.
