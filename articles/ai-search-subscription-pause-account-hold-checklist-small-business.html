Why pause and account-hold answers need a source card

Pause and hold policies sit between sales, billing, support, legal, and customer-success operations. If AI search or a chatbot guesses, it can promise no-charge holds, preserve access incorrectly, misstate reactivation dates, invent credits, conflict with cancellation terms, or confuse seasonal customers who need exact next steps.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI publish subscription-pause, account-hold, billing-freeze, seasonal-hold, access-retention, credit/proration, cancellation-conversion, or reactivation claims until the owner/support lead verifies the current policy source and approves exact public wording.

Customer-safe reply snippets

Thanks for asking. I do not want to guess about pause or account-hold rules. I am checking the current policy and billing record before confirming whether a hold is available, what happens to access, and whether any charges, credits, or reactivation steps apply.
This request needs billing/support review before we promise a pause, no-charge hold, credit, preserved price, access level, or restart date. Please share the subscription email, plan name, requested pause dates, and reason so the right owner can confirm the approved options.
Internal note: Account hold / subscription pause review. Verify plan, term, billing cycle, contract/legacy status, payment state, access entitlement, credit/proration rule, reactivation path, and approved public wording before replying.

Review before publishing

  • Does the public wording match the current billing system, terms, help center, renewal emails, cancellation page, support macros, and customer-success playbook?
  • Does it avoid unsupported promises about no-charge holds, credits, refunds, retained access, grandfathered pricing, automatic reactivation, support availability, or cancellation reversal?
  • Are legacy plans, annual contracts, failed-payment accounts, regulated services, enterprise terms, and high-value accounts routed to a human reviewer?
  • Is there a clear owner for approving exceptions, documenting source changes, updating AI-search-visible pages, and retiring stale pause-policy language?
