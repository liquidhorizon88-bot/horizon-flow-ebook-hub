Lease renewal notice question checklist for property managers

Use this worksheet before an AI assistant, leasing coordinator, resident-services inbox, or renewal workflow answers a tenant question about renewal notices, deadlines, rent changes, fees, term options, concessions, or move-out timing. It keeps the reply tied to verified lease and policy records.

Use the free missed-lead checklist

When to use it

  • A resident asks whether a renewal notice, rent increase, concession, non-renewal, or deadline is correct.
  • A leasing or property-management team needs to acknowledge the question without interpreting the lease, changing terms, waiving fees, or promising exceptions.
  • The manager needs a handoff record: source documents checked, owner/legal/accounting review needed, and the official channel for the final answer.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI or a front-desk script interpret lease rights, legal notice periods, rent-control rules, fee waivers, non-renewal decisions, concessions, move-out deadlines, deposit outcomes, or policy exceptions. Route those items to the property manager, owner, accounting reviewer, or legal/process reviewer.

Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

Thanks for sending this renewal question. We are checking the lease/notice record and will reply through [official channel] once the correct reviewer confirms the answer.
We do not want to guess on renewal deadlines, rent amounts, fees, or exceptions. I am routing this to [manager/team] with the source records attached.
We received your note about [question]. The next step is to verify [source record] and confirm whether a manager/accounting/legal-process review is needed before any resident-facing answer is final.
