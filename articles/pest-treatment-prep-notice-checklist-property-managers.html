Pest Treatment Prep Notice Checklist for Property Managers

Use this source-card worksheet when a resident asks about pest treatment preparation, bedbug or roach treatment steps, unit access, re-entry timing, missed prep, sanitation notes, vendor instructions, or responsibility for follow-up. It helps teams reply quickly without inventing lease terms, treatment guarantees, health claims, access rights, fees, or blame.

Best use

  • Resident asks what to do before pest treatment.
  • Staff needs a safe treatment-prep notice before a vendor visit.
  • A resident missed prep, disputes responsibility, or asks about re-entry timing.
  • An AI draft must not promise eradication, health advice, entry authority, fees, or blame.

Why this needs a source card

Pest-treatment messages can mix resident instructions, vendor recommendations, lease or community rules, access notices, chemicals, pets, children, health concerns, and sensitive blame questions. A rushed automation can accidentally tell residents to use unsafe prep steps, promise treatment success, reveal another resident's complaint, or state fee/violation outcomes that have not been approved.

PEST-TREATMENT-PREP-CARD-READY. STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI provide chemical-safety advice, guarantee eradication, assign blame, disclose neighbor reports, promise entry or re-entry timing, threaten fees or violations, or close a health/safety concern without verified vendor/manager instructions.

Access window / notice source: [official notice + delivery proof + date]
Re-entry / safety wording source: [vendor-approved text only]
Pets / children / health concern mentioned: [yes/no + route to manager/vendor]
Prior related ticket: [ticket ID + status]
Approved next step: [send prep notice / ask vendor / reschedule / manager review / emergency route]
CRM/PMS labels: [pest-treatment-prep / access-needed / vendor-review / health-safety-review / missed-prep]
STOP AUTOMATION if: chemical safety, health symptoms, blame, fee/violation threat, neighbor identity, entry authority, re-entry timing, or treatment success claim is not verified.

Resident-safe acknowledgement

Hi [Name], thanks for checking on the pest-treatment preparation for [unit]. We are verifying the current vendor instructions and property notice before confirming the exact next step. We do not want to guess about preparation, access, re-entry timing, fees, or treatment expectations from the message alone.

If a resident asks about safety or symptoms

We cannot give medical or chemical-safety advice in this message. We will route the treatment-instruction question to the approved vendor/manager process, and if there is an urgent health or safety concern, please follow the appropriate emergency or professional guidance for your situation.
