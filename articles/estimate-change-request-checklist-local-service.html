Why change requests need a guardrail

A small scope change can silently change price, parts, crew time, access, permit needs, warranty language, and schedule availability. If the team answers from memory or lets AI draft from incomplete notes, customers can receive a promise the estimator, dispatcher, or owner cannot honor.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not quote, approve, schedule, or send a revised estimate from AI output alone. A human owner must verify the original estimate, requested change, price impact, material availability, schedule impact, and any warranty/permit/policy limits.

Price/schedule impactPossible added cost, credit, deposit, crew time, access window, materials, and availability.Only the approved estimator/owner can confirm price or timing.
Customer-safe replyAcknowledge the change, explain the review step, and set a realistic next update time.Do not imply approval, discount, warranty, emergency priority, or same-day scheduling unless verified.
CRM closeoutLabel, owner, due time, revised estimate link, customer reply status, and next follow-up.Keep old and revised scope connected so teams do not work from the wrong version.

Copy/paste customer replies

Hi [Name] — thanks for the update. I have your requested change as: [customer words]. I am going to compare it against estimate [#] and confirm any price, material, or schedule impact before we revise anything. I will update you by [time/date].
Quick note: I do not want to guess on the revised price or timing. [Owner/estimator] needs to review [photo/measurement/part/access detail] first. Once verified, we can send the revised estimate or next-step options.
CRM note: change request for estimate [#]. Customer requested: [change]. Proof needed: [photos/measurements/approval]. Owner: [person]. Due: [date/time]. Do not schedule or promise revised pricing until owner review is complete.

Fast QA before sending

  • Compare the requested change against the original estimate and any revised estimate draft.
  • Confirm whether the change affects deposit, parts, labor, access, permits, warranty, or schedule.
  • Use a review-status reply if the answer is not ready; do not let AI fill unknowns.
  • Attach the revised estimate or scope note in the CRM before a dispatcher, technician, or property manager acts on it.
