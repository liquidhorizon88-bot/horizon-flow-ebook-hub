Local service lead response

Front Desk Call Scorecard for Local Service Businesses

Use this scorecard to review the first phone conversation after a missed call, web-form request, Google Business Profile message, or referral. The goal is not to grade people harshly. It is to find the exact handoff, note, or script that keeps good leads from disappearing.

Best fit: home-service teams, property managers, office managers, dispatchers, owner-operators, and agencies helping local businesses improve speed-to-lead and follow-up quality.

The five things a good first call should capture

1. Source

Where the lead came from: call, form, Google, referral, ad, repeat customer, or property manager.

2. Need

The job type, urgency, service area, access constraints, photos, and decision timeline.

3. Fit

Whether the request matches service area, job type, minimums, availability, and safety/legal limits.

4. Next step

Booked appointment, quote prep, photo request, dispatcher review, owner review, or not-a-fit closeout.

5. Follow-up owner

The person responsible for the next message, due date, CRM tag, and stop rule.

Starter scripts for the next step

After a good call

Thanks for calling about [service]. I have [next step] noted for [date/time]. If anything changes, reply here and we will update the job notes.

Need photos before estimate

Thanks — to prepare the estimate, can you send photos of [area/item] plus any access notes? We will review them before promising a price or arrival window.

Owner/dispatcher review

I have your details and am sending them to [role] for review. We will confirm the safest next step before scheduling or quoting.

Not a fit

Thanks for reaching out. Based on [reason], we may not be the right fit for this request. If helpful, the next best step is [neutral suggestion].

Human-review rule: do not promise price, diagnosis, arrival time, warranty coverage, emergency availability, refunds, legal/lease outcomes, or safety conclusions unless the right person has confirmed it.
