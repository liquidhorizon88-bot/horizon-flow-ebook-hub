AI Search PDF Download Review Checklist for Small Businesses

Public PDFs are easy to forget after a website refresh. A stale brochure, menu, rate sheet, intake packet, lead magnet, or policy download can keep showing old facts long after the main page has changed. Use this worksheet before AI search tools, staff, agencies, or customers quote an old file.

Use this when

  • Old PDFs, menus, brochures, spec sheets, lead magnets, or checkout downloads still rank or get shared.
  • AI answers quote a file instead of the current service page.
  • Pricing, hours, eligibility, guarantees, policies, or service areas changed.
  • A team wants a reviewed source map before refreshing pages or adding schema.

Why this matters

Downloads can become hidden source-of-truth conflicts

Small businesses often update the homepage, booking page, or service page first, while older downloads remain public. Search engines, AI assistants, directory editors, and customers may still find the file. If the file says “24/7 emergency service,” “free estimate,” “2024 prices,” “limited-time discount,” or an old refund policy, the business needs a safe review path before publishing more copy.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not reuse, summarize, cite, or rewrite a PDF/download claim until a human owner confirms whether the file is current, archived, replaced, redirected, or removed. Mark unsupported claims as NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.

AI audit prompt

You are helping a small business review public PDF and download files before AI search tools quote stale facts.
Use only the supplied file notes, current source URLs, screenshots, and owner-approved facts.
Do not invent pricing, hours, credentials, service areas, guarantees, legal/privacy/refund terms, customer proof, availability, or eligibility.
For each file, return: exact risky claim, current approved source, conflict type, customer impact, safest action, whether the public file should be updated/removed/redirected, and whether the claim is STOP PUBLISHING or NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.
If evidence is missing, write UNKNOWN and ask for the source instead of guessing.

Review questions before updating the page

  • Does the download have a canonical replacement page, or is it still the current approved file?
  • Are old files linked from emails, QR codes, ads, partner pages, marketplace listings, or social bios?
  • Does the file mention prices, discounts, hours, emergency availability, refund terms, financing, warranties, credentials, or regulated claims?
  • Can the team remove or redirect the old URL without breaking customer access?
  • Should the replacement page include a “last reviewed” date and owner so future AI summaries know which source is current?
