Vendor Access Window and Entry Instruction Checklist for Property Managers

Vendor visits can turn routine maintenance into resident frustration when access windows, notice rules, pets, keys, lockboxes, do-not-enter areas, or privacy limits are missing. Use this source card before staff or AI tools summarize vendor entry instructions, reschedule requests, or resident updates.

STOP AUTOMATION

  • Do not invent legal notice compliance, entry rights, exact vendor arrival times, lockbox/key details, resident consent, fee promises, or completion outcomes.
  • Escalate messages mentioning refusal of entry, pets or children, disability/accommodation needs, harassment, privacy, cameras, lost keys, damage, emergency access, or legal/fair-housing language.
  • Keep private access details inside approved work-order or property-management systems; do not paste codes, keys, alarm details, or tenant schedules into public/shared channels.

Please reply through [official channel] before [deadline] if the access window, pet instructions, do-not-enter areas, or best contact number need to change.

Vendor-safe handoff note

Work order [ID] / [unit]: approved visit window is [date/time]. Scope is [scope]. Entry method is [approved method]. Notes: [pets/do-not-enter/privacy restrictions]. Do not expand scope, photograph unrelated areas, or state completion until the work-order closeout is logged.

AI summary prompt for manager review

Summarize this vendor access message for property-management review.
Use only the provided work-order record, resident message, notice record, and approved access policy.
Return: property/unit, vendor, issue, access window, entry method, resident constraints, privacy/accommodation flags, vendor handoff, resident reply, CRM labels, and next update owner/time.
Do not invent legal notice compliance, entry rights, access codes, arrival times, completion promises, fees, diagnosis, warranty, or resident consent.
If the message mentions refusal of entry, disability/accommodation, safety, privacy/cameras, harassment, lost keys, minors, pets, legal/fair-housing language, or emergency entry, label NEEDS MANAGER REVIEW.

CRM labels to add

  • vendor-access-window-confirmed — visit date/window and resident-facing confirmation are recorded.
  • access-instruction-review-needed — keys, lockbox, escort, pets, cameras, or do-not-enter instructions need manager review.
  • resident-notice-source-needed — staff cannot verify the required notice/confirmation record yet.
  • vendor-closeout-due — vendor must log completion, photos, or next-step notes before resident update.
  • privacy-accommodation-flag — privacy, accessibility, fair-housing, safety, or legal language requires human review.
