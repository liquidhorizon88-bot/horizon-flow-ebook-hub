Quote Expiry Reminder Text Templates for Local Service Businesses

Use this worksheet when a quote, estimate, or proposal has a real approved expiration date and the team needs a respectful reminder that does not create fake urgency or change the offer.

Use this when: a quote is nearing its approved expiration date, a customer is quiet after receiving a valid estimate, or a dispatcher needs a clean CRM note before closing an old proposal.

Human-review rule: verify the quote number, exact scope, price, expiration date, payment/financing language, warranty notes, parts/material assumptions, access requirements, and last customer message before sending. AI can draft reminders; it cannot invent a deadline, discount, price hold, financing approval, warranty, diagnosis, availability, or customer intent.

1. Quote expiry reminder worksheet

Customer / job: [name, address or CRM id]
Quote or estimate id: [approved id]
Quote sent on: [date]
Approved expiration / review date: [date]
Exact approved scope: [scope]
Exact approved price / range: [price]
What changes after expiry: [needs re-quote / parts price review / schedule review / none]
Last customer message: [exact wording or none]
Consent/channel status: [SMS ok / email ok / call only / do not contact]
Risk flags: [financing, warranty, legal/policy, safety, permit/HOA, price increase, parts availability]
Recommended status: [expiry-reminder-ready / needs-owner-review / needs-requote / closeout-only]
Owner/reviewer: [name]
Next safe message: [script below]
Next check date: [date]

2. Safe quote-status labels

  • EXPIRY-REMINDER-READY: the quote is valid, contact consent is clear, and the reminder uses only approved wording.
  • NEEDS-OWNER-REVIEW: pricing, warranty, discount, financing, parts, legal/policy, or safety wording must be checked first.
  • NEEDS-REQUOTE: scope, parts, schedule, or pricing changed enough that the old quote should not be reused.
  • CLOSEOUT-ONLY: the team should ask whether to keep the quote open, revise it, or close it without pressure.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI create scarcity, invent an expiration date, extend a price, promise a discount, approve financing, diagnose the job, change warranty language, or mark the customer as lost without a human-reviewed record. QUOTE-EXPIRY-READY means ready for reviewed outreach, not automatic sending.

3. Copy/paste reminder texts

Gentle upcoming-expiry reminder

Hi [Name], quick reminder that the quote we sent for [project] is currently valid through [approved date]. If you have questions about scope, timing, or next steps, I can help get them answered before that date.

Review: confirm the expiration date and quote validity before sending.

Needs re-quote check

Hi [Name], before we move forward with the quote for [project], we may need to review [parts/scope/schedule/pricing] because the original quote window is ending. Would you like us to refresh the estimate for review?

Review: use only the approved reason for the re-quote.

Decision-maker handoff

Hi [Name], if someone else needs to review the quote before [approved date], I can resend the approved summary or help route questions to the right person on our team.

Review: do not send private job details to a new contact without permission.

No-pressure closeout

Hi [Name], I do not want to keep bothering you. Should we keep this quote open, refresh it for a later date, or close it out for now?

Review: only use after the approved follow-up cadence and contact rules are met.

4. AI assist prompt for quote expiry notes

You are helping a local service business review a quote or estimate before an expiry reminder.
Use only the facts provided. Do not invent customer intent, expiration dates, price holds, discounts, urgency, financing approval, warranty terms, diagnosis, parts availability, schedule availability, or prior conversation.
Return:
1. One-line neutral quote status.
2. Missing facts that must be checked before outreach.
3. Risk flags needing owner review.
4. Best status label from: EXPIRY-REMINDER-READY, NEEDS-OWNER-REVIEW, NEEDS-REQUOTE, CLOSEOUT-ONLY.
5. One safe reminder angle for human review.
6. What not to say.
Quote record:
[paste quote, CRM notes, expiration language, and last customer messages here]

5. Weekly quote-pipeline review questions

  • Which quotes expire in the next seven days without a next owner?
  • Which reminders need owner review because price, financing, warranty, or scope language may be sensitive?
  • Which expired quotes need a re-quote instead of another follow-up?
  • Which estimates were marked lost without a real customer response or approved reason code?
  • Does the team need better quote-expiry fields, next-check dates, or closeout labels in the CRM?
