Referral Source Lead Handoff Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Referral leads can be high-trust, but they often arrive with missing details, unclear ownership, and risky assumptions about what the partner promised. Use this copy/paste checklist to capture the source, customer context, next owner, and safe reply before the lead gets lost or overpromised.

When to use this handoff

  • Partner referral: a realtor, property manager, contractor, insurance contact, supplier, neighbor, or past customer sends a lead.
  • Warm intro with thin details: the referral names the issue but not the service address, access window, urgency, or decision-maker.
  • Shared accountability: the team needs to thank the source, help the customer, and record who owns the next reply.
  • Human review rule: do not publish or automate claims about discounts, priority, warranties, guaranteed scheduling, or partner promises unless an owner verifies them.

Copy/paste referral lead handoff card

Referral source: [person/company/channel]

Source relationship: [past customer / realtor / property manager / contractor / vendor / community partner / unknown]

Customer/contact: [name + approved contact method]

Need described: [service request in the customer/source's words]

Source facts only: [what the source actually said, not assumptions]

Missing detail to request: [address, photos, timeline, urgency, access, budget, decision-maker]

Next owner: [dispatcher / estimator / owner / tech / office manager]

Response window: [real callback or review window]

Partner/source follow-up: [thank-you sent / update after booked / no update unless permission]

STOP AUTOMATION: Do not invent referral relationship, consent, urgency, availability, price, diagnosis, warranty, discount, or guaranteed priority.

Three short scripts

1) First reply to referred customer

Hi [Name] — [Referral Source] suggested we connect about [service/request]. I have [known fact] and still need [one missing detail] so the right person can review it. The next real response window is [window]. If that works, reply here with [detail] and the best callback number.

Review: confirm the customer gave permission to contact them before texting/emailing.

2) Thank-you note to referral source

Thanks for sending [Name] our way. We received the referral and are reviewing [general service category]. We will handle the customer details directly and only share updates that [customer/business policy] allows.

Review: do not share customer details back to the source unless permission and policy allow it.

3) Missing-detail follow-up

Hi [Name], quick follow-up on the referral from [Source]. To avoid guessing, could you send [one detail/photo/address/access window]? Once we have that, [owner/team] can confirm the appropriate next step.

Review: ask for the minimum useful detail; do not request sensitive/private information unless necessary and approved.

AI summary prompt for referral handoffs

You are helping a local service business summarize referral leads. Use only the referral notes provided. Return: (1) source, (2) customer request, (3) missing detail, (4) next owner, (5) safe first reply, (6) privacy-safe source thank-you, and (7) OWNER-REVIEW items. Do not invent referral relationship, customer consent, availability, price, diagnosis, warranty, discount, priority, emergency status, or source promises. If evidence is missing, write NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.
