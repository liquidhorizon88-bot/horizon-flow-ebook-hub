AI Search Reputation Correction Log for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet when an AI answer engine, AI Overview, chatbot, or search-result summary repeats a reputation-sensitive claim about your business that is outdated, exaggerated, unfairly negative, or missing context. It helps you collect proof, fix the public source, and track correction follow-up without inventing counterclaims.

When to use this log

  • An AI answer summarizes a review, policy, incident, guarantee, ranking, credential, or customer complaint incorrectly.
  • A prospect asks about an AI-generated reputation claim that does not match your current public sources.
  • Your team needs a calmer response workflow before editing pages, submitting feedback, contacting a directory, or replying publicly.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not publish a counterclaim, testimonial, guarantee, ranking, or legal/policy statement unless the owner has verified the source URL and evidence.

Safer public-source repair checklist

  • Quote the exact wrong AI/search wording before making changes.
  • Repair the source page first: policy, FAQ, service page, review page, about page, trust page, or directory profile.
  • Add dates, eligibility limits, proof links, and contact paths where appropriate.
  • Use neutral wording such as “current policy,” “source page,” or “verified as of” instead of attacking the platform.
  • Schedule a re-check; AI answers may lag behind corrected sources.

AI audit prompt

You are auditing a reputation-sensitive AI-search correction workflow. Compare the wrong claim, proposed corrected wording, public source URL, and evidence. Flag invented claims, missing proof, risky legal/policy wording, review/testimonial overstatements, unsupported guarantees, and anything that should be held for owner/legal review. Return: safe to submit / hold, missing proof, safer wording, and next monitoring date.
