Elevator Outage Accessibility Update Checklist for Property Managers

When an elevator is down, a vague “we're working on it” reply can miss accessibility needs, move-in logistics, delivery issues, emergency access, and resident trust. Use this source card before staff or AI tools summarize elevator outages, repair timing, alternate access, or accommodation-sensitive updates.

ELEVATOR-OUTAGE-ACCESSIBILITY-READY

Use the resident issue triage mini-pack See Local Lead Rescue System

STOP AUTOMATION

  • Do not invent repair times, vendor arrival windows, inspection results, cause of failure, or compensation promises.
  • Escalate messages mentioning mobility/accessibility needs, medical equipment, emergency access, heat/cooling exposure, lockout, safety, harassment, legal claims, or fair-housing language.
  • Use the property's approved notice/accommodation process; do not diagnose ADA/fair-housing obligations from an AI reply.

Resident-safe outage acknowledgement

Hi [Resident Name] — thanks for flagging this. We have logged the elevator issue for [building/elevator] and are checking the latest verified status with [vendor/maintenance owner]. The next update will be sent by [time]. If this affects mobility, medical equipment, caregiver access, deliveries, or a scheduled move, please reply through [official contact path] so the property manager can review the right next step.

No-new-information update

Quick update: we do not yet have a confirmed repair-complete time. The current verified status is: [status]. Please use [approved alternate route/process] where applicable. We will recheck and send another update by [time], or sooner if the vendor confirms a change.

AI summary prompt for manager review

Summarize this elevator outage resident message for property-management review.
Use only the provided source record, resident message, and approved notice wording.
Return: property/building, elevator/outage scope, resident impact, accessibility/accommodation flag, approved reply, operations handoff, CRM labels, and next check time.
Do not invent repair times, vendor status, cause, compensation, legal obligations, or accessibility conclusions.
If the message includes mobility/accessibility, medical, safety, emergency access, legal, fair-housing, harassment, heat/cooling exposure, or lockout language, label NEEDS MANAGER REVIEW.

CRM labels to add

  • elevator-outage-active — outage or service interruption is currently unresolved.
  • accessibility-review-needed — resident reports mobility/accessibility, medical, caregiver, or official accommodation context.
  • alternate-access-needed — resident needs approved alternate route, staff escort, delivery coordination, or move logistics.
  • vendor-status-pending — staff cannot safely state repair timing yet.
  • resident-update-due — next status update time is scheduled even if there is no change.
