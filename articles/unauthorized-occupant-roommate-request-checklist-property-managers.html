Why this deserves a worksheet

Roommate and occupant questions often combine customer service, privacy, access control, screening, lease terms, and fair-housing-sensitive facts. A rushed AI draft or front-desk answer can accidentally approve a resident, disclose private information, promise a key or gate code, threaten a notice, or create inconsistent treatment. The safe move is to acknowledge, capture the facts, and route the decision through approved policy.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI approve or deny an occupant, interpret the lease, promise screening results, threaten eviction or fees, disclose private resident details, issue access credentials, decide accommodation/caregiver questions, or create legal/fair-housing-sensitive wording. A property manager must verify the source record and approved policy first.

Resident / property / unit: [name, property, unit]
Request or report type: [add roommate / long-term guest / unauthorized occupant / sublet / caregiver / access credential / dispute]
Message received through: [portal / email / SMS / phone / in person]
Exact resident words: [paste without adding assumptions]
Person involved: [name if provided / relationship / unknown]
Current lease occupants on record: [source + as-of date]
Access credential requested or used? [key / fob / gate / portal / parking / none]
Screening/application/policy source checked: [source link or file]
Accommodation, caregiver, domestic situation, protected-class, or safety language mentioned? [yes/no + manager/legal/fair-housing review]
Privacy concern: [yes/no + details not to disclose]
Approved escalation owner / due time: [manager/legal/compliance/accounting]
CRM label: [roommate-request / unauthorized-occupant-report / access-review / sublet-review / accommodation-review / lease-sensitive]
STOP AUTOMATION until: [manager verifies lease/policy/source record]

Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

Hi [Name], thanks for sending this over. We have logged your question about [roommate/guest/occupant/access] for [property/unit]. Because this can involve lease, screening, access, privacy, or policy review, we will check the official record before giving a specific answer.
Thanks for the update. I am routing this to [manager/team] so we can review the lease record and approved process. We do not want to guess about approval, access, fees, notices, or next steps until the source record is checked.
Internal handoff: Occupant/roommate request for [resident/unit]. Type: [type]. Exact message: [summary]. Sensitive flags: [privacy/access/accommodation/legal/fair housing/safety]. Source record needed: [lease/application/policy]. Do not approve, deny, threaten, disclose, or issue access until reviewed.

Fast QA before any answer goes out

  • Does the draft avoid approving/denying a person, sublet, access credential, fee, or legal outcome?
  • Does it avoid disclosing private resident, applicant, screening, lease, or complaint details?
  • Does it route caregiver, accommodation, domestic, safety, protected-class, or fair-housing-sensitive facts to a qualified reviewer?
  • Does the CRM label make the follow-up owner and due time visible?
  • Does the resident receive a helpful acknowledgement without unsupported promises?
