Pet Waste Complaint Triage Checklist for Property Managers

Use this free worksheet before replying to a resident, owner, vendor, or neighbor about pet waste, odors, common-area cleanup, hallway or lawn complaints, repeated pet-policy reports, or a possible service-animal/accommodation-sensitive issue.

PET-WASTE-COMPLAINT-TRIAGE-READY

Pet waste messages look simple until the reply accidentally names another resident, promises a fine, cites the wrong lease rule, ignores a service-animal accommodation issue, or escalates a neighbor conflict without enough evidence.

This triage card keeps the first response factual, calm, and manager-reviewed before anyone promises enforcement, cleanup charges, lease notices, vendor action, credits, refunds, or legal timelines.

Copy/paste pet waste complaint triage card

Resident / reporter: [name, unit, contact]
Property / affected area: [building, hallway, lawn, dog run, elevator, stairwell, amenity, parking area]
Issue type: [pet waste / odor / repeat complaint / common-area damage / off-leash pet / sanitation concern / possible accommodation-sensitive issue / other]
Source evidence: [photo, timestamp, location, vendor note, patrol/security note, prior warning, lease/pet addendum, none yet]
Potential sensitivity: [service animal / assistance animal / disability accommodation / neighbor conflict / private resident information / safety or sanitation hazard]
Resident-safe acknowledgement: [short neutral reply]
Manager / owner review needed: [yes/no + reviewer]
CRM label: PET-WASTE-TRIAGE / NEEDS-EVIDENCE / ACCOMMODATION-REVIEW / CLEANUP-VENDOR / OWNER-REVIEW
Next safe action: [ask for location/evidence / route to manager / verify policy source / schedule cleanup/vendor check / close with note]
Do-not-say until reviewed: [resident identity, pet owner accusation, fine amount, lease violation, eviction/legal threat, guaranteed cleanup time, refund/credit promise]

Resident-safe first replies

1. Need location and evidence

Thanks for letting us know. Please send the location, date/time, and any photo or detail that helps us route this correctly. We will document the report and review the applicable property notes before confirming any next step.

2. Common-area cleanup concern

We appreciate the report. We are documenting the affected area and routing it for review/cleanup follow-up. If there is an immediate safety or sanitation concern, please use the property's urgent-contact procedure as well.

3. Repeat pet-policy concern

We understand this has been frustrating. We are adding this report to the existing concern record and will review the available evidence and approved policy source before sending the next update.

Stop rules before automation

  • STOP AUTOMATION before mentioning fines, cleanup charges, lease violations, notices to cure, eviction, disability/accommodation issues, service-animal status, legal notices, fee waivers, credits, refunds, or guaranteed cleanup times.
  • Do not invent lease language, pet-addendum terms, resident identity, pet ownership, prior warnings, accommodation status, vendor actions, cleanup timing, or owner/HOA decisions.
  • Keep private resident and accommodation details inside the approved property-management system. A first reply should gather facts and route the issue, not publicly decide fault.
  • Use manager-reviewed follow-up for policy-backed enforcement, fee, vendor, or resident-specific updates.
