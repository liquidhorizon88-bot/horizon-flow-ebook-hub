Maintenance Priority Source Card for Property Managers

Use this source card before a property manager, resident portal, AI receptionist, maintenance chatbot, or CRM workflow labels a resident maintenance request as emergency, urgent, routine, duplicate, vendor-waiting, or owner-review. It keeps the next reply useful without inventing priority, access permission, costs, diagnosis, warranty, or completion timing.

Copy the source card Get Local Lead Rescue

Use this when

  • A resident reports a leak, appliance outage, lock issue, noise, pest, HVAC, access, safety, or repeat repair problem.
  • Staff need to separate emergency routing from routine scheduling before an AI tool drafts a response.
  • The team wants a consistent CRM label and manager-review path before promising timing or next steps.

Why maintenance priority needs a source card

Resident maintenance messages often arrive with partial facts: screenshots, portal notes, after-hours calls, vendor comments, owner approvals, and old work orders. AI can blend those fragments and overstate severity or timing. This card forces a human-readable record before automation decides what to say next.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI promise emergency response, habitability decisions, fees, warranty coverage, owner approval, legal rights, vendor arrival, completion timing, diagnosis, or resident access permission until a manager verifies the source card and local policy.

Copy/paste maintenance-priority source card

Resident / unit / property: [name, unit, property]
Request channel: [portal / phone / email / text / vendor / after-hours / other]
Issue category: [water / heat-AC / electrical / lock-access / appliance / pest / noise / safety / other]
Resident words: [paste exact resident description]
Photo/video/source links: [URLs, work order IDs, attachments]
Known immediate risk: [life/safety, active water, no heat/cooling, security, accessibility, none known, unknown]
Access status: [permission given / needs appointment / emergency access policy review / unknown]
Duplicate or related work order: [ID/status]
Vendor/tech status: [not assigned / assigned / en route / waiting parts / completed / unknown]
Owner/manager approval needed: [yes/no/why]
Policy/source record checked: [lease, house rules, maintenance SOP, local code, vendor note]
Priority label: [emergency / urgent / routine / duplicate / owner-review / needs-human-triage]
Resident-safe next reply: [approved acknowledgement]
CRM label: [maintenance-priority-source-card / emergency-review / owner-review / vendor-waiting / access-needed]
Human reviewer: [manager/maintenance lead]

Resident-safe acknowledgement templates

Thanks for sending this in. We are reviewing the maintenance details, access status, and any photos or prior work orders before we label the priority or assign the next step. If there is an immediate safety concern, please use the official emergency maintenance contact path listed in your resident materials.
Internal note: Needs maintenance-priority source card. Verify resident words, photos/source links, access permission, duplicate work orders, vendor status, policy/source record, and manager approval before AI drafts a priority label or timeline.
STOP AUTOMATION: Do not promise vendor arrival, completion timing, emergency status, access rights, fees, warranty, legal/habitability decisions, or diagnosis from an AI draft. Route to manager review.

Review before replying

  • Does the priority label match the resident's exact words and verified source records?
  • Are access permission, duplicate work orders, vendor status, and owner/manager approval clear?
  • Could the issue involve safety, accessibility, legal, lease, fee, warranty, habitability, or privacy concerns?
  • Does the draft avoid invented vendor timing, completion promises, diagnosis, emergency status, or access rights?
