Call Disposition Code Checklist for Local Service Businesses

When every missed call, estimate request, wrong number, duplicate lead, and no-answer attempt gets the same vague CRM note, owners cannot see which leads need action. Use this simple disposition code list to make next steps visible without letting AI invent what happened.

  • Local service businesses using spreadsheets, Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, HubSpot, or any CRM
  • Teams that want cleaner follow-up before buying more ads
  • Managers reviewing missed calls, web forms, texts, and marketplace leads

Copy/paste worksheet

Start with 12 disposition codes

Keep the labels short enough for a CRM dropdown, but specific enough that the next owner knows what to do.

CodeUse whenRequired next field
NEW-UNTOUCHEDLead arrived and no human response has happened yet.Owner + first-response due time
CALLBACK-DUECustomer asked for a callback or the first call was missed.Callback window + owner
NEEDS-ONE-DETAILOne missing detail blocks routing, quote, or scheduling.Exact detail requested
QUOTE-SENTEstimate/quote was sent and needs a follow-up date.Follow-up date + quote link/reference
SCHEDULEDAppointment is booked and confirmation is needed.Date/window + confirmation status
WAITLIST-CAPACITYBusiness is booked out or waiting on a cancellation slot.Earliest honest window + opt-in status
PARTS-DOCUMENTS-HOLDParts, permit, HOA, photos, certificate, or access details are missing.Missing item + responsible party
NOT-A-FITService, area, budget, timing, or policy makes the job not a fit.Reason code + approved closeout message
WRONG-NUMBER-DNCWrong number, opt-out, spam, duplicate contact, or do-not-contact request.Suppression/cleanup note
DUPLICATE-MERGEDSame lead arrived from more than one channel.Primary record + preserved source(s)
LOST-REASON-KNOWNThe team knows why the lead was lost.Evidence + reactivation eligibility
OWNER-REVIEWThe next reply touches price, diagnosis, warranty, emergency, refund, legal, or policy claims.Reviewer + question to answer

CRM fields to add beside the code

  • Lead source: phone, form, text, marketplace, referral, repeat customer, ad, organic, or unknown.
  • Last customer touch: date/time and channel of the latest real human or approved automated message.
  • Next owner: dispatcher, estimator, tech, office manager, owner, customer, partner, or no-action.
  • Next due time: a specific date/time, not “soon.”
  • Stop reason: opt-out, wrong number, safety issue, legal/policy review, not serviceable, duplicate, or closed.
  • Evidence link: call log, form entry, quote, photo, email thread, CRM note, or invoice reference.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI assign a disposition code from incomplete notes if the result would trigger pricing, diagnosis, emergency advice, warranty/refund wording, or do-not-contact decisions.

Two copy/paste internal notes

1) Callback due handoff

Disposition: CALLBACK-DUE
Lead: {first_name_or_record_id}
Source: {call/form/text/marketplace/referral}
Need: {short_service_need}
Last touch: {time_and_channel}
Callback window: {approved_window}
Owner: {name_or_role}
Stop rule: Do not promise exact arrival, price, diagnosis, warranty, emergency status, or service availability until {reviewer} confirms.

2) Needs one detail

Disposition: NEEDS-ONE-DETAIL
Missing detail: {photo/address/access/model/timing/budget/approval}
Customer-safe ask: {approved_question}
Owner after reply: {dispatcher/estimator/owner}
Due time: {date_time}
Stop rule: If the reply changes scope, safety, price, warranty, or policy, move to OWNER-REVIEW.

AI audit prompt for disposition cleanup

Use anonymized counts and notes only. Remove names, phone numbers, addresses, photos, payment data, and private job details before pasting.

You are helping a local service business clean up lead disposition codes. Use only the anonymized summary below. Return: (1) which leads are missing a disposition, (2) which leads have a disposition but no next owner, (3) which leads have a next owner but no due time, (4) which records need OWNER-REVIEW, and (5) a safer CRM dropdown list. Do not invent call outcomes, customer consent, availability, pricing, diagnosis, warranty, emergency status, or lost reasons. If evidence is missing, write NEEDS HUMAN REVIEW. Summary: {paste anonymized lead table}

Free first, paid if useful

Use codes to find the follow-up leak

After one week, count how many records sit in NEW-UNTOUCHED, CALLBACK-DUE, NEEDS-ONE-DETAIL, QUOTE-SENT, and OWNER-REVIEW. The biggest pile usually tells you which script or workflow to fix first.
