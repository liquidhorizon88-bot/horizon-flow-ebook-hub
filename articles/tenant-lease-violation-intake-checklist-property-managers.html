Tenant Lease Violation Intake Checklist for Property Managers

Use this free worksheet before replying to a suspected lease violation, community-rule issue, unauthorized pet/occupant question, parking complaint, smoking complaint, or amenity misuse report.

Copy the checklist Get Local Lead Rescue

Use this when

  • A resident, owner, board member, neighbor, or vendor reports a possible rule issue.
  • Your team needs to acknowledge the message without promising notices, fines, eviction, legal action, or outcomes.
  • Photos, timestamps, policy references, or manager review are missing.
  • You want AI to summarize an intake record but not decide enforcement.

Why this matters

Violation messages are high-risk customer-service moments.

Property-management teams often need to respond quickly when someone reports noise, parking, pets, smoking, guests, trash, access, or amenity misuse. A rushed reply can accidentally promise enforcement, reveal private details, escalate a dispute, or cite a lease rule that has not been checked.

This checklist keeps the first response useful: acknowledge receipt, collect source facts, route to the right reviewer, and stop automation before AI or staff invent lease terms, notices, fines, deadlines, or legal conclusions.

LEASE-VIOLATION-INTAKE-READY

Copy/paste lease-violation intake card

Resident / unit / property:
Reported issue type: [noise / parking / pet / guest / smoking / trash / access / amenity / other]
Reporter source: [resident / owner / neighbor / vendor / staff / board / other]
Date/time reported:
Date/time issue allegedly occurred:
Evidence received: [message / photo / video / inspection note / call log / none]
Lease/rule/policy source to verify:
Prior related tickets or warnings:
Sensitive details to avoid sharing:
Immediate safety or emergency concern: [yes/no + source]
Manager/legal/owner review needed: [yes/no]
Resident-safe acknowledgement sent: [yes/no]
CRM label:
Owner / next reviewer:
Next internal check date/time:
STOP AUTOMATION if lease terms, legal notices, fines, eviction, entry rights, privacy, discrimination, disability accommodation, safety, police involvement, or enforcement timing is unclear.

Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

Thanks for letting us know. We have received your message about [issue type] and are routing it for review. Please send any relevant date/time details, photos, or messages through [approved channel]. We will follow up after the manager reviews the record.

We understand this is frustrating. We cannot confirm enforcement actions in this message, but we are logging the concern and checking it against the property's approved process.

If there is an immediate safety concern, please contact the appropriate emergency service first, then update the office through [approved channel] so the record is complete.

Do not say yet

  • “They violated the lease” before manager/source review.
  • “We will fine / evict / remove / tow / enter” without approved authority.
  • Private resident details, disability/accommodation assumptions, police conclusions, or owner-only notes.
  • Refunds, credits, concessions, or retaliation-style language.

AI summary prompt for manager review

You are helping a property-management team summarize a suspected lease or community-rule issue for internal manager review. Use only the verified intake fields below. Do not invent lease terms, legal notices, fines, enforcement decisions, eviction steps, entry rights, accommodation conclusions, resident identities, police outcomes, refunds, credits, or timing promises.

Intake card:
[paste completed card]

Return:
1. Neutral issue summary.
2. Missing facts or source documents.
3. Privacy/sensitivity risks.
4. Recommended internal owner and CRM label.
5. Resident-safe acknowledgement draft that does not promise enforcement.
6. STOP AUTOMATION items needing manager/legal/owner review.

Manager-review stop rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION before mentioning notices, fines, lease violations, eviction, towing, police action, entry rights, owner charges, deposit deductions, disability accommodation, discrimination/fair-housing issues, or legal deadlines.
  • NEEDS MANAGER REVIEW if evidence is incomplete, the report involves harassment/safety/accommodation concerns, or the policy source is unclear.
  • Use approved channels. Keep sensitive details inside the property-management system, not in casual SMS/email threads.
  • AI is draft-only. It may summarize the intake card and draft a neutral acknowledgement; a responsible human decides next steps.
