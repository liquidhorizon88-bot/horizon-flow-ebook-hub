Why tax-exemption answers need a source card

Tax-exemption questions are easy for AI tools to over-answer. Eligibility can depend on customer type, jurisdiction, product category, resale purpose, certificate validity, timing, checkout platform behavior, invoicing status, and refund rules. A stale help article can become an answer-engine promise that exposes the business to accounting, compliance, and customer-trust problems.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI publish tax-exemption, resale-certificate, nonprofit, government, tax-refund, tax-removal, or state-specific compliance claims until an owner/bookkeeper verifies the current source of truth and approves the exact public wording.

Policy name: [approved public/internal name]

Last verified date: [date]
Customer type eligible for review: [reseller, nonprofit, government, exempt buyer, other]
Jurisdictions covered: [states/countries/locations where this policy applies]
Products/services covered or excluded: [SKUs, service types, digital goods, shipping, fees]
Required documentation: [resale certificate, exemption certificate, tax ID, form, account record]
When documents must be provided: [before quote, before purchase, after purchase, renewal]
Checkout/platform handling: [manual invoice, tax engine, marketplace, subscription tool]
Refund/credit rules: [can/cannot refund tax, timing, documentation, owner/bookkeeper review]
Do-not-promise list: [automatic exemption, retroactive refund, all states, blanket resale, legal/tax advice]
Manual review triggers: [missing document, expired certificate, mixed order, out-of-state, marketplace, disputed tax]
Customer-safe summary: [approved 2-3 sentence answer]
Internal routing label: [tax-exemption-review / resale-certificate / owner-review]

Customer-safe reply snippets

Thanks for asking. Tax-exemption eligibility depends on the customer type, location, product/service, timing, and documentation. Please send the current exemption or resale certificate so our team can review it before we confirm any tax adjustment.
I do not want to guess about tax rules or refunds. If the request involves a missing certificate, expired document, state-specific exemption, marketplace order, subscription renewal, prior invoice, or refund/credit, it needs owner/bookkeeper review first.
Internal note: Tax-exemption/resale-certificate question. Verify jurisdiction, product/service scope, certificate validity, checkout platform behavior, invoice status, refund/credit rules, and owner-approved public wording before sending a final answer.

Review before publishing

  • Does the public wording match the current checkout/tax engine, invoicing SOP, marketplace rules, and owner/bookkeeper-approved policy?
  • Does the answer avoid legal/tax advice and avoid promising automatic exemption, retroactive refund, or state-wide eligibility?
  • Are stale support macros, checkout notes, old PDFs, and sales emails updated or labeled as outdated?
  • Is there a clear manual-review owner for missing documents, expired certificates, mixed orders, marketplace purchases, subscriptions, and refund/credit requests?
