No-Answer Follow-Up Sequence for Local Service Leads

Some leads ask for help, get a first reply, and then go quiet. Use this sequence to follow up without sounding pushy, inventing availability, or letting the CRM become a pile of “maybe” leads. It gives the team a clean cadence, scripts, labels, and stop rules.

When to use this sequence

  • A missed-call, web-form, DM, marketplace, or referral lead received a first response but has not replied.
  • The lead is still plausibly active and has not opted out, booked elsewhere, or been marked not-a-fit.
  • The business needs a consistent closeout path instead of random “just checking in” messages.
  • Human-review rule: stop automation for emergencies, safety issues, warranty/refund disputes, legal/tenant matters, financing, pricing exceptions, or any lead that has opted out.

Copy/paste no-answer follow-up tracker

Lead source: call / web form / text / Google Business Profile / Facebook / Yelp / Angi / Thumbtack / referral / other

Original request: [short non-private summary]

Verified facts: service requested, location/service area if verified, best contact method, consent status, promised callback window, owner

Last reply sent: [date/time/channel]

Next touch: touch 1 / touch 2 / touch 3 / final closeout

CRM label: needs callback / waiting on customer / needs photos / estimate pending / quote sent / no answer / closed no response / STOP AUTOMATION

Do not invent: appointment slots, crew ETA, price, diagnosis, warranty, discounts, financing, emergency coverage, or prior conversation history.

Four-touch follow-up cadence

  1. Same day: send one helpful callback or text with the single easiest next step.
  2. Next business day: restate the request and ask for one missing detail, such as photos, address/ZIP, access window, or decision-maker confirmation.
  3. Two to three business days later: offer a simple yes/no path: still need help, pause for now, or close the request.
  4. Final closeout: politely close the loop, record the reason, and stop automated follow-up unless the customer reopens the conversation.

Copy/paste scripts

Touch 1 — same day: Hi [First Name], this is [Business]. We saw your request about [service/request]. The fastest next step is [one detail needed]. You can reply here, or call [verified phone] if this is urgent.

Touch 2 — next business day: Hi [First Name], quick follow-up on [service/request]. Do you still need help with this? If yes, please send [one missing detail] and we can route it to [owner/team] for the next step.

Touch 3 — 2–3 business days later: Hi [First Name], should we keep this request open? Reply 1 if you still want help, 2 if you are all set, or send [one missing detail] and we will update the request.

Final closeout: We have not heard back, so we will close this request for now. If you still need help with [service/request], reply here or call [verified phone] and we can reopen it.
