Use this checklist before your site, ecommerce pages, Google Business Profile, helpdesk macros, chatbots, proposals, FAQs, PDFs, or AI-search snippets repeat shipping, delivery, pickup, lead-time, cancellation, or exception promises.

Why this matters

Shipping and delivery promises are operational facts, not marketing filler. AI search tools can turn an old FAQ, abandoned PDF, support macro, or stale marketplace listing into a confident answer about delivery time, pickup availability, local radius, free shipping thresholds, rush fees, or exception policies. This worksheet gives one owner-approved source card before those promises spread.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI write, summarize, syndicate, or answer questions about shipping, delivery, pickup, fulfillment, ETA, rush service, free-shipping thresholds, service radius, refunds for delays, or exception promises until a named owner verifies the current source, allowed wording, effective date, and rollback plan.

Safe public wording starters

Shipping/delivery summary: Current source: [policy URL or owner-approved source]. Applies to: [locations/products/services]. Typical timing: [range or "confirmed after review"]. Fees/exceptions: [exact limits]. Last reviewed by [owner] on [date].
Delay-safe customer update: We are checking the current shipping/delivery source before confirming timing. We will update you by [owner/date/time] with the verified option, fee, and next step. We will not promise a delivery or pickup window until it is confirmed.

Fast QA before AI or search engines quote it

  • Search the website, PDFs, checkout copy, GBP, helpdesk macros, proposals, directory profiles, and chatbots for old shipping or delivery wording.
  • Check whether timing is counted in calendar days, business days, after approval, after payment, after inventory confirmation, or after vendor confirmation.
  • Separate shipping, delivery, pickup, installation, dispatch, and service-arrival promises so AI does not merge them.
  • Mark weather, carrier, vendor, backorder, stockout, staffing, and holiday exceptions explicitly.
