Move-In Utility Setup Confirmation Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before a leasing assistant, resident-services inbox, portal workflow, or AI receptionist tells someone that utilities are ready, transferred, billed correctly, or cleared for move-in. It keeps the reply useful without inventing utility-company status, resident account setup, billing responsibility, start dates, access requirements, or lease/legal conclusions.

MOVE-IN-UTILITY-SETUP-CONFIRMATION-READY

When to use this

Use this source card when a resident or applicant asks whether power, water, gas, internet, trash, sewer, billing, or required utility transfer proof is complete before move-in, key pickup, inspection, portal approval, or lease start.

Human-review rule: utility setup is a source-record and resident-responsibility question. A property manager should verify lease terms, required utilities, provider instructions, account/start-date proof, common-area vs resident responsibility, billing status, and manager policy before confirming utility readiness or move-in clearance.

Resident-safe reply snippets

  • Proof not verified yet: “Thanks for checking. I do not want to promise utility clearance until the required account/start-date proof has been reviewed against your move-in requirements.”
  • Proof uploaded, review pending: “We can see that something was submitted, but utility setup must be reviewed before we confirm move-in clearance or key pickup.”
  • Provider delay: “If the utility provider gives a later start date or appointment window, the office needs to review how that affects move-in timing before we confirm the next step.”
  • Wrong utility/responsibility question: “Utility responsibility can vary by unit, lease, and service. We will verify the approved source record before giving account or billing instructions.”

STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION if AI is about to say utilities are ready from a screenshot, uploaded file, or resident statement without source-record review.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if lease terms, utility addendum, provider account, start date, billing responsibility, or move-in clearance is incomplete or ambiguous.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if the reply would promise key release, possession, access codes, habitability conclusions, fee waivers, billing credits, legal conclusions, or provider action.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if the question involves outage, heat/AC, gas/electric safety, accommodation, vulnerable resident, emergency access, or utility shutoff risk.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if private account numbers, payment details, ID, lease status, or third-party resident information would be exposed.
