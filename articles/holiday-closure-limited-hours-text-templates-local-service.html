Holiday Closure and Limited-Hours Text Templates for Local Service Businesses

Use these scripts when the office is closed, the schedule is limited, or the team is handling emergencies only. The goal is to set a truthful expectation, capture the next step, and avoid inventing availability, prices, or emergency coverage.

Copy the templates Get Local Lead Rescue

When to use this

  • A holiday, team training day, inventory day, storm cleanup day, or owner-approved closure changes your normal reply window.
  • The shop is open for emergencies only and routine quote requests need a next-business-day callback.
  • A customer submitted a web form, marketplace lead, DM, or voicemail while the dispatch desk was closed.
  • You need a CRM note so the next person can see what expectation was promised.

Copy/paste text templates

1. Closed today, next-business-day callback

Hi {first_name} — thanks for reaching out to {company}. Our office is closed today for {holiday_or_reason}, but we received your request about {service_need}. We will review it and contact you by {callback_window} on {next_business_day}. If anything changes before then, reply here with the best phone number and address.

2. Limited hours with honest callback window

Hi {first_name}, thanks for contacting {company}. We are working limited hours today, so replies may take longer than usual. Your request is in our queue, and the next expected update is {time_window}. If this is no longer needed, reply CANCEL so we do not keep following up.

3. Emergency-only coverage

Hi {first_name} — our team is on emergency-only coverage for {date_or_reason}. If this is urgent or safety-related, reply URGENT and include {required_details}. If it is routine service or a quote request, we will follow up on {next_business_day}. Please do not rely on this text for emergency medical, fire, gas, or life-safety issues; call the appropriate emergency service if needed.

4. Reopen follow-up

Hi {first_name}, we are back in the office and reviewing requests from {closure_period}. Do you still need help with {service_need}? If yes, reply with the best callback time today and any photos, access notes, or urgency details we should review first.

5. Voicemail callback after a closure

Hi {first_name}, this is {sender_name} from {company}. We received your voicemail while our office was closed for {holiday_or_reason}. I am checking whether you still need help with {service_need}. Reply YES with the best time to call, or reply STOP if you do not want another follow-up.

6. Marketplace lead limited-hours reply

Hi {first_name}, thanks for your {marketplace_name} request. Our office is on limited hours today, but we can still start the intake. Please send your service address, a short description of the issue, and photos if available. We will confirm next steps by {callback_window}.

7. CRM note for the handoff

Closure/limited-hours reply sent: {date_time}. Promise made: {callback_window_or_next_business_day}. Coverage stated: {closed_limited_or_emergency_only}. Customer need: {service_need}. Missing info requested: {photos_address_access_best_time}. Follow-up owner: {team_member}. Do not promise: price, same-day availability, waiver, diagnosis, or emergency response unless confirmed by owner/dispatcher.

AI drafting prompt

You are helping a local-service business draft a closure or limited-hours reply. Use only the facts below. Do not invent availability, emergency coverage, prices, fee waivers, arrival windows, diagnosis, or discount promises. If a fact is missing, write [CONFIRM].
Business: {company}
Closure or limited-hours reason: {reason}
Date/time coverage: {coverage_window}
Next verified callback window: {callback_window}
Lead source: {phone_web_form_dm_marketplace}
Customer need: {service_need}
Emergency policy: {owner_approved_policy}
Opt-out wording required: {yes_no}
Draft one short customer-facing message and one CRM note.

Human-review stop rules

  • Do not say “24/7,” “same-day,” “emergency available,” or “we can waive the fee” unless the owner or dispatcher confirmed it for this exact date.
  • Do not diagnose from photos, voicemails, or AI summaries. Ask for information and route to a human.
  • Do not message people who opted out, asked not to be contacted, or came through a platform that forbids off-platform follow-up.
  • For SMS, use the business's approved consent and opt-out language.
