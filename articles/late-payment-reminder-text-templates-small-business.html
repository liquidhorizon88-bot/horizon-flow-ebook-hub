Late payment reminder text templates that stay firm without sounding hostile.

Small businesses need to collect overdue invoices, but the wrong reminder can turn a late payment into a trust problem. Use these templates as a safer starting point, then review the account facts before sending.

Before sending

  • Confirm the invoice number, amount, due date, and payment status.
  • Do not threaten fees, collections, service interruption, or legal action unless policy and human approval allow it.
  • Keep payment links, bank details, and personal data out of AI tools unless approved.
  • Escalate disputes, hardship, chargebacks, or repeated nonpayment to the owner/bookkeeper.

Copy/paste scripts

Template 1: friendly due-date nudge

Hi [First Name] — quick reminder from [Business Name]: invoice [Invoice #] for [Amount] is due on [Date].
If you already paid, thank you and please ignore this. If not, here is the payment link: [Secure Link].

Best for: reminders before or on the due date. The tone should assume good intent and avoid embarrassment.

Template 2: 3–7 days overdue

Hi [First Name] — checking in on invoice [Invoice #] for [Amount], which was due [Due Date].
Can you confirm whether payment is scheduled, or if there is a question we should route to the right person?

Review rule: ask for a status update before escalating. Many late payments are admin misses, missing PO numbers, or unanswered invoice questions.

Template 3: missing detail or disputed invoice

Hi [First Name] — thanks for flagging the question on invoice [Invoice #].
We are reviewing [Issue/Detail] and will follow up by [Time/Date] with the next step. Please do not resend payment until we confirm the correction.

This protects the relationship when the invoice might be wrong. Do not let AI invent policy, discounts, refunds, or revised totals.

Template 4: second overdue reminder

Hi [First Name] — following up again on invoice [Invoice #] for [Amount].
If payment has been sent, please reply with the date or confirmation. If not, can you let us know the expected payment date?

Best for: a polite second touch that asks for a concrete next step without adding pressure that the business cannot enforce.

Template 5: owner/bookkeeper handoff note

Account: [Customer/Company]
Invoice: [Invoice #] / [Amount] / due [Due Date]
Last touch: [Date + channel]
Customer status: [No reply / promised date / dispute / hardship / wrong contact]
Recommended next step: [Owner review / resend invoice / call AP / pause reminders / escalate per policy]

Use this internally before any stronger follow-up. The goal is to keep the next action factual and approved.

AI prompt to draft a payment reminder safely

You are helping draft a polite payment reminder for a small business.
Use only the facts below. Do not invent fees, penalties, policies, promises, discounts, legal claims, or payment status.

Facts:
- Customer/company: [Name]
- Invoice number: [Invoice #]
- Amount: [Amount]
- Due date: [Due Date]
- Last contact: [Last Contact]
- Allowed payment link wording: [Approved Wording]
- Escalation rule: [Owner/Bookkeeper Rule]

Draft one short text message and one email version. Include a friendly opener, the invoice facts, one clear next step, and a note to ignore the message if payment already cleared. Add a human-review checklist before sending.

Human review checklist: verify facts in the accounting system; confirm the customer has consented to SMS where needed; remove sensitive account details; and get owner/bookkeeper approval before mentioning fees, service pauses, collections, or legal escalation.

Next step

Turn reminders into a repeatable admin workflow

If payment reminders are a recurring bottleneck, document the trigger, timing, owner review rule, approved message variants, and stop conditions. That turns ad hoc chasing into a safer workflow.
