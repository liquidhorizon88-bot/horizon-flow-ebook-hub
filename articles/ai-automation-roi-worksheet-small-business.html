AI Automation ROI Worksheet for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before paying for another AI tool, prompt library, chatbot, consultant workflow, or subscription. The goal is not to prove AI is magical. The goal is to decide whether one specific workflow is worth keeping after human review, setup time, and risk controls are counted.

Best fit: owners, office managers, consultants, agency operators, and local-service teams evaluating AI for inbox triage, quote follow-up, review responses, SOP drafts, content refreshes, or weekly reporting.

The quick rule: ROI needs review cost, not just time saved

An AI automation can look profitable if the business only counts draft time saved. It can become expensive when someone has to fix invented details, rewrite tone, check policies, correct CRM notes, or undo customer confusion.

Use the worksheet below to compare the old workflow with the AI-assisted workflow. If the review burden is unclear, run a 7- or 30-day pilot before adopting the tool.

Step 2: calculate rough weekly ROI

AI automation ROI worksheet Workflow: [one repeated task] Owner/reviewer: [role] Weekly volume: [number of times per week] Current minutes per item: [minutes] AI-assisted draft minutes per item: [minutes] Human review/correction minutes per item: [minutes] Hourly cost of reviewer: [$] Tool/subscription/consultant cost per month: [$] Setup/training hours for first month: [hours] Risk controls required: [privacy, policy, legal, safety, customer trust, brand voice] Stop conditions: [invented claims, privacy confusion, customer complaints, staff non-use, no measurable time saved] Weekly current time = volume × current minutes Weekly AI-assisted time = volume × (draft minutes + review minutes) Weekly time saved = current time - AI-assisted time Weekly value saved = weekly hours saved × hourly cost Monthly gross value = weekly value saved × 4.33 First-month net value = monthly gross value - tool cost - setup/training cost Ongoing monthly net value = monthly gross value - tool cost Decision: keep / fix and retest / cancel
Human-review rule: do not count a workflow as profitable if it relies on unreviewed customer-facing claims, hidden data sharing, fake urgency, invented pricing, or unsupported legal/financial/medical advice.

Step 3: score the decision

Keep

Net value is positive, staff uses it, review is manageable, and no trust/data risk appears.

Keep with limits

It works in one narrow workflow but should not touch sensitive, legal, safety, pricing, or policy situations.

Fix and retest

Prompts, handoff notes, owners, or data boundaries were not clear enough to measure fairly.

Cancel

The tool saves little time, creates extra review work, confuses staff, or increases customer/data risk.

Common ROI mistakes

  • Counting draft speed only: time saved disappears if every output needs heavy edits.
  • Ignoring setup: prompt writing, staff training, QA, and rollback notes are real costs.
  • Automating risky promises: pricing, legal/policy, diagnosis, safety, and availability claims need human ownership.
  • Measuring too many workflows: pick one workflow, then expand only after it passes.
  • Forgetting the renewal date: a tool with no owner can become a quiet monthly leak.
