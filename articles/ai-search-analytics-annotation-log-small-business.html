Why this annotation log matters

AI answers lag behind your site

ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and other assistants may quote older pages or third-party snippets. Log page changes and proof URLs so you can audit what changed before blaming one tool.

Analytics can mislead

A single spike can come from a newsletter, referral, bot, seasonality, or tracking change. This worksheet forces the owner to label confidence instead of claiming “AI search worked” without evidence.

Small teams forget context

If edits, links, citations, and lead quality notes live in different tools, the weekly review becomes guesswork. The annotation log creates one plain-English change history.

Minimum fields before you claim a win

  • Exact public URL: the landing page, source page, Gist, directory confirmation, or educational post that changed.
  • Change type: content refresh, citation fix, schema update, source freshness update, UTM distribution, lead magnet, or internal link.
  • Baseline window: write down the date range before the change. If you do not have baseline data, label it “unknown.”
  • Lead quality label: do not count every form fill as a win. Separate qualified prospects from spam, vendors, wrong-service requests, and existing-customer support.
  • Confidence note: say whether the change probably helped, might have helped, or is only a hypothesis.
