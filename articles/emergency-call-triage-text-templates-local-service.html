Emergency Call Triage Text Templates for Local Service Businesses

Urgent leads need fast acknowledgment, but a rushed text can create safety, pricing, or dispatch promises the business cannot keep. Use these templates to separate true emergencies from urgent-but-safe requests while keeping a human in control.

Best fit: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, restoration, locksmith, pest control, appliance repair, roofing, garage door, property maintenance, and other local service teams that receive after-hours or urgent requests by phone, web form, text, chat, or Google Business Profile message.

Safety-first rules before any triage text

  • If there is fire, gas smell, electrical hazard, medical danger, flood risk, active break-in, or another immediate safety issue, direct the customer to emergency services or the utility/provider first.
  • Do not invent arrival times, technician availability, diagnoses, permit advice, prices, warranties, insurance coverage, or safety clearance.
  • Confirm whether the business actually offers emergency service for that job type, service area, and time window before promising dispatch.
  • Respect SMS consent, opt-outs, and official-channel rules. If the customer opted out of text, use the approved channel only.
  • Route ambiguous or high-risk messages to an owner, manager, dispatcher, or licensed professional before sending.

1. Immediate safety redirect

Hi [First Name], thanks for reaching out. If there is an immediate safety risk — fire, gas smell, electrical danger, medical emergency, or active flooding — please contact emergency services or the appropriate utility/provider first. Once the area is safe, reply with [basic details/photos if appropriate] and we can review the service request.

2. Emergency service availability check

Hi [First Name], I can help route this. Please reply with: 1) the service address or nearest cross streets, 2) what is happening, 3) whether anyone is in immediate danger, and 4) whether the issue can safely wait until [next business window]. A dispatcher/manager will review before we confirm availability.

3. True emergency dispatch handoff

Thanks, [First Name]. I am sending this to our dispatcher/manager for emergency review now. We will confirm whether we can dispatch, the expected next step, and any approved call-out/diagnostic details before you are booked. Please do not attempt any unsafe repair while waiting.

4. Urgent but non-emergency scheduling option

Thanks for the details. Based on what you shared, this sounds urgent but may not require emergency dispatch. We can offer [next available appointment window] or have a manager review if something changes. If the situation becomes unsafe, contact emergency services/utility support first.

5. Outside service area or unavailable emergency route

Hi [First Name], I do not want to overpromise. We are not able to confirm emergency service for [location/job type/time] by text right now. If there is a safety issue, please contact emergency services or the appropriate utility/provider. If it can wait, reply with details and we can review the next available standard appointment.

CRM labels to add

  • emergency-safety-first — customer was directed to emergency/utility support before service follow-up.
  • emergency-dispatch-review — dispatcher/manager must confirm availability before booking.
  • urgent-non-emergency — fast appointment needed, but no emergency promise was made.
  • out-of-area-emergency — urgent request cannot be safely accepted due to area, time, or job-type limits.
  • professional-review-required — licensed/senior review needed before diagnosis, quote, or instructions.

AI prompt to draft safely

You are helping a local service business draft an emergency triage text.

Business: [company]
Customer first name: [name]
Service type: [HVAC/plumbing/electrical/restoration/etc.]
Issue described by customer: [facts only]
Service area: [confirmed / unknown / outside area]
Emergency availability: [confirmed / manager review needed / not available]
Approved next step: [safety redirect / ask triage questions / dispatch review / standard appointment / decline emergency route]
Channel: [SMS/email/chat]

Draft one calm, concise message.
Rules:
- Do not invent diagnosis, arrival time, price, warranty, safety clearance, technician availability, or emergency promise.
- If safety risk is possible, tell the customer to contact emergency services or the appropriate utility/provider first.
- Include only the approved next step.
- Add a human-review checklist before sending.

Human-review checklist

  • The message does not replace emergency services, utility guidance, or licensed professional judgment.
  • Emergency availability, service area, and job type were verified before any dispatch wording.
  • No diagnosis, price, arrival time, warranty, or safety advice was invented.
  • The channel is permitted under customer consent and opt-out status.
  • A dispatcher, manager, owner, or licensed professional reviews high-risk cases before sending.
