AI Search About Page Template for Small Businesses

Your About page is not just a biography. For AI search and human buyers, it should clearly state who the business serves, what it does, where it operates, why it can be trusted, and what claims need proof.

See the full SEO After AI playbook

Use this before asking AI to rewrite your About page

Do not start by asking an AI tool to “make our About page better.” Start by collecting approved facts. Then use AI only to organize the page and flag missing proof.

AI prompt for an AI-search-ready About page

You are helping draft a small-business About page.
Use only the facts below. Do not invent history, awards, locations, team members, guarantees, customer counts, or certifications.

Facts:
[PASTE APPROVED FACT WORKSHEET]

Draft an About page with:
1. A clear first paragraph naming the business, category, audience, and location/service area.
2. A short section explaining what problems we solve.
3. A proof section using only approved proof from the worksheet.
4. A plain-language process section.
5. A trust and review note that avoids risky guarantees.
6. A final call to action.

After the draft, list:
- Missing facts that would make the page more credible.
- Any claims that need owner/legal review.
- Any vague sentences that should be replaced with proof.

Human review checklist

  • Entity clarity: the page says the business name, service category, location/service area, and audience without hiding it in slogans.
  • Proof match: every award, certification, team size, review claim, or result is backed by a real source the owner can verify.
  • Boundary clarity: the page avoids claims such as “best,” “guaranteed,” “certified,” or “licensed” unless they are true and approved.
  • AI search fit: the page gives answer engines quotable facts instead of generic founder-story filler.
  • Conversion fit: visitors can tell whether to call, book, request a quote, or read a related service page.

Stop rules

Stop before publishing if the AI draft adds fake service areas, team members, guarantees, awards, customer counts, or before/after claims. Stop if a regulated claim needs legal, licensing, insurance, medical, financial, or HR review. Stop if the page sounds impressive but cannot be verified by the business owner.
