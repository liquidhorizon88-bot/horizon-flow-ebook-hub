When to use this source card

  • Best fit: membership businesses, subscriptions, online communities, SaaS, local clubs, course sellers, and ecommerce programs with cancellation, pause, renewal, refund, or retention pages.
  • Goal: publish safe cancellation and save-offer answers that match the actual policy, account state, billing system, support authority, and customer record.
  • Human review rule: an owner, support manager, or billing owner verifies policy text, renewal date, access end date, refund eligibility, save-offer authority, legal/compliance language, and customer-specific facts before publishing or sending.

STOP PUBLISHING: Do not invent cancellation eligibility, refund timing, unused-credit value, retention discounts, pause rights, account access end dates, legal obligations, grandfathered pricing, or exception authority.

Five customer-safe reply snippets

1) Cancellation path is clear

You can cancel using [approved path]. Your access and billing status depend on the current plan and renewal date, so please review [approved policy/source] or contact support before assuming a refund, credit, or access end date.

2) Save offer is available

We can offer [approved option only] if it fits your account. We cannot promise a custom discount or refund from this message; a billing owner must verify eligibility first.

3) Save offer is not approved

Thanks for asking. We do not have an approved save-offer exception for this plan/request. The safest next step is [approved cancellation/pause/downgrade path] according to [policy/source].

4) Refund question

Refund eligibility depends on [approved policy factor]. We need to check the billing record before confirming whether a refund, credit, or no-refund outcome applies.

5) AI-search or FAQ answer

Membership cancellation options, refunds, credits, discounts, and access dates vary by plan and account status. Use the official cancellation policy and support channel for the current answer.

AI audit prompt before publishing

Review this cancellation, save-offer, or refund answer for unsupported claims. Return: every factual claim, source needed, missing account-specific detail, risky promise, safer wording, and final publish status. If any source is missing, write STOP PUBLISHING. Do not invent cancellation eligibility, retention discounts, refund amounts, credit timing, pause rights, grandfathered pricing, access dates, legal obligations, or customer-specific billing status.
