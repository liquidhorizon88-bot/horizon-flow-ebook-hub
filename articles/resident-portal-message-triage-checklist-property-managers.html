Resident Portal Message Triage Checklist for Property Managers

Resident portal inboxes often mix maintenance, package, payment, lease, noise, access, renewal, and complaint messages in one queue. Use this checklist before a staff member or AI assistant replies so every message gets the right lane, official channel, reviewer, and next-check time.

Use the worksheet Get Local Lead Rescue

When to use this

Use this when the resident portal receives a message that could be maintenance, rent/accounting, lease compliance, package-room, amenity, access, renewal, complaint, or emergency-related. The goal is not to answer everything instantly; it is to avoid unsupported promises while moving the resident to the right owner.

Resident-safe reply snippets

1. Mixed message, routing needed

Hi [Name], thanks for sending this through the portal. We logged it as [ticket/thread]. Because it may involve [lane/sensitivity], we are routing it to [team/manager] before giving a specific answer. We will update you by [time/date].

2. Need source details first

Hi [Name], to route this accurately, please add [photo / screenshot / account detail / date and time / location / work order number]. We do not want to guess from an incomplete portal message.

3. Emergency or safety redirect

Hi [Name], this portal thread has been logged, but if this is an active emergency or safety issue, please use [approved emergency channel/phone number] immediately. We are also routing the portal note to [manager/team] for review.

4. Sensitive policy/accounting/lease question

Hi [Name], I can confirm we received the question about [topic]. A manager must review the account, lease, policy, or source record before anyone promises a fee, balance, access, legal, or exception outcome.

AI review prompt

You are helping a property manager triage a resident portal message. Use only the verified source notes below. Do not invent lease terms, rent balances, payment-plan approval, fee waivers, legal notice language, emergency status, vendor diagnosis, entry rights, package-room logs, camera footage, refund/credit eligibility, or completion timelines.

Return: (1) best triage lane, (2) verified facts, (3) unknowns, (4) resident-safe acknowledgement, (5) owner/team to route to, (6) next-check due time, and (7) STOP AUTOMATION triggers.

Source notes:
[Paste portal message, system records, policy excerpts, work-order/vendor notes, and manager decision if any]

Human review rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION: do not send AI-written replies for safety, habitability, fair-housing/accommodation, legal notices, balances, fees, refunds, credits, lease violations, entry/access, police/security, surveillance, or vendor diagnosis without a human owner.
  • Do not invent: the resident's account status, lease rights, manager approval, vendor ETA, package-log findings, camera availability, policy exceptions, or emergency priority.
  • Keep the official channel: document the portal thread, source record, assigned owner, next-check due time, and final resident update in the property-management system.
