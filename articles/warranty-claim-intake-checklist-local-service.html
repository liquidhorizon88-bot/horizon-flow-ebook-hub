Warranty requests can become expensive fast when the first reply promises coverage before the original job, dates, parts, labor limits, workmanship terms, customer photos, and manager approval are checked. Use this worksheet to acknowledge the customer quickly while keeping coverage decisions evidence-based.

Use this when

A customer says the prior job failed, but the office still needs proof and policy context.

This is built for HVAC, plumbing, roofing, appliance repair, cleaning, pest control, landscaping, restoration, and other local-service teams where warranty, callback, workmanship, parts, and manufacturer obligations must be checked before a customer-facing promise.

Good fit

  • Warranty or callback requests after a completed job.
  • Customers reporting repeat symptoms, failed parts, leaks, noise, damage, or recurring issues.
  • Jobs where original scope, warranty window, labor coverage, manufacturer part coverage, photos, or manager approval affects the next step.

Do not use it to

  • Approve warranty coverage, free labor, refunds, replacements, or diagnosis without documented evidence.
  • Argue policy details before a manager reviews the job record.
  • Let AI infer the cause of failure from a short customer message.

STOP AUTOMATION

Do not let AI or staff promise coverage from memory.

  • The original job ID, date, scope, invoice, or warranty terms are missing.
  • The customer is asking for a refund, free service, replacement, damage coverage, or legal/policy commitment.
  • The issue could involve safety, active leak, electrical risk, property damage, mold, pest, contamination, or urgent hazard.
  • The proposed reply promises cause, coverage, arrival window, parts availability, labor fee, manufacturer approval, or refund without evidence.
  • The prior tech notes, signed scope, photos, or policy conflict with the customer's description.

Customer-safe replies

Acknowledge quickly while coverage is reviewed.

Thanks for letting us know. I’m opening a warranty/callback review and checking the original job notes, date, scope, and any coverage terms before we promise the next step.

I do not want to guess on coverage from this message alone. Please send a photo or short video of the issue if you can, and I’ll attach it to the job record for manager review.

We have your request. The next step is to verify the original invoice/scope and decide whether this is warranty, callback, manufacturer part review, or a new service request.
