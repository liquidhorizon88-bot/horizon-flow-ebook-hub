Why this deserves a worksheet

Access-device requests look routine, but they can touch identity verification, building security, resident privacy, fees, accessibility needs, after-hours access, and lease or house-rule exceptions. A rushed AI draft or front-desk reply can accidentally promise a lock change, fee waiver, same-day key, gate-code reset, or access exception that the team has not approved.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI approve access, waive fees, change locks/codes, promise emergency entry, disclose another resident's information, verify identity, override amenity rules, or decide accommodation/fair-housing-sensitive exceptions. A manager must verify the source records and approved policy first.

Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

Hi [Name], thanks for letting us know. We have logged your access-device request for [property/unit]. Before we confirm a replacement, fee, timing, code change, or lock change, we need to verify the account record and approved property policy.
Thanks for the update. I am routing this to [manager/team] because access devices and codes affect building security and resident records. We will follow up through [official channel] after the request is reviewed.
Internal handoff: Access-device request from [resident/unit]. Device/code affected: [details]. Verified identity/status: [source]. Sensitive flags: [safety / accommodation / fee / security / lock-change / after-hours]. Do not issue, waive, reset, or promise access until reviewed.

Fast QA before any answer goes out

  • Does the message avoid promising a replacement time, access-code reset, lock change, fee waiver, or after-hours exception?
  • Was the resident identity and authorized-occupant status verified through the approved system?
  • Could the request involve safety, domestic violence, disability/accommodation, harassment, or another fair-housing/privacy-sensitive issue?
  • Is the reply going through the official property-management channel rather than an unsupported side conversation?
  • Is there a named manager, maintenance, or security-system owner and a next-check time?
