AI Search Chatbot Answer Source Checklist for Small Businesses

Website chatbots, AI receptionists, and answer engines are only useful when their answers are tied to approved facts. Use this checklist to create source-backed answer cards, escalation rules, and an AI audit prompt before public tools invent service details, pricing, availability, guarantees, or policy promises.

Use this before

  • Launching or updating a website chatbot.
  • Feeding FAQ/support content into an AI assistant.
  • Publishing AI-search answer blocks for service, policy, or pricing pages.
  • Letting staff reuse AI-drafted customer replies.

Why this matters

Chatbot answers need proof, not vibes

Many small businesses add chat widgets, AI assistants, or FAQ answer blocks before they have a clean source of truth. The result is risky: the bot may make up hours, discounts, service-area coverage, warranty terms, refund rules, emergency availability, or next steps that the business cannot honor.

This worksheet gives owners, marketers, and operators a simple way to approve answer cards before they are reused by website chat, AI-search pages, or internal AI drafting workflows.

AI audit prompt

Check chatbot answers before publishing

You are auditing website chatbot / AI-search answers for a small business.
Use only the source cards below. Do not invent pricing, hours, service area, warranty, refund, eligibility, emergency response, credentials, discounts, or availability.
For each draft answer, return:
1. approved as-is / needs edit / do not publish,
2. unsupported claims,
3. missing source URL,
4. escalation or human-review trigger,
5. safer revised answer using only approved facts.

Human review

STOP PUBLISHING guardrails

  • Do not publish chatbot answers that create new prices, guarantees, discounts, service-area claims, credentials, certifications, or availability promises.
  • Do not let a bot give emergency, safety, diagnosis, legal, tax, medical, or financial advice. Route to a human or appropriate emergency/professional source.
  • Do not summarize old policy pages unless the owner has reviewed the source date.
  • Do not expose private customer examples, internal notes, or confidential vendor/pricing terms in public answers.
