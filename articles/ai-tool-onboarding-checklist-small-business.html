AI Tool Onboarding Checklist for Small Businesses

Buying or approving an AI tool is not the finish line. Use this worksheet before the first team rollout so the business knows who owns the workflow, what data is allowed, where prompts live, who reviews outputs, and when to stop the pilot.

1. AI tool onboarding card

Fill this out before inviting staff, connecting systems, or using the tool for customer-facing work.

AI tool/vendor: [name]
Approved use case: [one workflow]
Workflow owner: [person]
Reviewer: [person]
Users/seats invited: [names]
Allowed inputs: [public website copy, anonymized notes, internal SOPs, other]
Forbidden inputs: [customer PII, employee data, passwords, financial/legal/health/security data, other]
Customer-facing outputs allowed? [no / draft only after review / yes with approval]
Prompt/template storage location: [Drive/SOP/wiki link]
Pilot start and review date: [dates]
Success metric: [time saved, faster response, fewer errors, owner satisfaction]
Stop condition: [privacy concern, review burden too high, weak quality, no owner, low usage]

2. Setup checklist before rollout

  • Create or confirm the business-owned account, not a personal throwaway account.
  • Turn on available admin controls, two-factor authentication, workspace ownership, and billing owner visibility.
  • Store approved prompts, examples, and review rules in the company prompt registry or SOP folder.
  • Document which integrations are enabled: Gmail, CRM, website chat, calendar, Drive, Slack, Zapier/Make, or none.
  • Disable or avoid integrations that would expose customer, employee, billing, legal, health, or security-sensitive data before owner review.

3. Team rollout message

Subject: New AI tool pilot: [tool name]

Team — we are testing [tool] for [workflow] from [start date] to [review date].

Allowed use: [specific allowed tasks]
Do not paste: customer private data, employee data, passwords, payment details, legal/medical/financial/security-sensitive information, or anything not approved by [owner].

Save useful prompts here: [link]
Send any customer-facing draft to [reviewer] before use.
If the tool gives a price, policy, promise, diagnosis, deadline, legal claim, or security claim, treat it as unverified and ask [owner].

4. AI prompt for an onboarding plan

You are helping a small business onboard one AI tool safely.

Known facts:
- Tool: [name]
- Workflow: [workflow]
- Users: [roles]
- Allowed data: [data]
- Forbidden data: [data]
- Reviewer: [person]
- Pilot length: [30 days or other]
- Success metric: [metric]

Create a rollout checklist with:
1. account/admin setup,
2. allowed and forbidden uses,
3. prompt/template storage rules,
4. reviewer handoff steps,
5. weekly pilot check-in questions,
6. keep/fix/pause/cancel decision criteria.

Do not invent vendor policies, privacy guarantees, legal requirements, security promises, pricing, deadlines, or integrations. Mark unknowns for human review.

5. Human-review stop rules

  • Stop if no named owner is responsible for the AI workflow.
  • Stop if users want to paste customer, employee, legal, financial, health, security, or proprietary data and the data boundary has not been approved.
  • Stop if AI outputs will be sent to customers without a reviewer.
  • Stop if the tool connects to live inboxes, CRM records, billing systems, website chat, or scheduling before the owner approves the integration.
  • Stop if nobody can explain how success or failure will be measured at the pilot review.
