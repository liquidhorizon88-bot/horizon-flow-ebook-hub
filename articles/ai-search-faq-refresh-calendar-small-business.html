AI Search FAQ Refresh Calendar for Small Businesses

FAQ pages can become stale faster than service pages because policies, availability, pricing factors, tools, and customer questions change. Use this refresh calendar to keep answers useful for buyers and safe for AI search summaries.

Copy the calendar Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Your FAQ page has answers older than one quarter.
  • AI search tools may quote outdated policies or response windows.
  • Support, sales, or front-desk questions changed this month.
  • You want an owner-reviewed source-of-truth log before rewriting answers.

Why this matters

Old FAQ answers can become accidental promises

A stale FAQ can quietly claim a service, price, turnaround time, response window, product capability, refund rule, or service area that is no longer true. In classic search that is confusing; in AI search it can be worse because answer engines may summarize the old language without showing the whole context.

The fix is not to rewrite every answer every week. The fix is a small calendar, a stale-answer log, and a rule that AI only drafts from approved facts.

AI drafting prompt

Refresh answers from approved notes only

You are helping a small business refresh website FAQ answers for buyer clarity and AI search accuracy.
Use only the approved notes below. Do not invent pricing, availability, service areas, response times, legal rights, refund promises, warranties, guarantees, credentials, insurance, team names, or customer results.
For each FAQ, return:
1. keep/edit/remove recommendation,
2. revised answer in plain language,
3. source fact used,
4. missing fact or owner-review flag,
5. internal link suggestion,
6. schema/FAQ markup caution if visible text and markup may drift.
Approved notes:
[paste source-of-truth notes and stale-answer log]

Implementation

Run a 30-minute FAQ refresh sprint

  1. Export or copy the current FAQ questions into the calendar.
  2. Mark any answer tied to pricing, policy, scheduling, locations, credentials, warranties, or support as high-risk.
  3. Collect source-of-truth notes before asking AI to draft anything.
  4. Refresh the highest-risk five answers first; remove unsupported promises instead of polishing them.
  5. Set the next review date and keep the stale-answer log for the next pass.

Human-review rule: if an FAQ answer could change what a buyer expects, a responsible owner or manager reviews it before publishing.
