Unassigned Lead Escalation Checklist for Local Service Businesses

When a lead has no owner, no due time, or an overdue callback, it should not wait for another automation. Use this checklist to identify unassigned leads, assign the right human, protect contact consent, and decide when to pause AI-drafted follow-up until a manager reviews the situation.

Use this when a lead is not clearly owned

  • A missed call, form, voicemail, text, DM, quote request, or marketplace lead landed in a shared inbox but nobody owns the next touch.
  • The promised callback window has passed and the CRM only shows “follow up later.”
  • Dispatch, estimating, office staff, technicians, or the owner are each assuming someone else will reply.
  • A lead has sensitive details, price exceptions, warranty/insurance questions, safety concerns, or consent uncertainty that makes automation risky.

Escalation ladder

  1. Confirm the lead is real and current. Check the original source before replying. Do not invent customer intent, diagnosis, budget, urgency, or availability.
  2. Assign a temporary owner within 10 minutes. If the ideal owner is unavailable, assign a backup owner who can send a safe acknowledgement.
  3. Separate routine from exception leads. Routine leads can get a reviewed first reply; exception leads go to a manager or owner first.
  4. Set a due time. Every escalated row needs a next review time and a person responsible for that review.
  5. Send a truthful holding message if needed. Acknowledge the request, say who is reviewing it, and avoid price/diagnosis/timing promises unless verified.
  6. Close the loop in the CRM. Add the owner, due time, latest customer promise, and the reason automation was paused or allowed.

STOP AUTOMATION rules

STOP AUTOMATION and route to a human before any AI-drafted reply is sent when the lead includes:

  • Emergency, safety, active leak, electrical hazard, lockout, medical/health risk, or same-day service pressure.
  • Warranty, refund, fee dispute, legal, insurance, permit, HOA, tenant/owner, collections, or contract terms.
  • Angry customer, bad-review risk, repeated missed callback, promised timing that is already overdue, or manager approval needed.
  • Opt-out, wrong number, no SMS consent, platform-only contact rule, or unknown contact permission.

AI can summarize facts and propose options, but it must not invent availability, pricing, diagnosis, approval, warranty coverage, urgency, consent, or customer history.

Escalation-ready message templates

Safe acknowledgement while assigning owner

Hi {first_name}, thanks for reaching out to {business_name}. I’m routing your request about {verified_need} to {owner_role} now so a person can review the next step. If this is urgent or unsafe, please call {phone} for immediate help.

Overdue promised callback

Hi {first_name}, I’m sorry we missed the promised callback window. {owner_name} is reviewing this now and will follow up by {new_specific_time}. We’ll keep the note tied to your original request so you do not have to start over.

Manager review needed before answer

Hi {first_name}, we want to make sure we answer this accurately. {manager_or_owner} is reviewing {verified_issue} before we confirm the next step. We’ll update you by {due_time}.

AI assist prompt for escalation review

You are helping a local-service business triage an unassigned or overdue lead. Use only the facts below. Do not invent diagnosis, pricing, availability, consent, approval, warranty coverage, urgency, customer intent, or customer history.

Lead facts:
- Source: {source}
- Customer message: {customer_message}
- Verified need: {verified_need}
- Current owner: {current_owner_or_unassigned}
- Promised callback/window: {promised_window}
- Consent / allowed channel: {consent_status}
- Known exception risks: {exception_risks}
- Business rules: {business_rules}

Return:
1. Is this safe for an AI-drafted reply, or STOP AUTOMATION?
2. Best escalation owner role
3. One safe next action
4. Specific due-time recommendation
5. Holding message for human review
6. CRM note in 35 words or fewer
7. Missing facts to collect before any promise
