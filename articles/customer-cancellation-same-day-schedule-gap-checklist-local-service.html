Use when

  • A customer cancels, delays, or asks to move a same-day appointment.
  • A dispatcher wants to fill the newly open slot with a waitlist lead or quote follow-up.
  • The team is considering a cancellation fee, deposit policy, trip charge, or no-show label.
  • An AI draft is about to promise availability, waive a fee, hold a slot, or blame the customer.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not invent policy, availability, or fee decisions

Do not let AI, autoresponders, or rushed dispatch notes claim that a fee is waived, a new appointment is guaranteed, a technician can arrive sooner, a customer is at fault, a deposit is forfeited, or a slot is being held unless the schedule, policy, job file, and owner/dispatcher approval prove it. Escalate before making promises about refunds, cancellation fees, emergency priority, waitlist order, same-day arrivals, or technician availability.

Customer-safe reply snippets

1) Acknowledge cancellation without blame

Hi {first_name}, thanks for letting us know about the change. We have marked the appointment as {cancelled_or_reschedule_requested} and are checking the schedule before confirming any new timing or policy details.

2) Reschedule request with no confirmed slot yet

Hi {first_name}, we can look for another appointment window, but we do not want to guess on availability. {dispatcher_or_owner} is checking the calendar and will update you by {date/time} with confirmed options.

3) Same-day slot offered to a waitlist lead

Hi {first_name}, a same-day opening may be available for {job_type}. Please reply by {deadline} if you want us to check whether the slot still works. We will confirm before assigning a technician or changing your appointment.

4) Fee or deposit needs owner review

Hi {first_name}, we are reviewing the job notes and policy before answering the fee/deposit question. We will keep the update tied to the approved record rather than guessing or giving an unsupported answer.
