AI Search Compatibility Matrix Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this copy/paste worksheet before AI search tools, chatbots, comparison tables, marketplace summaries, or support macros answer “will this work with my model, plan, device, service package, or accessory?”

Why this matters

Compatibility mistakes create high-intent but high-risk support issues: a customer buys the wrong accessory, assumes a plan works with an older platform, or relies on an AI answer that merges two different model years. This checklist gives small businesses a source-of-truth matrix that keeps public pages and AI-assisted replies anchored to verified fit rules.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI claim that an item, service package, accessory, software version, file format, operating system, vehicle/model year, property type, size, plan tier, or integration is compatible unless the current owner-approved matrix verifies the exact match and its limits.

Customer-safe reply template

Thanks for checking. Based on our current compatibility matrix, {product_or_service} is verified for {supported_match}. It is not verified for {unsupported_match}, and {conditional_match} requires {condition_or_owner_review}.

Before buying or scheduling, please review {canonical_source_url} or send us your exact model/version/use case so a team member can confirm the fit. We do not want AI or support drafts to guess on compatibility.

AI audit prompt

You are reviewing an AI-generated compatibility answer for a small business. Use only the verified compatibility matrix source card. Do not infer support from similar products or old versions.

Return:
1. Safe to publish? yes/no
2. Unsupported compatibility claims to remove
3. Model/version/plan/accessory details that require owner review
4. A corrected customer-safe answer using only approved wording

Seven compatibility checks to run first

  1. Exact model/version: avoid broad “works with all” language unless every supported model is listed.
  2. Plan or tier limits: features that work in a premium tier may not work in the starter tier.
  3. Accessory dependency: adapters, mounts, cables, file formats, or add-ons should be named explicitly.
  4. Region/date limits: compatibility can change by country, supplier, fulfillment channel, firmware, or effective date.
  5. Use-case exclusions: commercial, outdoor, high-volume, tenant, regulated, or safety-sensitive use may need separate approval.
  6. Support boundary: clarify whether compatibility means “can connect,” “fully supported,” “limited support,” or “not recommended.”
  7. Fallback path: give customers a safe way to ask for fit confirmation before purchase or implementation.
