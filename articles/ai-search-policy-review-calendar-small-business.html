AI Search Policy Review Calendar for Small Businesses

AI answer engines can summarize refund, warranty, privacy, support, guarantee, delivery, accessibility, and service-area policies from pages that nobody has reviewed in months. Use this calendar to assign owners, source URLs, stale-risk levels, and next review dates before old policy promises become customer-facing AI answers.

When to use this calendar

  • A policy page mentions refunds, guarantees, support windows, privacy, data collection, accessibility help, service areas, response times, pricing, fees, or warranties.
  • A promotion, service, or operational rule changed and several public pages need the same reviewed wording.
  • An AI tool is helping draft FAQs, schema, comparison copy, or sales answers that might pull from old policies.
  • A chamber, SCORE mentor, agency, or consultant needs a lightweight worksheet to help a small business prevent stale claims.

30-minute review workflow

  1. List the public promises. Start with policy, FAQ, product, support, privacy, guarantee, and booking pages.
  2. Choose the source of truth. Link each promise to the approved internal or public source that controls the wording.
  3. Assign the owner. Put one reviewer on every row so stale claims have a clear escalation path.
  4. Check affected surfaces. Search the site, listings, marketplace pages, schema, email templates, and AI prompts for the same claim.
  5. Update or remove risky copy. If the source is missing, soften the claim or keep it unpublished until the owner approves it.
  6. Schedule the next review. Policy promises that affect money, privacy, support, legal rights, safety, or availability should not be set-and-forget.

STOP PUBLISHING guardrails

  • STOP PUBLISHING if a policy claim has no source URL, owner, or review date.
  • Do not invent refund rights, warranty coverage, support timelines, legal terms, privacy practices, compliance status, security controls, accessibility compliance, service areas, fees, or guarantees.
  • Do not copy private customer, employee, vendor, contract, or payment details into an AI tool while reviewing policies.
  • Do not let a directory, marketplace, Google Business Profile, schema field, FAQ, or email template contradict the current policy page.
