Use this when

A customer approved the estimate, but operations still needs clean scheduling facts.

This is built for HVAC, plumbing, roofing, landscaping, cleaning, pest control, restoration, and similar local-service teams where the estimator, office, dispatcher, scheduler, and crew may not be the same person.

Good fit

  • Approved quotes waiting for scheduling or parts confirmation.
  • Customers who paid a deposit but have not received a next-step message.
  • Jobs where access, HOA/permit, material, crew skill, or owner approval affects scheduling.

Do not use it to

  • Change price, scope, warranty, or payment terms without documented approval.
  • Promise a crew, arrival window, material ETA, permit approval, or start date from memory.
  • Hide missing facts behind an AI-generated confident answer.

STOP AUTOMATION

Do not let AI or staff guess scheduling facts.

  • The approved scope, deposit status, job address, or customer contact details are missing.
  • Parts, material, permit, HOA, access, tenant, or crew-skill requirements are unclear.
  • The estimator note conflicts with the signed quote or payment record.
  • The proposed text promises price, ETA, warranty, arrival window, crew availability, or material status without evidence.
  • The customer asked to change scope, payment terms, or schedule priority after approval.

Scheduling texts

Send a clear next-step message while facts are being verified.

Thanks for approving the estimate. I’m moving this from estimating to scheduling now. Before we confirm a time, I’m checking the approved scope, access notes, and any parts/material requirements so we do not give you the wrong window.

Thanks — your quote approval is in our scheduling queue. We’ll confirm the next available scheduling option after the dispatcher reviews crew availability and job requirements.

I do not want to guess on the schedule from here. I’m sending the approved quote details to our scheduling owner for review and will follow up with the safest next step.

AI prompt

Summarize the handoff without inventing schedule promises.

You are helping a local-service company move an approved estimate from estimator to scheduler.
Use only the quote, payment, estimator, customer, and scheduling notes below.
Return:
1. One-sentence approved-scope summary.
2. Missing facts the scheduler must verify.
3. STOP AUTOMATION triggers.
4. A customer-safe scheduling update that does not promise price, material status, start date, arrival window, warranty, permit/HOA status, or crew availability unless the evidence states it.
5. The CRM label and owner/due-time recommendation.
Do not invent price changes, customer approvals, payment status, crew availability, access permission, material ETA, permit approval, warranty coverage, or prior contact history.
