AI Search Terms and Conditions Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before publishing or refreshing terms, policies, payment rules, service limits, warranties, and cancellation language so buyers and AI answer engines have a reviewed source to quote.

Why terms pages matter for AI search

AI tools increasingly summarize business policies from scattered website pages. If your terms, payment rules, cancellation details, guarantee boundaries, and service limitations are vague or contradictory, AI answers can overstate what you offer. This checklist gives a small business a safer source-of-truth page to review before traffic lands there.

Human-review rule: do not use this as legal advice. Have the business owner and qualified counsel review legal, refund, warranty, cancellation, privacy, and payment language before publication.

Copy/paste terms-page worksheet

  1. Terms owner: who approves customer-facing terms and who updates them when policies change?
  2. Effective date: what date is the current version effective, and where are archived versions stored?
  3. Who the terms apply to: customers, website visitors, subscribers, clients, vendors, tenants, or marketplace leads?
  4. Payment rules: deposits, due dates, accepted payment methods, late fees, financing handoffs, and receipt expectations.
  5. Cancellation and reschedule rules: notice windows, fees, weather/safety exceptions, no-show handling, and owner approval requirements.
  6. Service limitations: what is explicitly not included, not guaranteed, not diagnosed remotely, or subject to site conditions?
  7. Warranty, refund, and guarantee boundaries: link to the source policy and avoid invented promises.
  8. Geographic or eligibility limits: service area, licensing boundaries, age/location requirements, or excluded job types.
  9. Privacy and data notes: what customer data is collected, where support requests go, and when sensitive details should not be shared through forms or chat.
  10. Contact and escalation path: the official email, phone, portal, or mailing address for questions about the terms.
  11. Plain-English summary: a short non-legal summary of the most common buyer questions, reviewed against the full terms.
  12. AI-search stop rules: phrases an AI summary must not invent, such as “guaranteed,” “free,” “same day,” “licensed,” “insured,” or “refund available” unless explicitly supported.

Before publishing

  • Compare the terms page against support, guarantee, pricing, privacy, booking, and checkout pages.
  • Remove vague claims that AI tools could summarize as broader promises.
  • Add internal links from pricing, booking, FAQ, support, and product pages.
  • Ask a human reviewer to search for unsupported words: guarantee, always, never, free, insured, licensed, certified, refund, same-day, emergency, unlimited.
  • Keep screenshots or source notes for any policy-sensitive wording.
