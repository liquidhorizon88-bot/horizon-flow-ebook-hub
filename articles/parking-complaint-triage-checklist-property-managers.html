Parking Complaint Triage Checklist for Property Managers

Use this free worksheet before replying to a resident, owner, vendor, guest, or neighbor about a parking-space dispute, tow request, disabled-space concern, blocked driveway, guest-parking issue, or repeated parking-rule complaint.

PARKING-COMPLAINT-TRIAGE-READY

Parking messages are deceptively risky. A rushed AI-assisted reply can promise towing, cite the wrong lease rule, expose another resident's information, mishandle disability-access concerns, or escalate a neighbor dispute without enough source proof.

This checklist keeps the first response calm, documented, and manager-reviewed before anyone promises enforcement, towing, credits, fines, lease violations, or legal timelines.

Copy/paste parking complaint triage card

Resident / reporter: [name, unit, contact]
Property / parking area: [property, lot, garage, street, reserved space]
Issue type: [blocked space / unauthorized vehicle / guest parking / accessible space / tow request / repeat complaint / safety hazard / other]
Source evidence: [photo, timestamp, license plate, lease clause, parking addendum, vendor/security note, prior warning, none yet]
Potential sensitivity: [disabled parking / accommodation / fair-housing concern / neighbor conflict / police/safety concern / private information]
Resident-safe acknowledgement: [short neutral reply]
Manager / owner review needed: [yes/no + reviewer]
CRM label: PARKING-TRIAGE / ACCESSIBILITY-REVIEW / TOW-REQUEST / OWNER-REVIEW / NEEDS-EVIDENCE
Next safe action: [ask for evidence / route to manager / confirm policy source / schedule patrol/vendor check / close with note]
Do-not-say until reviewed: [tow promise, fine amount, lease violation, eviction/legal threat, disclosure of another resident's status, guaranteed resolution time]

Resident-safe first replies

1. Need evidence before action

Thanks for flagging this. To route it correctly, please send the date/time, location, and any photo or vehicle details you can share. We will review the available records and respond through the property-management system.

2. Blocked or inaccessible space

Thanks for letting us know. If this is creating an immediate safety or access issue, please follow the property's urgent-contact procedure. We are documenting the report now and will route it for manager review before confirming any enforcement step.

3. Repeat concern acknowledgement

We understand this has been frustrating. We are adding this report to the existing parking concern record and will review the policy, prior notes, and evidence before sending the next update.

Stop rules before automation

  • STOP AUTOMATION before mentioning towing, fines, lease violations, police action, disabled-space enforcement, discrimination/fair-housing issues, legal notices, eviction, fee waivers, credits, refunds, or guaranteed resolution times.
  • Do not invent lease language, parking rules, vehicle ownership, prior warnings, accommodation status, police/security actions, or owner/HOA decisions.
  • Keep private resident details inside the approved property-management system. Do not expose another tenant's identity, disability/accessibility status, vehicle details, or complaint history unnecessarily.
  • Use the first reply to gather facts and route the issue; use the reviewed follow-up to state the actual policy-backed action.
