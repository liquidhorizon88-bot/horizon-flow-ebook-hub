Customer Refund Exception Approval Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this checklist before an AI draft, support rep, or busy owner promises a refund, store credit, replacement, fee waiver, exception, or chargeback response that the business cannot support.

CUSTOMER-REFUND-EXCEPTION-READY

Use this when

  • A customer asks for a refund, credit, replacement, or fee waiver outside the normal policy.
  • A support draft needs policy/source proof before it is sent.
  • The owner wants consistent exceptions without sounding robotic or defensive.
  • AI can summarize the case, but a human must approve the money, policy, and final wording.

Customer-safe reply templates

Need more facts

Thanks for flagging this. I’m going to review the order details, policy, and service notes before we give you an answer. We do not want to guess on a refund decision. I’ll follow up by [time/date] with the next step.

Approved exception

We reviewed the order details and approved [refund/credit/replacement] as an exception for this case. You should see [what happens next] by [timeframe]. This does not change the standard policy, but we believe it is the right resolution here.

Not approved, still helpful

We reviewed the request against the order details and our policy. We are not able to approve a refund for this case because [specific factual reason]. We can still help with [available option] if you would like to proceed.

AI review prompt

You are helping draft a refund-exception response for a small business. Use only the facts in the approval card below. Do not invent policy, legal rights, payment timing, chargeback outcomes, warranty coverage, tax treatment, or customer history. First list missing facts and risks. Then draft two customer-safe reply options: one if the exception is approved and one if it is not approved. Mark anything that needs owner, bookkeeper, payment-processor, or legal review.

Human review stop rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION if the requested refund amount, policy, fee, warranty, contract, or chargeback status is unclear.
  • Do not promise timing for bank/card refunds unless the payment processor or bookkeeper confirms it.
  • Do not claim legal rights, tax treatment, processor rules, or warranty coverage from memory.
  • Do not invent prior conversations, customer intent, product defects, delivery status, or manager approval.
  • Use one source-of-truth note so future replies do not contradict the approved decision.
