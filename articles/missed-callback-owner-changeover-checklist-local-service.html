Use this when

A missed call, voicemail, web form, inbound text, marketplace lead, or quote callback is still unresolved at lunch, shift change, dispatcher handoff, estimator handoff, after-hours coverage, or end of day.

Many local-service teams do not lose leads because nobody cares. They lose them because the callback owner changes and the next person cannot tell what was promised, what was already tried, or what must be reviewed before replying.

The safe pattern is to treat callback ownership like a task handoff, not a memory test. Capture the lead's latest confirmed facts, the next promised action, the owner, the due time, and the stop rules before drafting a reply.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not send an AI-drafted callback update if pricing, availability, diagnosis, warranty, safety, financing, refund, or appointment promises are unconfirmed. Route those items to the owner, dispatcher, estimator, or manager first.

Safe callback handoff summary prompt

CALLBACK-OWNER-CHANGEOVER-READY

Create a callback handoff summary for a local service lead using only the notes below.

Rules:
- Do not invent contact history, prices, appointment availability, diagnosis, warranty status, discounts, financing, safety advice, or promises.
- Separate confirmed facts from items that need owner/manager review.
- Create a concise next-owner task card with due time, latest customer need, attempts already made, and recommended next action.
- If a customer reply draft is included, keep it short and say only what is confirmed.
- Add a "NEEDS OWNER REVIEW" section for any pricing, schedule, diagnostic, safety, warranty, refund, or policy item.
