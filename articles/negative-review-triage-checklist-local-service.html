Negative Review Triage Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Use this when a review, complaint, or disputed job needs a calm, source-backed response instead of a rushed AI draft that invents warranty coverage, diagnosis, refunds, or blame.

Start with the free missed-lead checklist See Local Lead Rescue System

1. Review triage card

Copy/paste fields:

  • Review URL/platform:
  • Customer/job name or anonymous handle:
  • Job date, invoice, estimate, technician, property, or service type:
  • Claim in the review: missed arrival, workmanship, price, communication, warranty, refund, safety, property damage, or other:
  • Internal proof links: dispatch notes, photos, invoice, signed estimate, text thread, call transcript, warranty policy, inspection notes:
  • Owner/reviewer:
  • Due time for reply:
  • CRM label: negative_review_triage / warranty_claim / refund_request / safety_hold / needs_owner_review:

2. Public/private response split

Public reply skeleton: “Thank you for flagging this. We are reviewing the job details and have routed this to [owner/manager role]. For privacy, please contact us at [official channel] with your job reference so we can match the record and follow up.”

Private follow-up skeleton: “I found your review and I’m checking the job record now. Before I promise an outcome, I’m confirming the invoice/estimate, technician note, warranty terms, and any photos/messages tied to the visit. I’ll update you by [time].”

Do not include publicly: customer address, invoice totals, diagnosis details, blame, legal threats, medical/safety claims, private payment details, or unsupported “we always/never” promises.

3. AI draft prompt with guardrails

You are helping draft a response to a negative local-service review.
Use only the facts below. Do not invent job history, warranty coverage, diagnosis, pricing, safety status, refund eligibility, or customer intent.
Facts I can verify: [paste review triage card]
Draft two options:
1) a short public reply that acknowledges, routes to the official contact path, and protects privacy;
2) a private follow-up that says what we are checking and when the customer will hear back.
If facts are missing, list questions instead of writing a promise.
End with NEEDS OWNER REVIEW.

STOP AUTOMATION

  • Do not let AI promise refunds, free service, replacement parts, warranty coverage, appointment windows, chargeback outcomes, or legal positions.
  • Do not accuse the customer, technician, property manager, vendor, or platform unless the owner has proof and approves the wording.
  • Do not publish private job details in a public review response.
  • Escalate safety, injury, property-damage, legal, insurance, chargeback, or discrimination claims to a human owner before drafting.

4. Weekly review questions

  • Which review themes repeat: late arrival, unclear scope, price surprise, warranty confusion, poor handoff, no follow-up?
  • Which CRM fields were missing when the review arrived?
  • Which public-page claims or service promises might have set the wrong expectation?
  • What response template belongs in the team’s Local Lead Rescue swipe file?
