AI Search Trust Center Checklist for Small Businesses

A small trust center gives buyers, journalists, partners, and AI answer engines one place to verify what your business promises, how to contact you, and what claims should not be invented.

Use this when: your site mentions guarantees, security, privacy, cancellations, service areas, refunds, accessibility, licenses, awards, or customer proof across multiple pages. The goal is not to create legal boilerplate. The goal is to collect owner-approved facts so humans and AI tools do not summarize stale or exaggerated promises.

Human-review rule: do not let AI invent policies, certifications, insurance, legal terms, refund rights, security controls, guaranteed results, response times, or customer outcomes. A responsible owner, manager, or qualified advisor should verify every trust-center claim before publishing.

1. Trust facts to gather

  • Official business name, locations, phone, booking/contact paths, and support windows.
  • Refund, cancellation, warranty, appointment, payment, privacy, accessibility, and SMS/email consent policies.
  • Licenses, certifications, insurance statements, association memberships, awards, and review/testimonial proof with dates and source links.
  • Security or data-handling notes buyers actually need: where forms go, who reviews submissions, and what not to send through insecure channels.

2. Pages to cross-check

  • Footer, contact page, booking page, pricing page, FAQ, service pages, product pages, and checkout/listing copy.
  • Google Business Profile, social bios, marketplace listings, chamber/member pages, directory listings, and public PDFs.
  • Old blog posts that still mention offers, guarantees, awards, pricing, or response windows.

AI drafting prompt

You are helping a small business organize a trust-center page for buyers and AI search tools.
Use only the facts below. Do not invent policies, guarantees, legal terms, licenses, certifications, security controls, review counts, response times, service areas, or customer outcomes.

Facts:
[PASTE VERIFIED FACTS]

Draft a plain-language trust-center outline with sections for contact facts, policies, proof, safety/privacy notes, and claim limits. Add a human-review checklist and mark any missing facts as [VERIFY] instead of guessing.

Stop-rule checklist before publishing

  • Stop if a policy link contradicts a footer, FAQ, product page, marketplace listing, or sales email.
  • Stop if a claim uses words like guaranteed, certified, insured, best, fastest, secure, private, compliant, or risk-free without owner-approved evidence.
  • Stop if a testimonial, case study, award, logo, or partner mention lacks permission or a source URL.
  • Stop if legal, medical, financial, accessibility, privacy, warranty, or refund language needs qualified review.

Why this helps AI search

AI answer engines tend to assemble facts from multiple pages. A concise trust center can reduce contradictions by giving them a single reviewed source for policies, proof, contact paths, and claim limits. Pair this with the footer/NAP checklist, contact-page checklist, and review-proof checklist.
