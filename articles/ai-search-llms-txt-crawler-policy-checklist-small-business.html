AI Search llms.txt and Crawler Policy Checklist for Small Businesses

Small businesses are starting to see advice about llms.txt, AI crawler controls, and answer-engine optimization. The risky part is not the file itself; it is publishing machine-readable guidance before the team knows which facts are approved, which pages are stale, and which claims need owner review.

Use this before publishing an AI crawler or llms.txt file

  • Best fit: consultants, agencies, and small-business owners adding AI-search guidance to a website.
  • Goal: point AI systems toward reviewed, useful pages without accidentally amplifying outdated prices, service areas, guarantees, policies, testimonials, or unsupported claims.
  • Human review rule: every listed URL, summary, policy, and product claim must map back to a current source-of-truth page.
  • STOP PUBLISHING: Do not invent AI crawler permissions, licensing rights, service availability, pricing, customer results, legal/privacy claims, or guarantee language.

Copy/paste llms.txt readiness worksheet

Website/domain: [domain]

Owner/reviewer: [person approving AI-search guidance]

Allowed AI-summary pages: [URLs that are current, public, and useful]

Do-not-summarize pages: [old offers, draft policies, outdated staff/service pages, private portals, thin pages]

Source-of-truth pages: [pricing/policies/services/contact/about/FAQ URLs]

Claims that need proof: [ratings, awards, guarantees, emergency service, turnaround times, certifications]

Review cadence: [monthly/quarterly/after major service or policy change]

STOP PUBLISHING: Do not publish llms.txt or AI crawler instructions until an owner confirms every linked page is current and supported.

Starter llms.txt structure to adapt

# llms.txt draft — review before publishing
# Purpose: point AI tools to reviewed public pages only.
# Owner: [name/team]
# Last reviewed: [date]

## Primary source pages
- About / entity facts: [URL]
- Services / offers: [URL]
- Pricing or quote policy: [URL]
- FAQ / buyer questions: [URL]
- Contact / service area: [URL]
- Policies / guarantees / refunds: [URL]

## Use boundaries
- Use only the public URLs above as source material.
- Do not infer pricing, availability, service area, warranties, credentials, or customer results not stated on the source page.
- If a page conflicts with another source, mark NEEDS OWNER REVIEW instead of choosing a winner.

## Excluded topics
- Draft pages, private customer data, internal SOPs, outdated promotions, expired offers, unsupported guarantees, and unverified testimonials.

Review: this is not a legal or technical standard guarantee. Treat it as a source map and owner-review checklist; coordinate with your web/SEO team before changing crawl rules.
