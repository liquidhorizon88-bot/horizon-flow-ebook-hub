Service Address Correction and Wrong-Job-Site Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Use this source card when a caller gives a different service address than the CRM record, the billing address is not the job site, a property has multiple units or gates, or a technician may be headed to the wrong location. The goal is to keep speed-to-lead high without sending a crew to the wrong place or inventing arrival, fee, warranty, or access promises.

When to use this

  • A lead, tenant, customer, or property manager gives a corrected service address after the initial intake.
  • The CRM has a billing address, mailing address, unit number, gate code, subdivision, or site name that may not match the job site.
  • A dispatcher, answering service, AI assistant, or front desk needs safe wording before confirming travel time, fees, warranty status, or arrival windows.
  • You need a CRM note that separates address correction from duplicate lead merge, wrong-number cleanup, cancellation, or quote follow-up.

STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION before promising arrival time, dispatch priority, same-day service, emergency status, or fee waivers after an address changes.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if a technician is already en route, the job may now be outside the service area, or the corrected address changes travel/diagnostic fees.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if the corrected address appears tied to a different customer, property, tenant, unit, owner, or duplicate CRM record.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if warranty, quote, permit, safety, lockbox, gate-code, tenant-access, or property-management authorization is tied to the old address.
  • STOP AUTOMATION before admitting fault, blaming the customer, charging a missed-trip fee, or promising compensation for a wrong-site visit.

Customer-safe acknowledgement snippets

Address correction received

“Thanks — we received the corrected service address. We’re updating the job record and will verify dispatch details before confirming timing or fees.”

Technician may already be assigned

“Thanks for the update. Because this changes the service location, a person needs to review the dispatch record before we confirm the next arrival window.”

Possible duplicate or property-management record

“Thanks — we need to compare this with the existing customer/property record so we do not merge the wrong account or send updates to the wrong person.”

Service-area or access review needed

“We have the corrected address. Before we confirm scheduling, we need to verify service area, access details, and any travel or diagnostic-fee rules.”

CRM labels and next actions

  • address_correction: corrected address captured; dispatch map and job record need verification.
  • wrong_job_site_risk: technician, access, travel, or fee promise may be affected.
  • duplicate_record_review: compare phone/email/address before merging or sending customer updates.
  • service_area_review: owner/dispatcher checks travel zone, trip fee, and availability before promising service.
  • access_review: unit, gate, lockbox, tenant permission, or property-manager authorization is incomplete.
