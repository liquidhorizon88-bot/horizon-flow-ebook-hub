Amenity Reservation and Rule Question Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before AI, an after-hours assistant, leasing coordinator, resident-services team, or virtual assistant answers a resident question about reserving an amenity, changing an event window, using a common area, paying a refundable deposit, following guest rules, or requesting an exception. It keeps messages tied to approved community rules and routes fair-housing, safety, fee, access, and exception requests to a human.

When to use this checklist

Use it when a resident asks to reserve a clubhouse, pool, gym, elevator, loading dock, parking area, package room, grill area, rooftop, community room, or another shared amenity, or when they ask about cancellations, guest counts, deposits, cleaning fees, access fobs, quiet hours, alcohol/food rules, or event exceptions.

  • Separate approved amenity rules from guesses about availability, deposits, refunds, cleaning obligations, insurance, guest limits, parking, noise, or access rights.
  • Capture the source record before an AI draft turns a resident preference, staff memory, old flyer, or calendar note into a resident-facing promise.
  • Route accessibility/accommodation requests, safety issues, alcohol/event liability, fee disputes, damage deposits, security, and rule exceptions to manager/legal review.
  • Create a neutral resident update with the next owner, next check time, approved channel, and STOP AUTOMATION guardrails.

STOP AUTOMATION if the message involves accommodation/accessibility, discrimination/fair-housing concern, safety, alcohol/event liability, security, deposit/refund/fee dispute, damage responsibility, legal notice, lease consequences, access rights, or an unverified amenity rule.

Resident-safe reply templates

1. Request received, calendar/source check pending

Hi [Name], thanks for your note about [amenity]. We are checking the approved amenity calendar and community rules so we do not guess about availability, fees, guest limits, access, or cleanup requirements. We will send the next verified update by [time/date] or sooner if the manager confirms the details.

2. Reservation details verified

Hi [Name], the current approved record shows [amenity] is [available/reserved/blocked] for [date/window]. The verified next step is [reservation form/payment/deposit/access pickup/manager approval]. Please review [approved rules link or attachment] before the reservation is confirmed.

3. Exception or sensitive request

Hi [Name], because your request involves [exception/sensitive item], we are routing it to [manager/team] for human review instead of sending an automated answer. We will follow up through [approved channel] after the relevant rule, record, or approval path is checked.

Human-review stop rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION before promising availability, waiving fees, refunding deposits, approving exceptions, granting access, changing quiet-hour/event rules, interpreting leases, or assigning damage responsibility.
  • NEEDS MANAGER / LEGAL REVIEW if the message mentions fair housing, disability accommodation, discrimination, safety hazard, security incident, alcohol/event liability, injury, property damage, police, insurance, or repeated disputes.
  • Do not paste private resident, guest, access-code, payment, fob, or security details into public AI tools.
  • Keep resident updates tied to approved calendars, community rules, fee schedules, lease addenda, and manager records; include the next update owner/time when available.
