Guardrail first

Do not hide repeated AI corrections inside “editing time”

AI can save time, but only if the owner can see where drafts repeatedly fail. A correction log helps a small business decide whether an AI workflow is ready for a team, needs a better prompt card, or should stay paused.

  1. Log the correction, not the blame: capture the workflow, output, issue type, reviewer, and fix.
  2. Separate facts from style: wrong facts and unsupported claims are higher risk than ordinary tone edits.
  3. Update the source: if AI lacked the right policy, price, service area, or customer data, fix the source before the prompt.
  4. Use stop rules: pause workflows that repeatedly invent facts, expose private data, or create customer-facing risk.

Issue categories

  • Wrong fact: price, date, location, availability, policy, service, name, or claim is inaccurate.
  • Unsupported claim: AI added proof, guarantee, certification, performance result, or testimonial that is not documented.
  • Missing context: AI skipped a customer constraint, job detail, inventory count, attachment, or owner note.
  • Privacy risk: draft includes private customer details, sensitive access notes, internal codes, or unnecessary personal data.
  • Tone mismatch: draft is too pushy, defensive, vague, robotic, casual, or off-brand.
  • Action risk: AI recommended sending, buying, refunding, dispatching, ordering, or promising something without approval.

Prompt to turn corrections into a better prompt card

You are helping a small business improve one AI workflow. Review this correction log for {workflow_name}. Identify repeated error patterns, separate high-risk fact/privacy/action issues from tone edits, and suggest changes to the prompt card. Do not invent policies, prices, service areas, results, or customer facts. Return: 1) top 5 recurring corrections, 2) source-of-truth fields needed, 3) revised review checklist, 4) stop rules, 5) a keep/fix/pause/retire recommendation with reasons.

  • Which workflow should stay in owner-only testing before another employee uses it?
