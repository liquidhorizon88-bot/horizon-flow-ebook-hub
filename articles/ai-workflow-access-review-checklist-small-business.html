AI Workflow Access Review Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet when an AI tool, prompt library, shared inbox assistant, call-summary tool, or CRM add-on starts spreading across the team. The goal is simple: every AI workflow should have a named owner, approved users, approved data, and a human-review rule before customer-facing output leaves the business.

Best fit: owners, office managers, agencies, consultants, and operators who are adding AI tools without a full IT team.

The access rule: no owner, no shared AI workflow

Many small businesses test AI with one login, one shared prompt, and one enthusiastic employee. That is fine for a pilot. It becomes risky when old contractors still have access, staff paste private customer details, or nobody knows who approves outputs.

Human-review rule: Do not let AI send, publish, summarize, delete, price, diagnose, approve, deny, or promise anything on behalf of the business unless a responsible human has reviewed the source facts and final output.

Step 2: review the risky access points

Shared logins

Replace shared passwords with named seats where possible. If that is not possible, document who knows the login and rotate it after role changes.

Customer data

Write the exact data that may be used. Keep payment details, login credentials, medical/safety/legal details, employee records, and sensitive customer notes out of generic AI tools.

Integrations

Check CRM, inbox, calendar, call recording, form, or file-storage connections. Turn off integrations nobody owns.

Offboarding

Remove former employees, contractors, agencies, and test accounts. Confirm the workflow still works after access is removed.

AI prompt for the review meeting

Act as an operations assistant helping a small business review access to one AI workflow. Do not invent tool usage, security status, compliance status, customer permissions, savings, or ROI. Ask for missing facts instead. Workflow: [describe workflow] Tool/account: [tool and plan] Users: [users with access] Connected systems: [systems] Allowed data: [approved data] Forbidden data: [forbidden data] Human reviewer: [reviewer] Recent issues: [issues] Return: 1. access risks to review, 2. users or integrations to confirm, 3. data-boundary gaps, 4. offboarding checks, 5. a keep/reduce/remove recommendation that clearly labels assumptions.

Quick stop rules

  • Stop if nobody owns the workflow.
  • Stop if a former employee, contractor, or agency still has access without a current reason.
  • Stop if the tool can read customer inboxes, call recordings, CRM notes, calendars, or files and no one has reviewed the scope.
  • Stop if customer-facing drafts skip human review.
  • Stop if staff are pasting credentials, payment data, private customer records, or sensitive employee information.
