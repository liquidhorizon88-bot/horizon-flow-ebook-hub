Rental Application Fee and Holding Deposit Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before an AI draft, leasing assistant, or resident-services inbox answers questions about rental application fees, screening fees, holding deposits, admin fees, refunds, transferability, or approval timing.

APPLICATION-FEE-HOLDING-DEPOSIT-CHECKLIST-READY

Best fit: property managers, leasing coordinators, and resident-services teams who need a fast but careful way to answer applicant fee and holding-deposit questions without inventing policy, refund rights, approval promises, or screening outcomes.

Use with: Local Lead Rescue System for follow-up labels, owner review, and safe AI-assisted response drafts.

1. Capture the applicant request before drafting

  • Applicant/contact name: [name]
  • Property/unit/listing: [property/unit]
  • Question type: [application fee / screening fee / admin fee / holding deposit / reservation fee / refund / transfer / approval timing]
  • Amount mentioned by applicant: [$amount]
  • Payment status and source: [not paid / paid / ledger pending / receipt ID]
  • Source record to check: [application portal / lease criteria / posted listing / company policy / state/local rule / accounting ledger]

2. STOP AUTOMATION before these claims

  • Do not invent refundability. Verify the written policy and applicable local requirements before saying a fee or deposit is refundable, non-refundable, partially refundable, or transferable.
  • Do not promise unit availability. A holding deposit or application status may not mean the unit is held unless the approved policy and ledger prove it.
  • Do not promise approval timing. Use only verified screening workflow status and avoid predicting final decisions.
  • Do not alter screening criteria. Keep fair-housing-sensitive, source-based, and consistent criteria language; route exceptions to a manager.
  • Do not create accounting commitments. Fee waivers, credits, charge reversals, or deposit transfers require manager/accounting approval.

3. Applicant-safe acknowledgement snippets

Fee or deposit status check

Hi [Name] — thanks for reaching out. I’m checking the application record and the posted fee/deposit policy for [property/unit] before I answer. I’ll confirm what our records show and what the next step is after that review.

Use when: the applicant asks whether a payment was received, applied, refundable, or connected to a specific unit.

Refund or transfer question

Hi [Name] — I don’t want to guess on the refund or transfer rule. I’m reviewing the written policy and the payment record first, then I’ll send the confirmed next step or route it to the manager/accounting team if approval is needed.

Use when: the request involves a refund, cancellation, changed unit, denied application, or payment dispute.

Application timing question

Hi [Name] — your application status needs to be checked against the screening workflow before we can give a confirmed update. I’ll review the record and respond with the verified status or any missing items we can safely request.

Use when: the applicant asks whether they are approved, how long screening will take, or whether a fee/holding payment changes their place in line.

5. AI drafting prompt

Draft a short applicant-safe reply using only these verified facts:
- Applicant question: [paste]
- Property/unit: [paste]
- Fee/deposit type and amount: [paste]
- Verified payment status: [paste]
- Written policy excerpt/source: [paste]
- What is NOT verified yet: [paste]
- Required manager/accounting review: [yes/no]
Rules: do not invent refundability, unit availability, screening outcome, timing, waiver, transfer, fee credit, or legal policy. If the answer depends on policy or accounting review, say we are checking the record and will follow up.
