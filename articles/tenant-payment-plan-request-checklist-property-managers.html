Why this deserves a worksheet

Payment-plan requests are emotionally sensitive and easy for AI or busy staff to mishandle. A helpful draft can accidentally promise a late-fee waiver, legal pause, accounting correction, hardship approval, or timeline that the property manager cannot support. The safe move is to acknowledge the request, capture the facts, and route the decision through approved policy.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI approve a payment plan, waive fees, change balances, pause legal notices, promise collections will stop, interpret the lease, disclose private owner/accounting details, or create a fair-housing/accommodation response. A manager must verify the source records and approved policy first.

Copy/paste payment-plan request intake

Resident / property / unit: [name, property, unit]
Request received through: [portal / email / SMS / phone / in person]
Exact resident request: [paste or summarize without adding promises]
Verified current balance: [source + amount + as-of date]
Due date / late-fee / notice status: [verified source only]
Prior payment arrangements or promises: [none / list source]
Hardship/accommodation language mentioned? [yes/no + manager review]
Owner/accounting/legal/collection sensitivity: [yes/no + why]
Approved policy or escalation path checked: [source]
Safe first reply sent: [yes/no + text]
Manager decision owner / due time: [name + time]
CRM label: [payment-plan-request / hardship-review / fee-waiver-request / legal-review / accounting-review / owner-review]
STOP AUTOMATION until: [manager/legal/accounting approval + source record]

Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

Hi [Name], thanks for letting us know. We have logged your payment-plan request for [property/unit] and will review it against the account record and approved property policy before giving a specific answer. We do not want to guess or promise a change that has not been reviewed.
Thanks for the update. I am routing this to [manager/team] for review because it may involve balance, timing, fee, notice, or policy decisions. We will follow up through [official channel] after the account record and approved policy are checked.
Internal handoff: Payment-plan request from [resident/unit]. Verified balance/source: [source]. Request summary: [summary]. Sensitive flags: [hardship/accommodation/legal/accounting/fee/notice]. Decision needed by: [date/time]. Do not approve, waive, pause, or promise outcomes until reviewed.

AI review prompt for managers

You are helping a property-management team summarize a tenant payment-plan request for internal manager review. Use only the verified fields below. Do not invent lease terms, payment status, fee waivers, legal notices, hardship/accommodation decisions, collection status, owner approval, accounting adjustments, promises to pause action, or fair-housing/legal advice.

Return: 1) missing facts, 2) sensitive flags, 3) safe resident acknowledgement, 4) manager/accounting/legal questions, 5) CRM label, and 6) STOP AUTOMATION triggers before any payment-plan answer.

[Paste intake fields]

Fast QA before any answer goes out

  • Does the message avoid approving a plan, waiving fees, changing due dates, or promising legal/collection outcomes?
  • Does the balance, due date, late-fee status, and notice status come from an approved system of record?
  • Did the resident mention hardship, disability, accommodation, domestic violence, protected status, attorney involvement, or fair-housing-sensitive facts?
  • Is the reply going through the official property-management channel rather than an unsupported side conversation?
  • Is there a named manager/accounting/legal reviewer and next-check time?
