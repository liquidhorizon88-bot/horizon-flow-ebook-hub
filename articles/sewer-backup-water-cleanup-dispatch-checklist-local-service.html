Sewer Backup and Water Cleanup Dispatch Checklist for Local Service Businesses

A source-card worksheet for plumbers, drain cleaners, restoration teams, mitigation firms, property-service dispatchers, and after-hours answering teams handling sewer backup or contaminated-water calls without letting AI or staff invent cleanup scope, safety clearance, insurance coverage, arrival timing, disposal rules, or final pricing.

SEWER-BACKUP-WATER-CLEANUP-CARD-READY

Use this before sending any AI-assisted customer update

STOP AUTOMATION: do not promise emergency dispatch, extraction, sanitizing, habitability, price, insurance coverage, claim reimbursement, drain repair, mold prevention, or safety clearance until an owner/manager has checked the job record, call notes, photos, access constraints, contamination risk, crew availability, approved communication channel, and escalation policy.

  • Do not tell the customer a space is safe, sanitized, dry, mold-free, or ready for use unless the approved field/source record says so.
  • Do not guess whether the source is sewer, greywater, stormwater, toilet overflow, main-line backup, appliance leak, or another cause.
  • Do not promise insurance coverage, waived fees, reimbursement, or exact arrival windows from an AI draft or partial call transcript.

Copy/paste sewer-backup source card

  1. Caller / property / trigger: customer name, service address, caller role, after-hours status, affected rooms, active overflow status, number of occupants/tenants, and customer-authorized communication channel.
  2. Hazard and stop-rule notes: sewage/unknown water, electrical exposure, standing water, children/pets/tenants, odor, visible contamination, shutoff status, and whether the update must be acknowledgement-only.
  3. Evidence and access: photos requested/received, drain/toilet/floor-drain source if known, access limitations, lockbox/gate/tenant notes, parking, equipment access, and missing evidence.
  4. Dispatch and scope boundary: plumber vs restoration vs mitigation handoff, extraction/sanitizing estimate status, emergency/rush review, crew availability, disposal requirements, and owner/manager approval needed before promises.
  5. Customer-safe update owner: dispatcher, on-call manager, mitigation lead, plumber, claims coordinator if applicable, approved message template, and what cannot be promised before field review.
  6. CRM label: sewer_backup_emergency, water_cleanup_source_needed, contaminated_water_review, dispatch_manager_review, or stop_automation_safety_claim.

Customer-safe snippets

Acknowledgement without overpromising

We have your sewer backup / water cleanup request in the job record. Before we confirm arrival timing, cleanup scope, safety status, pricing, insurance details, or repair recommendations, our team will review the source notes, photos, access details, hazard flags, crew availability, and approved communication path.

Photos or access details still needed

To keep the emergency record accurate, please send only the requested photos or access details through the approved channel. Avoid entering affected areas if there is standing contaminated water, electrical risk, or any safety concern. We will review the source record before sending a source-backed next-step update.

Customer asks if it is covered or safe

We do not want to guess about coverage, reimbursement, habitability, sanitation, mold, or safety clearance. We can document the issue and response notes, but claim or safety conclusions need to come from the approved field/source record and manager review.
