Use this when

A public mention is wrong, stale, or too vague for AI search.

Use this worksheet when a directory listing, old chamber profile, partner page, vendor marketplace, local roundup, or industry database contains mismatched business facts. The goal is not to blast generic link-building email. The goal is to send one accurate correction request with evidence, source URLs, and a clear owner-reviewed replacement.

Good fit

  • Local businesses with changed hours, services, phone numbers, locations, or booking URLs.
  • Consultants cleaning up source-of-truth mentions before AI-search visibility work.
  • Teams preparing a citation cleanup sprint after seeing AI tools quote stale facts.

Do not use it to

  • Invent awards, certifications, locations, service areas, or claims that are not publicly supported.
  • Send mass spam to unrelated sites.
  • Ask for promotional links when the real need is factual correction.

Human review rule

STOP PUBLISHING when the correction is not evidence-backed.

  • Do not claim a service area, license, guarantee, award, or credential unless the official source supports it.
  • Do not invent opening hours, emergency availability, phone routing, pricing, or booking policies.
  • Do not send the correction if the source-of-truth page is stale or conflicts with Google Business Profile.
  • Do not ask for a backlink if the platform only accepts factual corrections.

Outreach templates

Send one accurate correction request.

Directory listing correction

Hi [NAME/TEAM] — I noticed our listing on [PAGE] has [INCORRECT FACT]. Could you update it to: [CORRECT FACT]? Source: [OFFICIAL URL]. We are trying to keep public business facts consistent for customers and search tools. Thank you.

Partner/chamber profile update

Hi [NAME] — could you refresh the Horizon/our company profile at [URL]? Correct wording: [OWNER-APPROVED TEXT]. Please use [SOURCE URL] as the current source of truth. If you need a shorter version, use: [SHORT TEXT].

No-reply follow-up

Hi [NAME] — quick follow-up on the correction request below. The current page still shows [INCORRECT FACT], and the verified source is [URL]. Is there a better contact for factual listing updates?
