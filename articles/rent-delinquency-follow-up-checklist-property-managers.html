Rent Delinquency Follow-Up Checklist for Property Managers

Late-rent follow-up is not the place to let AI improvise. Use this worksheet to confirm ledger facts, separate reminders from notices, and stop messages before they invent fees, payment-plan terms, legal steps, lease language, or fair-housing-sensitive claims.

RENT-DELINQUENCY-FOLLOW-UP-READY: copy/paste the fields below into your property-management CRM, task board, or manager review queue before sending any rent follow-up.

Use this before you send

  • Ledger balance, due date, grace period, and prior payment history are verified from the system of record.
  • Lease, property policy, and local notice requirements are reviewed by the property manager or approved process.
  • Message is a factual reminder unless a qualified person has approved formal notice language.
  • AI is not allowed to invent fee amounts, legal deadlines, eviction steps, payment-plan approval, or tenant intent.

Tenant-safe reminder starter

Use this only for a routine reminder after the ledger facts are verified. Do not use it as legal notice unless your approved property-management process says it is appropriate.

Hi {tenant_first_name}, this is a quick reminder that our records show a balance of {verified_balance} for {property_or_unit}. You can review or make a payment through {approved_payment_path}. If you already made this payment or believe the balance is incorrect, please reply with the payment date, confirmation, or question and we will check the ledger before taking the next step.

AI review prompt

You are helping a property manager organize a late-rent follow-up. Do not invent lease terms, legal notices, eviction steps, fee amounts, payment-plan approval, tenant intent, fair-housing facts, or local-law requirements. Using only the verified notes below, produce:
1. missing fact checklist,
2. tenant-safe reminder draft,
3. manager review questions,
4. CRM labels and follow-up owner,
5. STOP AUTOMATION warnings.
Verified notes:
{paste ledger, lease/policy source, and prior communication notes only}

STOP AUTOMATION rules

  • Stop if the message becomes a formal notice, eviction threat, collections message, legal claim, or fee/penalty explanation.
  • Stop if the balance, fees, ledger date, tenant identity, payment status, or lease source is unverified.
  • Stop if AI suggests payment-plan approval, waiver, lockout, service denial, legal deadline, or tenant motive not found in source notes.
  • Stop if the situation involves hardship, accommodation, dispute, protected-class sensitivity, local legal notice rules, or manager/legal review.
