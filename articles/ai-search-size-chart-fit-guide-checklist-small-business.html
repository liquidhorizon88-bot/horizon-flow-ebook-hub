AI Search Size Chart and Fit Guide Checklist for Small Businesses

Size charts, fit guides, compatibility tables, and “runs small” notes are exactly the kind of details AI search tools can flatten into overconfident answers. Why fit and size claims need a source card

A product page may have a size table, a PDF spec sheet, a manufacturer chart, review snippets, support macros, and return-policy exceptions that disagree. AI tools can turn that mess into invented certainty: “true to size,” “fits all standard models,” “free exchanges,” or “compatible with every version.” A source card gives the business one approved reference before AI-assisted content or answers talk about fit.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not let AI invent body measurements, model compatibility, installation fit, sizing advice, exchange eligibility, restocking fees, return windows, warranty coverage, customer-specific fit guarantees, or medical/safety/performance claims. Route sensitive sizing, compliance, accessibility, health, safety, or legal wording to the owner or qualified reviewer.

Product and variant scopeProduct name, SKU, variant, size system, model/version, region, material, package, and last-review date.Do not reuse one fit answer across variants, regions, models, or bundles unless the same source applies.
Measurement sourceOfficial chart, manufacturer spec, internal measurement, photo/spec proof, tolerance range, and source URL or file.If measurements are approximate, conditional, or tolerance-based, say so clearly.
Fit or compatibility boundaryWho/what it fits, who/what it does not fit, prerequisites, exclusions, installation limits, accessibility/safety notes, and support handoff.Owner/vendor review is required before public wording changes compatibility, suitability, safety, or performance claims.
Policy connectionReturn/exchange window, restocking fee, packaging condition, custom-order exception, warranty/guarantee boundary, and escalation path.Never imply free returns, exchanges, or fit guarantees unless the active policy source says so.
Customer-safe wordingApproved short answer, measurement instructions, “confirm before ordering” note, and support link.Use cautious wording: “check the chart,” “measure before ordering,” “contact support if unsure,” or “compatibility review required.”

Safe public wording templates

Fit-guide answer: Use the current [product/SKU] size chart before ordering. Measurements, compatibility, and fit can vary by [variant/model/material/body measurement/installation context]. If you are between sizes or unsure about compatibility, contact [support path] before purchase.
Policy-safe answer: Returns, exchanges, restocking fees, custom-order exceptions, and warranty coverage follow the active [return/fit/warranty policy URL]. Do not rely on AI summaries for customer-specific eligibility.
Internal AI note: STOP PUBLISHING. Do not invent sizing, measurements, compatibility, fit guarantees, returns, exchanges, restocking-fee waivers, warranty coverage, safety claims, or customer-specific advice. Use only the approved source card.

Weekly source-maintenance questions

  • Do product pages, size charts, PDFs, marketplace listings, ads, support macros, and return policies say the same thing?
  • Are measurements, tolerance ranges, compatibility limits, and variant differences dated and linked to an official source?
  • Can shoppers confirm fit through an official path without relying on an AI summary?
  • Which return, exchange, restocking, custom-order, warranty, accessibility, or safety claims require owner/vendor review?
