AI Search Title Tag and Meta Description Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before rewriting snippets, page titles, SEO titles, or meta descriptions with AI. The goal is not clickbait. The goal is to make each page's promise specific, supported, and easy for buyers and AI answer engines to summarize accurately.

Copy the worksheet Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Google snippets or AI summaries make the page sound vague.
  • Old titles promise a service, area, price, or outcome that changed.
  • A contractor, consultant, clinic, agency, or local business wants clearer service-page snippets.
  • You need a no-invented-claims prompt for content updates.

Why this matters

Snippets become source material

Title tags and meta descriptions are not only search-result dressing. They are compact claims about what a page covers. If they are vague, stale, or exaggerated, customers lose context and AI tools may summarize the page around the wrong promise.

The safest update starts with verified source facts: the page topic, buyer problem, service area, proof, next step, and claims that need owner review.

Examples

Good snippets sound specific, not inflated

  • Too vague: Best services for every need.
  • Safer: HVAC repair request form for [CITY] homeowners with response-window and photo-upload instructions.
  • Too risky: Guaranteed same-day emergency service.
  • Safer: Request urgent service review; our dispatcher confirms availability before scheduling.
  • Too generic: Learn about our company.
  • Safer: Meet [BUSINESS], a [CITY] [SERVICE] team with verified licenses, project photos, and contact options.
