Lease Signature and Document Upload Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before a leasing assistant, resident portal, inbox workflow, or AI receptionist says a lease, upload, insurance file, ID, payment, or move-in document is complete. It keeps the reply useful without inventing signature status, portal proof, policy exceptions, payment receipt, key eligibility, move-in clearance, or legal-sensitive paperwork promises.

LEASE-SIGNATURE-DOCUMENT-UPLOAD-READY

When to use this

Use this source card when a resident or applicant asks whether paperwork is complete, says they uploaded a document, requests move-in clearance, cannot find a lease-signature link, has renter-insurance or ID proof questions, or asks whether keys can be released after a portal upload.

Human-review rule: a document upload is not the same as approval. A property manager should verify the source record, lease status, required documents, payment ledger, identity/account match, insurance policy proof, and local/legal policy before confirming move-in readiness, keys, fees, or exceptions.

Resident-safe reply snippets

  • Upload received, not approved: “Thanks — we can see that a document was submitted. Our team still needs to verify that it is readable, current, and matches the requirement before we mark this step complete.”
  • Signature still pending: “We are checking the lease-signature record before confirming completion. If a signer or countersignature is still pending, we will send the exact next step through the approved portal/email path.”
  • Move-in clearance not verified: “I do not want to promise key pickup or move-in clearance until the lease, required documents, and ledger status are verified by the office.”
  • Need safer channel: “For account security, please upload documents through [approved portal] or contact [office channel]. Please do not send sensitive IDs or payment details by text.”

STOP AUTOMATION guardrails

  • STOP AUTOMATION if AI is about to mark paperwork complete from “uploaded,” “sent,” “viewed,” “received,” or “screenshot attached” without source-record approval.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if signatures, co-signers, guarantors, countersignatures, expired links, screening, insurance, ID, payments, or assistance paperwork are incomplete or ambiguous.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if the reply would promise key pickup, possession, move-in clearance, fee waivers, refunds, application approval, exceptions, or legal/lease conclusions.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if sensitive personal data, IDs, payment details, insurance documents, disability/accommodation details, or third-party assistance documents are mentioned.
  • STOP AUTOMATION if the requester is not clearly authorized to receive the account or paperwork status.
