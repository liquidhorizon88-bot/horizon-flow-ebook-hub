AI Search Shipping Damage and Carrier Claim Checklist for Small Businesses

AI-SEARCH-SHIPPING-DAMAGE-CARRIER-CLAIM-READY — Use this worksheet before your FAQ, chatbot, product page, support macro, marketplace answer, or AI-search snippet tells a customer what happens after a damaged shipment, missing carrier scan, delivery-photo mismatch, or carrier insurance claim.

Shipping damage creates high-risk public wording because order records, delivery photos, carrier rules, insurance coverage, replacement inventory, refund timing, and customer-provided proof may all live in different systems. A source card keeps AI-generated answers from inventing refund approval, replacement timing, carrier liability, reshipment promises, claim deadlines, or packaging requirements.

1) Shipping-damage source card

  • Order and shipment: {order_id}, {customer_name}, {ship_date}, {carrier}, {tracking_number}, {delivery_scan_or_photo_source}
  • Damage report: damaged item, damaged packaging, missing item, wet/soiled package, delivery-photo mismatch, porch/theft concern, carrier handling issue, warehouse packing issue, or unknown source
  • Proof required: customer photos, package photos, item serial/SKU, delivery photo, carrier scan, packing slip, return authorization, internal inspection note, or manager approval
  • Approved options: replacement review, refund review, repair/part review, carrier claim, return/inspection hold, store credit review, or no immediate promise
  • Owner/reviewer: {support_owner}, {operations_owner}, {claim_owner}, {review_deadline}, {approved_contact_channel}

STOP PUBLISHING guardrails

  • Stop if the answer promises refund, replacement, reshipment, carrier reimbursement, insurance coverage, claim approval, deadline extension, pickup timing, or liability without the approved source record.
  • Do not invent whether the damage was caused by the carrier, customer, warehouse, manufacturer, weather, theft, or packaging.
  • Do not publish legal, insurance, chargeback, warranty, hazardous-material, food/health, or regulated-product claims without qualified review.
  • Do not ask customers to discard packaging, damaged goods, labels, serial numbers, or photos if the claim process may need them.
  • Use cautious wording like “we need to review” until a human owner approves the exact outcome.

2) Customer-safe wording snippets

Proof request: “I’m sorry the shipment arrived that way. To route this correctly, please send photos of the item, packaging, label, and packing slip through {approved_channel}. Please keep the packaging until we confirm the next step.”

Review hold: “We need to compare the order record, carrier scan, and damage photos before confirming a refund, replacement, claim, or other option. {team_owner} will review and follow up through {approved_channel}.”

No immediate outcome: “We can’t confirm the final outcome from the first message alone, but we’ve logged the damage report and will use the approved support process to review it.”
