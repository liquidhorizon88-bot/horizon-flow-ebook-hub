Hiring interview question AI prompt for small business owners

Small teams often interview in a hurry, reuse vague questions, and compare candidates by memory. This prompt helps draft consistent, job-related interview questions, a simple scorecard, and follow-up notes while keeping hiring judgment and legal review with a human owner.

Use the free AI Prompt Quality Scorecard

Use this when

  • You need interview questions for a real role, not generic personality questions
  • Several managers need a consistent scorecard before speaking with candidates
  • You want AI help drafting, but not making hiring decisions

AI can help turn a job description into a cleaner interview plan. It should not decide who to hire, infer protected characteristics, write discriminatory questions, or replace local employment-law guidance. Treat the draft as a starting point for a manager or HR reviewer.

Stop rule: Do not ask questions about age, family status, disability, medical history, religion, nationality, citizenship beyond legal work authorization process, pregnancy, military status, or other protected/private topics. Keep every question tied to job duties, skills, availability requirements that are truly necessary, and workplace expectations.

Hiring interview question AI prompt

You are helping a small business owner draft a structured interview plan. Role: {role_title}. Business type: {business_type}. Core duties: {core_duties}. Required skills/tools/licenses: {required_skills}. Schedule or physical requirements that are truly job-related: {requirements}. Company values/work style: {values}. Create: (1) 8 job-related interview questions, (2) 4 practical scenario questions, (3) a simple 1–5 scorecard with what a strong answer includes, (4) a candidate follow-up note template, and (5) a human review checklist. Do not include questions about protected/private characteristics, personal life, health, family, age, religion, nationality, disability, or anything unrelated to job performance. Mark missing facts as [confirm].

  • Human review rule: A manager/owner should confirm each question is job-related, consistent across candidates, and aligned with local rules before using it.
  • Data rule: Do not paste resumes with sensitive personal details into an AI tool unless the business has permission and an approved privacy process.
  • Decision rule: AI may draft a scorecard; a human makes the hiring decision using documented, job-related criteria.

Question bank

12 starter interview questions to adapt

1. Role experience

Tell me about a time you handled a task similar to [core duty]. What steps did you take, and what was the result?

Score for: relevant example, clear process, honest outcome.

2. Tool or system skill

Which tools or systems have you used for [required tool/process], and what would you need to learn here?

Score for: current skill plus realistic learning plan.

3. Customer communication

A customer is frustrated about [common issue]. How would you respond and when would you escalate?

Score for: empathy, facts, escalation judgment.

4. Prioritization

You have [urgent task], [routine task], and [customer request] due at the same time. How would you prioritize?

Score for: safety, customer impact, communication.

5. Accuracy check

How do you check your work before sending an estimate, customer note, order, or internal handoff?

Score for: repeatable review habit.

6. Mistake recovery

Tell me about a mistake you caught or made at work. How did you handle it?

Score for: accountability and prevention.

7. Team handoff

What information do you include when handing a job or customer issue to another teammate?

Score for: completeness and clarity.

8. Learning curve

What part of this role do you expect would take the most training, and how do you learn new processes?

Score for: self-awareness and coachability.

9. Work standards

What does “done correctly” mean for [specific duty]?

Score for: quality markers tied to the job.

10. Scenario judgment

If a customer asks for something outside company policy, how would you respond?

Score for: policy awareness and escalation.

11. Availability fit

This role requires [specific schedule/availability requirement]. Are you able to meet that requirement?

Score for: direct answer to job requirement only.

12. Candidate questions

What questions do you have about the role, training, performance expectations, or schedule?

Score for: preparation and role understanding.

Scorecard

Simple interview scorecard

  • 1 — Not demonstrated: no relevant example or answer does not address the job requirement.
  • 2 — Weak: partial answer, unclear process, or major gaps for the role.
  • 3 — Acceptable: meets the basic requirement with some training needed.
  • 4 — Strong: gives a clear example, shows sound judgment, and maps to the role.
  • 5 — Excellent: strong relevant example, clear process, measurable result, and good escalation/review habits.

Keep notes factual and job-related. Avoid labels such as “culture fit” unless the scorecard defines observable work behaviors like responsiveness, safety habits, documentation, or customer communication.
