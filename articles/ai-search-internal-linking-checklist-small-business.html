AI Search Internal Linking Checklist for Small Business Websites

Use this worksheet to connect the pages that explain what a business does, where it serves, why buyers can trust it, and what a customer should do next — without keyword stuffing or invented claims.

Copy the worksheet Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Important service pages are orphaned.
  • FAQs, reviews, booking pages, and proof pages are not connected.
  • AI summaries quote a general page instead of the best answer page.
  • You want a safer SEO improvement than publishing more thin content.

Why this matters

AI search follows context, not just isolated pages

A small-business site can have strong pages that still underperform because the pages do not point to each other. A service page should link to relevant proof, FAQs, pricing factors, booking details, and location information. A homepage should guide visitors to the pages that answer their next questions.

The goal is not to manipulate AI answers. The goal is to make the site easier for humans, search crawlers, and AI systems to navigate accurately.

Internal-linking map worksheet

Site section reviewed: [homepage / service page / location page / FAQ / booking page]
Reviewer: [owner, manager, marketer]
Last reviewed: [date]

1. Primary buyer question
What question should this page answer before a buyer contacts you?
Supported answer: [answer using only approved facts]

2. Next-step links
Does the page link to the most useful next step?
- Contact / booking page: [URL]
- Pricing factors or estimate page: [URL]
- FAQ or preparation page: [URL]

3. Proof links
Does the page link to approved proof that supports the claim?
- Reviews/testimonials with permission: [URL]
- Case study or project example: [URL]
- License/certification/process proof: [URL]

4. Service and location links
Does the page point to the most specific service and location pages?
Use descriptive anchors such as "emergency plumbing in Austin" only when the page actually supports that service and location.

5. Resource links
Can the page link to one genuinely helpful guide, checklist, template, or FAQ that helps the buyer make a better decision?
Resource URL: [URL]

6. Anchor text check
Replace vague anchors like "click here" and repetitive exact-match anchors with honest descriptions of what the reader will get.

7. Orphan-page check
Which important pages have zero or very few internal links pointing to them?
Add links only where they help a buyer continue the journey.

8. Human-review stop rules
Before publishing, verify prices, guarantees, service areas, legal/compliance language, health/safety claims, reviews, and performance outcomes. Remove unsupported claims.

Implementation

Make five useful links before publishing more content

  1. Link the homepage to the two most important service or resource pages.
  2. Link each high-intent service page to a relevant proof page and a booking/contact page.
  3. Link FAQ answers back to the exact service, pricing-factor, or preparation page that explains the topic.
  4. Link location pages to service-area proof, hours/availability, and booking instructions.
  5. Remove links that imply claims the business cannot verify.

Human review rule: an owner or responsible manager should approve any link text that mentions prices, guarantees, service areas, credentials, legal/safety topics, reviews, or outcomes before the edits go live.
