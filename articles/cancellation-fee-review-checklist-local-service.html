Cancellation Fee Review Checklist for Local Service Businesses

Use this when a customer cancels, reschedules, disputes a no-show/cancellation fee, asks for a credit, or wants an exception. It helps the office respond without inventing refund rights, fee waivers, schedule priority, warranty coverage, or policy language.

2. Customer-safe reply snippets

Reviewing before a decision: “Hi [Name], I see your note about [cancellation/reschedule/fee]. I’m going to review the appointment record and our policy before we promise a waiver, credit, or refund. We’ll update you by [date/time].”

Approved reschedule without fee promise: “We can look at the next available appointment window. I’m marking the fee question for manager review separately so scheduling does not accidentally create a refund or waiver promise.”

Policy explanation: “Our record shows [verified policy/source]. Based on that, the current status is [fee/credit/refund status]. If you want us to review an exception, I can route it to [owner/manager] with your reason included.”

Do not say: “we will waive it,” “you qualify for a refund,” “the fee never applies,” “we can guarantee the earlier slot,” “the technician was wrong,” or “policy requires this” unless the approved source and reviewer support it.

3. AI draft prompt with guardrails

You are helping a local-service business draft a customer-safe reply about a cancellation, reschedule, no-show fee, fee waiver, credit, or refund request.
Use only the verified facts below. Do not invent policy terms, refunds, waivers, credits, legal rights, payment status, technician fault, appointment availability, warranty coverage, customer intent, or owner approval.
Facts I can verify: [paste cancellation-fee source card]
Create:
1) a short internal owner/manager summary;
2) one customer-safe reply under 650 characters;
3) missing facts/questions that must be resolved before promising a fee waiver, refund, credit, charge, reschedule priority, or policy decision.
If the policy or approval is unclear, write NEEDS OWNER REVIEW instead of a promise.

STOP AUTOMATION

  • Do not let AI approve, deny, refund, waive, charge, credit, or reschedule based on incomplete notes.
  • Stop if the message involves legal/policy interpretation, card disputes, chargebacks, refunds, credits, recurring billing, technician fault, safety, warranty coverage, or a high-value customer relationship.
  • Do not invent appointment history, consent, notice timing, arrival attempts, payment status, or exception approval to make the reply sound complete.
  • Escalate to an owner or manager when the policy source, customer reason, dispatch record, and payment record conflict.

  • Which exceptions were granted repeatedly: weather, illness, technician delay, access issue, late part, dispatch error, or relationship save?
