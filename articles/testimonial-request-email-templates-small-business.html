Testimonial request email templates for small businesses

A happy customer is a useful proof source only if the request is specific, pressure-free, and privacy-safe. Use these templates to ask for a testimonial, case-study quote, or permissioned result note without inventing claims or over-automating trust.

Best fit

Owners, consultants, agencies, local-service teams, and small businesses that need customer proof for websites, proposals, referral packets, and sales follow-up.

Many small businesses ask for reviews, but fewer ask for clear testimonials they can use in proposals, landing pages, and referral follow-up. The safest workflow is simple: identify a real customer win, ask for permission, offer a short prompt, confirm the exact words, and keep private details out unless the customer explicitly approves them.

Copy/paste emails

Five testimonial request templates

1. After a clear win

Subject: Quick testimonial request?

Hi [CUSTOMER_NAME], thanks again for trusting us with [PROJECT/RESULT]. If you were comfortable sharing a short sentence about what changed for you, it would help future customers understand what to expect. A simple line like “Before [BUSINESS], we struggled with ___; afterward ___” is perfect. No pressure either way — and I’ll send you the exact wording for approval before using it.

2. Case-study quote permission

Subject: May we quote this result?

Hi [CUSTOMER_NAME], you mentioned [SPECIFIC RESULT/FEEDBACK]. Would it be okay if we turned that into a short testimonial or case-study quote? We can keep it anonymous, use first name only, or include your company name only if you approve the final version in writing.

3. Referral partner proof

Subject: A quick proof note for referral partners

Hi [CUSTOMER_NAME], we’re putting together a short referral packet and your experience with [SERVICE/WORKFLOW] is a good example of the kind of problem we solve. Would you be open to sharing one approved sentence about the outcome? We will not include private details, pricing, or project specifics unless you explicitly approve them.

4. Lightweight prompt for the customer

Subject: Easier way to write the testimonial

Hi [CUSTOMER_NAME], if helpful, here are three prompts you can answer in one or two sentences: What problem were you trying to solve? What did we make easier? What would you tell another owner considering the same help? Short and plain is better than polished.

5. Approval before publishing

Subject: Please approve this wording before we use it

Hi [CUSTOMER_NAME], here is the exact testimonial wording we drafted from your feedback: “[QUOTE].” Please reply “approved” if this is accurate and okay to use. If anything feels overstated, private, or unclear, I’ll revise or remove it.

Copy/paste AI prompt

Draft a privacy-safe testimonial request packet

Act as a careful small-business marketing assistant.

Use only these facts:
Customer type: [CUSTOMER_TYPE]
Service/product delivered: [SERVICE_OR_PRODUCT]
Approved outcome or feedback: [APPROVED_OUTCOME]
Privacy boundary: [PRIVACY_BOUNDARY]
Where proof may be used: [WEBSITE/PROPOSAL/REFERRAL_PACKET]
Approver: [APPROVER]

Create:
1. A short testimonial request email.
2. A customer-friendly fill-in prompt.
3. A final approval email before publishing.
4. A CRM note with approval status and usage boundary.
5. A list of missing facts that require human confirmation.

Rules:
- Do not invent numbers, results, names, titles, deadlines, or endorsements.
- Do not reveal private customer, pricing, health, financial, legal, or project details.
- Do not pressure the customer or imply the testimonial is required.
- Keep every claim verifiable from approved customer words.
- Flag anything that needs owner/legal review before publishing.

Human review rule

Before publishing or reusing a testimonial, a human should confirm the quote is accurate, permissioned, current, and not misleading. Never use AI to manufacture proof, inflate outcomes, or turn private customer details into marketing copy.
