AI Search Competitor Mention Tracker for Small Businesses

When ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, or Google AI Overviews mention a competitor instead of your business, the useful question is not “how do we trick the AI?” It is “what source, proof, page, or answer is missing?” Use this tracker to turn competitor mentions into proof-safe website updates.

Use this when

  • AI-search answers name competitors for service, location, price, proof, or FAQ queries.
  • Your team wants an evidence log before rewriting pages or making claims.
  • You need to compare source gaps without inventing competitor facts.
  • You want a weekly action queue for AI-search visibility improvements.

Goal: convert AI-search competitor mentions into a prioritized source-of-truth, proof, FAQ, schema, and page-update queue.

STOP PUBLISHING: do not claim a competitor is wrong, worse, cheaper, unlicensed, unavailable, or less qualified unless a human has verified public evidence and legal/owner review approves the wording.

AI audit prompt

You are auditing AI-search competitor mentions for a small business.

Inputs:
- Buyer queries tested: [PASTE]
- AI/search answer excerpts with dates: [PASTE]
- Our current relevant pages: [PASTE]
- Competitor names mentioned: [PASTE]
- Approved business facts and proof URLs: [PASTE]

Task:
1. List each query where a competitor was mentioned and our business was absent or weakly represented.
2. Identify only observable source gaps: missing FAQ, stale service page, weak proof, missing schema, missing location/service-area page, unclear offer, or missing internal link.
3. Suggest one proof-safe update for our website using only approved facts.
4. Do not invent competitor claims, performance comparisons, price differences, licenses, ratings, customer outcomes, guarantees, availability, or legal conclusions.
5. Output a table with: query, surface, competitor named, likely source gap, owner action, safe update, review flag, next check date.

Weekly review workflow

  1. Pick 5–10 buyer-intent queries: service + location, “best,” “near me,” pricing, emergency, comparison, proof, policy, and booking questions.
  2. Record the date, AI/search surface, answer excerpt, competitors mentioned, and source URLs if visible.
  3. Map the mention to a missing source-of-truth item: page, FAQ, proof, citation, schema, review policy, service-area detail, or booking answer.
  4. Create one owner-approved update at a time. Avoid attacking competitors; improve your own source clarity.
  5. Recheck the same query after the update has been crawled or visible, then mark the row monitor/needs-more-proof.
