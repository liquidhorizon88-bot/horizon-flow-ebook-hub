AI Search Content Pruning and Consolidation Checklist for Small Businesses

Publishing more pages is not always the safest AI-search move. This worksheet helps a small business decide when an old page should be refreshed, merged, redirected, deindexed, or left alone without inventing traffic data, rankings, or business facts.

Use this when

  • Several old service, blog, FAQ, or location pages answer the same buyer question.
  • Search Console shows impressions for pages that no longer match the current offer.
  • AI answers quote a stale blog post instead of the best service or proof page.
  • You need a safer cleanup plan before deleting, redirecting, or rewriting content.

Stop rule

Do not invent page performance or redirect history. If traffic, backlinks, conversions, crawl status, or replacement URLs are unknown, mark them unknown and pull the real source before changing the page.

AI audit prompt

Prompt

You are helping a small business review website pages for AI search visibility. Use only the verified URLs and data below. Do not invent traffic, rankings, backlinks, conversions, redirect history, services, prices, guarantees, locations, or legal/policy details. For each page, return: (1) keep/refresh/merge/redirect/noindex/remove/monitor recommendation, (2) exact facts to preserve, (3) risky or stale claims to verify, (4) possible source-of-truth page, (5) internal links to add, (6) redirect or canonical questions for a developer, and (7) the human approval required before publishing. Data: [paste worksheet rows].

Human-review rules

  • Never delete or redirect a page because an AI tool says it is thin without checking Search Console, analytics, backlinks, and customer usefulness.
  • Do not let AI choose redirect targets for legal, pricing, warranty, emergency, medical, financial, or regulated pages without owner/developer review.
  • Keep a backup of old copy, title, meta description, target URL, and decision reason before changing public pages.
  • After redirects or merges, verify the old URL, new URL, sitemap, internal links, and important buttons in a browser.
  • Schedule a follow-up check for impressions, clicks, conversions, and AI-answer accuracy after the cleanup goes live.
