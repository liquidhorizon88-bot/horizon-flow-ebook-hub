Resident Staff or Vendor Conduct Complaint Checklist for Property Managers

Use this worksheet before AI, a leasing coordinator, maintenance dispatcher, resident-services team, or virtual assistant answers a resident complaint about an employee, vendor, contractor, inspector, security guard, concierge, maintenance tech, or service partner. It keeps the first response neutral, protects privacy, captures source facts, and routes discrimination, harassment, safety, employment, legal, and vendor-performance issues to a human.

When to use this checklist

Use it when a resident reports rude behavior, missed service, inappropriate comments, privacy concerns, discrimination, harassment, entry/access concerns, vendor conduct, security interactions, suspected retaliation, or a professionalism complaint involving staff or a third-party provider.

  • Separate what the resident reported from verified facts, staff notes, work orders, access logs, vendor logs, camera/access records, and manager findings.
  • Send a neutral acknowledgement that does not admit fault, dismiss the resident, promise discipline, or reveal private employee/vendor information.
  • Escalate fair-housing, discrimination, harassment, safety, threat, privacy, retaliation, police, legal, HR, and insurance triggers before AI drafts a detailed reply.
  • Create a clear next-owner and next-update time so the resident is not left without a response while the issue is reviewed.

STOP AUTOMATION if the message involves discrimination, harassment, retaliation, threats, safety, entry/access rights, privacy, police, legal action, employment discipline, vendor contract terms, insurance, or an unverified finding.

Resident-safe acknowledgement templates

1. Complaint received, review pending

Hi [Name], thank you for letting us know. We are documenting your concern and routing it to [manager/team] for review so we do not guess about what happened or share private staff/vendor details. We will follow up through [approved channel] by [time/date] with the next appropriate update.

2. More details needed without pressuring the resident

Hi [Name], to help us route this correctly, could you share the approximate date/time, location, and any work order or message reference connected to the concern? Please do not send private access codes, payment details, medical details, or information you are not comfortable sharing in this channel.

3. Sensitive issue routed to a human

Hi [Name], because this concern may involve [sensitive trigger], we are routing it for human review instead of sending an automated response. We will keep the review tied to the records available and will follow up through [approved channel] after the appropriate reviewer checks the issue.

If a required fact is missing or the issue is sensitive, output NEEDS MANAGER REVIEW.

Create: (1) internal triage summary, (2) missing facts, (3) escalation bucket, (4) resident-safe acknowledgement under 100 words, and (5) CRM/PMS note.

Human-review stop rules

  • STOP AUTOMATION before admitting fault, promising discipline, sharing employee/vendor details, deciding a complaint is false, waiving fees, approving credits, or giving legal/HR conclusions.
  • NEEDS MANAGER / HR / LEGAL REVIEW if the message mentions discrimination, harassment, retaliation, threats, violence, entry/access rights, privacy, disability accommodation, police, insurance, employment action, or repeated unresolved complaints.
  • Do not paste private resident, employee, vendor, access-code, medical, payment, or legal-sensitive details into public AI tools.
  • Keep updates factual: receipt confirmed, review owner assigned, next update window, approved channel, and no unsupported findings.
