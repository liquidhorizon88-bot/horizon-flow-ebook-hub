AI Search Image Alt Text Checklist for Small Business Websites

Photos can prove real services, real projects, real people, and real locations — but only when the surrounding text is clear and honest. Use this worksheet to write useful image alt text, captions, filenames, and proof notes without stuffing keywords or inventing claims.

Copy the checklist Get SEO After AI

Use this when

  • Service or portfolio photos have vague filenames.
  • Project pages rely on images but lack captions or proof notes.
  • AI summaries misunderstand what a photo represents.
  • You want image SEO improvements that also help accessibility.

Why this matters

Images need verified context, not keyword stuffing

A small-business site often has real proof hidden in photos: completed work, staff, storefronts, service vehicles, menus, before-and-after examples, materials, and customer environments. Search engines and AI answer systems still need surrounding context to understand what those images show.

The safest improvement is simple: describe the image accurately, connect it to the right page, and document what claims a human has verified. Do not use alt text to add services, locations, awards, results, or before-and-after claims that the image does not support.

Copy/paste artifact

Image alt text and proof worksheet

Image reviewed: [filename or URL]
Page where image appears: [URL]
Reviewer: [owner / marketer / manager]
Last reviewed: [date]

1. What does the image actually show?
People / service / product / project / location / document / screenshot: [describe]
Visible facts only: [what can be confirmed from the photo or approved notes]
Unknowns: [what should not be claimed]

2. Alt text draft
Write one accurate sentence for accessibility and context.
Alt text: [example: Technician inspecting a residential HVAC unit before a maintenance appointment]
Avoid: keyword lists, sales claims, hidden promotional copy, or unsupported locations.

3. Caption or nearby text
If the image supports a service or proof claim, add a visible caption or nearby sentence.
Caption: [what the buyer should understand]
Proof note: [permission / project date / service area / reviewer]

4. Filename check
Use descriptive filenames when practical.
Good: hvac-maintenance-inspection-cincinnati.jpg
Bad: IMG_4821-final-new2.jpg
Do not rename files to claim a location/service that is not verified.

5. Link and page fit
Does the image support the page topic?
Relevant page: [service / location / portfolio / FAQ / booking / about]
Next helpful link: [portfolio, booking, service page, FAQ]

6. Permission and privacy
Customer permission confirmed? [yes/no/not needed]
Faces, license plates, addresses, private documents, or sensitive details visible? [yes/no]
If yes, crop, blur, replace, or remove before publishing.

7. Human-review stop rules
Owner or responsible manager must review before publishing if the image implies price, guarantee, certification, safety outcome, health/legal/financial advice, before-and-after result, customer identity, location coverage, or emergency capability.

AI drafting prompt

Use AI to draft alt text from approved notes

You are helping a small business improve image alt text and captions.
Use only the approved image notes below. Do not invent or infer services, locations, credentials, guarantees, outcomes, customer identity, safety conditions, or before-and-after claims.
Return:
1. concise alt text for accessibility,
2. optional visible caption,
3. safer filename suggestion,
4. privacy or permission risks,
5. claims that require owner review before publishing.
Approved image notes:
[paste image notes, page URL, service, location if verified, and permission status]

Implementation

Upgrade five high-value images first

  1. Start with the homepage hero, main service page, portfolio/project page, About/team page, and booking/contact page images.
  2. Replace vague alt text with one accurate sentence that describes the image.
  3. Add visible captions when the image supports a buyer decision.
  4. Update filenames during the next planned upload cycle; do not break live image URLs casually.
  5. Remove or blur images with private details before asking AI to summarize them.

Human review rule: If an image is being used as proof, a human must verify permission and factual support before the copy goes live.
