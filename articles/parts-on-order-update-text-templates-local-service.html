Parts-on-Order Update Text Templates for Local Service Businesses

When a job depends on a part, silence creates churn. Use these templates to explain the delay, set the next check date, and keep the customer confident without promising what the supplier has not confirmed.

Copy the templates Get Local Lead Rescue

Use this when

  • A repair, install, or callback is waiting on a part.
  • The supplier ETA is unknown or changed.
  • The customer needs a next-check date instead of vague reassurance.
  • The team needs CRM labels for parts-on-order follow-up.

Why this matters

Part delays are service moments, not just scheduling problems

Customers usually forgive a real supply delay faster than they forgive silence. A short update can prevent duplicate calls, frustrated reviews, and avoidable cancellations. The safest message separates confirmed facts from estimates and gives a clear next step.

This resource is built for local-service teams such as HVAC, plumbing, appliance repair, garage door, roofing, landscaping, and property-maintenance providers.

Copy/paste artifact

Parts-on-order update templates

1) First parts-on-order update

Hi [Name] — quick update on [job/service]. We have [part/material] on order for [job/site]. The current supplier status is [confirmed status]. We will check again on [date/time] and update you by [channel]. I do not want to guess at an arrival date until the supplier confirms it.

2) ETA changed or slipped

Hi [Name] — the expected timing for [part/material] has changed from [old window] to [new confirmed window/status]. I know delays are frustrating, so our next step is [reschedule / hold appointment / check alternate supplier / call you on date]. We will not mark this complete until [next verified step].

3) Appointment hold while waiting

Hi [Name] — we are holding your appointment request while [part/material] is on order. To avoid wasting your time, we will confirm the visit only after [part/material] is available or has a firm arrival date. Next check: [date].

4) Alternate option review

Hi [Name] — one possible alternate is [alternate option], but we need to verify [fit/warranty/price/availability] before recommending it. If you want us to check that route, we can review it and send the confirmed tradeoffs before you decide.

5) Closeout when the part is unavailable

Hi [Name] — after checking [supplier/source], [part/material] is currently unavailable through [known channels]. I do not want to keep this open without a real path forward. The best next step is [manufacturer support / replacement estimate / alternate provider / revisit date]. If the status changes, we can reopen the request.
