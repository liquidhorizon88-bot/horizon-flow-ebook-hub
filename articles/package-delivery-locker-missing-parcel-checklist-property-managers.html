PACKAGE-DELIVERY-LOCKER-MISSING-PARCEL-READY — Use this worksheet before a leasing office, resident portal, maintenance coordinator, concierge, or AI-assisted reply answers a missing-package, parcel-locker, mailroom, carrier-scan, or delivery-access complaint.

Use when

  • A resident says a package was delivered but is missing.
  • A package locker, mailroom, key, or access-code process failed.
  • A carrier scan conflicts with the resident's account or building log.
  • An AI draft is about to promise camera review, reimbursement, liability, or carrier action without proof.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not invent custody, liability, or camera proof

Do not let AI, autoresponders, or rushed office scripts say the property has a package, lost a package, reviewed footage, contacted a carrier, approved a reimbursement, or accepted liability unless the building log, locker record, carrier proof, office policy, and manager approval support it. Escalate before mentioning refunds, credits, police reports, camera footage, resident blame, staff blame, access codes, or private delivery details.

Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

1) Start the search without admitting custody

Hi {resident_name}, thanks for letting us know. We are checking the package-room and locker records for the tracking details you shared. We do not want to guess, so we will update you after reviewing the building log.

2) Carrier scan conflicts with building log

Hi {resident_name}, the carrier scan and our current building record do not fully match yet. Please keep the carrier tracking number handy while we review the locker/mailroom log and confirm the next appropriate step.

3) Access-code or locker issue

Hi {resident_name}, we are checking the locker/package-room access record. For security, please do not send private access codes or package contents in this thread. We will confirm the approved next step through the office channel.

4) Needs carrier claim or manager review

Hi {resident_name}, we do not have enough verified information to promise an outcome yet. The next step is manager review and, if needed, using the carrier's claim process tied to the tracking number.
