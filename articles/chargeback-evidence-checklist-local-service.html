Chargeback Evidence Checklist for Local Service Businesses

When a customer disputes a card payment, the fastest reply is not always the safest reply. Use this worksheet to collect source proof, separate facts from assumptions, and stop AI-assisted messages before they invent policy, legal, fee, refund, or completion claims.

CHARGEBACK-EVIDENCE-READY: copy/paste the fields below into your CRM, job note, payment-dispute workflow, or manager review queue.

Use this before you send

  • Payment processor dispute deadline and case ID are verified.
  • Invoice, estimate, approval, job completion, photos, and messages are attached.
  • Refund, warranty, discount, fee, and legal wording is manager-reviewed.
  • Customer reply is factual, calm, and does not threaten or overpromise.

Customer-safe response starter

Use this only after a manager confirms the facts and payment-policy wording.

Hi {customer_first_name}, thanks for flagging this. We are reviewing the payment record, invoice, job notes, and any service proof tied to {job_or_invoice}. We will respond with the confirmed facts rather than guessing from memory. If you have a specific receipt, message, photo, or concern you want us to include in the review, please send it here and we will add it to the file.

STOP AUTOMATION rules

  • Stop if the dispute involves legal threats, chargeback rules, collections, financing, refunds, warranties, fraud, or regulatory claims.
  • Stop if the job completion proof is incomplete or contradicts the invoice.
  • Stop if AI suggests a refund, fee waiver, penalty, legal statement, or payment-processor argument not found in the source notes.
  • Stop if the customer message could sound accusatory, threatening, or like legal advice.
