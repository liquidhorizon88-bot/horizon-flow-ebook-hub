Trash Chute and Bulk Pickup Notice Checklist for Property Managers

Use this source-card worksheet when a resident asks about a trash chute jam, overflow, bulk-item pickup, missed haul, odor, contamination, disposal rule, access issue, or possible fine. It keeps replies fast while stopping AI or rushed staff from inventing vendor timing, fees, lease violations, health claims, responsibility, or pickup guarantees.

Best use

  • Resident asks where to place a bulk item.
  • Trash chute is jammed or a room is overflowing.
  • Staff needs a safe notice before mentioning fines or vendor pickup.
  • An AI draft must not promise pickup times, blame residents, or create lease/policy language.

Why this needs a source card

Trash and bulk-pickup questions often combine resident behavior, sanitation, vendor schedules, city rules, access, camera evidence, leasing policy, and fee language. A small mistake can turn a helpful reply into an invented fine, an unsafe odor or pest claim, or a false pickup promise. This worksheet keeps the answer tied to verified sources.

Resident-safe acknowledgement

Hi [Name], thanks for reporting the trash/bulk-pickup issue at [area]. We are checking the current property record and vendor/source details before confirming the next step. We do not want to guess about pickup timing, fees, responsibility, or access from the message alone.

If the issue may affect safety or sanitation

We are routing this for property-team review because the message mentions a possible access, sanitation, pest, odor, or safety concern. Any official notice, fee, or vendor timing will come from the approved property process after the source records are checked.
