Pool Closure and Chemical Incident Checklist for Property Managers

Use this source card before a leasing office, resident-services team, or AI workflow answers a pool, hot-tub, spa, chemical-balance, contamination, health-department, or reopening question. It keeps the reply helpful without inventing safety clearance, chemical readings, reopening timing, fee credits, legal conclusions, or vendor/inspector status.

Copy/paste pool closure source card

POOL-CLOSURE-CHEMICAL-INCIDENT-READY
Resident / unit / contact channel: ________________________________
Amenity: pool / spa / hot tub / splash pad / deck area / other
Closure reason source: chemical reading / contamination report / equipment issue / weather / lifeguard/staffing / health-department notice / vendor instruction / unknown
Date/time closed and who authorized: ________________________________
Verified source record: vendor ticket / pool log / inspection note / manager note / incident report / health-department notice / none
Any injury, illness, accessibility, discrimination, harassment, safety, legal, insurance, or media issue? no / yes — STOP AUTOMATION and route to manager/legal/qualified professional review
Reopening criteria approved? no / yes — source/approver: ________________________________
Resident-safe acknowledgement: “Thanks for checking. The pool is temporarily closed while we verify the approved source record and reopening criteria. We will post/send an update through [official channel] when the responsible reviewer confirms the next step.”
CRM label: pool-closure-source-needed / pool-chemical-review / pool-vendor-ticket / manager-review-needed / resident-update-sent
STOP AUTOMATION before promising reopening time, chemical safety, refund/credit, health-department status, legal conclusion, injury/illness cause, or vendor arrival.

Safe resident reply snippets

1) Temporary closure acknowledgement

Hi [Name], thanks for asking. The [pool/spa/amenity] is temporarily closed while we verify the approved source record and reopening criteria. We will update residents through [official channel] after [manager/vendor/qualified reviewer] confirms the next step.

2) Missing source record

We do not want to guess about safety, timing, or credits. The team is checking [pool log/vendor ticket/inspection note/manager approval] before confirming the reopening plan.

3) Escalation required

This involves [safety/health/accessibility/legal/incident/fee-credit] review, so it has been routed to [manager/vendor/legal/qualified reviewer]. We marked it as [CRM label] and will not promise reopening or compensation until the source record is approved.

AI drafting prompt

You are helping a property-management team draft a resident-safe pool closure or chemical incident update. Use only the verified fields below. Do not invent reopening timing, chemical readings, health-department status, safety clearance, vendor arrival, credits, refunds, legal conclusions, injury/illness causes, or amenity-access exceptions. If facts are missing or the issue involves safety, health, accessibility, legal, insurance, media, or compensation review, output NEEDS MANAGER REVIEW. Return: 1) short acknowledgement, 2) verified source record, 3) missing information, 4) manager/vendor/qualified-review route, 5) CRM label, 6) STOP AUTOMATION reason.

Human review checklist

  • Confirm the closure source before stating why the amenity is unavailable.
  • Separate a courtesy acknowledgement from safety clearance, reopening, or compensation decisions.
  • Use only approved health, vendor, inspection, or manager records for reopening criteria.
  • Escalate injury, illness, accessibility, legal, insurance, contamination, or media-sensitive issues.
