AI Decision Log Template for Small Businesses

Small AI decisions pile up quickly: one prompt becomes a team habit, one vendor gets connected to customer data, or one automation starts sending drafts. Use this decision log to keep owners, reviewers, data boundaries, and rollback rules visible before the workflow spreads.

Best fit: owners, ops managers, office managers, consultants, and small teams testing AI prompts, automations, workflow changes, or new tools without a formal IT governance process.

Use the log before the decision becomes a habit

  • Record the exact decision in plain language: what is changing and where it will be used.
  • Name the owner and reviewer. If nobody owns the decision, do not roll it out.
  • List the approved source data and the data that must stay out of the tool.
  • Choose one rollback trigger before launch: bad customer output, too much review time, privacy concern, or unclear ROI.
  • Do not invent approval history, usage data, performance lift, compliance status, or customer consent.

Copy/paste AI decision log columns

Decision date | Decision owner | Reviewer | Workflow/tool/prompt | What changed | Why now | Approved source data | Data not allowed | Customer impact | Risk level | Test group | Success metric | Human review rule | Rollback trigger | Next review date | Final status

Decision card template

Decision: [plain-language change]
Owner: [person]
Reviewer: [person]
Workflow/tool/prompt affected: [name]
Approved use: [internal draft / customer reply draft / research / admin / other]
Source data allowed: [specific documents, CRM fields, notes, public pages]
Source data not allowed: [private/customer/financial/medical/legal/secrets/etc.]
Human review rule: [who checks what before use]
Rollback trigger: [stop/pause condition]
Next review date: [date]
Status: [proposed / testing / approved / paused / retired]

5 decisions worth logging

  1. New prompt shared with staff: who can use it, where it is stored, and who approves changes.
  2. Customer-facing draft workflow: what must be checked before any reply, quote, or policy wording leaves the business.
  3. AI vendor trial: what data is allowed during the test and what stops the pilot.
  4. Automation trigger: what starts the automation, what it is allowed to send or change, and who monitors failures.
  5. Retirement or rollback: why a prompt, tool, or automation was paused so it does not quietly return later.

Safe AI prompt to summarize decisions

You are helping a small business summarize AI workflow decisions.

Use only the decision-log rows below. Do not invent approval history, success metrics, usage data, compliance status, customer consent, or owner names.

Decision-log rows:
[paste rows]

Create:
1. A five-bullet summary of active AI decisions.
2. A list of decisions missing an owner, reviewer, source-data rule, rollback trigger, or next review date.
3. A risk note for any customer-facing workflow.
4. Three next actions for the owner.

Stop and write "NEEDS OWNER REVIEW" if the log has missing fields or unclear authority.

Owner review checklist

  • Can a new employee understand what the decision allows and forbids?
  • Is there a named person who can approve, pause, or retire the workflow?
  • Does the log separate verified facts from assumptions or AI suggestions?
  • Is the next review date close enough to catch early problems?
