Lead response SLA checklist local service teams can adopt this week

A lead response SLA is not a corporate document. It is a short promise the team can actually keep: who replies first, how fast each lead type gets touched, when to escalate, and what never gets promised without human review.

Copy/paste lead response SLA

Start with targets that match current staffing. A realistic SLA that is reviewed every Friday beats an aggressive promise nobody can keep.

Lead source: {{phone | web_form | Google_message | social_DM | referral | marketplace}}
Lead type: {{urgent | estimate | booking | question | warranty/callback | stale_lead}}
Target first reply: {{within_5_min | within_15_min | within_1_hour | same_business_day | next_business_day}}
First owner: {{role_or_person}}
Backup owner: {{role_or_person}}
Escalation trigger: {{urgent_safety | high_value | angry_customer | no_owner | no_reply_after_target}}
Approved first reply script: {{script_name_or_link}}
CRM label/status: {{new | contacted | needs_info | booked | estimate_sent | follow_up_due | closed}}
Customer promise allowed: {{what_can_be_said}}
Customer promise forbidden: {{price | arrival_time | diagnosis | guarantee | discount | refund | policy_exception}}
Review cadence: {{daily_queue_check | Friday_20_min_review}}
Metric to watch: {{response_time | booking_rate | follow_up_due | no_owner_count}}

Suggested starting windows

  • Urgent safety-sensitive requests: reply as soon as staffed, route to the dispatcher/owner, and avoid diagnosis or exact arrival claims until confirmed.
  • New phone/web leads during business hours: aim for a first touch inside 5–15 minutes when staffing allows.
  • After-hours non-emergency leads: send an acknowledgment and next-business-day window instead of pretending the team is dispatching now.
  • Estimate or quote follow-ups: assign an owner and due date so the lead does not stay in “sent estimate” forever.
  • Stale leads: cap the sequence and stop after the approved final closeout message.

Escalation and stop rules

A response SLA should protect the team from overpromising. These stop rules keep AI drafts, front-desk replies, and CRM automations inside safe boundaries.

  • Do not invent availability. If the schedule is unknown, say a dispatcher will confirm the window.
  • Do not diagnose from a short message. Ask for photos or route to a qualified human when risk is involved.
  • Do not quote final price. Use approved estimate, inspection, deposit, or financing language only.
  • Do not ignore opt-outs. Respect SMS/email consent and stop rules immediately.
  • Do not blame staff from one data point. Improve routing, ownership, scripts, and queue visibility before assigning fault.
