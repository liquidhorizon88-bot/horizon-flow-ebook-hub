Why this deserves a worksheet

Snow and ice reports sound simple, but winter property messages can involve vendor schedules, changing weather, accessibility requests, documented injuries, municipal rules, insurance records, camera footage, owner budgets, and liability-sensitive wording. A rushed AI reply can accidentally promise a treatment time, say an area is safe, admit fault, deny responsibility, or discuss legal obligations before the manager has verified the record.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not let AI state that an area is safe, unsafe, negligent, compliant, inspected, treated, salted, cleared, liable, reimbursable, or legally required without manager/source review. Do not invent vendor ETAs, weather observations, camera findings, injury conclusions, insurance advice, accommodation decisions, fee/fine outcomes, or legal deadlines.

Lease/policy/municipal/insurance/legal question raised? [yes/no + manager/legal review]
Accessibility/accommodation concern raised? [yes/no + human review required]
Approved escalation owner / due time: [maintenance / manager / vendor / owner / legal / insurance]
CRM/PMS label: [snow-ice-hazard / sidewalk / winter-access / vendor-follow-up / injury-mentioned / accessibility-review]

Resident-safe acknowledgement snippets

Hi [Name], thanks for reporting the snow/ice concern at [location]. We logged it and are checking the site condition and maintenance/vendor record before giving a specific update.
Thank you for the details and photos. I am routing this to [maintenance/manager/vendor coordinator] so we can verify the location, current condition, and approved next step. We do not want to guess about timing, responsibility, reimbursement, or safety status until the record is checked.
Internal handoff: Winter hazard report for [property/location]. Type: [type]. Evidence: [photos/source]. Sensitive flags: [injury mentioned/accessibility/legal/insurance/vendor ETA]. Source records needed: [work order/vendor log/site inspection/weather note/policy]. Do not state fault, liability, safety status, treatment completion, reimbursement, or legal duties until reviewed.

Fast QA before any answer goes out

  • Does the draft avoid saying the area is safe, treated, inspected, negligent, compliant, or cleared unless the source record proves it?
  • Does it avoid promises about vendor arrival, salt/sand, snow removal, reimbursement, insurance, fees, lease enforcement, or legal responsibility?
  • Does it route fall/injury mentions, accessibility or accommodation concerns, emergency-access problems, and legal/insurance questions to a human owner?
  • Does it keep the follow-up owner, due time, vendor/source record, and exact location visible?
  • Does the resident get a useful acknowledgement while the source record is checked?
