AI Request Intake Checklist for Small Businesses

Use this worksheet before a teammate asks, “Can we use AI for this?” It turns a loose idea into a reviewable request with a business owner, approved source data, customer-impact level, and clear stop rules.

When to use this

Run this intake when someone wants to create a new AI prompt, connect a new AI tool, automate a recurring task, summarize customer notes, draft customer-facing copy, or publish AI-assisted website content. The goal is to approve useful experiments without letting unowned, unreviewed AI tasks spread through the business.

Request namePlain-English task, not a tool name: “draft missed-call replies” or “summarize weekly customer feedback.”
Business outcomeTime saved, quality improved, faster response, safer handoff, or better owner visibility.
Approved source dataDocuments, CRM fields, policy pages, price sheets, job notes, or examples the AI is allowed to use.
Forbidden dataPrivate customer details, payment data, legal/medical/HR facts, unapproved pricing, or anything outside the tool boundary.
Customer-impact levelInternal only, customer-facing draft, public website content, pricing/policy, or high-risk decision support.
Owner and reviewerOne person who owns the workflow and one person who signs off on facts, promises, and tone.
Definition of doneWhat a good output must include, what must be checked, and where the final approved result is stored.

Approval checklist

  1. Does the request solve a real workflow problem? Reject vague “use AI more” requests until the requester names the task and outcome.
  2. Is the source material current? Check policy pages, pricing sheets, service limits, and customer notes before allowing AI to draft from them.
  3. Can a human review the output quickly? If review takes longer than doing the work manually, simplify the request or pause it.
  4. Is there a stop rule? Name the exact conditions that require owner review, manual handling, or no send/no publish.
  5. Is success measurable? Pick one metric: minutes saved, response time, edit rate, fewer missed handoffs, or fewer reopened tasks.

STOP AUTOMATION rules

  • Stop if the request would let AI invent customer history, approvals, prices, discounts, deadlines, policies, legal wording, hiring decisions, or outcomes.
  • Stop if nobody owns the final review or the requester wants outputs to send/publish automatically.
  • Stop if the source data includes private customer details that have not been approved for the tool or workflow.
  • Stop if the request changes a business promise, refund/warranty policy, service-area rule, or pricing detail without owner approval.
