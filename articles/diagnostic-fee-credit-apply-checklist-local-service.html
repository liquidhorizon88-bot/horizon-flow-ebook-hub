Diagnostic fee credit and trip charge apply checklist for local service businesses.

DIAGNOSTIC-FEE-CREDIT-APPLY-CHECKLIST-READY — Use this worksheet before a CSR, dispatcher, estimator, owner, technician, or AI-assisted reply says a diagnostic fee, service-call fee, or trip charge will be credited toward a repair, waived, refunded, or applied to an invoice.

Use when

  • A customer asks whether the diagnostic fee applies to the repair.
  • A tech recommends a repair and the invoice needs clear credit wording.
  • A CSR drafts a quote follow-up that mentions trip charges, waivers, or refunds.
  • An AI draft is about to invent a discount, credit, fee policy, or repair approval.

STOP AUTOMATION: do not invent fee policy or invoice credits

Do not let AI, autoresponders, or rushed scripts promise that a diagnostic fee is credited, waived, refunded, transferable, or included in the repair unless the quote, invoice policy, service ticket, and owner/manager approval prove it. Escalate before making claims about refunds, discounts, warranty coverage, financing, taxes, trip charges, or final invoice totals.

Customer-safe reply snippets

1) Policy needs review

Hi {first_name}, we are checking the job record and fee policy before answering whether the diagnostic charge applies to the repair. We want the update tied to the approved invoice rather than guessing.

2) Credit confirmed, amount still pending

Hi {first_name}, the diagnostic fee is marked for review against the repair invoice. We will confirm the exact credit/total on the written estimate or invoice before you approve the work.

3) Fee does not apply automatically

Hi {first_name}, the diagnostic/service-call fee does not automatically change the repair price unless the written estimate or manager approval shows a credit. We can review the record and send the confirmed options.

4) Repair approval handoff

Hi {first_name}, before we proceed, please review the written estimate. It will show the approved repair scope, any diagnostic-fee credit if applicable, and the amount that needs approval.
